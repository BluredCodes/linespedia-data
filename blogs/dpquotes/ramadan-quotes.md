❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ): Ramadan 2026

When does Ramadan 2026 start and end?Based on astronomical calculations, Ramadan 2026 is expected to begin on or around February 18, 2026, and conclude with the celebration of Eid al-Fitr on March 20, 2026. However, as per the Sunnah of the Prophet (peace be upon him), the official start depends on the sighting of the new crescent moon (Hilal). //”Fast when you see it (the moon) and break your fast when you see it.” — Sahih Bukhari.

What is the most powerful Dua for Ramadan 2026?While any sincere supplication is beloved to Allah, the most authentic and recommended Dua—especially for the last ten nights—is the one taught by the Prophet (ﷺ) to Aisha (R.A):
“Allahumma innaka ‘afuwwun tuhibbul ‘afwa fa’fu ‘anni”
(O Allah, You are Forgiving and love forgiveness, so forgive me). — Sunan At-Tirmidhi.

Can I fast if I missed the Suhoor (Pre-dawn meal)?Yes, your fast is still valid as long as you had the Niyyah (intention) to fast before Fajr. While Suhoor is a highly recommended Sunnah full of Barakah (blessing), it is not a mandatory condition for the validity of the fast. //”Take Suhoor, for in Suhoor there is blessing.” — Sahih Bukhari.

How can I stay spiritually motivated if I am busy with work or school?Ramadan is about Taqwa (God-consciousness), not just ritual. You can earn rewards by:

- Listening to the Quran during your commute.
- Keeping your tongue moist with Dhikr (remembrance of Allah) while working.
- Maintaining excellent Akhlaq (character) with colleagues, as the Prophet (ﷺ) said: “Fasting is a shield; so when one of you is fasting, he should neither indulge in obscene language nor should he raise his voice.” — Sahih Muslim.
5 What are the best deeds to do in the last 10 days of Ramadan 2026?The Sunnah emphasizes increasing Ibadah (worship) during the final stretch. Focus on:

- Qiyam-ul-Layl: Night prayers (Tahajjud).
- I’tikaf: If circumstances allow, secluding oneself in the Masjid.
- Sadaqah: Increasing charity, as the Prophet (ﷺ) was most generous during this time.
- Seeking Laylatul Qadr: Searching for the Night of Power in the odd nights (21st, 23rd, 25th, 27th, and 29th).

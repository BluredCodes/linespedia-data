MORNING SLUMBER

A hand softly touches her shoulder
Gentle, insistent shaking 
She slowly opens her eyes
Searching the room for whoever it is
Nothing, she is alone
But she can feel a presence unseen
She stands and walks to the window
The sun just cresting over the hill
Casting dawn’s shadows over the meadow
Drawn to the door she steps outside
And makes her way slowly across the lawn
She feels the dew on her bare feet
As she continues to the meadow
Daisies peek through the tall grasses 
She stops here and there gathering her flowers
Feeling a hand in hers as she wanders
Hearing his soft whisper in her ear
Stopping she lies down in the damp grass
Closing her eyes she listens to the birds’ morning song
She awakens in her bed, released from the dream
There beside her, a bouquet of daisies

title: "A Tower Times Two"
writer: "DoubleD"
slug: "a-tower-times-two"

As I sit at my desk, at the end of the hall,
At the top of the building, 
Called the World Trade Center;
I feel a rumble, and I hear a crash
One so loud, and so blunt;
There is fire, and smoke
Along the walls, windows, and floors;
I wonder if KaMorge will be in to clean;
Along the corridor, and down the hall;
Are shouts and screams of a fire;
And another bang and another rumble,
And I feel as though I am weightless,
As though I am on a ride like the Mission 2 Mars; 
I fall, but for only a minute or two,
And a pile of ruble 12 tons, more or less, 
Falls onto my back, 
And the backs of the 5,000 people still in the building;
I lay there still as I can, 
For moving helps no more than lying, 
I gasp for air, but there is none to be found, 
I move my arms around to try to create an air pocket,
And I see light, Some one else is moving too;
But the shadowy figure is one that I can not remember,
Short, white, with spots of black is this stranger, 
And I feel as though I am dead, for there is no such creature;
I hear a calling, for a woman named LaKisha, 
I feel as though she didn't make it,
My named is called out, 
By a woman, one I have never heard before,
I try to scream out to her, but my voice has left my body;
I grab at my throat, with the little room I have,
And I feel a thick liquid leaving, At once I think its blood,
A red mist leaves my mouth, as a brick is moved from my head;
And a creature from heaven has come to release me from my body;
The woman yells out my name again and shakes my broken hand, 
I try to smile but the pain is too much to bare;
At the hospital I hear about the crash,
Two hi-jacked planes have hit the building I once worked in;
At the top of the World Trade Center,
At the end of the hall,
In my own little desk.can you tell that the stranger (from heaven) is a dalmation?

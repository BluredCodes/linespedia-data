There is a garden not of flowers and beauty.
The garden blooms year round not with colorful petals,
but rather with stones of granite evenly spaced.

Each rock is planted with a seed of death.
Each root reaches six feet under.
A garden which needs not rain nor shine.
The water the stones thrive on is the tears of
those who visit the garden.

It is a garden of death, darkness, honor, villany, and respect.

                josef david

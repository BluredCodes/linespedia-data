A cool shade of new
A new shade of cool, not so
	Graded gray-blue
Red horizon red aligning, redefining the view
Fosters ultra “V” declining, grants a clearing,
	Spurn the glare and stare in secret
			Anonymity in tinted hue.
Bar the framework, frames the brow
Bar the “T” bar, bridge the golden matching now
	A shaded spectacle, bright fades in dissipation
				Shadows dark accentuation
Sky blue burnout cools a better blue
Greenfield waves afire, made just hot enough to cool.
I see you
So do you, 
See only you, not I
Reflecting dark, concealing smiling eyes
Cognito gazes casting
Casting back the stares, indifferent,
	Curiosity refracting.
Prideful suave your cool delight
	No longer seem they all that bright
Rapid fire brush-off with a glance
Veil the window, power black displays that give them not a chance
	Polarize and victimize
		Photo-gray to scrutinize
			Darken down to fantasize
				Lighten up to realize,
				Be realized?
	-Retreat!
		Double mirrored smoke protection
			Screen the ego refugee
		Hide the fatal self-reflection
			Do they answer, “Look at me?”
		Can’t you say?  
                You can’t see?
	Shade reflecting shade
	Dark reflecting double darkness multiplied
	Shades the fashion, true opinions hide
	Like all others all alike unknown
	Alone in shaded self-concealing symmetry
		(Vanity?)
	More than cool, a spirit freezing mystery
	Serious in taken all too seriously?

Vuarney vanish, how about it?
Ultra coolness banish, sans synthetic visual tint?
Chrome flash motorists will doubt it?
Teach them starlight moon awaiting, meanwhile squint.

Such this self-imposed reflective third degree
See me clearly, look the look return, the optic soul of me.
Heed the warning, let the seer true be seen,
Shaded false reflections past redeem.

Sober thoughts my friends and I examine and review
	Then smiles or devilish grins are traded as we,
	Spurning revolutionary sunglass liberty
Once more don our tried and true, ultra cool, graded blue hypocrisies
	And flash a final double shaded  ‘Jokes-on-you-know-who,
						Hey-ain’t-we-cool!?’Another College Years composition (April 14, 1985).  Most ambitious work I did back then - also easiest and most fun.

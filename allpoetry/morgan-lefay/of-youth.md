What wonderment do little faces hold
As through their eyes the world is glorified
What gladness is theirs whose souls are never old
And whose thoughts are always justified
Oh merriment of childhoods sweet fancy
Oh beautiful delight of its laughter
Where have you fled to hide yourself from me
Why have you gone to leave me here after
No wrong did I ever do to you
Yet you have passed and I am only left
My glow of youth lost to an elder hue
And my mind and hands are no longer deft
But oh to sit and dream in solitude

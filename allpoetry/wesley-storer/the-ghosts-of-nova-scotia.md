---
title: "The Ghosts of Nova Scotia"
writer: "Wesley Storer"

Where thundering breakers claim
The sea sprayed rocky maine
Sailing spirits forever remain
Blood kin ties to salty brine
The arctic terns joyful climb
Mid light swept circling beams
Where peaceful  ocean's lull
Transcends to furied  squalls
Like jealous lovers at a seaman's ball
Maddened by love's hellish call
There in the land of Winslow Homer
Pilgrim Fathers and John Paul Jones
Where swelling swales of aqua tones
Ceaseless tales call the sailor home
To its freedom's peace of mind
With jib and beam and mermaid dreams
Rubies and Emeralds golden gleams
Buried treasure by tropic streams
Where thundering tides pound the main
And the sea sprayed breakers reign
The sailor it claims forever remains

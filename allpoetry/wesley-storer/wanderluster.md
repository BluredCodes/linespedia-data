---
title: "Wanderluster"
writer: "Wesley Storer"
slug: "wanderluster"
source: "AllPoetry"

Where daisies glow like summer snow 
Clusters of gin berry juniper grow 
Frisky nocturnals furry frantic play 
In mossy glades of emerald jade 
Query from what calling to what end 
Do rustic aspens quake in wind 
Wrapped in raspy golden shrouds 
Of symphonic lost eternal sounds 
Where songs of eagles and the lark 
Lay seige in dreamings of the heart 
With crystal breaths of fragrant May
Visons endless scintillating grace 
While fluted calls of bushy deer 
Kiss the indigo glazed veneer 
Where verdant shadows in patience wait 
The comming Autumn's crimsoned wake 
Such solitudal silence wanderers seek 
Down rutted roads with restless feet 
Mother wanderlust so kindly bestows 
For wanderers grow old well she knows 
Seeking the worthiness of her soul 
Her blankets of comfort she'll unfold 
For they are hers and hers aloneYes indeed, I am a nature lover, and my idea of a perfect world is one where all people everywhere can travel anytime they want to their heart's delight.

The Cold Gray Stone 
 (Song for George Jones)

All of my life I've wandered alone
Never found nothing to call my own
Just a lonely old highway walking alone
Walking along like an old cold gray stone

Living for nothing leaving nothing behind
Heartaches and trouble with no peace of mind
Just a lonely old soldier walking alone
Walking along like an old cold gray stone

refrain

( I always forgive Christ told me forgive
Don't know if God will that's up to him
On that gray morning When I'm dead and gone
Won't even find me on an old cold gray stone )

An old cold gray stone Lord walking alone
On some lonely hillside where nobody roams
Never found nothing to call my own
Walking along like an old cold gray stone

I went down to Texas seen the Alamo
Wondered what it felt to die far from home
Knowing your goin' to an old cold gray stone
They never got one so I'm not alone
Lonely old flowers over old cold gray stones
Never found nothing to call my own
Walking along like an old cold gray stone

* author's note

The heroes of the Alamo were not buried,
but gathered together in heaps and burned.
Years later, what was left of their ashes, 
were gathered up and placed in a corner of St.
Guadalupe Catholic chruch in San Antonio. It
was years later, before proper monuments were
set up.

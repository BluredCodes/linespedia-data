WONDERS OF A SENIOR’S LIFE


It’s seven AM, just barely light
The mall is still closed for business
But still people are arriving.
How strange to see men walking in a mall
Not being dropped there by spouses
Caring ahnds or shopping for last minute gifts
Women walking briskly, step by step,
Swinging their arms in time
But no answer do I hear,
No beat of a drum to march by
People of all ages, descriptions, are found within these walls
New freinds are made, conversations abound
It’s a large indoor mall
Places to shop, explore, meet friends
Crowded by day and night, bustling people stride
Making their bodies ache with strength never known
Hopes of healthy limbs and minds abide
These are the joys of the mall-walker’s life.

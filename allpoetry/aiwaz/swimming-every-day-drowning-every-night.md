---
title: "swimming every day, drowning every night"
writer: "aiwaz"
slug: "swimming-every-day-drowning-every-night"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/39289-swimming-every-day--drowning-every-night-by-aiwaz"
---

another long night of rain,
and water falls like sheets of eyes,
thunder drums the sea
like a child slapping at waves.
i dreamt i was a sailor
sleeping on the ocean floor
the fish fluttered by my fluttering lids
like breezes from a lover's hold.
if evening finds me here in cold and black
in this swaying moss of coral claws,
return my oars to distant shores
to burn in winter cold
and drive the darkness back.
life is for the living
and in this dream of life i took not one step back,
asleep all ye swimmers
who drown in memory black.
to think, to act,
to stroke against destroying waves
and swim with strength we never lack
takes more than starry faith,
but the swimmers stroke, without hope,
must all the fickle currents obey.
chart new routes
find deep port
float breadcrumbs to find yr way,
swimmers will always drown
but sailors will sleep til day.

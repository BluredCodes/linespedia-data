Ancient town of cobblestones and inns,
anchored in mysteries of whaling men,
your history breathes of Ishmael
and fog-pooled piers, the lighthouse
and widow's tears.
In sunshine your quaint avenues
smile comfortable and smug.
But reality does not lie in harpooned whales,
instead, whispering for recognition,
it lurks in misty darkness:
in the shroud of manors veiled
and the tombs that troll the depths,
and in the sighs of spirits hovering
'neath the patrolling beam's caress.

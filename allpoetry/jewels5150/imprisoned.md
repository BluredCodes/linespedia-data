Slender beams of accusation enter
this darkened chamber as I kneel,
always a slave, always somber,
frozen here,
waiting.

Haloed forms wrought in panes of glass loom as
dust dances in the air,
forming an image in my mind,
sparing not my shamed flesh.

Blood on a child's face.

I raise my head, now crying out for
this oblivious mortality.

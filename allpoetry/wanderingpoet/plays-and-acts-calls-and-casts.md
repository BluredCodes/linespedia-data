Players play as the merry, 
Loud and gay, Sprayed the room with Alcohol 
Players stayed in the wisps of May 
As the Final Curtain Fell. 
Yet David stayed on the stage 
Away from the alcohol and the Players gay 
Remembering days of 
Plays and Acts, Calls and Casts 

Age attacked David’s Aching bones. 
He sang aloud once more 
This day of merriment left for him only one thing 
Not gay play 
Nor festivities playing in the heat of May, 
But a cage of players who have played their last act. 
No more 
Plays and acts, Calls or Casts 

The Player recalled an age of achievement and assonance 
An age of accusation in his acting. 
Youth had seen him as a wonder, 
Attacking audience with his amazing acting 
Sprouting sonnets and playing acts along with the best. 
During his academy days, professors had seen him bow. 
During his playing days audiences had seen him fly, 
Aviate above them, alleviate their ails for minutes at a time. 
Elevating them higher and higher with every act he cast. 
But the age for him has passed, this day 
His Last Play and Act, Call and Cast.Unknown,

Children at the table
with Boyardee 
or bacon in the skillet
(cheese toast if it snowed)
chatterings behind the aluminum tumblers

Then, with a breathy slobber,
siblings whisper
in hushed stressed laughter,
(things that Mother can’t hear)-
we belly laughs to tears

Toe to toe, ‘neath  broken chairs,
feet touch in this funny for keeps
the secret forever sealed
from youth’s treasury
joys hardly ever returned
chiseled mem’ries in jeweled stone~

Vanilla thoughts
from yesterday's vaults!Written March 12th, 2002 ©  on Oct 30 2002 11:19 AM PST, M. Dianne Grotius Berry    other

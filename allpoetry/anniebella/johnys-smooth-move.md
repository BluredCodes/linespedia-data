Mary was a "Harvord girl"
Or so he folks would say
"Her beauty and wit whould take her far"
But fate wouldnt have it that way

Johny was a "mixed up kid" 
Who's mind would often wonder
His curiosity and attitude
Would often lead him a-sunder

Mary drove a pick-up truck
Saving up for college
Went straight home to hit the books
Adding to her knowledge

Johny drove a nice corvette
Roomy enough for all his buds
Parties and bars night after night
Where ever they found the suds

Mary worked at Pick-A-Flick
To earn much needed dough
Watched love stories all day long
And wished she had a beau

Johny was a ladies man
Women heard he came from money
Was said he could have any girl
Cause his kisses taste like honey

Mary couldnt help but wonder
What it was like to have some fun
Just to cut loose and let it all go
And maybe love someone

Johny stopped by Pick-A-Flick
Depressed he had no date
Took one look into Mary's eyes
And decided it was fate

Mary knew she shouldnt have
A mistake ever so slight
Could mess up what she's worked hard for
But, still, she agreed to Saturday night.

Johny wanted to impress this one
She was different than the rest
He would pull out chairs and open doors
And try to act his best

Mary wore a cool red dress
Nervously bit her bottom lip
took one last look in the mirror
And slid in her last hair clip 

Johny pulled up in her driveway
Revved his engine once or twice
Turned it off and went to the door
He was speachless- she looked so nice

Mary was dropped off at midnight
Reluctant to get out of the car
Let herself be kissed on the cheek
Afraid she might go too far

Johny went home happy
Almost disposed of his little black book
Studied it carefullyand then decided
To give it one last look

Marry had almost forgotten
She borrowed Jahny's hat to cover windblown hair
And with careful thought and a final decision
She drove it straight over there

Johny wasnt all that bright
Thought he could have his cake and eat it too
Dialed a number out of his precous book
And over came Betty-Sue

Mary smiled from ear to ear
Couldnt wait to see Johny's face
Was amazed when she drove up
Betty-Sue's car was in what was s'pose to be her place

Mary, devistated by what she'd seen
Didnt want to go home just yet
Decided to drive til she ran out of gas
And see how far she'd get

Johny turned on the news the next day
To hear what he'd feared the most
He had called all night long
Mary had never came home

Mary's eyes had filled with tears
She's wipes them away so she could see
But the tears came back again and again
And she ran into quite a large tree

Johny went to counceling
To hear he wasnt to blame
He paid good money but it didnt work
Gee-what a shame

Mary was a "Harvord girl"
Or so her folks would say
"her beauty and wit should take her far"
But fate wouldnt have it that way.

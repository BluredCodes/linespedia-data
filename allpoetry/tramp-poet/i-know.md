I Know

I have known the despair
of losing one’s child.
The hopelessness, the anguish
the inability to act.

I have known the pain
of being homeless.
The possession of a key
seemingly out of reach.

I have known suffering
teeth falling from ones mouth.
Bones broken and never set
not a dime to spare.

I have known hatred
being attacked for appearance.
Singled out for abuse
because one was different.

I have known evil
as it lied, coaxed me.
Tempting with dreams
laughing with defeat.

I have known poverty
no clothes, no food.
Nothing left but despair
I know of these things.

And through it all...
I know in my knower;
that in God’s eyes
I am precious.

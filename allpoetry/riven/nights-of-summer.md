The midnight air blows across my face
Too hot indoors so I sleep outside
In this surreal place
Opposite of my state of mind
I do not wish to define the place or time
The thoughts of when I dream
Chalkboards that were once green
They had their share of equations
And vocabulary words
But now as I lay here
The only sounds are that of insects and birds

I can't sleep
I leave the back porch and take a seat on the grass
As you join me
Old friends talk and the time begins to pass
Now I can rest
Woken up an hour later
Pitch black as the thunder roars
The ones outside seek shelter indoors
The night thunder and rain
Is music to my ears
As I think about past experiences
And past years
I look over to see if you're okay
And see that you are
As I now patiently await the coming dayBased on true story.  Too long to type.  But it took place last summer in Texas at a lakehouse.  I slept on the backporch with some people and ended up talking to one of my good friends for a couple hours and then we became better friends.  Later that night there was a huge thunder and lightening storm.  Pretty cool

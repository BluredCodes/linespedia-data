An old man as old as the year
Came to me one eve
And warned me of a future near
A grave prophecy he preached
"Happiness in seclusion is what you seek
Why else would you be in the night desert by fire
If isolation was not your desire?"

He had good points and views
Showed me love could not be abused
As the flames flickered 
And danced a wicked jig
The answers could be found
I just have to know where to dig

He told me my quest had not even begun
"Too you of age
In time you will see the unison between moon and sun"
The midnight air had a bite
But I realized his words were right

Seize the day or something along the lines
Do not worry about the sands of time
In time with wisdom
The sands will pass and grow
As the seeds of mankind will be sowed

His words guided me into a state of sleep
I awoke the next day
With the rising sun
The fire had died, but not inside
My journey had just begunFor some odd reason something struck me when I was writing this in my notebook last night around the second stanza.  I was overcome with some feeling

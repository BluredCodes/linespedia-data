Matthew was the one
That sat in the back of class
He knew how to write
And he could write fast
Livin his life on skid row
With mortal devils
He always went with toe-to-toe

Poetry was his outlet
The best way to get his feelings down
He looked at life differently
And was able to walk on the other side of town

Years of abuse made him emotionally week
Father would drink a bottle of Jack
And hit him like concrete
Daddy split and left mom the kids
Odds were against them
Constantly raised with the bids

Now he writes in his notebook
That he carries with a pen
Writing images of love
The future
And an original sin
Nobody paid attention to the Silent Genius

Matthew had only a few close friends
And kept a secret from them for all time
People assumed he was fine
Until his mother found him
Slit wrists and at the end of his line
His mother found the notebook
And published the stories his mind would tell
Of teenage heavens
And teenage hells

Writing images of love
The future
And an original sin
Nobody paid attention to the Silent GeniusThis was supposed to be a song at first.  But oh well.

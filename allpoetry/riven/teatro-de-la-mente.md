Late one night to insomnia I awoke
The dark air around me had a tight grip
And began to choke
I put on my shoes and decided to take a walk
Hopefully it would clear my head of voluminous thoughts

The city lights
Illuminated the midnight air
And all I could do was gawk and stare
I came across an old theater
One I went to back in the day
I would come here on the weekends
And watch the stars of the silver screen play

The door was open just by chance
So I walked in and with nostalgia I began to dance
I tore the ticket I gave myself
And the memories came flooding back
I walked down the hall
And in a theater I found myself alone
I sat down in one of the worn out seats
And used the one infront of me to prop up my feet

The images on the screen
Seemed strangely familiar
And then I realized what they were
Images from my dreams
It seemed odd that all of this was true
Suffering from a case of deja vu

I shouted, "Hey who's running this flick?"
I went up to the projector room to see
And the sight suddenly made me sick
A skeleton with my face
I awoke in my bed
As if I had never left this place

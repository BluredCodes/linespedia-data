expectations in life-
 the things they want you to be.
 the lies to tell,
 the rules to break.
 remember the rebellions-
 the silent oaths you take.
 all those secrets-
 you can't trash,
 or your ass could be grass.
 remember the silliness-
 your youth,
 the perfect innocence you once had.
 then abused in  your life-
 with infatuations and confrontation,
 remember the days...
 of when,
 when you couldn't choose-
 your choice was all made;
 now those choices get tough-
 you may kneel and cry.
 expectations to you-
 what to do before you die,
 the days of purity leap by.
 adolescence at full force-
 it leaps off a page.
 into your face-
 comes virginity and pride,
 words now make sense.
 and your heart breaks with doubt,
 the confusions overbearing.
 oh to hide from thee!
 expectations of one-
 none you see,
 could you be?
 worthless or ordinary,
 my god could this be?
 you set the goals-
 accomplishment and peace.
 throughout one's life-
 expectations won't cease,
 quite prevailent they'll be.
 oh you live-
 your life you like,
 with out the sin.
 you like goodness-
 and pray hard from with in,
 release my soul;
 help me be good.
 let me expect heaven-
 heaven to welcome me,
 cos it's the place i want my heart to be.

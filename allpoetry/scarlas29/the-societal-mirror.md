Looking in the mirror,
 The mirror of society-
 Pressure points of perfection,
 Acupuncturing my soul.
 Plastic surgery to my being,
 Not the way society perceives me.
 Natural build of slender,
 Natural grace of spiral curls-
 To my shoulders.
 I can look unto the mirror and wish these things,
 To be banished from me.
 Let me be a carbon copy,
 A slave to the waves of trends-
 Society places unto my feet;
 In the mirror of society.
 Instead of self-acceptance,
 I can crave…
 I can wish…
 Different objects to be part of me.
 Spend the dough,
 To be a creative “sculpture”-
 A model of medical discovery.
 Forget natural anatomy,
 And have plastic inserted upon my heart-
 Where it can seep and soil my tarnished, superficial cravings.
 Societal imagery,
 The fake, unimaginable trash-
 Many pray to be?
 Society lies, with airbrushed Hollywood scripts-
 Of fake realities we don’t need.
 The beauty we hunger for-
 Can’t be established by society,
 It must blossom from within;
 Be discovered through tribulations,
 Starved ribs won’t excel your esteem-
 It’ll tear you down and kill,
 Softly and slowly, with insanity,
 As the ribs and plastic become what you used to be.cheers to britney spears!

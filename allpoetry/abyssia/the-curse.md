---
title: "The Curse"
writer: "Abyssia"
slug: "the-curse"
source: "AllPoetry"

---

Come inside 
My self-made cage;
Hold it dear, 
As it is not your own. 
Tame your urge
To abuse it,
I beg -
My special curse 
That lives within us all.

Casual in essence, 
Potent in force,
The hassling burden
That strips away our will,
And closes the solid metal doors
Behind which true freedom lies
With a harsh slam.

To own it 
Cannot be realized,
As hidden obscure 
From the eye of consciousness, 
We are owned
By our varied blessings;
Made prisoner 
By the will of our hearts.

I welcome you 
Into my world of restraints.
A strange, yet 
Welcome vacation
From your own inadequecies.

Make yourself at home 
In this cell of 
My moral's making;
Find a part of me
That benefits your cause 
And tuck it away 
To remember me by - 
Confined it holds no use... 
To me anyway.
For embracing the curse 
That binds my thoughts 
Has shaped my life

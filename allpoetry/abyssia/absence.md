---
title: "Absence"
writer: "Abyssia"
slug: "absence"
source: "AllPoetry"

---

Your presence took with it a
Piece of me, only to replace
It with a staggering gravity
That weighs down on
The wings of the clock -
Her ascension plummets
And she is reduced to
An agonizing crawl.

I long for the days when Time
Sky-rocketed us through Her storms.

The shadow of your absence
Looms over my bedroom window.
Nights of rest turn into
Dawns of madness,
For the bed has become
Suddenly much too big - 
I fear it may grow teeth
And swallow me whole. 

Our laughter in the midnight has
Left me with a horrible empty ache.            

Vulture panic tears
At the carcass of
My stillborn character.
You sped by too quickly
On the highway of treachery...
This hitchhiker awaits the 
Smell of burnt rubber, and
The neon red of brake lights.

Your smoke has left me gasping.
I ask you just one thing -

Let me back in the passenger seat.

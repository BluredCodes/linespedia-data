In the beginning - 
The rain came hard,
And the hefty breeze
Always just overcame
The warmth of the sun 
That beset our faces.

Rainbows were few
And far between,
And never quite so bright
Against the clouded sky
As the ideals that we 
Had painted as children.

Then, out of nowhere
A seed was planted -
And with a little rain,
A little more sun, 
And a lot of patience,
Roots were formed. 

It seemed it took forever
Before it finally bloomed.
The suspence seemed consuming,
Though in retrospect short-lived,
And often I wondered if our
Labors were a fruitless effort. 

In time the roots sprung to life,
Limbs grew thick and strong.
Branches extend, reach skyward
For the heavens to kiss angels' wings,
And tiny twigs and leaves appeared,
Whose vitality weaved a joyous blanket. 

A shelter from the showers;
Shade that comforts from the
Raging mid-day summer sun.
Vibrating with a growing force,
Tall and beautiful to the eyes
Of all within it's view -

My love for you grows stronger,
More lovely,
Higher, 
And more pure,

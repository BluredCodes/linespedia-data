The Vanishing Act


It was on a beautiful moonlit 
winter night when old Ernie 
in his lonesome cabin died.

His dog howled its sorrow to
the oblique moon which 
through a dusty window shone.

For days it sat by his side till 
hunger tolled and it took a timid 
bite of Ernie’s upper arm.

Spring, people came looked for
Ernie everywhere, this vanishing
act a mystery yet to be resolved 

They patted his mournful dog, 
which looked remarkable well? 
Fed and gave it another home.

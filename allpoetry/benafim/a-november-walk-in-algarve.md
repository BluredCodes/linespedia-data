November Walk (Algarve)



It had been raining all night, quiet rain 
that caressed the roof tiles of my cottage.
By mid morning it stopped and in early 
afternoon the ground was dry enough 
for a long walk. Bambi, my dog, was 
ecstatic she liked hunting for rabbits, 
never caught any, like me she was too 
slow, too fat and made too much noise 
We followed our usual track, minutes 
from the road and anyone who didn’t 
know the area would be lost. This time 
of the year (November) the landscape is 
very green from shimmering verdant to 
serious olive, only tall, bragging weeds 
resist the temptation to conform and are 
resolutely yellow. While my myopic dog 
raced clumsily after something she thought 
was a rabbit I found one, a bunny, sitting 
very still by the exposed root of a blighted 
tree, picked it up stroked its neck and ears, 
soft like a cuddly toy and it looked at me 
without fear, put it down again told it to run 
away, it didn’t. By now Bambi was back, 
saw the bunny and quickly killed it, the only
time she ever caught a rabbit

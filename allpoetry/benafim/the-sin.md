The Sin.



The sky was immense, limitless, off school 
on wooden clogs, I flew across a clover field
till I was stopped by a mundane bird which 
appeared to have a broken wing.
With a stone I killed it 'cause that's what one 
does with wounded birds…Right!

Then I noticed its nest, four modest green 
speckled eggs, the product of a bird which wasn't
going to win any beauty contest. In self disgust
I trampled on the eggs, an omelette of hate, since
they were not going to hatch anyway.

Looked up, the sky was no longer limitless, but
cryptic and I said: God, forgive me."
I was met with deep ear shattering silence and
knew from that moment that there is no God,
and that I've to carry my cross of guilt alone.

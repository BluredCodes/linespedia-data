Sharing a Moment


With sticky fingers a girl of three
touched my knee and offered me
a sweet which I accepted but didn’t
eat in case she wanted it back 

I was sitting in my café at the time
reading the news and since I don’t 
know what to say to a three year old
child returned to my paper.

I was deeply moved by the child’s
gift that letters on the paper blurred.
The girl said something I didn’t 
catch, then went back to her mum.

I only looked up when mother and
daughter left they both smiled and
waved. I smiled too and when they 
were safely out blew my nose.

It was the pleasure and pain of
this little encounter that I tried 
to share with you, but you are 
always so busy and bristling.

You said something about “Spilt
Milk”, and returned to your PC.
Guess I’m better off keeping my
sorrow and pleasure to myself.

The New Year Man



Naked and shivering he awoke on the strand 
of life, all while the ocean of forever lapped 
at his feet. Kind people gave him a woolly 
overcoat and told him that he was a king for 
a year and they hoped that his reign will be
of perpetual peace. Warm in his coat it had 
once belonged to a senile dictator- it still had 
pockets full of boiled sweets he used to hand 
to children on his walk about- his first act was to 
abolish free speech, that all those who didn’t 
agree with the western way of life should hence 
forward be regarded as terrorists and what is 
dizzily called democracy, where winners take 
all and losers live in penury, will be the only 
legal force, that criticism is sedition and 
disbelievers of our great democratic system, 
shall be sent to an island and shot one by one 
until all oppositions have ceased and gloomy 
peace reigns from pole to pole till ice caps melt
like vanilla ice on a summer’s day.

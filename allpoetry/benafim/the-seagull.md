The Seagull


A large seagull, spotless white and
grey, circled my cottage three times.
So beautiful that first I thought it was
an angel in disguise, its eyes held 
all of nature’s wisdom, aristocratic
its beak like a roman senator, but
not a Brutus though for he was fat
and looked like Marlon Brando.


I noticed that the seagull only had 
one leg and knew then that it was
the king, of the reefs where the outer
sea begins, looking for a retirement 
home.” You’re welcome to stay on
the roof and terrace” I said. It nodded
nobly flew high, and on mighty wings
glided back to the outer sea.

Harvest.



The morning was hot and humid 
a breeze dragged itself along 
a boiling asphalt road, tired of sifting 
through discarded junk, it tried to get 
lift by releasing lumps of damp air.

A forest of humans were stuck on 
a molten asphalt road they couldn't 
scream as humid air had rusted their 
vocal cords into stillness.

Then giant, green crocodiles came and
tore them off just below knees, 
dragged bodies down to the stream, 
they were as quiet as trees cut down 
by a chain saw. 

The road looked like a bombed out
rice paddy and on the lazy river 
bloodless bodies bobbed up and down 
while green eating machines feasted.

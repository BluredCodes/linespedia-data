The Meeting


When I drove to the meeting 
the evening was dark, the hall
badly lit, cold and chairs were 
creaky and hard.

One by one they told how 
happy they were now that they
were no longer in the grip of 
fearful tyrant alcohol.

They spoke about anxiety, hate
and resentment, like they were
unique that no one in the world
suffered like them.

A lull, I felt that I was expected
to speak, told lies and wondered
if I was the only mendacious 
person the hall.

Meeting over everyone seemed
happy but me, it was raining 
when driving home and knew 
that I had missed the message.

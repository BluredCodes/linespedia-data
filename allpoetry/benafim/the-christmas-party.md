The Christmas Party 


Outside the block of flats 
I could hear the mind numbing
beat of drums and trumpets.
Seven floors up and I was met
by African hospitality, food
drinks and warm embraces.
Grapes of love I drank and 
herbs of hope I smoked till 
I lost track of time. The beat
kept on and on and blissfully
not a carol was sung. 


Dawn streaked across a yawning 
night sky when walking back to 
my hotel through a park named:
Heavenly Peace… and so serene
was the morning that the shyest 
of creatures, sew rats doomed to 
a life in darkness and hated by
mankind, had come out, bathed in 
dewy grass; shrieking and baring 
teeth in delight for once able to 
see the promising light of dawn.

…And So We Die a Little


Died a little last night, staggered 
about light-headed and numb. Feet 
didn’t follow brain’s command.
Wished the phone would ring; didn’t 
expect it though, three in the morning. 
Not much fear I was prepared, but 
wished someone would be here with me.


Tried to make coffee, hands useless 
spilt milk on kitchen floor Stumbled 
into bed left side of my body useless.
Waited for the moment, fell asleep, 
awoke at seven, numbness gone, 
morning sun through windows and 
sparrows, on the roof, argued as usual.

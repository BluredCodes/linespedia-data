The Sporting Life



I don’t really like fish, when angling 
for trout in the river, near our lodge, 
it’s the peaceful scenery and the colours 
of a rainbow trout just caught that I like. 
Mind colours fade fast when it’s wriggling
on the rivers bank fighting for life.

If alone, I unhook the poor thing and 
throw it back into its true element. If not 
I break the trout’s neck, say something 
masculine and try not to throw up.
My wife like fish, she waits for me to 
come home with a frying pan in her hand. 

I eat little (not hungry tonight dear) thinking 
of the snap when I broke their necks.
I do like fishfingers though, five of them on 
a plate doesn’t conjure a picture of distress.

title: "Burial of a Life"
writer: "sweetbrother"

You cradle it in your arms
like a lover,
then polish it to a dusky sheen
looking to see your face reflected

But it is of no earthly use to you
it is not a talisman
that protects you from harm

You can only be
drawn by its seductive power
like a moth to a flame

Now, your only course
is to light candles for it
and say an incantation

With your newly-calloused fingers,
scoop up handfuls
of the crumbling soil
that makes your bed-
and lay that thing to rest
then turn and walk away
for you must go
and build a new one.

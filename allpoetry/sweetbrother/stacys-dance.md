title: "Stacy's Dance"
writer: "sweetbrother"

I watched her dance
on Beale Street,
her long hair flipping in the breeze,
slender limbs in constant motion
as if worked by puppet strings
in the hands of laughing gods

I had seen her, just that day,
reserved and cautious like a scholar
but then night came
bringing a crescent moon
and Stacy's reckless passion
I sat intoxicated by her supple movement
but did I dare to tell her?
No, my flirtation
was brushed back
with a casual flip of her haughty head

I have seen her
bundled like an Eskimo,
the only hint of her dangerous grace
a look of defiance in her smoldering eyes

Beale Street reveals her secrets
to be peeled back in the layers
of her careful resolve
and her nocturnal dance
leaves me spinning
into the depths of helpless passion

I have seen her in renewal
of those seeds that fell in fall.
I have traced her errant footfalls
as she stumbles or enthralls.
With a musty taste of rousing
on the surface of my tongue
I have walked her moldy pathways
bathed in rays of warming sun.
Caught shy glimpses of bright yellow
from the birth of daffodils
on those mellow colored mornings
when soft green anoints the hills.
I’ve rejoice in flights of  sparrows
singing high upon the wing
She has touched me in the moments
and the minutes that are spring.

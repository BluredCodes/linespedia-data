---
title: "Bullying The Flies"
writer: "Ouijammin"

Several spiders sitting on a sill 
Asked spider riddles as they drained their kill 
With mouths so small and voices so meek 
No human had ever heard them speak 

Diamond asked, as he slurped quite loudly 
And Linus answered and smiled proudly 
Pasha whispered a secret puzzle 
That Carmela solved with a merry guzzle 

Down below on the bathtub lining 
The brother of the prey heard the spiders dining 
He gnashed his mandibles and took to the sky 
And screamed, “ Clever spiders, Prepare to Die!” 

Carmela looked up trembling, “Trouble” 
Pasha hid behind a large soap bubble 
Linus spun a thread out of pure dread 
Only Diamond managed to keep a cool head 

Adrenaline pumping, the fly swooped down in low 
Across the windowsill a wind started to blow 
The wind blew the bubble trapping Pasha inside 
Legend says she cursed soap the whole time she died 

Carmela and Linus both witnessed the fray 
And decided to save pride for an auspicious day 
As the two deserters made a sluggish retreat 
The fly plunged from the sky and took fifteen of they’re feet 

Diamond looked up his eyes bleeding with hate 
And belched in sarcasm, “Was that your kin I ate?” 
The fly hovered in horror and Diamond launched a fine thread 
That was sharp as a needle and pierced the fly’s head. 

The spider chuckled cruelly at the fly who lay dead 
Then turned to see a shadow closing in on his head 
The black and white print was growing larger in size 
And hypnotized diamond through his prism-like eyes 

The newspaper read, New York Times Sunday Edition 
Under the title in blood was Diamond’s addition 
“ Massacre On Windowsill, Several Spiders Die!” 
And the moral of the story is don’t Bully the flies

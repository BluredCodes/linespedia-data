Below the lines
I find myself
cut and bleeding
on the basement floor.
Reaching for the lamp string
to find the light,
to touch my fingers 
to something pure.
Something warm.
I pull the string
and fall;
light illuminates
on my pale shoulders.
Reflecting the cold
of the cement floor.
I gather myself
bleeding from 
my discarded soul
and I weep
for you,
my ignorance,
and myself.

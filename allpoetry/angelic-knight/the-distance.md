Stormy day
Rain won't stop pouring
Why does death strike so hard?
Blown away
I'm no longer soaring
Lord, I simply can't go this far
Anyway
This lion's stopped roaring
Clay's all dried up in this jar
Down to lay
My tears are of mourning
Lost a loved one, now taken afar

I'm drowning in tears of woe
This burden's too heavy, please free my heart
I failed to save him, now he has to go
Confused and empty, my soul's ripping apart
Why does this nightmare have to be so?
Lift my spirit, Lord, I'll make a new start
Lift my spirit, Lord, I'll make a new start

[REFRAIN]
I'll go the distance
That I once could not see
I'll shout the message
Even if I'm dropped to my knees
I'll be the person
Better than I ever could be
I'll go the distance
The slaves gotta be set free
I'll share the life you've poured into me

A ray of light pierces the storm
Death is but a beginning to live
His spirit's elsewhere, no longer need to mourn
To you, life is but a gift to give
You know the death, for you once were in my form
I guess that's why you're so willing to forgive
I guess that's why you're so willing to forgive

[REFRAIN 2X]

Now the light is filling my soul
I stand up, knowing you're in control
Crying no longer, I burst with a shout
Death isn't final--there is a way out
He's watching me from a cloud above
A smile appears, full of pure love
Thus I know my path's only begun
Out of my way, devil, there's a race to be won!This was a song I wrote during my senior year of high school, when I was even more an amateur writer/musician than I am now.  This was written to reflect on losing my grandfather in '96, and how it changed my life forever.  I did a rough recording of the song with a friend who would lead me to become the guitar player I am today, and I also did a theatrical performance of this song in my theater class my senior year.  This one serves as a personal reminder of who I am even today, and always will.  Coming back to this definately brings back memories . . .

The night is young 
the sky so grey 
Scorched with the souls that have died today. 
And down in the trench 
A man’s body there lays 
and will be refereed to as MIA 

but while out in the ocean 
A ship sinks to the floor. 
Down to the bedrock, to be seen never more. 
While surrounded by oil 
survivor’s cry and they plea 
to be taken aboard, out of the fiery sea. 

And as POWs lie 
about their rank and their name. 
They're put through much torture, and even more pain. 
But still others die 
after telling the truth 
for the scares that some carry, are substantial proof. 

And finally our men 
return to their homes. 
Only to find them destroyed crosses laying in rows. 
But some shells are still grounded 
hundreds of soldiers enraged 
how could this have happened, the wars so far away? 

They could just imagine 
the words they would say. 
To the mad man that broke the Rules of War on this day. 
But civilians still died 
and soldiers will fall 
and survivors will wonder how they survived through it all. 

But if the Geneva Convention 
had not happened at all. 
All hell would brake lose, and millions would fall 
But that’s why we have 
the Rules of War today. 
To keep battle from going, completely insane.For those who don't know MIA is Missing in Action and POW is Prisoner of War.

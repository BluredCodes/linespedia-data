He would have been eleven today,
Eyes glinting by candles, making wishes,
Dreaming of things he would never have
Places he would never go.

Instead we drive through granite rows of
Immortalized names, yellow flowers
Nestled in my lap as his coffin had been, 
Silently rocked by his last lullaby.

Sweet little one, we sing for you today
To the rhythm of the raindrops 
On your gravestone.

“State”


Morning came and gave me shock
For in my dreams the life seemed sweet
But simple sun rays can’t deplore
From telling him our love’s no more

My head it twisted into bed
And grappled with the threat of day
The oft-planned words I had to say
Describing love that now had fled

He in his chair and ambiance
With claims of morning, news, and state
Not letting in the truth I sent
Not hearing what I had to say

I clamored for his bartered ear
Sung it from my broken soul
He turned but couldn’t escape my song

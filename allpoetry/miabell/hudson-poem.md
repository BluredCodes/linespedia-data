These hills here
Differ, from those of home
     And blue in their rolling
Stretch behind the river like a
    Napping man
Mild and solid, yet terribly unsmooth
    While the green of 
The banks bubble heartily
    With life, these trees the
Strong, warm arms of mother
    Protecting, undying, and warming
The long body of the man
    As he lays, she cycles,
Each spring a new dress, each summer
    In love, they ripen and release hundreds 
Of sounds
    The warm whisper of winds, the crickets
Rhythmic thumping, and the rains’ honest cry
    In balance, the sun
Is willing to fall

*Poem Concluding Of My Christian Faith*

A Most Peculiar day

Today, was one like any other day,
but being the day, after the snow-
Ice patches were all over the road,
yet there was one place, I had to go-

Schools were opened, duty called,
thinking, here I must go again.
But being safe, I let my van thaw,
For a great fog had rolled in-

I couldn't see 50 feet down my street.
Being leary, I wanted to do it right.
I used my signals, every turn met,
being nice to any driver in sight.

I finally got my son to his school,
after waiting and waiting to get in.
The bridge was closed the other way-
I thought patience would run thin.

All of us drivers had the same mindset-
Concern for another will do us well,
because everywhere I went, was calm,
I think we each have a story to tell.

Today was really a most peculiar day;
For everyone on the road was too kind.
Something I've rarely seen happen,
Something that really blew my mind.

I dropped off my son, said good day.
Then, for the longest I waited to go out.
With hundreds of cars stalled, one way,
Teachers kept pointing to another route.

Finally, being in the far left lane,
I motioned a teacher I'll turn right;
she acknowledged, consented, gave sign-
And I got away from that little plight.

Another dreadful day, danger everywhere,
I finally found the road back to home.
Other places with risks had their share.
But on the road no one wildly roamed.

I saw no speeding nor heard horn blowing,
It was a very peculiar day indeed.
Drivers allowed broken lane crossovers,
it was agreed, no accident was in need.

I was in a lane end, that's big trouble-
The guy let me over in front, I waved-
saying thanks, for we often struggle;
Quite possibly, my own life was saved.

While passing the last light, being clear,
I thought wow, this has really been nice;
A pleasant journey and a lesson learned-
Being thankful, I was safe on the ice.

After seeing people be so decent today,
when I turned, relieved on my street.
I thought- up there, God has His way-
where Divinity and humanity meet.

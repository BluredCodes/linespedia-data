---
title: "The First Symphony"
writer: "duderag"
slug: "the-first-symphony"
source: "AllPoetry"

The city lights reflected into the rippled river like a thousand stars
Shining, shining all at once and with such orchestration 
As if practiced into perpetuity
I took breath slowly, deeply
Taking in light, taking in sound as if part of the still night air
Now as throughout the night I turned to centre stage
The symphony began...
Her sweet smile invited the violins to a perfect pitch
Her eyes were introduced by the passage of strings
Composed of brass, so bold, so blue
Yet intricately intertwined
With soft and demure notes flowing from flutes 
I closed my eyes and took my place in the orchestra
Trembling I touched her body, each touch, each caress, a single note
Played with such hesitancy, yet with so much surety
Swept up in the symphony of sound, of colour and passion
I kissed her lips, and like a final crescendo
Batons raised high and drawn ever so slowly low
The kiss like beating drums and crashing cymbals
Was brought back to the soft touches of sweet stringed notes
In silence now, all I could hear was my beating heart
Cheering, cheering, cheering...

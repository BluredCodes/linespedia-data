I never knew my biological father
Always hated him
But needed him to be there
Or at least thought I did

What I didn't realize was that I had you, all along
To teach me to play baseball
To help me learn how to ride a bike
To guide me and mold me into a man

I never realized that you were there
I always resented you
That you were not my real father

I see now
That I was wrong
You are the only father
I have ever known

I never thanked you for that

Thank you

Thank you for being there
Thank you for teaching what I needed to know
Thank you for protecting me
Thank you for being the best father you could be
Even though I was a rotten son

I'm sorry

I got through 19 birthdays without him
I got through my first date without him
I went to the little league playoffs three times without him
I got a great job without him

I am going to go to college without him
I am going to marry without him
I am going to be a father myself, someday, without him

Who needs him?

You were there for 19 birthdays
You were there when I left for my first date
You were there at all my games
You were there when I got that job

you will be there then I start college
You will be at my wedding
You will see your grandchildren

I'm not glad he's dead
I wasn't sad when I heard the news

This poem is just to say
I'm sorry for never being there for you, as a son
Like you were there for me, as a father


And to say
Thank you for being there for me
Even though I never knew it at the time

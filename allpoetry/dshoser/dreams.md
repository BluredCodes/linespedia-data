Dreams

I hate my dreams 
For in them seems
To lie the truth.

And I can’t hide
For there abides 
In sleep a sleuth

Who with magic
Often tragic
Will uproot

My tree of life
From which I strive 
To harvest fruit.

But if I weep not
For my loss
My dreams produce

Another tree 
That cannot be
Yet I deduce

From its perfume
Here lies the womb
Of deeper truth

And in its shade
When I am laid
Will fall its fruit.

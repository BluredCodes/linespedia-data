The Crippled Dancer

There was a crippled dancer
Who could not stop to rest
Leapt she ever, fantasies 
Of endless pirouettes.

The twisted boned reality
Of faithlessness of flesh
Demands she dance on endlessly
She dare not risk to rest

For dancer’s spirit, left long furled,
Time surely would ensnare,
And strike the twirling dancer down
To a cripple in a chair.

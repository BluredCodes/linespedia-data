A Mother’s Hope


Why this feeling of doom?
As I witness the animosity
Of my sons toward each other

What could I have done so wrong
While you were at my bosom
To turn each of you against your brother

I have loved you both the same 
Would have given my life for either of you
And would to this day

But your young comraderie has turned to hatred
Though you’re both now middle aged 
Still the anger has it’s way

I hope, though my heart is filled with despair
For you both to rekindle the love
God offers through his grace

To fill your lives with renewed brotherhood
And  renew a mother’s hope 
By accepting each other in God’s embrace

By Dee Garner

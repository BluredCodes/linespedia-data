On Getting Old (a rambling)

According to what I read and hear
I’ve become what is commonly known as ‘old’
I strongly object to this generalization, of course

For old is …well… old, and that I’m not
Just because some of the symptoms are there
Like a few wrinkles, (from smiling, that’s all)

My hair has lost some of its golden highlights
But silver is almost as good, isn’t it
Besides, I’ve discovered Lady Clairol, so no matter

I’ve grown hair where there used to be none
And don’t grow it where it used to be
But that’s just a change, doesn’t mean I’m old

If it takes me a little longer 
To get from here to there, it’s okay
Not much time left maybe, but it’s all ‘spare’

Speaking of ‘spare’, I have others
Like the spare tire I carry around my middle
And the spare belly hanging below the other one

Well, I’ll admit there are a few things sagging
Like my boobs and my butt just a little
But never my spirit

Over all, for the shape I’m in
I’m in pretty good shape, mind and body
Getting better all the time

See, I’m not old, just improved with age
I’ll tell you like it is, for sure
If you don’t believe me consider this

I’m much wiser than before, much bolder
Have complete control …well, almost anyway
Of my life and what goes on in it

Bet you never thought of ‘old’ people still making love, did ya?
Well, we do, and it’s better than ever
Middle of the day, middle of the night….anytime

I have the world at my feet, just waiting
For me to decide what I want to do next
Take a trip, go fishin’ or just plain nothin’

How many of you ‘young’ people can honestly
Say you can do what you want, when you want
And still have it be legal and moral

Look at me, I do that and even more
Us ‘old’ folks have the advantages of age
And we can take them all if that’s what we choose

Like senior discounts at restaurants
And the picture show and museums
Even our property taxes are discounted, so there

Are you envious yet? I would be, I think
If I knew then what I know now
I’d never look at age as old or young

For we’re as old or as young as we feel
And you better believe…I’m as young as you
Maybe younger, even if I don’t look it

Just one more of the advantages of being ‘old’
I can ramble on like this and get away with it
But I’ll try to cut it short now, for you

Impatient youth as you are
Someday you’ll understand what I’m saying
Savor what you have now, cherish your youth

Let every moment count, mean something
Youth wont last forever, except if you allow it to
In your mind and in your heart

So when you look at me and see an old lady
Don’t fool yourself, I do all the things you can do
And more…with the advantage only age can bring

Some advice though, my dears, be happy, 
Smile a lot, love a lot, take responsibility for yourself
Try not to hurt others, most of all, live life to the fullest
 
So that in years to come when others call you ‘old’
You can look them in the eye and say
Old is in the eye of the beholder 

.....your day will comeLike I said... just rambling....

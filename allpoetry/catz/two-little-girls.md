Two Little Girls

Two little girls dressed in yellow pinafores
Playing happily at Mama's feet 
Always in the way as she goes about her chores
Their energy boundless, their laughter so sweet

But she doesn't seem to mind so much
She stops her work for awhile
It does her heart good, you see
To hear their laughter, to see each smile

The twinkle in her eyes reflects the glow within her heart
If only they could stay this way
To never know the pain of the world
Or the stress she feels each day

My sister and I were once like this, she thought 
No cares, no tears, no sorrow
My two little girls can stay like this
Happy and carefree forever, at least until the morrow

Dee Garner November 26, 2000This is about my sis and me, our mother used to always dress us alike and I can remember a particular day, in our yellow pinafores, playing on the kitchen floor and mamma stopping to hug us and talk to us.  Now, having raised my children and been a part of my grandchildrens lives, I've often thought about how the world must have been when I was a child, how it is today and how it may be in the future.... and I think my mother must have had similiar thoughts when I was a child.

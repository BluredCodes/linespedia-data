When First We Practice To Deceive

I thought about it,
before I opened my mouth
to utter those false words to you
I thought about it, 
planned on what I’d say
the deed done, the story untrue

We’ve always been so honest 
up ‘til now anyway
I believed you and you believed me
Never a doubt, not for an instant
each word between us so true
and now this, how can it be

I told one lie, then tried to explain
but then another came out
crap, why did I say that, why
I should start all over 
tell the truth from the start
I almost made it then began to cry

Damn, if I’d only told the truth, 
would’ve been only one hurt instead of two 
but I told another lie
Just to cover up the first one
never again I said to myself
this is terrible, I’d rather die

As soon as I get out of this 
it’s the straight and narrow for me
back to the old honesty and trust
But…now that you believe this
I could fudge a little more
Cripes! stop this, I must

One lie leads to another
where do we stop, when does it end
what can I do to make you believe
Can we make it back from here
what could we be thinking
when first we practice to decieve

Dee Garner
November 10, 2001

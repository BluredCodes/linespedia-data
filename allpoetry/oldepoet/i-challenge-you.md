I challenge you
I challenge myself
And I challenge life

Can you tell me one simple tune
That danced away from this summer June?

Up, where and above in the sky
Do tell me where the moon abides,

I put my palm faced down to the ground
I look and see and say, why do I play?

Tulip, such beauty contain within,
For I didn’t say flower for it hasn’t bloomed within the hour

It’s an opera of the stars!
While day belayed it warmth around, why?

Tingles! What daring nerve my skin should feel
Love, What mystery it carries; long soulful deep

Does and is it ever a reality
For a poem to understand with a sane-full mentality?

-Nick Audet

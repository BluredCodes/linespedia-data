---
title: "Passover  Lamb"
writer: "Channa"
slug: "passover-lamb"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/7274-Passover--Lamb-by-Channa"
---

I was 'baptized' in the river of Jordan
not on the lake of Teheran
I did not dwell in Constantinople
I came to live with my people
Yet they did not recognize 
nor did they realize
That it was I
who set them free 
from bondage and slavery
I walked with them in the desert
For forty years I did assert
the leadership over these children
and freed them from their burden
I became their Passover Lamb
they did not understand
it was I who lead them on
under that hot scorched sun
I gave them manna for food
they did not obey as they should
now here I am in their mist
broken and pierced, I didn't resist
I died for them, they cannot see
that all along it was Me 
who bought them out of slavery
they turned their back, they denied
the sacrifice that satisfy
the penalty that was theirs
I paid gladly to make them heirs
I am that Lamb who was slain
for perfection you couldn't attain
I came to earth to save my own
another they'll have, me they disown
I love them with an every lasting love
I am the Bread of life, the Light from above.

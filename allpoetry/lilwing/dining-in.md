Dining In

I gave a pity party,
     to celebrate just me,
and I invited, not one, 
     nor two or three.
I invited only you my love,
    to come and sup with me.
I set the table as of old
    my finest white china,
trimmed with gold.
    Richest linens to unfold,
crystal goblets bright,
    clear and chilled cold.
Flowers arranged, with
    colors, strong and bold.
I asked you dearest,
    please sit down. I
joined you at the table
    in your favorite nightgown.
I poured the wine
    and raised my glass,
a toast to us my dear,
    I hope you do not mind,
for lonely me, just one last?
    This cup now empty, I
can fill with tears.

So come love and with me
     celebrate our finale.
You may wear your vest
    of drollness and jest,
a laugh or two at me,
     by your punnish wit,
on this elegant occasion 
    would suit it best.
After all I do not ask
    just anyone to observe
these precious tears of mine.
     Darling, this is you know
no more than you deserve.
    Come liven our splendid table!
Have I not given to you,
     Exoctic foods, rare delicacies,
Spared no expense, a dish
     of heart and rue,
Easy to find, but so hard
     to prepare.
Dearest, tell me, how did 
     I do?
What? the party is over,
    You leave without dessert?
Ah  yes...I remember, it is true
     dessert was the very
first course I served to you.
     Leave your chair, vanish!
From this room take your coat
     woven of lustful dreams.
Begone, my pity party has
     begun in earnest.
It is time, oh foolish man, 
     that you I banish.

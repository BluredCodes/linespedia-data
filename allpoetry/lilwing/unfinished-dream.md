Unfinished Dream

Now the purple-pink streaks
have vanished from my sight,
leaving only the heavier blues
that deepen into black night,
as they lie o'er the silent dark
of the ocean's still green,
pressing on the horizon, drawing
the last of God's daily scene.

The sea gulls are stacked neatly
in row upon silent, sturdy row.
No sand rustles or shifts as the
wind rests, stirring nothing below.
In the quietude of sunset gone,
the intermezzo of sleeping time,
my heart awakens, hoping for dawn,
only to find, no sun giving gleam.
There is just the remnants of my
unfinished dream.

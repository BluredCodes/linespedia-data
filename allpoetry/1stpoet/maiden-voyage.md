Maiden Voyage

He called her the Emerald Sunset
for her eyes were as blue as the ocean
and her hair the color of sunsets

she was his new vessel
anchored and weathered from storms
there were cracks in her hull, and the brass was tarnished
Yet to him, she was divine

When the weight was lifted from the floor 
of the ocean of tears she was berthed in
the winds of his song filled her heart like a sail

she moved slowly at first
saying Captain, my Captain
there are rocks and storms beyond the shoreline
I feel the journey is not safe

He calmed her with a steady hand on the wheels of her mind
steering her clear
of the breaking waves that before had caused shipwreck

Onto the open seas they ventured
hoping to find the lands of Nirvana
that lived in the mind of dreams

William S. Dawes

Black Widow

I was languid
for love had left me wounded
You were an arachnid
waiting for a feast

You watched me with what seemed like
Eight eyes, for you missed not one step
As the web woven by music caught me
I danced upon wings towards you

The dim lit bar, and alcohol
had me buzzing frantically
and as I approached to ask you to dance
I could not see the tattoo on your back

A red hour glass
my demise, for time would soon run out
and I would never have this life again

Your kiss as we embraced
was like venom.
I lost all strength, 
and the will to run vanished

As your arms spun me
I could feel the breath within leaving
trance-like I became
entombed

When the music stopped
I found myself lifeless
motionless
sucked dry.

William Dawes
December 7, 2000

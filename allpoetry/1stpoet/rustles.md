Rustles

Walking, I became intrigued by faint footsteps
rustles in remains of fallen leaves, dry
Winter was looming on the graying horizon
and winds sang songs of snow

my hurrying stopped to watch the scurrying
of furry forest creatures, storing for storms of white
that soon would blanket this ground they rambled upon
and cover these oaken morsels they gathered

today the weather was warm
and those fur coats glowed in the sunlight
almost an added weight that would be treasured by Monday
when temperatures would cause teeth to chatter more loudly
than the chipmunks and squirrels 

the moss covered rock I sat upon
would soon have a cap of white
the green would be gone until spring's return
when the sun would burn away an ice shell

I would come back to this place
under more tepid climes
and remember this time

For to this spot, this moment, these
God's creatures, I would be in hibernation
as they, though, would frolic in the winter wonderland
but the memory of this day would be frozen in my mind

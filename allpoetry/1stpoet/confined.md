Confined

He kept her in protected tower
she said to his royal highness
“thou hast an awesome power”
speaking out from behind her shyness

he ruled this, his domain
and her, the captive he gave no light
but it was not in stone and steel that he did constrain
‘twas the strength of love that gave her delight

his arms were the chains that held her
to the walls within his heart
and it was within this coffer
that she never would depart

she looked forward to the beatings
that pushed the love though his veins
these were his pleasant greetings
that tightened the restraints

he whipped her with words of desire
as her fingers left marks upon his back
he would soon ignite the ravenous fire
creative tortures he did not lack

as she screamed at a pitch which shook the room
a wicked smile arced across his face
never would she avoid the doom
that made her own emotions race

this was not your normal Bastille
in which he kept her quite secure
she knew that his love was real
there was no escaping , that much was for sure

king and captive, most content
their roles played out with glee
for with this type of confinement
one was prisoner, yet free

William S. Dawes

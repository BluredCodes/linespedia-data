Black Angel

With wings white, 
the Black Angel (his color of garment) soared the heavens
touching earth with wind from wings
he dusted  sand from a desert princess

She had been in an ocean of darkness
among the dunes she fled, to the light of night
Where dreams were made to chance
and hearts were cold.

She was different there, and knew it
a soft heart remained within walls
built also within mind
The angel knew her feelings, and cried tears to a page

He sensed the love that longed to be awakened
The Nirvana she knew existed was on distant shore
that only he could show her a glimpse of
So he took her there in his flight

She rode in pale moonlight upon his back
strength she needed to rise above and see
that below was not a barren desert, but
an Oasis existed within reach

Now awakened by the angel’s soft touch
she longs for flight of her own
His wings no longer needed…He gives them freely
So she too, can soar the heavens within her mind

Her sleeping heart belongs to a fine prince
who has awakened her with a soft kiss
The Black Angel smiles from the night sky
knowing she is safe again.

William Dawes

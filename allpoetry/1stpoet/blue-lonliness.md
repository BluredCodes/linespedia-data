Blue Loneliness


We wed, and the 
blue ice was placed upon her finger
A finger that began digging its way into my soul
my whole existence poured out by the puncture

I became like a sky with brightest sun
a blue so clear, so calming
Now the sun has gone, and the sky is
as coal, black

I had been Royal Blue, with her my queen
and I her king, cerulean coverings for each other
yet the courts held and now
I am as an ocean, blue from the depths of sorrow

My heart is now blue ice, frozen in time
beating no more to the sounds of whispers
moved no more by the glance from azure eyes
I see the color of Loneliness is , yes, blue

William Dawes

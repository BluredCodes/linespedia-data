Fairy

I see fairies dancing 
In the darkness just beyond twilight
Their reflections dance among the star ripples in the water
As I venture towards sleep by the fire

Gossamer wings with lace tracings
Carry them aloft
Through the tree branches
And trails of stardust fall upon me, their gift

Magically I am taken
By the soft beating of their wings
The spell causing my heart to take flight
Balanced on the edge of a dream

This dream spell, causing moonflight
I am taken to where you reside
For your breath of love is 
Like a pool of light

Awakening in slumber
A half-real state
I feel the moon mist fall upon me
As I fly on the wings of a fey being

I have been transformed to their kind
By a touch so soft
It only comes from the heart
Of those who are free

Free to see the serenity of life
With wisps of wing beats
Carrying this star stream
To those who yet believe

I do not wish to become mortal again
I take my wings with pure abandon
And fly off
Into contented night dreams

In daylight your breath of love
Gives my wings lift
So that I am not trapped in the spell to which they are bound
And I can carry you aloft

William Dawes
June 18, 2000

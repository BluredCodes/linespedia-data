Release

In the hour of midnight
moonless the sky
coal black heavens above
stars but a distant flicker

a break of a branch
twigs snapping
a cry and a sound of a thud
from a creature much larger than I

into the dark woods I race
dodging rocks and fallen trees
I fall and scrape my knees, my nose
broken drips sweet blood to my lips

the animal must sense this
for his footstep sounds are faster
dead leaves give away his position
or I would be seized by now

I feel as a trapped rat
running through this maze of forrest
yet without whiskers I cannot feel my way
and peril draws closer

the ground becomes loose
I hear a roar now different from that of the
beast which chases me
It is the ocean, many feet below

a jump would be speedy
death upon a hard jagged rock
is a fate better than mauling
and being torn apart limb by limb

I leap

William S. Dawes

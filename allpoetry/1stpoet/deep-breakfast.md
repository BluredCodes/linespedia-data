Deep Breakfast

We made love
passionate, consuming
and drifted to sleep

I awoke to make you breakfast
and my thoughts returned
to last night

As I took the potatoes
and onions
sautéing them, I connected to our differences
man and woman, combined as one, yet
separate

The eggs, 
you, sunny side up, the yolks reminded me of your breasts
upward reaching, calling me to break the film of your soul
and enter you…and I became scrambled

As the bacon sizzled, curling up inside itself, 
entangling with the other strips I saw us
the juices flowing   as we  arched, and curled
and embraced
 legs, and arms, bodies intertwined

And in the coffee, 
hot, steamy, and strong as our love
waking us in passion
good to the last drop.

William Dawes
Nov 2000

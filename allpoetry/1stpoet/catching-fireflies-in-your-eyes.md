Catching Fireflies in Your Eyes

At the lake after sunset
the fire crackles in the still air
embers float aloft
as I look at your sweet face

And I am catching fireflies
in your eyes
dancing to the clouds on your soft sighs
this moment catches me by surprise

Drinking champagne at home by candlelight
the bubbles rising in the glass
we toast to the love we are sharing
and by vision, the optical effervescence floors me

And I am catching fireflies
as the sparkles reach your eyes
getting drunk on the sound of your soft sighs
and passions start to rise

Making love in the backyard
as meteors race in the blackness above
I look softly into your eyes dear
and see the comets in your mind

I again am catching fireflies in your eyes
your moans are like sweet lullabies
together we take wings to the skies
catching fireflies in eachother’s eyes

William S. Dawes

Don’t

Don’t brush your hair
with fingertips
letting the fragrance of sunshine carry
the golden glow that was locked
freed with new found warmth

Don’t whisper my name
upon soft breath
the moisture on your lips glistens
as words float upon air

Don’t look upon me tenderly
your eyes glistening in the moonlight
I feel close to death
for I look into heaven’s gate

Don’t lie with me naked
for the tunnel of your wanting begs my entrance
If I loose myself within your boundaries
shall I be found again?

As I speak these words
I only ask one thing.
Don’t ever stop loving me

William S. Dawes
February 10, 2001

Elements

I opened my hand to you, and held fire
you opened your heart, and gave me ice

Glacially stoic you stood there
time had left you barren, the glare
even in your eyes gave evidence of the depth
its grasp has had

Frozen in this moment, as if winds had dropped you
stunned like a flightless bird

I cupped you in my hand
releasing my fire for life into your still body
the chill entered into my skin

as the flames licked your wounds
you began to melt
the water and fire mixed producing a thick cloud of steam

Oxygen and hydrogen, splitting
currents of air again began to lift the feathertips of desire
the hydrogen increasing the flame as I consumed it through nostrils flared
by the fragrance of daffodils and lilacs, the perfume at first obscure due to the frost

in the waters that dripped from your cheeks
I saw my road to nirvana
the droplets cascading down your neck, between your breasts
to where your heart still yearned for warmth

I touched your lips with my finger, they parted
I lightly touched my mouth to yours, my tongue, like a flame danced
the result was instantaneous as the pulsation within your chest flushed the remaining chill
from deep within you

the chemistry was there, the carbon, oxygen, hydrogen
our skin glowed like neon, as we both began to burn

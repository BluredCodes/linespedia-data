Shrouded

She was shrouded,
and my perceptions of this angel were clouded

upon her head she wore an enormous hat
what color hair was under that?
was it red as fire, or golden like straw
I have no idea, the hat was all I saw

glasses on her face secluded her eyes
were they green as grass, or blue like the sky
I have no idea what was behind those frames
of her eyes was she ashamed

she also wore this enormous coat
within my mind visions did float
I could not tell if she were thin or fat
so staring from my park bench I just sat

upon her legs were these baggy slacks
the color gray of railroad tracks
oh, just a glimpse of those legs
please oh please, this I beg

I could not even see her feet, she wore big shoes
girl watching is giving me the blues
I would love to touch her hands of love
but you guessed it, she even wore gloves

of how she looked, this I asked
covered by a clothing mask
she still remains the girl of my dreams
since nothing of her is ever seen

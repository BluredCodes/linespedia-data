Cry Wolf

It was high noon that Friday
the first one of the month
like clockwork the civil defense sirens blared

commuters and the like
housewives, bankers and such
acting as if nobody cared

they were used to this drill
the electronic trill
that was meant to be a check

but none realized
that out of the skies
a storm their lives would wreck

the clouds appeared as bread mold
an ominous green, blue-black shade
from which a  funnel would soon unfold

but just like before
when those sirens would roar
in bliss the thoughts were ignored

it was like crying wolf you see
for in complacency
many went about their lives

not knowing per-say
this would be the day
some lost friends, children or wives

So during the drill
the storm came at will
and caused such a demise

the next time I hear the wail
I will without fail
turn my eyes upon the skies

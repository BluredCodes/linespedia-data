Black, White, and Silent

Picture a poem
as a silent film
the era of the twenties

the poet and verse are the actors
the page is the silver screen
the music comes from the heart

lips are silent here
the words dance to the screen
one time, perfect

if something is missed
or spelled wrong, the viewer
will not get the meaning meant

the actors will go on
as if nothing happened
and the words, black and white
will still remain 
Silent.

William S. Dawes

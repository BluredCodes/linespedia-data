Snow Blanket

Under a crystal canopy 
the earth
appears to be sleeping

White wisps whirl in the wind
as the apple tree, barren
peers upon red-cheeked children

The grass, in slumber dreams
of young lovers frolicking upon
the carpet she provides

A rock protrudes through cover
unlike the red-soldier lichen
(its normal shroud) that retreated to the onslaught

The memories of droning bees
hang like petals
for the flowers, heads tucked in mother earth

The ice upon the lake
allowing mortals to walk on water
another transformation of being

I pull wool around my neck
and study the portrait I possess
trying to capture the cold in warm words

For as chilling as the truth is
sometimes death must touch life
for new growth to occur

The fire warms me
as the snow blanket covers the earth
and touches me from within

William Dawes

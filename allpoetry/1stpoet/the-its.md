The Its’

The Its’ bought a house that was fine
and said “it’s a grand place, let’s have some wine”
so the Its’ drank the wine, found it divine
then cried when the bottle ran dry

The Its’ house stood on a hill
It’s a hard one to climb said It Jill
But with bucket she trod, and Jack like a clod
fell down and both buckets did spill

they said being an It does not give one rest
it’s so hard being Its’, at its best
so their names they did test
and concluded they were in a jam

for some would use their name in vain
The Its’ knew that it’s quite a pain
when their name was spoken, was the apostrophe “pokin”
yet in print it’s always quite plain.

William S. Dawes (by Jove, I think I’ve got IT)
January 27, 2001

MY SALVATION
I have tasted the tears you shed
While the thorns were placed upon your head
I tasted your blood represented by wine
You gave your life to save mine
I ate of your body, when I took the bread
And I remember the words 
While dying you said
Unto your Father you said a prayer
You thought of me, 
Even though I was not there
My sins were carried off into the air
You died for My Salvation
I sit and wonder as I write this verse
How with such love you could disperse
A gift that all must come to know
If to see the Father they wish to go
For it is written in Holy Text
That after this life-where we go next
Is based on our Faith, our Works, and Love
And on the Gift from God above
The Gift of His Son; for those on earth
E’n though a King, a simple birth

William Dawes
12/25/1999

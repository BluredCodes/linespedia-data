Bliss of Marriage

Her dues to him
they seemed to be obligatory
the bliss was gone, yes 'twas grim
not quite heaven or hell, but purgatory

on their wedding day he was her sire
now he asked for sex when drunk
so in her mind she did conspire
a way of escaping from this funk

in this state he was a Despot
and would hold her against all will
but how could she perform this hidden plot
to again her happiness fulfill

she talked to counselors at length concerning
the loss of pure passion in her life
there was inside an incessant yearning
to be living a utopia as husband and wife

the gist of this story, breviloquent
was to get him sober and clean
yet, it was her intent
to have a marriage, serene

William S. Dawes

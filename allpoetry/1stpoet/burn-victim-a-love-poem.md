Burn Victim

With a twinkle from your eye
A spark was cast 
To my heart, like tinder, dry
Small amounts of smoke rose so slow
As your breath on my cheek
Gave breeze to the glow
I began to feel the heat from the kindling
Emotions stacked neatly
Ready for burning
I became engulfed in the consuming blaze
My body an inferno
Eyes showing a smoky haze
Do not call rescue
The structure can not be saved
From a holocaust such as you
For I am to become ashes, in your hands
Placed in an urn
To be poured out at your command

William Dawes
July 3, 2000

Sun Sounds

Sit and listen to the sounds around
the cars, birds, planes
the noises that make the day
loud upon the ears

manholes rattle as traffic passes
horns, and bells 
people talking
as if oblivious to the world around them

I look upon the fire in the evening
and think about the sounds the sun must produce
the crackles and roar, distant so many miles

I feel the heat upon my face as I smile
even in the depth of space the Earth inhabits, the cold vastness of black surrounds
and I wonder where is the tap upon tympanic membranes
that all else is heard with

my ears do not perk
and I realize space is a vacuum 
where sound does not travel
air must be stirred for me to hear a whisper

if not for this
we may be deaf

Painted Tears

When we wed
and you said you loved me
you were as a clown
crying painted tears

only as realistic as dime-store magic
were they there to entertain
or distract me from the truth;

that your love was only a circus
a facade that was all illusion
and that I would be the one shot
through burning hoops

why did I walk this tightrope
that would have been better suited around my neck
for choked was the feeling I had

did you ever think about taking off the big shoes
that stepped upon me constantly
removing the bright colors that blinded me
kept me laughing and thinking you were safe

No……

for monsters do not cry tears
when they devour prey with fangs
destined to rip the heart out first

rosy cheeks, a white face
and painted tears
hide so much

William S. Dawes

Lyncean

I awaken to a screeching cry
a piercing from a clear blue sky
my eyes to the heavens they do pry
but the sight of the hawk I am denied

my eyes are watery as I stare
I am blinded by the sun’s bright glare
yet still in this morning air
the winged one says it’s still there

I feel his talons around my frame
for in the night a hard frost came
is the creature now calling out my name
or echoing out his cry of shame

what has man done since Adam’s birth?
but brought death and destruction to the earth!
this scream is not that of a chickadee’s mirth;
but one for warning and all its worth

can this be cleaned up?, what all call home
a round planet upon which we roam
from mountain tops to ocean’s foam
an accipiter’s plea, voiced in a poem

Desire

Writing poem without a meter
you know it just don’t fit
it’s like doing oral sex
and then you skip the clit
you leave the lover wanting
after you are completely spent
yet in those deepest caverns 
you never left a dent
the lover feeling jilted
a toned down form of rage
although they had some pleasure
from the words upon the page
but something still was missing
a feeling one would trust
words felt just a little more
from the emphasis of thrust
next time when writing
think of faces that are blushed
knowing that those begging cries
have successfully been hushed

William Dawes

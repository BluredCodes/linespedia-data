Old Before My Time

White is the color of old age
sad as it may seem
For on the eyes are cataracts
no more that youthful gleam
Teeth forever glistening
soaking in a glass
and a baggy diaper
has made me loose my class
My brain is white also
no more that shade of gray
like an empty page within a book
no words can thoughts convey
growing old before my time
some think I've gotten dense
I sit at home in ragged sweats
When I once was eloquent
fingers frail and gnarled
barely hold this shaking pen
I wish I could again be a child
at that blissful age of ten
Age is oh so greedy
It took away my prime
I live my life to the fullest
while I still may have the time.

William Dawes

Stained Glass	

I’ve seen him before
The old hippie who still wore
Hawaiian shirts
And tie die pants

His fingers discolored
From years of stirring luke-warm coffee
And smoking
Hand rolled cigarettes

Eyes, seeing the light
Of a different shade
His mind fragmented
From the jungles of Vietnam

Thoughts
His own religion
Believing the truths
Only he has seen

Why he thought he could fly
One will never know
As he lay here
Fractured, stained glass

William Dawes

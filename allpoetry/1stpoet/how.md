How

How can I touch you, 
sheltered: hidden.
Under cloak of sadness, 
raw from fear.

How can I love you,
lifeless, cold.
Flames no longer burn
dust remains.

How can I heal you, 
crying, shaken.
wounds run so deep
I fall into your darkness

How can I hold you, 
fragile, precious
You must hold me
with your soft touch

How you touch me
with eyes, on my words
softness of senses
thought devoid come to life

My love on the page
reaches, moving
Embers now glow
within your mind

Your darkness recedes
I work in your breath
calmness prevails
you heal from within

I hold you forever
within my own world
reader, friend
How I love you

William Dawes

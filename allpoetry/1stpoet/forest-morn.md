Forest Morn

Charcoal shadows smudged
across a watercolor sunrise
dawn slowly creeps, and rain-cloud remnants dance

Morning mist moves magically 
as night seems to end tragically
the last billows shed a tear
unto the forest I turn my ear

Crickets and tree frogs cease their song
to the day they don’t belong
above, a lone Blue Jay
sings his welcome to the day

Calling from this tall oak tree
he stays hidden mystically
my eyes strain in mere twilight
my ears capture this delight

As rays of gold crest, shadows fade
daylight comes across the glade
a proud Buck stands in majesty
amongst the forest tapestry

Wildflowers swaying in subtle breeze
I have seen forest through the trees
to have this experience is such bliss
having tasted the lips of Nature’s Kiss

William Dawes
October 2000

Driven

Six beers
four AM
and you are still there
on my mind

the dim lights
the smoke in the air
and the hard rock pounding my brain
did not remove your memory

as each bubble rose in my glass
new thoughts surfaced of what we had
those intoxicating moments
and the hangovers of bad arguments
all went to my head

I got in my car
turned the key
cranked Joe Cocker’s “Unchain My Heart”
and hit the highway

75 in a 30 gets you nowhere fast
reflecting as the lights bounced off the windshield
seeing everything around me double
yet you clearly in the rearview mirror

that red light of reality
stopping me
bringing me to the curb
with skid-marks etched in my heart

someday that open road will greet me
where fields of amber will sway
with every cleansing breath I take

but for now I am driven
to escape

William S. Dawes

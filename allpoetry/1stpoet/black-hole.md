Black Hole

She stared at the gold band
Bonding her to spouse
In her mind when she chose it to be
Opportune

In the reflection of diamond from starlight
the twinkle of the stranger’s eye caught hers
and she knew again she could escape
connection

This new prey, uneducated in the means
of time travel
would be caught in the vacuum of her
black hole

Rocketed to heights of passion
centripetal forces bringing them together
Looking to the stars for refuge
only to find darkness within

His dreams, risen within his manhood
were thrust into the abyss of no escape
and the gravity of the situation would in time
crush each of them

The glass house of his heart
was cut by the cold hard diamond
as her touch held, then released
any thoughts of a journey home

Now floating in a timeless orbit
circling himself
he sees the darkness
he may never escape

The cold, black reality
that the glimmer of the stars
was held in the sparkle of 
exploding dreams.

William Dawes
Dec. 2001

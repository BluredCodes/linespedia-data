Knighthood

I was given the duty to slay souls
not bringing death, but renewing life
words sharpened as a broadsword, the tool I use

mountains crumble to the steps my steed takes
rivers crossed by bridging thoughts
dragons drop their scales, those tears you shed
when I cut you with love’s touch
or run you through with fear thrusted strongly by language
that only you can feel

my armor is open, a link of text that even with holes
is stronger than titanium
more valuable than gold, and treasured within my mind

I joust with fairies in the night
my pen chases their flight across the page
and the streaks become blended into words

I carry the colors of my muse
the black silk shrouds me
the presence of all colors, blended, 
light, wind, fire, passion, all captured in a garment
that flows behind me as I ride

follow me, 
ride dragon wind at midday
catch the moon in your hands
drink from rivers of crystal
bathe in my words.

If Time is the time needed 
for morning to become night...
or to walk from home to school...
then Time is the measured delay, 
in between, to move from Here to There,
from Now to Then (Now to Then).
And if time is what happens 
during the while of distance
in between leaving and arriving 
then Time is a road 
travelled by a traveller,
moving at whatever rate of speed.

Were a traveller to move
so, so...so unimagineably fast--
Faster than Light, perhaps--
would all distance 
ever, concievably, disappear?
If so, the Time it takes
would be no time at all.

--Uncle Greg

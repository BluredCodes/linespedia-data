Were the fastest man imaginable
to play ping pong alone,
Could he stand at opposite ends of the table
And see his own face?
Could a guest at the door
Also be the host that answers?

One needs a mirror,
to see his own face.
Yet even with but one mirror,
He cannot see the back of his head.

Imagine so fast a man
on a treadmill that is a globe.
He walks, then jogs
Turning the globe with each step.
Running, he races...faster, ever-faster,
Like a dog chasing its tail
Hoping to see what is just over the horizon.
Were he to glimpse the back of his head
He would still not see his face.

From inside, how to get a view 
from the outside looking in?
How fast must one move
to see both sides of a coin at once 
and be in two places at the same time?
Until acheiving such a rate of speed,
there are no priveleged observers.

--Uncle Greg

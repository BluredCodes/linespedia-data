Under morning skies and the rising sun
The cowboys gathered for their last cattle run
Bacon and eggs and pancakes for all
Washin’ it all down with black coffee
Swapping stories of ropin and brandin
Cleaning up and the pots all washed
Cowpokes preparing their horses and gear
Rounding up the cattle and moving them northward
Along well worn trails used in the years before
The weather is dry and the dust is kickin
Bringing them doggies from their winter range
To feed on the grassy eastern Colorado plains
Their families are waiting at the end of the trail
Meals are prepared to welcome them soon
The children are excited and soon want to be
Heroes on horses just like their DaddyNot really a tidy, flowing poem... more of a story to tell..

title: "The Old Gunfighter"
writer: "TractorMan"

Out just beyond the town
Way out here in the West
Lived an old gunfighter folks call Gus
Some say he was the best
Long since retired now
And working on his farm
If you went by to visit
Look for him working in his barn
Gus lives a peaceful life now
No need anymore to prove himself
What he did for our small town
Is greater than any rich man's wealth
Many a young gunslinger
Having heard the legend of his name
Would ride confidently into our town
But the results were always the same
A challenge to our local hero
They would promptly lay down
Hoping to goad and rope him into
Proving who really was the fastest around
When it came time to shootin’
Gus’d calmly unholster his gun
Onto the ground he'd toss it
Turn around and away he’d run
Flustered and confused
The young gunfighters they’d say
Would saddle up their horses
And ride quietly away
You might think it cowardly
That he ran home to his wife
But Gus had spared the town a funeral
And also saved another man’s life

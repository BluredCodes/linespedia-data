Rising from the ocean
Three miles from the coast
Stands a tree-covered island
Of which now I boast
All over that island I played
When I was but a small boy
Exploring the many tidal pools
Was one of my great joys
Exploring the old lighthouse
And around it I would roam
Still standing as a guardian
Leading sailors safely home
Through the seasons we lived
Though not always pleasant
The winters were pretty rough
And the wind seemed ever present
But nothing seemed so awesome
To watch waves crash high on rocks
Even when we knew our way
To the mainland was blocked
And so years later still here I live
Perhaps even a little more bold
Hoping to pass on this great way of life
To my kids as they grow old

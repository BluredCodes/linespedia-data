Out in my yard
I dig me a few holes
To hang my feeders
On eight foot wooden poles
The birds were there
Waiting to be fed
I put up for the Hummers
Some feeders colored red
Seed for the blackbirds
And seed for the Horned Larks
The House Finches fly away
When both our dogs bark
Suet cakes and bird baths
I have put for them there
A family of Barn Swallows
Nested with pine needles and hair
Sunflower and White Millet
Are their seeds of choice
When all the birds are feeding
It’s hard not to rejoice

Back some Summers ago
When I was an older lad
I would borrow the car keys
From my dear old dad
Head across the bridge
And down Commerical street
Park the ’70 Maverick
Where land and ocean meet
Take in the salt air
Along with the sunny day
Get out the rod and reel
Find me a spot on the pier to stay
Nice shiny jig
At the end of the fishing line
Reeling in the Mackerel
It was just a matter of time
The bay was bustling with
Tankers, tugs and sails
Red and yellow ferry boats
Out to the islands with mail
As the tide was coming in
And fish hitting the lines
All of us that day were
Having the time of our lives 
I sold my catch for a nickel a piece
To folks passing by the way
Bring a few home for mom to cook
And do it again the very next day

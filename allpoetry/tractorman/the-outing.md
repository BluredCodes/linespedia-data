It was the first Sunday in October
That a bunch of us drove
Into the Pike National forest
Somewhere south of Lake George
The weather was brisk
With snowflakes sprinkled here and there
Our suburban packed full
Motoring into the back country somewhere
Paved road turned to gravel
And then a rut filled trail
Slowly and surely we went
With all that ground clearance we sailed
Stopped in a group of Aspens
With all the others right behind
None of us worse for wear
We had all made it just fine
Out we jumped into a cold stiff wind
Everyone getting on the coats
To keep the warmth in
Looking at the beauty
Of the changing trees all around
Kids all running to climb rocks
Jutting out of the hard ground
The pines smelled crisp
With an aroma so clean
There were even orange leafed Aspens
Mixed in with the needled greens
The views were stunning
And the colors so bold
The hillsides were painted
With splashes of yellow and gold
A few hours later
We packed our load
Headed home eastward
Driving down the road
It was really great
To get into the hills
A wonderful Fall day
Full of beauty and thrillsJust trying to capture this fun time in words...

AUTUMN’S ASSAULT

They started falling early September
Drops of colour landing on the lawn
Nature’s dead skin dropping in my garden

In October it began to rain
The ground beneath our feet became swampy
Leaves sodden and trodden into the ground

By mid-November all the leaves were raked
autumn’s colours heaped by the dustbin
Trees stood bare, and the lawn prepared for winter.

December brought high winds and biting cold
The smart money was on a white Christmas
And the garden pond wore a sheet of ice

Two long, cold weeks into January 
And the promised snow finally arrived
The garden was pretty; yet alien

By February the snow had melted
Dissolved into the ground, revealing
A sick looking lawn, more yellow than green

As prophesised March winds did indeed blow
A branch was torn from my apple tree
Leaving behind it a large gaping wound

In April the weather settled down again
New leaves were unfolding on all the plants -
Even in my weather-ravaged garden

May found me outside in my oldest clothes
Secatures once again clutched in my hand
Gently repairing Mother nature’s care.

June came and brought some sunshine with it
Weeding, planting, pruning, seeding, mowing
All suddenly seemed much less of a chore

July brought long evenings and barbeques
Blues, reds, whites and pinks adorned my garden -
I watched with eyes proud as any mother’s

August drew to an uneventful close
Lingering on the edge of summer

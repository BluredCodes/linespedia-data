THE SILENT CLASS

There is a school near here
where the children make no noise
if you walked past the window
you’d see silent girls and boys

The lack of talk in classrooms
Might make you stop and look
To watch for just a moment 
Each head bent over a book

And it may strike you odd
To see such silence in a school
You may wonder if the teacher
Holds the class with iron rule

But look a little closer
Just take another peek, and
see how these special children
Must use their hands to speak

So though the class is silent
And not a sound is heard, there
is lots that they are saying
Just without the spoken word

You may not hear their voices
Nor yet see them laugh and play
If you pass the classroom window
And go quietly on your way

So take yourself a moment
Just stop to look and hear
The lessons that are taught
Without use of word or ear

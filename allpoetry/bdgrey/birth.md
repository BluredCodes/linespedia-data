---
title: "Birth"
writer: "bdgrey"
slug: "birth"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/7095-Birth-by-bdgrey"
---

There is a light at the end of this tunnel.
Resisting I fight against the current,
but I am constantly pushed forward despite my efforts.
Helpless I slide further and closer to this light.
I hear voices beyond, but I see nothing.
This tunnel I am in is so soft and satin,
and I do not want to leave, but I am forced to do so.
I can feel the cold sting my eyes,
and the pressure around gets tighter.
I can still hear that constant beat behind me,
but new sounds enter my ears with each moment.
I feel two strong hands grab my head and pull.
Nothing so powerful have I ever felt.
Now I hear shouts and heavy breathing in pain.
I want to go back to my home for so long,
but I won't and I don't know why.
The light is now blinding, and everything is cold.
Suddenly I am struck hard so I let out a cry,
"What is happening, and please tell me why."
No one will answer and no one cares. 
Quickly I am wrapped and suffocated,
but not for long as my head is uncovered,
and all I see still is a blinding harsh light.
I am lowered into arms and now I feel safe,
for pleasant words are whispered my way.
Lips touch my head, and now all is well,
my mother, I know, will protect me for now.

I enter this world, so scared and unsure,
but now feel love, so simple and pure.
Thank you Mom, for all you have done,
a new life begins, and I am your son.

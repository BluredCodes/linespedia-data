Sacrifice




He said take this cup away take it from my lips take it away take it give it
to some other son of god. It shouldn’t be this hard to ransom all
humanity it shouldn’t fall on a single set of shoulders. If I offered
this sacrifice I offered foolishness I offered a blind man’s vision
I offered dust in a flagon. Let them bear their burdens by
themselves let them cry their own tears let them pay 
their own price. Whose dumb idea was this anyway
who thought I could come here as a mewling
baby born among them and not become
among them like they are, wounded
before the knife afraid before the
dark? It is too much it is too
little it is too dark it is
too unholy. Yet
I have promised
I have been
promised
I am
given
I have given
me on
their behalf
like wine
spilled on
an altar
like blood
from 
a given
calf.
Take me away
take away my own hard
choice take away their sins some
other way. Let me live to see suns and sons
and the risen tides of my own blood thickened by my half
humanity. Take this cup away from me or I will die willingly among them.

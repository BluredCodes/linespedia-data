NETHER SONG


I believe there is a god of ice.

I have found his glassy dung
Across my path.

I have breathed his sharpened breath.

His tongue
Tears away my sail.


In my village a child died one winter night 
frozen into a little bundle of salvation 
baptized in sleet 
entombed in a drift of innocent snow 
abandoned by the farmers 
as a gift to the god of crystal. 
And he took her 
and he kept her.


In his voice
I hear her voice.

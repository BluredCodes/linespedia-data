Geotoy


Great Child disassembled the entire rotting and regenerating mechanism
bit by bit by byte by cog by cognizance into its constituents its gums its lips
its gizzards its polychambered hearts its drawstrings its bowstrings its stringy
bits of protoplasmic tin its ragged raging so-unnecessary-parts its pink matter
gray matter muttering vowel-clogged vocal chords its cordite stench its retching
birth and rebirth and rerebirth its fixed fixations its ears its eyes and again its eyes

as if Great Child could resurrect its superb dissimulation
could work the wreck into a breathing breeding thing again
as if it were again the gelatinous clock the rain-sucking
pudendum the voice with the cockroach hum of cellular
gluttony the fornications of a minor heated star

Great Child does not know what he does
Great Child does not see what he sees
Great Child thinks daddy can fix it
when it breaks Great Child thinks
mommy’s dugs will always give
him rich green milk Great Child
does not understand there is
no other toy once this 
disarticulated toy
is gone.

Parting of the Ways




I did not think of us
as people. No, I
thought of us as gods
driving through rainy nights
to the City of Gods.

I held the steering wheel.
You ate your late night snacks
absent-mindedly,
crunching an undersong
to the clicks of the rain.

We drove across the thin
expressiveness of plains
and into the mountains
looking for the mountain
of holy rising stone.

Along the switchback roads
hunting for foundations
of the City of Gods
we found nothing, nothing
but endless mountain rain.

We turned ourselves seawards,
down the cordillera
to the white waiting waves.
“I must be a Sea-God”
you said. I understood.

Sea gods live by themselves.
No need of companions
no need of dry lovers
no need of life-giving
rain across the road.

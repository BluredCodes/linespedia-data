You're not really gone
you're there laughing like you do.
None of this is real,
 you can't be dead.
I just saw you last week
doing what you do and being you.
This is all some cruel joke
some bad dream.
When I wake up,
 you'll be right there...alive.
You can't be gone at 21,
your whole life is still ahead of you.
Why did you do it
you knew that shit was bad.
There are so many people
who love you and miss you...
You're not really gone,
I just can't believe it.
I just want to hug you,
just one last time.
Just to see your face,
hear you laugh,
touch you again.
Rest in Peace my friend,
and know that you were loved.This poem is dedicated to a close friend, Christopher Marvin Krueger who passed away on February 5, 2002

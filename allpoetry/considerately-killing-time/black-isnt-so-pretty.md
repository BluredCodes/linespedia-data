Black isn't so pretty
While it runs in your blood.
Asphyxia taunting you, 
screaming,Inviting 
the fear that you've fought
So long to supress, 
into the only comfort 
you know.It's in you. 
You're too afraid
Of hearing the words spoken.  
His silence prolongs false comfort.
He struggles to be heard,
But you leave him dangling 
on a thread Of regret. 
Chasing you patiently 
along lines of demise.
Nothing seems to escape you of cold,
Your hands become numb with a thought.
It fits you like a wet dress.
You can't postpone a feeling while
You twist to the rythm of dying.

How to say good-bye?
It’s been over three years yet I still hide.
I stood at your grave,
Dropped my poem into your grave.
You were my best friend, 
Yet like my other, you left me alone.
You never saw me as a problem,
Always wanting to see my little drawings,
My colorful words on paper, on my voice.
You taught me how to act with my own talent,
You told me stories as I sat by your feet, 
My first picture you drew me, I will always keep
With my unicorn drinking from it’s pond.
You found me charming, likeable, an angel, perfect,
Your fond blue eyes saw the real me for who I was.
I was your shy little flower, still waiting to bud
You left me alone before I bloomed Grandma
Died before I was ready to let you go. 
One thing I will always know, deep into my soul.
I will always be your little girl, and now I see some of your finer things.
I see the world through your eyes.Like, like True Believer, I wrote this poem for my English class project.  I wrote this one in reply to one my grandmother wrote a year before she died.

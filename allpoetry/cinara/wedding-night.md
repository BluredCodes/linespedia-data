Wedding Night

I would not wish you harm in any sense
nor sorrow to invade your splendid night
For I will bear the heavy pain and plight
for both our hearts, And I will, in pretense,
put on a veil of joy and innocence.
And mindful not to tremble in your sight, 
my hands will shun the glow of candlelight
The tears of grief that fall in my defense
will mimic tears of laughter that abound
on this the night of nights, when you shall wed,
and bridal guests will celebrate around
a wedding feast. Then after all are fed
I beg your eyes not meet with mine, and drown
in blissful wish and love’s tormenting dread

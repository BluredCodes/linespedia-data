The Golden Locket

He handed her a little box
And brushed aside her curly locks
A birthday present wrapped in blue
A little tag marked “I love you”

She looked into his smiling eyes
Held to her heart the small surprise
Then opend up the cherished gift
Much greater than she could have wished

A golden locket on a chain
A father’s love the gift contained
Inside the heart two pictures placed
She and her father, face to face

He placed the chain around her neck
And said some words she didn’t expect
“My love for you is in this heart
And from it I will never part”

She wore the necklace year to year
Through childish play and teenage tear
And on her blessed wedding day
She touched it as she knelt to pray

Her locket held the love she’d known
The tenderness he’d always shown
He promised he would never part
From love within the golden heart

The day he died, her spirit cried
Her love would never be denied
She opened up the golden clasp
And placed the heart within his grasp

The locket glimmered in a way
Just as his eyes that special day
She said, “My love is in this heart
And from it you will never part”

She bought a locket made of gold
Two tiny pictures it would hold
She brushed aside her daughter’s locks
And handed her the little box

Through golden hearts of precious worth
Is passed the greatest gift on earth
From age to age, from heart to heart
From children’s love we never part

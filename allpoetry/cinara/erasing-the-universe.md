Erasing the Universe

Golden light sifts through the heartache
for only a  few soft smiles worth
of you, then the sun sets again
dipping beyond the reach of contentment
leaving an opal moon rising in laughter

A cold oyster-colored ball
freshly dipped in a pungent sea 
slowly ascending
out of the cold, tainted brine,
it’s illumination
not warm enough to dry tears
that drip of midnight sorrow. 

The stars twinkle in delight,
friendly with the moon,
and I feel deserted by the night sky
that I once cherished
as they kept me 
in their spell,
holding captive all of my
most splendid dreams

now, only a cardboard ceiling
with a hand painted sphere 
that wears a costumed halo,
surrounded by a trillion 
winking eyes 
spattered across the night,
laughing and blinking

stage props of a tragic comedy
in the theater of despair,
laughing and winking
at me

until the dawn wipes the wetness
from my eyes
with a few exquisite
moments of love

and erases the rest of
the universe

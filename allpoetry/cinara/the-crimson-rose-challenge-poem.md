The Crimson Rose 

One day a woman, old and frail, held a rose of crimson red 
She gently handed me the lovely flower and softly said 
Look very deeply, dear, into the center of the rose 
And it will tell you stories that no other flower knows 

I found it strange, but somehow felt compelled by unknown power 
I folded back the petals and gazed deep into the flower 
And from the bloom a lovely verse of poetry arose 
To tell me of the grand and glorious beauty of a rose 

“A rose of red’s the greatest gift that you shall ever give 
And the greatest gift that you’ll receive as long as you shall live 
A rose is splendid and magnificent, it’s fragrance is divine 
It’s the most beloved flower in the history of mankind 

A rose is loved by king’s and poets, known around the world 
It has the power to change a nation, as velvet wings unfurl 
It’s a dainty bit of splendor, it’s the force of noted worth 
It’s value is much greater than all the gold on earth” 

So ended the sweet and captivating poetry of the rose 
It’s message to the entire world I wanted to disclose 
I quickly ran to find my daughter, with her little child 
I placed the rose into her hand, she looked at me and smiled 

Look very deeply, darling , into the center of the rose 
And it will tell you stories that no other flower knows 
It will speak to you in poetry, an unknown verse, and yet 
It’s words will be a message that you never will forget 

For you will understand its meaning from the very start 
A rose, my dear, is the wondrous love within a mother’s heart 
Look deep into the rose and marvel at its poetry 
Look deep into your heart, and there a crinson rose you’ll see 

Remember that a rose is the greatest gift you’ll ever give 
And the greatest gift that you’ll receive as long as you shall live

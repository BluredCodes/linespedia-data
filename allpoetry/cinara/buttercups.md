Field of Buttercups

I’ll never find another field
Of buttercups of yellowness
Where I can sit by old stone barn
In shadows of its mellowness

And read my book of poetry
In solitude and pensive mood
Each line is wine meant for the heart
And for the soul a wanted food

A place where field is all my own
Not even sparrow interrupts
There’s no more peaceful place I know
Than a field that’s laced with buttercups

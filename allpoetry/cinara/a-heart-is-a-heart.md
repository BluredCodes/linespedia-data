A Heart is a Heart

A heart is a heart and a rose is a rose
A  heart is still fragile however it grows
It loses its grace in the seasons of time
Then blooms once again to its glorious prime

So tenderly rosebuds are nourished at first
Abundantly showered with loving at birth
When the arbor is full and the shoots thickly grown
It’s assumed that the rose can survive on its own

A heart’s like a rosebud, it beckons the eye
To know the full measure of beauty inside
But a rose is a rose and will always possess
Those fragile soft petals as velvety dress

So easily damaged and shredded apart
Please handle with care, for a heart is a heart

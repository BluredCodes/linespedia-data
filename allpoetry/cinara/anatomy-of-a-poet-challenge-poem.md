Anatomy of a Poet 

The poet’s mind is sliced and diced 
Upon the parchment’s weathered page 
His wisdom oozes from the vice 
Of vintage brews of mellowed age 

His heart is slivered  paper thin 
Each chamber wall exposed to sight 
And any love that dwells within 
Is bathed in rainbow’s prism light 

A poet’s spirit is engraved 
In golden ink upon the leaf 
His character in feathered phrase, 
Between the words, his core belief 

His face reflects on silent pond 
The nose in scents of morning mist 
His voice within a sparrow’s song 
His feet in footstep’s reminisce 

His hand writes letters slowly etched 
That flow from life’s sweet laden quill 
A poet’s inner eye is etched 
On petals of a daffodil 

The eardrum vibrates ocean’s roar  
As tides of passion’s power rips 
Across the  silver sands on shore 
And on the warmth of wine wet lips 

A poet’s heart beats to rejoice 
And sings in timbres of the rhyme 
Aesthetic grace is spirit’s voice 
And beauty is his love sublime

Where Willows Sing

Where willows sing and swallows call
And waters from the foothills fall 
The purple martin finds a hedge
To perch along the water’s edge

I placed a gourd, a little dome
That he would find to make a home
On bough of needled evergreen
To mark his yearly spring routine

The swallows come a thousand fold
And stay until the winds grow cold
Before the fall of downy flake
In grasses near the hidden lake

More splendor I have never seen
Than here among the hills of green
The purple martins come to use
The summer home that I would choose

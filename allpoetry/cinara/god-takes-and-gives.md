God Takes and Gives

For everything God takes away
It seems He gives back even more
A hundred favors in a day
Of wonders to be thankful for

He took away my lively step
Perhaps I was too in a rush
He slowed me down so I could hear
The morning song of feathered thrush

So I could witness every bloom
And every bud along the way
Every petal opening
Its velvet wing to light of day 

He took away my steady hand
Which wrote in fine calligraphy
But gave a keyboard full of words
To write these lines of poetry

He opened up the skies to me
And gave me friends around the world
For everything He took away
A finer gift was then unfurled

He took away the ability
To walk a distance, run a mile
But then replaced it with a gift
An inner happiness and smile

He takes the words from out my head
And slows me down in simple speech
But fills my soul with endless joy 
Which always seemed beyond my reach

He slowly takes my life from me
In little steps, in little parts
But gives it back to me in love
A thousand fold to fill my heart

He’s taken all my strength from me
But gave me love to so uplift 
That nothing I could ever give 
Would compensate this wondrous gift

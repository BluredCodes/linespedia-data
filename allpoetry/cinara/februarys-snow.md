February’s Snow


My thoughts have turned to springtime
And the warming of the earth
The colors of the garden 
And the redbud tree’s rebirth

I’m weary spent of winter
And the north wind’s blowing chill
My eyes now yearn to see the bright
Of yellow daffodil

I gaze through morning windows
And yet marvel at the show
There’s something very lovely 
In a February snow

The field’s aglow with diamonds 
Strewn across its glittering breast
As sunbeams capture every icy
Flake on windblown crest

The holly bush and maple tree
Show off their silhouette
Each twig and leaf in snow adorns
A crystal white vignette

Heavy is the laden pine
Its branches bending down
And  hanging glassy icicles
On rhododendron’s bough

The early robin sits above
And contemplates the floor
And waits as I for March’s melt
And green to grow once more

Oh spring, please come and melt this glaze 
And feed the flinch and crow  
But let me cling just one more hour
To February’s snow!

Day of Birth

She wakens me in dark of womb
In halo’s corner sings a tune
A splendid loving melody
Of twinkling crests on rippled sea

And patiently I wait my turn
No hurried step, no timely yearn
No lighted candle do I burn
So patiently I  wait my turn

Then gracefully she cradles me
No vision of her face I see
Life’s throbbing heartbeat I can hear
Of ocean’s rhythms in my ear

The hand of God is reaching in
To bless my tiny soul within
The undertows are pulling me
My cradle drifting out to the sea

A flash of panic, heart’s unrest
Pushing me from comfort’s nest
A flair of pain, a smaller space
I’m taken from the warm, dark place

Then all is cold and all is bright
I’m laid upon a bed of light
I feel her eyes across the room
My tiny face in love consume

They place me in her loving arms 
Wrapped up in warmth and safe from harm
A fragrant rose is now my bed
A halo lit around her head

To her, an angel, I was bound
With golden ribbons winding round
And now I feel caressing hands
Are strengthening the golden bands 

This is the place where I will rest
The petal’s bed, the angel’s breast
My day of birth God’s grace bestows
The gift of life, a velvet rose

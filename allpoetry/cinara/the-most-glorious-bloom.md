The Most Glorious Bloom

To look upon a palette’s hues
Of golden bronze, and pastel blues
Laid gently on an emerald bed
With spattering of scarlet red

To see a quilt so neatly sewn
And every colored patch its own
Displayed in silken wrap of folds
Of fragile ever changing molds

Not one more splendid than the next
I reel and spiral down perplexed
As where to lay my gentle kiss
As I descend through morning mist

I twirl and float and gently spin
In morning drops and gust of wind
The sunlight penetrates and glows
Upon the petal of a rose

I spot a patch of purple bloom
And settle on a petal’s plume
To rest my fragile powdery fold
And spread my stained-glass sails of gold

All the world would love to seize
My beauty from the morning breeze
There is no flower as grand as I
A dazzling floating butterfly

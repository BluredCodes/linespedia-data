Here He Comes Again

What is sorrow doing on my doorstep once again?
I have no other choice but to allow him to step in
His old familiar ramblings are tales I know so well
Painful  recollections of events he likes to tell 

What is sorrow doing in the verses that I pen?
I was so certain that his face would not show up again
And now he’s back to torment me, to wake me in the night
To lace my words with teardrops in the poetry I write

Why does sorrow cling to me, he’s not a friend of mine
But ventures back into my life again from time to time
I think this time he’ll hit me hard, but I’m prepared to fight
And not fall victim to his phantom in the dark of night

Sorrow comes intruding and expects to fill the whole
But he should know the depth already carved into my soul
There’s lots of space in sorrow’s well, the hollow’s deep and dark 
I doubt this time if he will even reach the half-way mark

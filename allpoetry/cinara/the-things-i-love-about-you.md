The Things I Love About you


Never a spectacle, never a show
Small simple pleasures wherever we go
We stop for a sunset, forgetting our srife
And pass by the major attractions in life
A coke and a sandwich to carry us through 
These are the things that I love about you

A candle-lit meal on a dock by the lake
A hand written card with a crossed out mistake
A birthday rendition on your old guitar
Beats the greatest symphonic performance by far
A small weathered vase of forget-me-not blue
These are the things that I love about you

Getting prepared for a great interview
Going to bed early, then talking til two
A scheduled appointment you don’t want to miss
In the driveway you stop for a five minute kiss
While the kids in the window are waving to you
These are small pleasures known only to few

If you were a millionaire with just one wish
You’d trade it all in for that getaway fish
A local bred boy without fortune or fame
But down at the diner they all know your name
And you treat that old chef like his blood was pure blue
And that is the thing I love most about you

A dance in the kitchen, the red, white and blue
Honesty, honor, the essence of you

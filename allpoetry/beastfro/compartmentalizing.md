These years; 1984 – 1988 I shall
place in Beautiful Struggle.
Memories of hot, humid summer days,
Winter days with ocean winds
eerily cutting through my coat.
Breakdancing and my Commodore 64.
(Amazing what 64K of memory could 
accomplish!)
Beautiful girls from Japan, Korea, Guam,
Philippines, Taiwan, and, of course, Okinawa.
Those teen years when all is known,
And all is new.  Marveling that Mom
and Dad finally don’t know something
I know.  This box is sealed;
Until next week when I look back.

And then there is College.
(Or should I say Collage?)
That time when I ran to freedom
only to realize that I don’t 
have an idea what is required
of me.  At least I had keg parties
when I got too overwhelmed.  Or
just because it was Friday...
Both reasons equally important.
I grew and came to see the possibilities.
Of what, I’m still not sure, but
then it made sense.  I had my
plan to conquer the world.
And friends to help.

This box: Darkness.
So unsure and that potential
being worked on.  Nights spent
staring at clocks wondering; when?
Empty promises to me from me for me.
Because wandering at night brings
fear, anticipation, confusion, and comfort;
others are there with you.  you feel them
there.  You hear them.  And still you are
alone.  But not by yourself.
This one I want to burn,
But keep as a reminder.

Now this vessel.  I line with cedar
chips and build a casing of stainless
steel.  Into it goes today, and all the 
todays to come.  No specifics: They aren’t
memories yet so I can’t give details.  This
one I will call Future.  No – 
Near Future.  Too close to call future;
And too important to put off.

Finished.
Now I’ll look through that blue
chest I labeled Childhood again.
That one always makes me laugh –

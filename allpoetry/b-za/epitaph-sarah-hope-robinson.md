Epitaph - Sarah Hope Robinson 

Mom said to be home by 10
The seconds were closing in
Only 5 blocks to go, 2 already past
Come on, drive quick and fast
That is what I told my friend
Who knew it would be the end?
We turned the corner way too fast
That memory was my last
She was a little drunk you see
But that didn't matter much to me
The car skidded out of control
We ran into a telephone pole

This kind of scene
Happens to many teens
When will we learn?
I never knew life was such bliss
My lips will never feel another kiss
But it was my choices, and mine alone 
So for all teens that may drive home 
Don't drink and drive, the life you save may be your ownNo this did not really happen. Thank God. It was a school assignment. Write a epitaph (something on a tombstone, so she's speaking from the dead) for a fictional person. So here it is. Tell me what you think.

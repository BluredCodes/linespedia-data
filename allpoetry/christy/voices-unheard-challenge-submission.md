february exhales, 
settles against the house 
where plain brown panneling 
muffles those sugar-sad 
notes, music others 
attribute to 
tempermental winds, 
groaning timber, 
cats stretching 
their lean throats for 
sexual play. 

the roses are blooming 
again, just as they 
always do 
at this time, when 
the house remembers her 
fleshwarm moments with 
unseasonal blushing. 

i imagine what 
it must have been like, 
her silver birdvoice living, 
her delicate strokes 
waking the canvas, 
the landscape 
squeezing itself 
into her eyes like 
eager children pressing 
to fit inside 
a photo. 

i imagine the 
mournful bellow from 
the earth's throat 
when her gifts trickled 
over chalky lines, 
no witness to her 
murder but these 
misunderstood walls.

each year 
the body of the oak
knows another ring,
wider than the one before,
a symbol of increasing thirst
and wisdom sucked 
from soils.
he is the 
dark fruited king
pendulous with grace,
courting autumn with gifts of
russet spangles, amber-browns
to dance in her honor.

and like the oak,
the poet grows in 
his rough skin, each year
experience birthing a rosy
excrescence on
the heart.  his spirit
feeds on naked earth
and bitter wines 
and passion drawn into
every aching root.  
in his autumn, blood-rich words
will unfold to
dance inside our eyes.Happy birthday a couple of days early, big brother!!!!!

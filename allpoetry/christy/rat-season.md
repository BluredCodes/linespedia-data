When the sky grows thick and muddy
and the bitter chill wraps you in a thin
sheet of needles...

they come.

Like little diseases with soft feet
and growling bellies, they infect
the shadows of my house, pillaging 
and nursing the spills of my kitchen 
as if latched to a 
forbidden teat.

The rats the rats the rats 
are here,
chewingandclawingandsqueaking,
making my flesh crawl with
tickly tiny bumps.

They rape the bread box
scurry neath furniture, tease
me to screams till
the metal hinge booms and I

laugh my silly ass off!

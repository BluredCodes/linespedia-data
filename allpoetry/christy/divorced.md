(for my sister)

Upon passing you 
in town,
words lay dead in 
our throats.

Only the darkness round
your eyes
spoke of nights
when you can't find 
sleep in cruets or 
little pills,

when stillness is
disturbing the same way
silence sometimes
roars,

and I smiled secretly
at the whisper of 
my pulse,
the coolness of 
my palms,
your wickedness fading 
behind my eyes,

while the guilty echo 
of a decade
still shook your
aching skull.

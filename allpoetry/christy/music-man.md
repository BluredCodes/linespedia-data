He played his music 
with thick fingers
while the skinny children
danced in the streets like
wild stick figures
shaking in the wind.
Even the rain hushed
to hear his song.

Passersby tossed change
until way past starlight
to save those chords
from extinction,
chords that burned like
sweet fever, and the

skinny children danced on.

I want to be opened wide
so that these bones
may know the warmth
of a red sun.

I want to be as wild and free
as the Mississippi wind
rushing through golden fields.

I want to be a whisper of rain
falling to lull
the tired eyes of children
to sleep.

But, most of all
I want to be full of you,
giddy and breathless,
wearing moonlight to bed.

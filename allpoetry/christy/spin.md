9:00pm.
The virago is dead
and solace lives on a  
moon slathered bench
hours after 

this house spins madness
like tornados 
whirling shards of sound 
from children, 
appliances, and television
till my brain
seeps vinegar. 
Every day is the same,
the same, the same,
the same.

The voices employ my 
hands to smooth laundry,
prepare food, caress little
faces to the 
curve of my belly.
My name clicks like a 
nauseating drum.

You smile and say I am
paying my dues, 
but I'd rather just 
take out a loan.

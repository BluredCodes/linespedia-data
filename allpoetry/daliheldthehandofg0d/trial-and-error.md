you wrapped your barbed wire arms around my chest and squeezed until I burst
spoke up in class with a bombast aura that came off unrehearsed 
I followed you around, your stupid and uneasy minion
absorbing your shadow and swallowing
your battery acid complements
that stung my nerves and
ate some little holes
here and there-
wherever

don’t cry
over spilt milk
you’re not to blame
no one is to blame for this
but me and my own ambivalence
I turned my head for plenty of time, so
I can’t hold you responsible for reaching into my pocket
and dragging out some secrets that I’d hidden deep beneath my skin
but I've moved on and since grown razorblades for teeth and a headful of sin

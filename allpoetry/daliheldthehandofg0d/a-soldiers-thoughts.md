A good deal of war is waiting
which wouldn’t be so bad
if a good deal of waiting wasn’t
thinking-
as I pull the cigarette 
from my lips I can only ponder
on my mother and her greyish eyes
her pecan pie and fragile build
I wonder what she’s doing right now
at home.
I let the warm smoke ascend my throat
and remember the last time
mother kissed me on the forehead
like she always had after tucking me
into bed.

Allen tapped my shoulder and whispered
“looks like we got a kraut at nine o’clock
200 yards, 225 maybe. just beneath
that café window, third from the left.
put the bastard out of his misery.”
I adjusted my scope and swung it around
to nine o’ clock, third window from the left.
he sat, leaning against a wall and staring
into the sky, smoking a cigarette
oblivious to any sort of danger.
there he was, the German, the enemy
as I steadily pulled back on the trigger
I could only consider what it could be
bringing him such a pleasant smile
maybe it was his mother.

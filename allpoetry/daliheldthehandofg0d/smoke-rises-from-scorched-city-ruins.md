fire flies with formative lissomeness
down stairs and through cathedral doors
to greet the midday Monday’s prate
about policemen, bankers and whores

the lonesome eyes catch light and pirouette
up walls and upon misty shelves
the hour glass pours somber silhouette
as boys read on and on by themselves

illuminated signs glow beckoning
off lurid streets and by the park
clean sheets and pillows soft for sleep
thick curtains keep it dulcet and dark

the remnants fuse us with congested hope
within the vase withholding breath
we smile and talk, then all shake hands
now all evading untimely death

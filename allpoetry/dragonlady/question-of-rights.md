It’s a question of rights 
That they ignore
It’s something we can’t let go on anymore
We have voices
That no one will hear
They just dismiss
They say that we’re queer
It doesn’t matter where
We could be anyplace
They just judge us
By the look on our face
Or the way we dress
Or wear our hair
What’s inside doesn’t matter
They don’t really care
They’re to busy looking out for Uncle Sam
When it comes to us
They don’t give a damn
They say we’re all delinquents
Rebels without a cause
They never notice anything good
They only see the flaws
They don’t have to hide it
Because we know the truth
They’re just trying to steal away our youth
Are they going to keep after us until we die
And if they are
Will you please tell me why
Why are we juveniles 
In their sights

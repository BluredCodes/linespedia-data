hiccup and slack
hiccup and slack
I’m building this track

it leads home,
you know

I willing me back
To what I’ve known, 
“You know ?”

Whadda mean to say
Is
Weren’t you there?
Back in the day?

it’s one morning,
He blinked
“Oh, stop your moaning”
I thinked

Everyone was gone
to hither and yawn
Why was I still here?
and goddamit, where’s my beer!

it was two in the morning
my watch told me so
I’m dried, salty and boring
the waitress told me so

it was the still morning
with me lying there 
half dead, coughing and swollen

with a hiccup
and slack
I’d found my back
through two sheets to the wind
and four bottles of sin
so this seems OK

if 
at least 
I’m drunk today…I beseach your critique. I wrote this when I was drunk, about being a drunk.

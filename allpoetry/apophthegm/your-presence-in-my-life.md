To me it was a world so vile
All felt sore, fake and guile,
I had lost all my hopes for bliss-
‘Till you came, like an angel but wings.
It’s your charm that taught me love,
 To swim through its enchanting cove;
Your smile a magic kissing me hope-
Mesmerized was I, as you spoke
Of love, of life, and of dreams,
And thrill filled my life to brims.
You touched my life, revamped my visions,
I had no hopes, you showed me reasons
To be with you, to jog with the world-
As to you I was dearer than gold,
But to me, all these are useless
If unflavored with your charm;
Love me forever, come, hold my arm
And never let go, for it belongs in your hold
Bliss engulfs me as we enfold.

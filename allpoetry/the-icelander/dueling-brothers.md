---
title: "Dueling Brothers"
writer: "The Icelander"

A minds satifaction is the hearts content,

My heart is not content,

So my mind is troubled,

The woman I seek,

Has been taken from me,

By a man not worthy,

To hold her as own,

Even though I have not shown,

The way I feel,

The man and I had a deal,

Not to push,

The way we both feel,

Towards a certain lady in waiting,

He knew that,

Now, I must take her back,

Oh! How I this compicates things,

To slay the man I cannot!

Spare his life I shall,

For love is stonger than hate,

I go through the door,

Running with fury raging in my heart,

I thrust my sword forward,

And thrust it into my enemies chest,

I fall back knowing what I've done,

I take the lady, as a tear falls slowly from my face,

I take my leave with my lady,

The next day has come too fast,

Now, I must bury my only brother.



Who said that love doesn't have a price?

She's said it now
that irreversible tinge that lingers in my stomach 
it is over.

What about the promises
the plans
the furniture we shopped for to make the home we both dreamed of, forever?

we were so close
and now she's leaving.

Only the details remain
the distribution of emotional severances
heaviness of heart
unspeakable sorrow.

I wonder what I'll do without her
how long will tonight be
how long will life be, alone?

What about our families
the holidays
the gifts I had so painstakingly selected
and hidden for so long?

My breath weighted with unspoken apologies
searching for any obscurity that could make her stay
I have none.

My heart calls out for begging
The door swings closed behind her

Why does it have to end?
Why does it always have to end?

It came today on the breeze I hadn't expected
 our sunset and my wine gone long ago
 I had lingered there, in that moment,
 for an eternity
 now I was home
 
 Our chair and blanket grow lonely
 I suppose I should give them away
 to a charity, or someone, but I can't
 
 The long night alone draws uncomfortably near
 sheets too crisp
 space in the bathroom for days
 no smell of conditioner
 no blush in the sink
 wonder how you are
 
 I dim the lights
 fluff your pillow
 and miss you
 
 Love posessed of me, obsessed of me
 I am not beyond reproach
 It was my fault
 I trew us away
 
 and I'm sorry

it seemed dark there
all I could do was sit at the edge of the bed
as she wept
and watch her tears
my elbows in my knees
my shame and my face weighing heavy on the heels of my hands
I waited
hair clinging to her face
her curls waves across the skin I had forgotten how to love 
I hurt
what would become of her
of me
once the worst had passed she laughed
not joy though
the other laugh  
her grace reminded me before she’d even left
I was a fool
I had forgotten how pretty she was
my intolerance clouding long lost visions of our lives together
when we were young
or maybe not so long ago
it had all seemed so right

title: "The Toilet"

Ring around the toilet.
To bad you had to soil it.
Flush it.
Flush it.
It all goes down.

Now you have to get
a brush and scrub
around the bowl.
You scrub it hard,
it comes right off
and falls down in 
the hole.

When you go to someone's
house, their toilet is
not clean. You look 
around for a toilet
brush. It cannot be
seen.

For those of you who
leave your toilet's
with poo all in a 
splatter. We'll scrape
it off, bring it to
you, served on a
silver platter.

My poems these days
are written about
very natural things.
But choices aren't
easy anymore, now it's with
or without wings.

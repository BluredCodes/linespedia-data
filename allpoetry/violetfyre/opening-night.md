The hallway seems longer from the end of it
Frustrations were growing
The roll of duct tape was almost gone
We needed vacation
The smoking lounge is too small
For so much childishness
And so many egos
I wanted to be out there
In our pseudo art gallery
But the attitudes push me away
So I sat on the floor
At the end of the hall
By a pay telephone
Writing more bad poetry
That would never be
Featured at an art gallery.

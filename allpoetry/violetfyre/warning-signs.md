Another innocent bystander
Became a casualty of sunny day frisbee
Metal clinked upon metal
Warning of a horseshoe game close by.
Caffeine spilled into my veins,
Filling every pore,
Calming me for the first time
Since his lips touched mine.
Conscience drained away in a flood
Of pretentious indie rock.
This shirt still smells like yesterday,
My eyes still ache from driving and seeing you.

I don't want to wash it away.
We'll never get it back, never get this back.

I built up an immunity
To a conscience that was starting to scream
While grass was trampled
To signal our unofficial start of spring.
Daylight's wasting, days are wasting
Never think, never think, just do
Never think, never think, just do what you want to.
And I'll say goodbye
And bury my head into a song like peaceful drowning
While the flies buzz around me,
Signifying our official end of winter.

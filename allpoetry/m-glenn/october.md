October

I take October into my lungs.

I drink it's ethereal cocktail

	of change, with

winter' s desperate eyes on me.

Raking leaves of a tree, whose 

	name I once knew.

Nature undressing before me, I stride

into a memory of better days

	when I was young, 

days were full, 

	and no one I love was dead. 



I receive leaves of my life,

from a tree whose nature escapes me,

	with quiet dignity,

in October 

where nothing lasts.

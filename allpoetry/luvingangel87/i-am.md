I am an American
I am a child
I have my free opinion
but I am yet not able to vote

I am an American
I am fifteen
I am able to drive
but I am yet not able to vote

I am an American
I am a Christian
I am able to beleif in what I want
but I am yet not able to vote

I am an American
I am a student
I am able to learn about democracy
but I am yet not able to vote

I am an American
I am a girl
I am able to speak my mind without being shut away
but I am yet not able to vote

I am an American
I am a black
I am able to sit in the front of a bus without trouble
but I am yet not able to vote

My life is changed mostly by society
but I am yet not able to vote...

(written from a child's point of veiw)This is just my inky way of protesting to the age thing with voting...just my own personal opinion.  And no I am not fifteen, I am not black, but I am an American.  I basicly placed myself in another person's shoes but I am not that person.  Sorry of any offences to anyone, if anyone has a problem I will remove the poem.  If not, hope you enjoy.

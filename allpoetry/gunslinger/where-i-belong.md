---
title: "Where I Belong"
writer: "Gunslinger"
slug: "where-i-belong"

Sitting by the highway, 
With my bag, and my guitar-
On my way to Nashville,
Where I hoped to be a star.

But that was sixteen years ago-
When I was thirty-two.
I'm crowding fifty mighty hard,
What's left for me to do?

I'll pack my old blue suitcase-
And take that same old box,
And lug them back to Texas-
With my holes in both my socks.

With a lot of painful memories-
And my notebooks full of songs.
You're looking at a cowboy-
Headed back where he belongs.

I thought that I had talent-
My friends all told me so.
Said I needed exposure,
Told me where I ought to go....

A long streak of the one night stands
And fifty dollar gigs,
At last I finally realized
I'd never make it big.

I look back on my past mistakes-
I'll never right the wrong-
So I'll just pack my bags, and then
Head back where I belong.

Not Even If Could

I did a lot of drifting, back when I was young.
I really cannot understand, why youth equates with dumb.
I saw a lot of country, while living on the road...
When freedom was my motto, hitch-hiking was my mode.

No house, no debts, back in those days, no place I had to be.
Not one soul in the universe, who worried about me.
A cowboy and a drifter, out in the great Southwest-
The mountains and the desert, were the life I loved the best.

But years and miles behind me, have changed the way I think-
Have changed the style of life I lead, and taught me not to drink.
I've found we can't undo the past, exchange the bad for good-
But I'm not sure I'd change a thing, not even if I could.

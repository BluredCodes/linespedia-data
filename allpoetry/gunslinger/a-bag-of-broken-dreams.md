While browsing through a resale shop,
A bag I chanced to see.
A sense, almost, of deja vu-
Came stealing over me.

I placed it on the counter,
And pulled it open wide...
I almost dreaded looking at-
The things I found inside.

I found a glove, now dry with age
Palm black with rosin, too
The spurs came next, now red with rust-
Their leathers far from new...

The chaps were now more pink than red
The years had took their toll,
The rosin bag, I pulled out next...
It helped him grip and hold...

A bull rope and a tarnished bell-
There's something else I see...
A buckle made out, "Abilene"
And "Nineteen eighty-three".

A tear rolled down my leathered cheek-
I stood there all alone...
The bag reminded me a lot,
Of one I used to own.

I asked the lady what she take-
For this old riggin' sack...
She said, "I'm asking twenty bucks.
I found it in the back."

I handed her a twenty-
A bargain so it seems...
I walked out of the resale shop,
With a bag of broken dreams.

I rolled into Cowtown-
Feeling pretty low.
I’d mailed ahead my entries-
To the Fort Worth rodeo.

I checked into a Motel 6-
Old Tom left on the light...
To try and catch me forty winks,
I have to ride tonight.

The bulls are getting tougher-
Or I am slowing down-
I can’t seem to remember,
My last real good go-round.

It’s burger-deals, and chips and dip-
And coffee by the pot...
Most nights will finding me sleeping
In some Shoney’s parking lot.

All my friends can say is that-
“He’s got a lot of try!”
My youth I may have wasted,
But I’ll cowboy till I die.

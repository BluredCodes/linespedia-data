I was sitting in my pickup,
Just watching cars go by.
My mind began to wander,
I began to question, "Why?"

I thought of Arizona-
The sage brush in the spring;
Of coffee on an open fire,
The song, coyotes sing-

Cowboys in their soogans-
Gazing at the Milky Way,
I'd give a million dollars,
To be back there today.

'Twas a life of simple pleasures-
Of strong, hardworking men...
A handshake was a contract,
O, if it were so again.

But the past is passed forever,
And I've seen a lot of change.
My life would be more simple,
Out there on the open range.

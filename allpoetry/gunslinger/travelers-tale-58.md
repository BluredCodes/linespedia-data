Tale 58

Ho, Miss, 'twill tarry for a bit?
I'd swear I ken yer face.
Ye move like creatures o' the wild-
Such delicacy an' grace...

Aye, I wou' vow we met before-
But when? Ah, 'tis to ponder...
For many ports ha' seen me mug-
Aye, I've been known to wander.

'Elen? Wou' tha' be the name?
Ye favor 'er o' Troy...
Before the canny Greeks di' coom-
Wi' famous equine ploy.

Or, Venus? Aye, 'twould fit ye well-
A goddess, I believe-
To think tha' ye shou' cross me path,
On this damp, chilly eve.

Ah, lass, a pity I'm no swell-
To sweep ye off yer feet...
An' bear ye off, wi' me to dwell...
Where sky and mountains meet.

But I'm a wolf...no lap dog, me-
I must be on me trail-
But faith ye are a sight to see!

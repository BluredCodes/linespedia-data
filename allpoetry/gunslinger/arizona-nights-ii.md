title: "Arizona Nights II"
writer: "Gunslinger"

I slept last night in Houston-
And I liked those city lights.
But I recall the starry skies,
Of Arizona nights.

Coyotes’ eerie howling-
Out around the Mogollon... 
The big cats softly prowling
Now I wonder where they’ve gone...

I recall the smell of sagebrush-
And the tumbleweeds around...
The thirsty desert licking
Every raindrop from the ground.

The Gila monsters lying-
In the sunshine on the rocks,
While a weary hand is riding,
As he looks for missing stock.

But those days are far behind me,
And I know I’m growing old.
But the memory’s more precious,
Than the world and all it’s gold.

I’ve been a lot of places-
And I’ve seen my share of sights-
But I love those open spaces...
And those Arizona nights.

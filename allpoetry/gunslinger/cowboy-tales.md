I was sitting in the bunkhouse-
With “Sunny Jim” Malone...
Outside the rain was falling,
And we both were far from home.

Early Sunday morning,
The coffee strong and black,
I said, “It’s better settin’ here-
Than in some old line shack .”

We started tellin’ stories-
Of the places we had worked.
Of dinks, and solid horses,
And hands who hadn’t shirked...

Of wrecks, and them near misses-
That make a person think,
And of a hundred cowboys-
Who’d drowned themselves with drink .

We talked of good bronc riders-
And men good with a rope.
About the things the Good Book says,
To give a rider hope...

We talked of places we had been
And girls that we had loved,
And how we’d saddled up and left
When push turned into shove .

We’d talked of driving cattle-
And going to the sales...
We whiled away the hours,
With our old, cowboy tales.

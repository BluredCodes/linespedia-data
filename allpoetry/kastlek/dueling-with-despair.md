I was locked in a timeless death battle with Despair

We circled each other and squinted

Like in old Clint Eastwood Westerns

We faced off in a duel

Death was the penalty



We met in the middle of the dusty town square

Face to face

Our guns holstered but our hands at the ready to draw



We faced, we turned, we walked our ten paces

We whirled back and fired

Smoke and dust swirled

The outcome was unclear



The bottle of pills lay at my side

Fallen to the ground

My hand dropped limp off the side of the sofa



* *



I woke up in a sterile room

A ray of sun shimmering with dust motes caught my eye

The kindly voice of Hope sat at my side

Holding my hand

The Angel of Mercy in a white uniform

Whispering words of forgiveness, love and peace



I lost the battle but won the war



He looks through my window now and then

I pass him by on the street

I ignore him

He retreats



He glances back just to make sure

I defy him to come and get me



I scream at him, "You got my brother you motherf*#*r!!"

But he won't get me
Another picturesque dramatization....of dangerous behavior

---
title: "Summer in Indiana With A Friend"
writer: "jules"
slug: "summer-in-indiana-with-a-friend"

A PLACE TO STAY
FORGET ABOUT WORRIES
TO REMEMBER GOOD FRIENDS
ALL THE WAY HOME

THE TRAINS SO LOUD
SO LONG AND BIG

SUCH FEW TREES
NOT MUCH BREEZE
ONLY HOT AIR THAT BLOWS

THE LAND SO FLAT
FIELDS FULL OF CORN AND WHEAT
CORN AND WHEAT GROWS AND GROWS
BEFORE YOU KNOW IT
SOON IT WILL BE IN OUR PLATES
SO LONG AGO...
WHEN CORN WAS JUST A SEED
NOW IT'S JUST DIPPED IN BUTTER
FOR OUR SUPPER
LOTS OF HEAT
SUN SO HOT
FILLS NIGHT AIR
CAN'T BREATH AT NIGHT

CATTLE THEY RAISE
TURN GREEN GRASS BARE
NO IDEA OF WHAT'S IN FOR THEM

THE TORNADO'S THAT COME AND GO 
BIG FUNNELS OF GREENISH-BLACK CLOUDS
DESTROY SUCH BEAUTIFUL LAND AS 'INDIANA'

A PLACE TO STAY OR GET AWAY
FORGET YOUR TROUBLES 
TO MEET NEW FRIENDS
AND CHERISH THE MEMORIES
OF OUR TEENAGE YEARS 
DURING SUMMERS AGO

I CONFIDE IN HER FRIENDSHIP 
HER LOVE WAS DEAR AND SPECIAL
TO GET MY THOUGHTS TOGETHER
AS WE BOTH TALKED AND LISTEN
TO SEE A SMILING FACE OF A FRIEND
WHO HELPED A FRIEND TO SEE
A PLACE SET ASIDE FROM THE REST
TO HOPE AND DREAM THAT A FRIEND DOES EXSIST

SO OFTEN THROUGH THE YEARS
I HAVE FOND MEMORIES OF YOU 
OF TIMES WE'VE SPENT TOGETHER
AND THE THINGS WE USE TO DO 
ALL THESE YEARS WE STOOD MILES APART
BUT YET SO CLOSE IN OUR HEARTS
WE ARE STILL THE BEST OF FRIENDS
AS CLOSE AS SISTERS COULD BE
WE GREW OLDER AND HAD FAMILIES OF OUR OWN
TILL THIS DAY WE ARE BEST OF FRIENDS
AS LIKE ON THAT SUMMER DAY IN INDIANA

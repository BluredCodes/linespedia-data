I knew a fat bastard, he hit the mat faster
Than a statue of Bill Gates at Apple made of black plaster
I tried ignoring, just like my mother taught in my youth
But he kept raging on until Immortal brought him the truth
He was a liar, and kept attacking all that I know
Morality was lost he faced my verbal missile silo
I didn't have to swing once the demon stayed asleep
He will wake up in time but for now I'll let my rage be weak
It's hard for my to keep mouth un-pottied when I hear his name
But I'll keep my composure because noone now fears his fame
One day our paths will cross, and then he'll know hurt
Thunderbolts'll fly so fast it will give him a slow jerk
Unless he tries to contact Zeus and speed the pace up
I'll acquire something new to feed my basement
Until then I'll sit back with sounds
For my day to come to attack his crownThat prick

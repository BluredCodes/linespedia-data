title: "Rose Upon The Hill"

A rose atop the yonder hill grows.
Pink as sunset clouds.
She sings across the mountain wind
A song to strong to sing aloud
I alone may hear this tune.
My own special bequest.
It wakes me to the morning welcome
And sets me to my rest.
And who so ever shall find that rose
And unearth it to their pleasure 
Shall have the Everlasting Joy;
A love that knows no measure.
Perpetual gifts this rose does bring
To the man that holds its prize.
No hate or woe this person feels
(if the case would ever arise).
And why, you ask, have I not picked it
And all those joys pursue?
Well, I have. And here it is
For me to give to you.

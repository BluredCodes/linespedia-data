title: "NeverLand"
writer: "silver"

I want to lift myself above on wings,
Only to discover that my childhood things,
Aren't real.
My wings were in my mind,
But I can still see them so clearly.
Almost as if I could fly again,
If only I could still clap my hands,
With Peter Pan,
For Tinkerbell,
And say,
"Believe."

Was it called Neverland for a reason,
Or only a challenge,
To make it never,
Daring us not to hold onto hope,
Of fairy dust,
And mermaid lagoons,
daring for us not to believe?

It was a warm spring night,
we were all at your house,
drinking laughing. 
You and I'd been friends for two years.
You graduated last year, 
I was a Junior. 
How the time flew by.
I don't remember why we were in the hall alone.
You were drunk,
I was getting there. 
You said to me,
"Why didn't we ever hook up?"
I smiled behind my brown eyes,
you leaned over and kissed me.
I tugged at your waist. 
I followed you to your room,
we fooled around for a little,
and then you asked me if I wanted to have sex.
A few minutes later you had the condom,
and it was gone like that, 
16 years of innocence.

Death

Old ladies perfume,
Smelling of roses.
People are crying.
The smell of magnolias.

An open coffin,
Lay in place of a bed.
And I am still sobbing 
because you are dead.

I think of your smile,
your sparkling green eyes
as you look at me fondly, 
like I was a prize.

But those days are gone. 
You’re cold to a chill.
The lawyers are wondering, 
who inherits what in your will.

A kiss of death
Still lies on your lips
Your pale lips,
Still remembering their first kiss.

Everything falls silent
as the service begins.
I take a deep breath,
Take everything in.

Life as I know it,
has ended for me.
But I will always remember,
How you love me.10/?/99,

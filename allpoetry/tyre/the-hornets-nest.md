---
title: "The Hornet's Nest"
writer: "Tyre"
slug: "the-hornets-nest"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/3852-The-Hornets-Nest-by-Tyre"
---

My heart is heavy, sinking deeply away
As the world seems distant in its display
Of actions taken from an explosive affair
Wondering how far they are willing to dare

Grave images fill a darkened sky with dread
Grieving over the lives of thousands now dead
Whose many worlds must now suffer great loss
Being destroyed by the madness they cross

Where is this enemy we must now face
Whose cowardly voice hides, hoping not to trace
For fear that we may crush their boastful stand
And crumble into dust, their host's noble land

Come now brave leaders, where is your bold speech
What splended lessons do you dare to teach
You have our attention, now make it known
Or slither under a rock until your cover is blown

Fear was intended through the terror you laid
Sorry to disappoint you, are you now dismayed
For anger is the might that will lay you to rest
Open your eyes, you have stirred the hornet's nest

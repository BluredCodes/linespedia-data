Old Town bricks, spread like a sheet.
The fame, the glory,
The woes, the worries,
Lay black at my feet.

They flow away from the square,
Over the river,
With saintly procession,
Into the castle's care.

They surround the good king
Wencenslas, and fill his rivers
With other feet,
Feet not of my doing.

Here feet leave no footprints on the bricks,
Rather, brickprints are left on feet.

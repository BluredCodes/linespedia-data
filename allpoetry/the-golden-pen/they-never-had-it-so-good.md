Eons ago,
Our grandmothers tilled the feild 
While our grandfathers on their cartwheeled-ploughs
Readied the soil for its yearly harvest yeild.

They didn't have a telephone...
They didn't have an electric stove..
Not even a measily electric mixer did they own.
Yet, By Jove!
They never seem to complain at all!
They toiled all day way into night
Churning milk into butter
Our grandfathers walked miles and miles just to pump their water!

The school children on their feet wore boils
As they carried merrily their satchels on their backs
True, there were brats back then and some children were spoiled
But their teachers cracked their whips and brought them back on track.

Today we live in the lap of luxury...
With technology growing stronger every day
Computers do our homework...easing the worries
That burdened our aunts and uncles burning their brains away...
Now we can communicate easily
Even if far away
The telephone, a modern day miracle..
United us all 
And the cellphone beefed up our connection 
by letting us talk as we travel miles and miles away.

They never had it so good way back when..
Our grandparents milked the cows..by hand!
Yet we continue to complain
We pull our hairs on end and go stir-crazy,
Like ungratful and spoiled little jerks
With our every-day-to-day little luxuries
That make our living easy
And yet create more work!

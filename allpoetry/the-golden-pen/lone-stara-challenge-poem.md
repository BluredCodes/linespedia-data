'Twas in early March 
Underneath a purple-tinted sky, 
Daffodils decked the meadow, making a lovely path 
Under the Elm tree saw I 
the figure of a colt thathered there, 
A beautiful little colt with the gentlest brown eye, 
With golden-honey sun-tossed hair. 
There was something strange about the colt, I thought 
As I walked towards it to catch a closer glimpse 
Might its beautiful body be caught 
Stabbed by a branch or the lance of some wicked nymph? 
In his forhead I could see 
Grew a branch from a lowly Sycomore tree.. 
Or was the poor pony stabbed with a lance, 
Trodding along on his journey 
As I drew near, catching a better glance.. 
This was no ordinary colt tied there... 
But a beautiful unicorn standing next to me.. 
On bended knee I stood in awe and wonder 
Never had I seen such beauty before.. 
I petted him gently and carressed his silky mane 
On his neck he wore 
A golden necklace, Lone Star was his name 
The last of an ancient breed of ancient lore 
Now, I thought, how rare... 
It must be a sign from the heavens revealing my life's destiny, 
My heart believed then and there, 
In the power of love, fairy-tales and the answers to life's mysteries.. 
For standing before me was a creature so rare, 
I thought only exsisted in fairy-tales and fantasies. 
Yet fate proved me by far 
How wrong and faithless I've been, 
This encounter I'll  remember forever..my rondezvous with Lone Star 


Mary Gonzalez

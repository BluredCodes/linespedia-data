It once was a pool
so beautiful and clear
with images of fish and sea,
And the children would play
in the warm summer sun
singing the songs of laughter.

But then one day
a raft was left
on the waters, once so clear,
So the evening frogs
came out to play
and on the raft, they did sing.

Several days later
the waters were interrupted
by squiggly little things,
And the little children
watched with delight
not daring to take a swim.

They fed the little fishies
blades of grass
and watched them grow some legs,
And the poor little pool
once beautiful and clear
was now a swamp of fish-like things.

"So what do we do now?"
the parents did ask
of the children who caused these things,
But the answer was clear
as the pool had been

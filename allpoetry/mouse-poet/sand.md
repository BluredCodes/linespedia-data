The night,dark and heavy,weighed upon my brow.The concerns of the eveningwere many,though one stood outabove them all.It was the passingof time.There was nothing I could doto stop it.All my attemptsended in failure,in aggravation,in agony.It just kept slippingthrough my fingers,yet holding me captive.Now as I am aged and tired,I am forced to submit to it's power.I am forced to watchthe grains of sand,slipping through my handsinto nothing.2001,

---
title: "Unknown Soldier"
writer: "Jet"
slug: "unknown-soldier"
source: "AllPoetry"

---

I lost a friend today.
In this our finest hour.
His death has stained the world with red
And turned our victory sour.

Today the world rejoices
For the war is at an end.
I look around but all I see
Are the bodies of my friends.

In fields of waste, where the dead lie
Amid the melting snow
I stand and listen quietly
For the bugles' final blow.

As the sad notes fade away
You may rest at last,
Knowing that your memory
Will stop repeats of the past.

Lest we forget the prices paid
By the friends who've gone away,
I can only ask forgiveness
Of those who died today.Wrote this for a Rememberance Day ceremony years ago.

LEAVING  ON  A ...*He sang about leaving on a jet planeNow he will never be back again...Irony with a measure of pain.With a simplistic eloquence wrote he,My first step exploration of poesy.He sincerely made me believe,Though now it’s hard to conceive,Reacquainting oneself with natureWould exorcise the human creaturesDemon of industrial greed,From financial gain its soul be freed.Late teens turned into adolescenceI identified with an irate Zimmerman,Even that drove me around the bend,Now I relax to an Irish Morrison,Yet I still hide my tattered heart fromThe ruthless world that shred it apart.On the days I feel no one is listeningI take the liberty of reminiscingAbout wide-eyed days of innocence.Days that left,long ago, on a jet planeAnd will never be back again…*(To Henry John Deutschendorf, 1944-97)

LOOKING BACK

I'm looking back 
At days of collegiate affection,
A time when self-centered
Sheer aplomb mixed with
A gleeful lack of direction...
A million cups of instant coffee,
Two million bummed cigarettes,
A time when we were
Still a bit young to,
As yet,have any regrets.

I’m looking back 
At the taming of radical needs,
For each revolutionary theory 
That’s yet barely breathing,
Half a dozen are deceased...
Like old,fallen leaves replaced
By new,different ones,
At you and I and
How far from our
Past selves we’ve come.

I’m looking back 
At the demise of naïve cheer,
Thrown blocked in cement
Into the cold waters of disinterest
By my sophisticated years...
Searching for sunken treasure,
Hunting for missed chances,
Then slowly admitting 
They perished in the depths
Of their circumstances.

I’m looking back
At what I can never reclaim.
Each year as I grow older,
I look back more frequently
With increasing pain...
Yet in all these years
So very little has really changed,
Even today,what I have to do
And what I want to do
Are rarely one and the same.

title: "and will thee speak?"
writer: "S undine"

and will thee speak...
for angels wait
in keeping
as this
heart
doth
quake... 

such words leave sorrow
inside out
for all to see; for none
to doubt 

will thee then cease this
silent pall
this black, unending 
icy hall has ne'er seen tears a'flood as these 
they crest as waves; 
they warm dead
seas 

for spirit's sacrifice
be true
'tho here must serve as life
in lieu 

and ever, anon, ringed
with grace
this tender time; this
softer place
holds one lone heart left
beating slow
in echo
to where love did go 

and dirges haunt this
these caverns
cold
dark empty paths
where wisdom's
old
creased furrowed brow did once decree: 
Be nothing now of life to me. 

Be as a softly whispered prayer.
Be mourning dove.
Be ev'ning's
care. 

Turn back away thy
face of love
Hide warm, enchanted
traces of
each, ev'ry gift. 

Be naught to me
but indian summer's
reverie 

Oh God!
for this? this wicked fear?
this cruel, piercing
silence
here? 

s 
.
. 
in waiting...14aug01,

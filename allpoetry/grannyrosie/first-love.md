FIRST LOVE
 
Single red rose bud on your grave,
Dead, you are forever gone.
The rose to epitomise once great beauty,
The thorns reminders that yet, I mourn.

Folded petals conceal great love,
I prayed in vain, would be for me.
Fate dealt cards in her favour,
Setting your spirit free.

I hold you still, deep within,
Eternally, your flame will burn.
Tho you are gone, I am not alone,
You are with me, at every turn.

I see your face in music,
Your voice is carried by the breeze.
Nights are comforted in your arms,
You, I yet aspire, to please.

Every rose I see gives me recall,
Special moments that we shared.
Drinking life’s cup, in excess, together.
For your departure I was ill prepared.

Dear love, there will be others.
We both know this to be true.
No matter where my heart goes,
First, it belonged to you.

Hugh

Dedication to Hugh James Newby, his Mum, Dad and Sister and extended family, and All families who have lost a treasured cherub 
*****************************



Hugh 

I can hear you calling Mummy 
But I can't come back just yet 
I've got my screwdrivers with me 
And I think I'll fix the step 
An' the fence, an' the door an' everything I see 
Don't worry Mummy cause Nuggets here with me 

I can hear you calling 'Neke 
But Ive got some things to do 
I'm going in next door awhile 
Then I'll come back to you 
And we can play awhile before I have to go to bed 
I think we'll draw and colour in or watch TV instead 

I can hear you calling Daddy 
But I have a problem see 
I fell into the water and its very cold for me 
Nugget tried to get me out 
He couldnt quite reach I 'spose 
I wished you come and get me 
Cause I don't like water up my nose 

I can hear you calling Mummy 
But a bright star came to me 
I think it was an angel, then a little kid like me 
Reached out his hand, he knew my name 
Said 'Hugh come with me 
You can be a heaven cherub 
And a bright star too you'll be' 

I can hear you crying Mummy 
As you dress my body cold 
I wish that I could tell you that Im never getting old 
I'll always be your little boy 
I'll always love you so 
But sometimes when God needs us 
I guess we have to go 

You dressed me in my favourite clothes 
My working boots as well 
Remember Daddy, the big bulls 
I chased them off to hell 
I'm glad you put my screwdrivers 
In this place for me 
Cause up there in heaven maybe 
A fix it man I'll be 

I can see you sobbing Poppa Les 
As the sand trickles and falls 
You are the very bestest mate 
A Granpappa could ever be 
And when you come to heaven 
I reckon it will be fun 
You wont be so old anymore 
And we can jump and run 
We can play trucks and footy 
And go for walks and things 
And it will be the greates time 
Coz we will both have wings 

I see your tears my Uncle Rob 
And I worry bout you so 
You are the bestest Uncle a kid could ever know 
Remember how you swung me high 
With laughter in your heart 
Well if you hold that memory close 
We'll never be apart 

In heaven there are lots of kids 
Cause God needs us you see 
There's Mums and Daddys left their kids 
And miss them constantly 
So God gives us the job of smudging tears from misty eyes 
And handing out the biggest hugs 
And helpin them realise 
Heaven is a great big home 
Where I guess I like to be 
And I know that in the future 
You'll all be here with me 

*********************************************

Hugh was drowned in his next door neighbours spa at 18 months of age, a sad lesson for us all.

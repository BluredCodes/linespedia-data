MESSY BUSINESS

Just the perfect length,
To fit snugly in my hand,
Softness surrounds the hardness,
It is really something grand

The bulbous bit, perched there on top,
Holds a pure delight,
I wish it were available,
Each, and every night.

Sometimes I think I could have four,
Then even go again for more.
It’s messy when the centre runs,
Down my hands, sticky thumbs.

My tongue gets very busy,
Flicking, licking up so quick.
I hate it when it is all gone.
I still have to learn the trick.

To make it last forever.
It would be so good you see,
If my ice-cream didn’t melt so fast,
Slop all over me.

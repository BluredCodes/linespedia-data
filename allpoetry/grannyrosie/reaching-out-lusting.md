REACHING OUT, LUSTING
 Sitting there, enticing me,
The taste of you, so sweet.
You have all that I desire,
My every expectations meet.

Not to fresh, not too old,
Just right, I must confess.
You fill my heart with longing,
Emphasise my wickedness.

I know, you are not good for me.
My heart pounds with each new taste.
I must beware the dangers there,
Yet, I run in, with a carefree haste.

Having walked upon this road before,
Achieving nothing less than pain,
Convince me please, before I succumb,
This time will be worth the gain.

I would pray on it, but lacking time,
Submission invades with a gentle sigh.
Would I could resist you, wicked one.
Reaching out, lusting for, this Pecan Pie.

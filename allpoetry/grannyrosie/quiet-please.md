QUIET PLEASE

Why must my neighbour mow his lawn,
Just when my Muse arrives?
He stops, I start,
Oh!, Bless his heart,
The whipper-snipper up the path.

The kids WILL belt that great big ball,
(It's made of lead I think)
Up and down the pavement,
Could drive a girl to drink.

The whining of electric drills,
Will send me mad I'm sure,
When I think quiet has set in,
They party on next door.

The man across the road he has,
A voice of foghorn sound,
While dogs around this neighbourhood,
In packs, I'm sure, abound.

I wander to the rivers edge,
Tranquility to seek.
An outboard motor rushes by,
Then from the corner of my eye,
I shriek in terror as i see,
In the distance, A jet-ski.

I only want some quiet place,
To entertain my Muse.
The frig is running,Oh!,So loud,
And in the park there is a crowd,
Laughing, and a drone (not bee),
A noisy model plane I see.

I hide my head beneath a rug,
Ear plugs are firm in place.
A torch to see, a pen in hand,
the batteries die, cant understand!

Into the country for a drive,
A sheltered spot to find,
Apart from ants and buzzing flies,
This scene will hasten my demise,
PICNICERS!!!!!!

I settled for the local pub,
You scarcely would believe,
A heavy metal band arrived,
With haste I had to leave.

I want to write some poetry, 
My Muse would like it too.
With all the noise around me now,
I think I'll try the zoo.

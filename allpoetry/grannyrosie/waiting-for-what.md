WAITING. FOR WHAT?
 Solitude, silence, surrounding,
Only the wind murmuring by.
In the distance, the muffled noise of a clock.
A knock! A slow relieved sigh.

Rush to the door with a smile on my face.
Smile fades, replaced by a lost frown.
Someone seeking the address of a friend.
New to this part of town.

Moments plod by, stretching to hours,
No reprieve for this lonely pursuit.
Find a CD with our favourite tune,
Tears fall to the sound of a cascading flute.

Turn on the radio, no relief there.
Go to the PC, pull up a chair.
Can't face the thought of any but you,
Go bad to solitude, feeling so blue.

Lie on the bed, at the ceiling I stare,
Thought harvesting at random in my mind.
Drift into blackness, awake with a start,
Open my eyes, such a treasure they find.

Held up unavoidably, yes dear, I know.
Same old excuses, disbelief doesn't show.
Pride nonexistant, at least you are here,
Lie by my side, whisper your lies in my ear.

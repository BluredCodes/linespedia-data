It happens that I remember you to forget you.
I'm afflicted with inundating thoughts of you
which peck at like birds at my head.
I don't know perhaps there are travelled rivers
tired of the water they carry.
As if by revenge they sink boats as they tarry.
Even in this state I carry my sustenance
in a crate filled with precious moments I miss,
and are implacably recorded like age rings
in the veins of the trees.

Thats how I wanted to love you forgetting you!
Like blue tassels gone with the wind in its invisible
hands.
They dissapear and nobody knows where.
I find you alone and abandoned in the ruins of your own
turbulant impulses.

And I hear lamentations and crying from angels
disillusioned by your inept and elusive search for
happiness that only emanates from within.
I only ask that you remember me like a bough of red roses
that with time wither and die.
But what they were is alive and living in an oasis
behind the sun!

I want to remember you to forget you
with maternal instincts.
That will never forget how you were
innocent and frail
full of life and tender.
I'll wait for you my love! my life!
like a port waits its ship.
But if one day you look back
and find there are steps missing
remember they can only be filled
with my love and prescence.

Some nights back I dreamt of thee
I thought the whole world was with me,
As I am blind and cannot see
Oh what, what will become of me.


From the day that I became blind,
No remembrance of you could I find,
But I hope you have thought of me,
In love you have sought for me.


`Tis saddening to become blind like I,
`Tis like to say to the world goodbye,
Yet I beg that you won't forget me,
In my love, you will never desert me.


Then,in my dream that night I thought I saw,
That in this poetry there was a flaw,
For you had already abandoned me,
So tell me, what will become of me.


`Tis like I am lost at sea,
And no one hears a blind man's plea,
Oh, what will now become of me?
Oh, what will now become of me?

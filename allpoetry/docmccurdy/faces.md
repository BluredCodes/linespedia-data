---
title: "Faces"
writer: "DocMccurdy"
slug: "faces"
source: "AllPoetry"

---

When I look around myself, I see faces,
When they converge, then, my heartbeat races,
I tell myself to wake up, they are just dreams.
For of reality I can find little, but only traces,
It's so difficult to live in, these confined spaces,
Oh to be able to flow, like unabated streams.


I live in a dream world, by myself alone,
 A world of fantasy, of men made of stone, 
 I shun from the living, I stifle my screams.
Those ambitions of mine, the wind blown,
And I remain to the world, hidden, unknown,
My visions are darkened, yet my eyes gleams.


When I look around myself, I see these flares,
they stalk me at all times, like alarming nightmares,
Entering my mind and body, that's what it deems.
Yet, when I gather my spirits, my substance scares,
Strength that once possessed me, no longer dares,
Then have I lost my senses; or what it seems.


When I look around myself, and see these faces,
Then freedoms round the corner, to take me places.

---
title: "The Score"
writer: "Beyond Zion"
slug: "the-score"

Behind the bar,
with a lit cigarette
I see him.
Beautiful, above the filtered smog
He sips a Corona.
I wait
Wondering if the music will draw him back,
or lead him forward.
I take a drag, 
then a stranger says "Hello"
But my, bloodshot eyes never leave him
The hello turns into a fuck you,
And then he moves.
Closer.
My cigarette gets cashed out,
so I light another one.
My beer gets warm waiting for him,
but the bartender slips me one more.
Patience lulls me into a  catatonic state,
but I'm still watching him.
The dance fades into a drag show,
and he walks to me.
I pull the cigarette 
and sip the beer as he approaches.
We look at eachother,
He smiles. 
I pay for the Corona he ordered
and we chat.
Small talk, name,age,
Blahzae, blazae,
Then the applause break the conversation 
and we focus on the dance floor.
But he whispers a sin in my ear,
So we leave.
And sin, without remorse.

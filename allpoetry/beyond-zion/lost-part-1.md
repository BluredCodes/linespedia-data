This twisting street,
Littered with beggars is familiar.
These lamps that cast shadows 
over dirty sidewalks are draperies
made to disguise it's apathy.
Past the park, I remember the breeze.
And, as a blanket of newspapers
frolick in the wind
His footsteps echo.

(He's coming)

The REAL Christmas Story


T'was the night before Christmas
And in our dear house
The kids had gone crazy
They jumped on the couch
"Where's Santa, where's Santa?!"
"I want my presents!"
"Shut up you damn kids
Santa ain't here yet!"
They bounded around
Too excited to sleep
They listened for Santa
But heard not a creak
They sat out their cookies
Beside them some milk
And put on their nightgowns
All made of silk
They looked out the window
To see if they could
See old Saint Nick
And his sleigh made of wood
We told them to go
And get ready for bed
They told us no
And they wished us dead
They wanted to stay up
And see the reindeer
Their father just glared
And said, "Get over here!
Now you go to bed
You ungreatful devils!"
But of course they stayed up
They were young rebels
They waited and waited
All the while getting roudy
They began to get sleepy
And soon they got pouty
Fnally at midnight
We scared them to sleep
We said if they didn't
Their presents we'd keep
They told us we couldn't
That Santa would stop us
We threatened to run
Over him with a schoolbus
So finally in bed
Those kids did go
We set out there presents
Little did they know
And in the next morning
Guess what they found?
Bundles of presents
All sitting around
They jumped and they squealed
And dashed to the boxes
Expecting race cars and Barbies
And boxes of chocolates
They found sweaters and socks
And a few bouncy balls
We were bombarded by anger
And upset little calls
Those ungreatful devils
Had evil in their eyes
But soon those devils
Would be quite surprised
We opened the door
And what did they see?
A brand new corvet
And a huge ass TV!
Mommy and daddy
Had bought quite a Christmas
They got everything
That had been on *their* wishlist
The kids had to settle
For unwanted gifts
And needless to say
They were quite pissed
The moral of the story:
Don't rely on your luck
At least once in your lifetime

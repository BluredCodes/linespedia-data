The night drew cold, so dark and so still
A limitless vista, so somber and chill
I extended my hand, deep into the night
My tired, sore heart was pounding with fright

Bright piercing stars, cold distant fire
The promisse of youth had been sold by a liar
Small and alone I reached for the sky
My leaden chest shook as I started to cry

I sank to my knees, my eyes open wide
Soul too exposed, with no place to hide
Great rivers of suns gleam in my mind
Leaving the smallness of life far behind

From places unseen and time yet uncounted
Beyond what I see, touch and feel and have doubted
A great love opens me up like a book
And my father tells me softly, you only need look

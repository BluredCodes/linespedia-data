Tonight I walked alone among the stars,
And came upon my favorite resting place.
I leaned against the fighting form of Mars,
And turned to look sweet Venus in the face.
My right hand rested on the good queen's throne,
My left hand held the pen that wrote these words.
The sun, just gone, still cast a mellow tone.
Comets soaring, sly galactic birds,
Around the moon, who lay there on her side.
And cleverly she smiled that Cheshire grin
And shared with me a secret I must hide,
A thrilling voice that chilled my open skin.
And there, among the final dying light,

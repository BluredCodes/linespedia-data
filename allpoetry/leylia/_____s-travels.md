---
title: "_____'s Travels"
writer: "Leylia"
slug: "_____s-travels"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/46168-_____s-Travels-by-Leylia"
---

"I'm off to find my story!"
she decided with a laugh,
and she packed her bags
and said good-bye
and started down her path.
Her mother watched her leave
from the window up above,
and knew that she
had raised her right:
She'd act in wisdom and love.

"I'm off to make my fortune!"
he proclaimed to all he knew.
They laughed and said
He'd never win,
He didn't have a clue.
He'd never had a job,
And he never finished school.
They watched him run
And begged the world,
"Take pity on the fool."

"I'm off to get my life back,"
someone whispered in the dark.
She packed her things
And planned the trip
On which she would embark.
She tiptoed past the broken glass,
Still bloody from before,
The man she loved
Slept soundly,
And she walked right out the door.

The soldier seemed so youthful
To someone far away
Up close, his eyes
Betrayed his age,
His heart in disarray.
His eyes revealed the death he'd dealt,
His mind a grisly tome.
He walked 
and walked
and walked
until he
finally
came
home.

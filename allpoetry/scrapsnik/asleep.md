The smell of your presence, beautiful face
Shape of your body, my fingered trace
Wouldn't things have been easy 
If we'd learned to leave them be...

Those were happy days, before turning memories
Muted nighttime, our private revelries
Adages of a life better lived.
Your sleeping mind's eye
Roll soft beneath your lids

I wonder where you play, the curl of your lip
That mischievous grin I saw in the early day
Before the dreams stole you away
In a vice like grip
My daytime trance,
As still as her clothes on the floor

It would be a fine thing,
If you still slept by my side
Sleep the only robber of your once everpresence
But long ago you awoke from our ephemeral time
Taking you to some better place

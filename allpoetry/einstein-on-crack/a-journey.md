Landing on the headland
I kissed upon the spot
Where upon I‘d landed
But there I could not stop
Looking all around me
Then saw the mountain peaks
That seemed to call me to them
The voices didn’t speak
Working my way to them
I looked around in awe
The beauty laid before me
I’ve never seen before
I reached the peaks quite easily
Drawing breath of wonder
Not beholding such before
It really made me ponder
Before me lay a wondrous scene
Of hills and dales pure
Leading to a valley
Though this I was unsure
Transcending down to take a look
At what bedazzled my eyes
Making my way forward
To look through it’s disguise
Entering the valley
So smooth and pure in form
Just a cave resided there
It seemed to be so warm
The heat down there was sultry
No cover, but so moist
A hidden cave not used much
My only path of choice
I entered it with caution
Damp and warm seemed nice
How had I arrived here?
And what would be the price?
Can I ever leave this place?
I pondered in my head
A feeling that’s so comfortable
Should I stay and make my bed?

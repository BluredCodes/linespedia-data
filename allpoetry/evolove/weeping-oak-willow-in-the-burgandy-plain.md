Weeping Oak Willow in the Burgandy Plain



I look to this tree /with branches/ that lift off into every direction\

no leaves are dressed on this mighty bare statue like monument

and the bark is gray with just tones~ carrying no shades~ or tints 

rest in an area of land that no one comes to anymore because

no one ever did

they choose not to see the ground when its so bare and trees

just run like animals in a forest vacant ~ to all shrubbery

we need paper to write our love across and send it to the 

submarines that sit and wait on the ocean floor

presidents wipe their mouth on the resting place of that 

hummingbird ~ little winged elf doesnt want to hum anymore

no one understands that he has no where to land

on the vacant lot in the forest that reminds the statue of 

the bare monument ~ on the piece of freedom no one chooses

to visit anymore ... because no one ever did~

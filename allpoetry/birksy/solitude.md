I am in a hollow,
deep, so deep, and this
solitude makes me brittle,
keeps me down, so down.

No footholds in the rock,
no space to hardly breathe,
I am lost within myself,
There’s many needs I’ve lost.

Hungry for a spoken word,
thirsty for a gentle kiss,
please come quickly, overhead
I hear the morning breeze.

And when I wake, if I
wake again, and find you near to me,
I will call you solitude, and drink
my fill of you.

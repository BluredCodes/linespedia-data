That cough’s from her smoking,
you can hear it deep down,
you can tell that she hardly
knows it’s there.
Her children have the
same cough, she thinks
it’s hereditary, in a
way, I suppose it is.
Though when she’s dressed all in
black, slowly coughing, slowly
crying, she won’t remember,
won’t ever blame herself.
That cough’s from her smoking,
you can hear it, hope it
gets her, hope at last, the
downward trend might be stopped.

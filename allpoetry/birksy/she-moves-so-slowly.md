Across the square, before the bench,
there’s the woman who moves slowly,
and her legs used to work, she’s a woman
who’d rather be not there,
missing from the square.

The fresh wind blows her scarf, she
stops, though it’s hard to notice,
I don’t wait to see her go,
There’s more that I must see,
She moves so slowly.

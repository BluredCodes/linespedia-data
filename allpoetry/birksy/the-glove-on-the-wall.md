title: "The Glove on the Wall"

I am drawn to the wall opposite.
On it sits a glove, so old its colours fading fast.
It’s been there at least a week, and still
no one has claimed it.

Beside it, sits a snail, its shell
broken, insides pecked into mush,
curiosity for a tatty old glove,
the only crime.

The sky starts to cloud over, but still the glove
sits, and I remember now, the date
the time, and I'm able to rise, to
cross the road, reach down and take the glove.
Take my glove home once more.

Huddled on a station,
No-one’s going to say hello,
No-one’s going to admit you exist,
But I guess you’ve got used to that.
We’re trained to ignore you,
The tannoy bleats the warnings,
But I know you’re just me,
Any moment from now.
I’d give you some money
But I wouldn’t know how,
So, instead, I overlook,
And board my comfy train.

These walls aren’t as thick as I first thought,
I hear it all, though silence
should surround me. I think,
though thinking should hardly
be a part of me.
There are others, who can tell my sins,
but they, too, have doubts. I can’t
let them know just how much I’m
lost.
Then I look outside, steal glances
I should not afford, and am
torn between a love for the world,
and a love for my Lord.
I’ll take a walk, maybe a last
one, through the trees, down
to the gate, and with my trembling
weak hands enter, stand and
weep for troubles past and present.
Then, when my tears are shed, I shall rest,
once more, on walls, not as thick
as I first thought.

title: "A Space for the Weeds"

There’s a space to hide,
at the end of the track, nettles
on either side and in front,
but enough space for one person
to sit, pull the petals from a flower.

And I cannot lie, it is I who
frequents it, whenever the need
falls upon me, I sit upon
the nettles and the weeds, and
look up to the birds flying high.

When I go, I have asked for
a piece of me to stay there,
keeping watchful eyes on the weeds
for I fear it might be, for a while,
looked after, and that would never do.

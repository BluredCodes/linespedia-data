She passed right through me,
as if she couldn’t remember what we were.

I caught her arm, pulled her round,
I pushed her auburn hair back behind her ear.

‘Don’t you remember me?’ I thought,
but she frowned for a second, looked away.

‘We were different people, then,’ she said,
‘How different can we have been?’

We shook hands, parted, I waited to
see if she’d look behind again.

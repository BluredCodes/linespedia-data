On my shelves, a book on
fatherhood, hardly opened,
now likely not to be,
a present for the charity
bag.
Such physical reminders
I can do without, need
to try and limit the pain
to everything I feel
inside.
I leave the book upon
the shelf, walk away
smile at my lads, I
hope one day I can make them
understand.

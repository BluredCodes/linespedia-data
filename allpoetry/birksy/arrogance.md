If he didn’t look so arrogant,
I wouldn’t mind so much, the
carton of drink, the wrapper he
threw on the floor.
But he sits like a plump turkey
on a perch, as if he owns
the place, as if he
could tell each one of us what we should do.

But whatever my angst,
I know there can be no retribution,
know that if he didn’t get away
with it, he wouldn’t do it.
Oh, I hope there’s double
purgatory this morning.

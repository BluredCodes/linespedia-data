The Christmas tree,
The last thing we bought together,
Still trussed up, leaning heavily on the 
fence panel, the broken fence panel
as you liked to refer to it.
We’ll be celebrating apart,
Apart from the screaming rows, the
disconcerting silences, the
strained tensions that
Christmas always brought.
And that day we promised
to decorate the tree, is long gone,
If I’d have known then, the
baubles would have stayed on
the shelf, maybe that’s where I
should have stayed.
The Christmas tree, that
Christmas tree, must be as cold
as me.

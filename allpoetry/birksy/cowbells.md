My foot struck
the cowbell, and alone
in the field, I saw
the debris and
stared.
Kicking a tune of
metal, the Spring's
not come again,
and we're left
with Autumn's rot.
So I took a day,
piled the bells
into the middle
and walked away,
but whenever
I turned, they stood
just as high,
just as high.

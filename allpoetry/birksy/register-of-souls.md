---
title: "Register Of Souls"
writer: "birksy"
slug: "register-of-souls"
source: "AllPoetry"

Pressed back into holes
in the tunnel wall, he
stands, sees our faces
looking out at the bricks,
in his fingers a black
biro that silently ticks his
register of souls.

He’s not a bad man, you know,
just doing his job, it’s the
government that pays him,
though you won’t see him
on any wage slips, he's paid
slightly differently,
not the usual range of perks.

For when there’s a crash,
that injures some folk,
you’ll see him walk through
the wreck and the smoke,
quietly counting the
people at his knees, and tick,
ticking, quite dispassionately.

So, when next in a tunnel,
and you see those strange
holes, remember he’s there
with his register of souls,
give him a smile, make
it quite plain, that you’ve
no intention of seeing him again.

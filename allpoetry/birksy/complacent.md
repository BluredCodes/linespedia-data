Sitting still in the centre
of a soccer pitch, no-one else
around no-more, can’t ask
for a light, can’t get a back
scratch, sitting still on
a soccer pitch, forgot to be
nice to the others.

The dark sky’s arrived,
and wind blows through the
grass, he’s the only object
to hit, so target practice
is all about him, before
he might have liked it,
before he didn’t care.

Now he’s on his own,
he knows what put him
there, complacency is
a delicate beast, he
throws the dirt for
real, but there’s
no-one left to hit.

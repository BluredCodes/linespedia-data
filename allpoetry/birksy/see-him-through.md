---
title: "See Him Through"
writer: "birksy"
slug: "see-him-through"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/38211-See-Him-Through-by-birksy"
---

Quietly, oh so quietly, that's what it felt like,
he moves, but it makes him hurt to move
without her, and there's so much space
to navigate, he wonders how he can.
The dark night makes him cool, he stands
on the grass, looking up into the sky,
feeling fate heavy on his shoulders, here is
a man who is as unsure as he's ever been.
He runs his hand through his hair, leans
against the bark of an old apple tree, apple
trees get it easy, he thinks, but of course they
have their own drawbacks, he wonders if they hope.
And if a star falls across the sky, he will make
the deepest wish upon it, perhaps even try to
catch it, but the fence looks high, weren't
many fences needed jumping where he's been.
The air tingles in his mouth down his throat,
and his heart is saturated in ice particles,
ache particles, and he can only think of her
to try and melt them away.
So much behind him, the weeds are thick
at his ankles, he waits so alone, but not so
patiently, quietly, oh so quietly, he hopes
the world will see him through.

On my face, the cracks
appear, the cracks of stress,
I feel no pain, but that's
deep inside.
I sit, great fissures
on my skin, yet no-one
sees, they just look out
windows thick with dirt.
I can only close my eyes,
pretend I can hold myself
together through my mind,
but in truth, it is my
mind that has the cracks,
my mind that needs so
much mending.

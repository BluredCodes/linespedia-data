She used to knit all day,
click, click, like wallabies doing
two-steps, never dropped a
stitch, never looked to see
us children making silly faces.
She’s a pillar of my youth,
holds a high and mighty place,
though she used to make us
breathe her smoke, get her
fags from the shop when
required, light them up so
she didn’t have to stop clicking.
Now she’s gone, her unfinished
work a jumper for me, it read
best nephew, but the W
was unfinished, and no-one
here knows what else to do.
I fold it up and place it
on the box, she’ll need
something to do wherever
she ends up, she was the
greatest knitter I’ve ever
known. Just not the best
Auntie.A purely fictional character.

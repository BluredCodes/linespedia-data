The black leather goddess,
looks over to me, never a
smile, she’s far too restrained,
a sip of her hot drink, a
look to the heavens, I
wonder how tall she thinks
her pedestal is.
Still, the black leather coat
forgives all of her sins,
shame, to me, at least, if
it’s really only PVC.
Flick of her scarf, sigh
of her contempt, so difficult
to stand, among us lesser
beings.

There’s a mother and a daughter, who
sit very close on the bus.
I know that they are, mother and daughter, for they
don’t speak, just thumb through the magazine,
one looking over the others shoulder.

They always get on at the same place, day
upon day, but in the early evening
they board separately, not sitting together,
how good to find their own space.

The mother and daughter keep looking out for the
other. And there’s always a seat to fit in.

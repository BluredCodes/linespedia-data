---
title: "Scissors"
writer: "birksy"
slug: "scissors"

I never used to carry scissors,
never saw the need,
that’s changed now.
I hold them tightly in my bag,
Just waiting for the moment
when I snatch them out
to right that injustice in the world,
the quicksilver prongs, snip, snip, snip.
Oh, the sound, leaving, relieving my hands,
my eyes, of those monstrosities, how dare
they come in here, it’s all gone very wrong.
Then, when I can stand no more, when
need has turned into oily pleasure, I flee,
scissors with me, though once again
returned to the bag.
When they awake, they will see that
the flick of hair they
thought was covering the head,
is no more, and, yes!, they are
truly bald.

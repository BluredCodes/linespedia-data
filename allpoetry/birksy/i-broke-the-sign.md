I broke the sign, clean in two,
Ran for the side street,
Trained feet passing pavement three
slabs at a time.

Then later, I walked past again,
slowly from the top of the road,
watched the work I’d done
saw some plastic on the floor.

Now it’s the next morning,
And from across the road
I stand, draw on a cigarette,
And light another.

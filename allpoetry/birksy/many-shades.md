I lie, the water sustains
my frame, so close
to sinking, I twitch my
eyes, but little more,
what’s lurks beside it best
ignored.

The sky has its many shades,
today it chooses blue,
and I watch the fancy
birds swoop and dive low,
they think I'm scenery,
too.

And I've thought of
turning over, swimming to
a different spot,
but in truth there's little
chance of finding something
better.

The water just supports me,
as I lie, or perhaps it
doesn't, perhaps I may have
grown enough, learned enough,
to float all by my very
self.

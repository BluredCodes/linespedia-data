title: "The Lane"

The lane, framed by
evergreen, sits in shade,
the hushed air scarcely moving
the lightest leaves.
Natures sounds float
in from gardens, full of
bloom, the grass awash
with green, that waits the days
first footfalls.
I stand amidst this scene,
in awe of sights
not spoilt by our own greed,
and I want to stay awhile,
keeping guard lest simple
things should fall beneath
another’s gaze.
Then, when at night, I
find my soul still resting here,
I need not ask what more
I need, for, like the leaves,
I need not move
at all.

title: "I Kneel In A Skip"

I kneel in a skip,
dust has settled on my
shoulders, I wonder how
long I've been here, I've
done nothing but look down.
On my hand there's a spider,
got a rumble in his tummy,
he can't quite work out
how he's gonna eat me up.
And the sun's kind of warm
my back stopped aching long
ago, the wind sometimes chills
but it's always served me well.
I wonder if they'll move me,
must be somewhat in their way,
the builders sometimes look at me,
before they have their tea.
I kneel in a skip,
someone's discarded rubbish,
hoping that there might be another
who needs something just like me.

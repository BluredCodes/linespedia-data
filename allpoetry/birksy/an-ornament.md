So I find myself
less of an ornament
than before,
but just as breakable,
fragility’s not a quality
that is lost with age.
I find myself a
broad shelf, best
for the spread, for
the wait I’ll endure,
might as well be comfy
for the ride.
On my toe, a price
tag dangles, though I
dare not look, can’t
stand the thought
of it blank, like to think
I have some chance
of a buyer.

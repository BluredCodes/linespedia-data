It must be hard to walk six feet,
To wash your hands,
To say thank you,
To shut the bloody door.
Yet you walk through us,
Who can do it all the day.
I do not take your view,
I care enough to not be
rude, to choose to move,
to close the bloody door.
Maybe – I’ll ask you, too.

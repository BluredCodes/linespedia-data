I've sliced the cake,
got the eighth part
of a circle,
can't do what I want
to do, can't eat it
in front of you.
And a little fizzy
orange, eighth part of
a can, I hear it's
effervescence calling
me, but I sip, and no more.
You see, the sweets
are just temptation
that sometimes you can take,
but most others you
just look at them,
and wait.
Tomorrow they may be gone,
I drink some orange, eat some
cake, dare not blink just in
case the meal in front
of me is fake.

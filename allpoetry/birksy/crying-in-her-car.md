title: "Crying In Her Car"

Crying in her car, the morning
mist still rests above the grass,
she’s trapped, and her world is
getting smaller.
Bleak seems no lesser word,
where is there left to run, the
world has new eyes everywhere.
Caught up in the safety net,
perhaps it’d be better if she’d
hit the ground, there wouldn’t
be these tears anymore.
Tissues erase the physical,
but no mental tissues exist,
for what’s going on inside,
tonight, when she returns,
she’ll cry once again.

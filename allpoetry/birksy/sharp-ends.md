On a wet October afternoon,
The lazy rain runs down the hill
And two old women meet again
Their faces long, their lives are still.

He went before they had the chance,
To know the man he really was,
Till now in grief they stand alone,
Two women united by their loss.

Through the years they’ve both met here,
His grave has been the meeting place
Two Mrs. Sharps bow their heads,
To a bigamist who had no disgrace.

And back at home one makes the tea,
The other plays some lonely chords,
Another year they’ve passed alone,
To them life holds no more rewards.

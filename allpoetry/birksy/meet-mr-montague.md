Meet Mr. Montague,
A man whose life adds up
to no more than a few
dozen hello’s, less goodbye’s,
and no conjoining words at all.
Yet he smiles, he grins
and wants to be liked,
expects nothing, just hopes
that he’ll be your topic
of conversation.
Of course, now, he knows,
that the talks all about him,
not with him, he gave that up
years ago.
Huddled whispers from schoolgirls
and businessmen alike, he’s aware
we can all
sneer the same.
But, at least, Mr. Montague,
you know what is wrong with you,
which, of course, in fates way,
makes you one step ahead
of us all.

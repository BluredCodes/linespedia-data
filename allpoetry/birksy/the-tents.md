Camped in a field,
The 7:55’s no way to be woken,
Where are we today?
Another green field of no distinction.
Put on all my clothes, then venture out,
No sooner have I left, my feet are soaked right through.
Brush the horse, stroke his long back,
Breathe out plumes, breathe in ice drops,
Dip my head in the cold stream, hold my
breath for a time, then soap out the
dirt of yesterday, let it dry in the
winter sun.
Watch the tents, they are my home,
But I want more than this, keep
on writing, will be one day, the
thief of all I see.
Another train rumbles by, I
stand as proud as I can, catch the
eye of a young man, who thinks
he might know me.

The tree has a kink
in it, bold as brass,
smack in the centre,
it stands so bent, as
if it laughs at the
other trees around.
I can’t help but wonder
what caused this shape,
as if it thought twice
about what way to grow,
perhaps the sun was hidden
cold behind the clouds.
But it just is, I suppose,
a thing of great beauty
kinked in the middle, a
crooked grin to the world,
showing that things are
never that straight up
and down.

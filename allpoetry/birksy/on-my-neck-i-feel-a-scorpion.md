On my neck, I feel a scorpion,
though not awake, he slumbers
there, in then out, a threat.

His harsh feet tickle, but
I dare not scratch, he is
a foe not to be reckoned with.

I fear he's not for any other,
he is my own, feel it will live
upon me, die with me.

Oh, a scorpion I am home to,
and if ever I am happy,
I only have to think of he.

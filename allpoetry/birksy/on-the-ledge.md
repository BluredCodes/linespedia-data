On the ledge again,
been here before, haven’t
I? Got a bandage on my
toes from all the times
they’ve been stepped on,
a bandage round my mouth,
from all the things I could’ve
said, and a bandage across
my eyes, from all the things
I pretended not to see.
On the ledge with just the
wind, and the feeling of
height beneath me,
knowing the trick is
to wait for the ground to
meet you before
stepping off.

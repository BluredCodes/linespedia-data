They have a routine,
Stand up, sit down,
Open diary, open mail,
It’s a feet of engineering,
At times they even manage words,
Though a sentence goes begging.

They both seem very busy,
The pen marks all the dates,
Routine is the key,
Organisation a God,
Measured progress to Him now,
No free time any more.

In a way I’m jealous,
They never seem to argue,
Always know what they’ll do next.
But I can never be them,
It just wouldn’t suit my day,
Wouldn’t suit my own routine.

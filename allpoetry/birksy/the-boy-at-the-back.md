title: "The Boy At The Back"

It was me, the
boy at the back,
no new shoes, no
new bike, trying to 
keep up, just to be
liked, not doing very
well.
I was the back of
the queue, staring at
my feet, ashamed to
be without, still putting
on the bravest face,
would not admit
defeat.
And now I drive my
nice new car, watch
the school kids go
to class, that boy
at the back makes me
smile, he won’t always be
last.

Mama, you were everything to me
You were my husband
You were my children
You were my happiness 
You were my life

I did everything to prevent you from going
And when I knew I was heading to nowhere
I gave in and said to you, I better go with you
On this you said: “God will decide”
You consoled me

I know you are alive
I believe in the life after
I believe in the living dead
I believe in the resurrection
I know you are in the company of others before you

I know I am not alone and even
when I am alone I am not lonely
Your spirit is with me. You come with the trinity
You are closer to me than when you were alive
You are powerful, strong and compelling

Lusabe N. Len. 12/10/01I dedicate this poem to my friend whose mum we buried today in the morning. She is alone. She lost her job her job years ago because of some illness in her neck. She loved her mother dearly, but now she is gone. Ever watched a movie by the name "Bouble Jeopardy? Hers are multi-tragedies. But there are many more out there who share this same experience. My sympathies and prayer to you all.

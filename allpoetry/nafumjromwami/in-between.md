I am no longer a child
But I am not yet there
I am nineteen
Soon and very soon I will be there
But from here to there
It is like eternity

Last night I had a dream
In the dream I saw myself great-
in my own house, with my own car
With friends, with everything. 
Life was to its pick

Oh, come morning!
Usual story, usual song from mama and baba:
”when our daughter completes college, when
we start saving for our old age, when she gets a job
when she moves from here….

I get pissed of
I feel like telling them:
Dad, give me a break!
Mama, give me a break!
Give me time!
For I am nineteen!I know parents will be resentful of this. But after talking to young fellows of 19 and 20 from a cross section of the country, I realize they are anxious, stressful, uncertain of the future... These years are trying moments for them.

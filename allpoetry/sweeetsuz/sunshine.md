---
title: "Sunshine"
writer: "Sweeetsuz"
slug: "sunshine"
source: "AllPoetry"

Sunshine peeked through the clouds
sweeping open places in the sky,
showing the Sierra’s blanketed with
the winter’s mantle of fluffy white.
The panoramic majestic beauty 
reminding me why I love living here!
Starkly naked black tree fingers, tracing
beautiful definitions thru greening fields.
A man came here one fall, from a place
with more rainfall, he commented, catching
this place resting from the enormous bounty 
she usually provides, how ugly she looked 
all brown, and yes resting, from the normal
cornucopia of plenty which springs forth.
I smiled at him tolerantly knowing,
He would never understand. 
I sent him back to where the trees 
are only leafless in the snow,
but plenty does not grow.
Raindrops pound the streets, 
awash, with lakes standing in fields
promising waters to feed masses again.
Today sunshine, peeked thru,
winds chased some open spaces,
through the firmament to reveal
the beauty of the heights across
the greening fields to glistening
white parapets and I wonder if you
are looking down over me?

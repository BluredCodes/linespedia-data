Long ago when I was very young
My experiences were all in the hue of green
My loves songs yet remained unsung
You had not appeared in my scene

In my later days of youth, my dreams
As my eyes, my sexuality seemed sea blue
Seeking love I pondered all my various schemes
Somehow I knew for me, there was, only you

Suddenly I discovered I was woman grown
My charms abounded full and glowingly gold
Learning I studied all of love that were known
Praying soon you would sweep me off, brave, bold.

Now my youth has gone and see life’s “Fall”
Maturing like great wine with cinnamon brown
Working my art, my charms are at my call
Watching the door that you come with regal crown

I feel my time creeping away, oncoming age
Seeing my reflection, former beauty dawning gray
Pleading with the very fates with growing rage

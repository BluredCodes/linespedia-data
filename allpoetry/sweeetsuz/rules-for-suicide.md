The song says that suicide is painless
Me I think it is for fools
The very thing seems to be brainless
Unless you know the rules.

There are always rules for suicide, I’m sure
They must be in good taste.
Such as for illnesses with no cure
Lives filled with such a waste.

One strict never break-um rule for suicide.
Never take anyone with you.
This will make people more than glad you’ve died
But then not wanting to go too.


One must always be aware of others
Never ever leave a note
They fret the aunties dads and brothers
Caste no blames nor ever quotes.

Be very careful to never leave a mess
When the deed is done.
A kiss, a glass and then a guess
They might miss all the fun.

Do not be hanging around the house
Nor all puffy in the tub
Be very quiet like the tiny mouse
Rather than doing chafe and rub

Be sure you do not leave a mess upon the floor
To clean up all the blood
Nothing’s a worse memory than a lot of gore
Even when you wish you could

So my friend of all the rules for suicide
The one I love the best
Is after the deed and the innocent has died
They say now he is blest

He/she was never happy ever anyhow
With the troubles of this life
It has to be better for them to be gone now
From all this time of strife

So, there really are some suicide rules
Not only for the life takee
But, yes for all those other fools
Who will not let us be.

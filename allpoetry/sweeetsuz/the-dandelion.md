The lowly Dandelion is just a weed, a pest to most;
She peeks her little yellow head up everywhere;
In a while she erects her fairy seedling stalk;
A breeze or a passerby, her children take flight.

My baby granddaughter calls them wish flowers;
To make your wish, you blow the gossamer seeds.
The baby wishes for her mommy and daddy;
Mommy does drugs and daddy hurts she and her brother.

She and her little brother stay with Grandma to be safe;
She blows her fairy wish flowers and hopes;
Grandma has a lot of dandelions in her garden;
Little boys and girls need to believe in magic.

Grandma calls them wish flowers too, now;
The pretty yellow eyes will always have a home;
Years, there will be Dandelions in Grandmas yard.

Give me some “coke” “crack” “blow” a fix!
Make my problems go!
Think some Jack Black will?
No!
Faced with hunger, poverty and shame…
Racist dissemination, discrimination,
And pain,
Light up, tune in, turn on, and drop out,
Given em what they want, is about,
What they say we will
Yet we grovel and still….
Burn our babies rape our girls
Club and provoke us, then give us dope, 
And we use it!
We get high
Trapped in ghettos, poverty meant to demean
Food stamps welfare fraud, shooting galleries,
Drive by’s and baby killers
We “do” ourselves and they take it to
The bank!
Screams!
Injustice, addictions, afflictions and racists,
Minorities, majorities
They stand upon our heads
Breaking our backs, spirits, hopes, dreams.
Toss some programs and dollars, at it,
Come election day!
Flood the “hood” with AID’ s, drugs, crime.
Do the time!
Little brothers and sisters, look at you,
While you “fix it”.
Tripping, tipping, falling through the crack,
No way back!
It hurts to realize you respond
As they intend you to..
The kids at the playground
Will look up to you as the man
When you stand..
There and “fix it”!

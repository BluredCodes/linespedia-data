Always been a repremand
the world beckons the unborn children
happiness at same
we watch and wait for our redemption
great enough to hate you
death shall bid you sweet repose
there's a numbness in my room
a bridge is dimely seen through the fog of your misfortune
won't follow your command
a new dawn will surface againThis poem was written with a partner. We wrote each line blindly back and forth folding down the paper as we wrote it. It doesn't really have a theme but I thought it was still interesting somehow.

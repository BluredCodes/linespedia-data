title: "Speed Chessing"
writer: "Burnt at both ends"

There once was a fish,

who splashed in the sea,

his heart was in zugzwang,

his mind was en-prise.



down the exchange,

but on the attack,

damn the torpedoes!,

he went for the sac.



a pawn,

then a bishop,

his rook and a knight.

it was a glorious moment

and a beautiful sight.



his slack-jawed opponent

just drooled in a trance,

God couldn't save him,

he hadn't a chance.



king on the fifth,

a forced mate in three

his humbled stone silence,

was my ecstacy.



and then something happened

that I'll never forget,

when he offered his hand

and our bloodshot eyes met.



three little words,

even now make me gag,

a polite little whisper:

sorry, your flag.Well, if you don't know the smoke, fire 

and splinters of speed chess, 

this one will probably zoom over your head,

If you do... i am sure you have been there.



written in '94

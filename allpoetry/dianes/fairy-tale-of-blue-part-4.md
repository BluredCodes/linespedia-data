Blue the fairy was now a man,
He had flown for years,
Searching and learning,
Still he had not found another like himself.

Now, loneliness was a way of life,
His flight of fancy,
Was now his travel of strife,
He now knew why fairy men took a wife.

His flight of North,
Had turned to West,
Over rivers and mountains,
It was time to rest.

Blue decided if he was to live alone,
It was time to find a home,
No more flights of fantasy,
He had seen all that he could see.

Looking for a place to reside,
Blue thought of all the things he would need,
Thinking the thoughts of a fairy man,
Knowing that he would live off the land.

One day Blue spied a forest hidden deep,
He flew into its green lushness to seek,
Was this the place for him to reside,
A place for an old fairy to eventually die.

As he was flittering to and fro,
On his daily trips of foraging,
Blue discovered the perfect place,
A hollowed out tree stump by the lake.

He busied himself everyday,
Making his house a nice place to stay,
But Blue's dream of it being a home,
Would never come true while he was still alone...

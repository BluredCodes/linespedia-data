Up in the morning
At the crack of dawn
Alarm going off
It’s life down on the farm
Down to the barn
My white stallion and me
Getting ready for work
Thank God it’s Friday

I saddle him up 
Settle into the seat
Of my blue velvet perch
At the wheel of my steed
Off we go down the drive 
At a snails pace at first
Cars they screech past
As we head off to work

Down the road to the highway
He goes at a canter
My white stallion takes care
Not to scare at the banter
Tho not many cars pass us by
He watches with a close eye………..

Off on the highway he strains at the reigns
Taking off with a pace I can hardly restrain
It seems my white stallion can’t wait to arrive
But I know he is only keeping up with the drive
Maybe he feels just like me…...
The need to run to feel free….

Now I sit at my window 
Looking out at my horse
My white stallion awaits
He knows he is my source
Of escape at the end of the day…
He looks back as if he seems to say….

“I can’t wait till you come and sit on my back
And hold onto the freedom your think your life lacks….
I’ll wait here for the ride home……..
A few more hours and we’ll be gone…….

Dedicated to my white Chevy Cavalier, my “White Stallion”
August 10, 2001

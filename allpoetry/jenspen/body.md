My sweet incumbent body
You bend and twist
I see your toes, they wag
Your nose, your wrist
I will keep you
Wrap you in a thick
Veil of conceitment
	That no one will
	Uncover you insecurity
I will thrust high your head
	Fill you with false truth
	Inject you with pride
I will hold you up to the multitude
And let the millions
Grapple with the ability
To touch your warmth
       (How sad that you may want the warmth as well)
You will never cry
Only briefly will my tears
Befall you chin
We will show the world you strength
        (How sad that you have none to show)

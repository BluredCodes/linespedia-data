Allen you gave us so much and now you are nothing 
Three years gone and your words hang flaccid 
Unheard and unused 
Allen I'm sick as hell 
Your words fell on deaf hearts 
But managed to catch a dreamer or two 
Or a vagabond 
A stoner 
The artist 
Tell them to fuck themselves with their labels 
Allen, when will Amadou Diallo be avenged? 
I'm sorry I can't stop bothering you 
Allen we need you still 
Allen it's the year 2000 
Thich Quang Duc still sits burning 
Two dollars and twenty-seven cents won't get you past the fine print 
Allen 
How shall we honor the 6 million 
Lhamo Dhondrub's in exile
That draconian society has stole from them 
They have stolen from us 
They've smashed the youth 
Under tanks and TV crews 
All we did was watch 
Allen the Bronx 4 walk free
We heard you died early in the morning
And Burroughs died in Kansas
Of a broken heart 
His other half before him 
Allen 
I wish you could see the TV 
I wish you could hear the commercials 
I wish I could hear you 
Allen, when will it matter 
Them Russians, Them Russians, Them Russians 
Are listening to you right now 
And waiting for Red Mac’s 
And driving shinny Red Fords 
Allen 
We don't say Nigger anymore 
But we're not afraid to call you 
Faggot 
Allen when will I be ready for 
Your Gay parades 

Allen the corporate world is serious 
And They are mad at me 
Because I'm still talking about you 
And still talking to you 
And it occurs to me 
That you are America 
And I'm talking to myself again 

Allen why did you not spell it out? 
Why didn't you give me more? 
Allen I need you still 
And we still need you 

Allen Ginsberg 
America misses you

And all the hills echoed
And all the hills echoed
And all the hills echoed
And all the hills echoed

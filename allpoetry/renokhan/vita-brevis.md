Amadou had a gun 

Or so it seemed 

But none of that matters 

Living the ghetto dream 



Amadou had his wallet 

Standing in the door 

Murphy and the Mellon 

Saw his dark form 



"Do as we say" 

The words so strange 

The green card he kept 

Now red with blood stains 



"He's got a gun" 

Said the Boss to the Bronx 4 

"Get him down" 

They called for 41 more 



Vita brevis

 The empty shell

 Shot through the chest

 And down he fell 



"Don't say murder 

Don't say sin 

It woulda been us 

So it had to be him"* 



"A wallet a gun 

How could we tell 

We told him to freeze 

and he ran like hell" 



"The Boss shot him first" 

"But Murphy killed him last" 

The Bronx 4 like rats fought 

Trying to save their own ass 



The jury spoke 

And as it had to be 

The Boss and Bronx 4 

Were finally set free 



"What I learned from Police Rex" 

Amadou ghost howls of this 

"Do as they commands 

For you never know vita brevis"I wrote this in response to the killing of West African immigrant, Amadou Diallo, in 1999 by 4 police officers in the Bronx during their search for a rape suspect.  He pulled out his wallet to show his green card and was shot 41 times.

On cold, white winter nights haunted melodies take flight

and linger in the air like ghosts without a prayer. 

And to the troubled sound a shadow crowns you king, 

but you wake to find it all a dream 

and no one’s there to warn you: the world changed while you 

slept; children grieved and poets wept, 

but no one chose to stay 'cause

all that glitters also fades. 

And in the end there’s only 

shadows crying for another melody.

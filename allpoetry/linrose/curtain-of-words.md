---
title: "Curtain of Words"
writer: "Linrose"
slug: "curtain-of-words"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/40165-Curtain-of-Words-by-Linrose"
---

Your words brush past my ears – 
	Whispering,
		like a gentle breeze;
	From my eyes,
		they draw a trickle of tears – 
Then travel through me as they please;
	Your words,
		tease and caress me – 
As they pass through my mind;
	Creating images of you,
			for me to see – 
	Yet hidden,
		so I can only find – 
The ones which were a part – 
	Of your mystery,
		        but now revealed,
As I come to know and cherish you in my heart;
	Yet, 
	        others remain sealed – 
	From my probing view,
Until such time, that we love as one;
	Then your words,
			will clearly undo – 
The ribbons of mystery and let the light of the sun – 
	Dispel all fear, 
			of being shown – 
	As you really are;
For our hearts will respond to the same tone – 
	And who I am will shine for you,
			like a brilliant star;
All words will fall away as gently as they came – 
	No longer needed,
			for our souls will know,
						we are the same.Written on August 15, 1992

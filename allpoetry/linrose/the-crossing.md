---
title: "The Crossing"
writer: "Linrose"
slug: "the-crossing"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/6166-The-Crossing-by-Linrose"
---

Within the deepest Darkness ~
One finds the greatest Light;
Contrasts of brightness and starkness ~
Needed while in the fight;

Man gazes at the stars ~
Gleaming in the night;
Blind to how those lights afar ~
Reflect his own birthright;

Man prays or uses rituals ~
To ease the pain of his being;
He moves from grace to pitfalls ~ 
Never sure of what he is seeing;

He finally lies broken ~
in the ruins of his doubts;
Then hears a whisper spoken ~
Amidst the turmoil and shouts;

It speaks through his open heart ~ 
Which sent its own request ~
To bridge the soul, long apart ~
From Self, which was its quest;

Compassion flows through his heart ~
For this man weary and lost;
Reviving him with a start ~
When had his mind and heart crossed;

A surge of fiery Light ~
awakens his cells with Love;
He sees clearly in the night ~
Shining like the stars above;

His pulse is one with Being ~
All knowledge crosses his path;
He knows what he is feeling ~
His own truth within him at last.

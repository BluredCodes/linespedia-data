title: "Choices"
writer: "Linrose"

How much easier to ride the crest of a wave,
While to swim against it, an act so brave;
How much faster a weed will shoot to the sky,
While a rose ascends slowly, then dies ~
After a brief exquisite display to the world,
Its essence lingers to remind us of how it unfurled;
Its layers of petals, reaching inner ones refined,
Where at the center lays nectar, so divine;
Prickly thorns protect this delicate flower,
It may grow among wilder species,
but won't cower,
Remaining untouched by all that surrounds it ~
Then unfurls its love to enrich and uplift 
man’s soul to a state more desirable ~
Where he too can shine forth with virtue so noble.

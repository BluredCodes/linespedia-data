Someday I'll marry a nice guy
Who won't make me cry
And won't ever lie
And can break through this hard shell
That's only been slightly cracked once.
Someday.

Someday I'll have a family
And a white picket fence,
I may even drive a mini-van
To soccer practices and girl scout meetings;
Bake sales and Church groups.
I'll have cute children
With names like Katie and Michael.
Someday.

Someday I'll give up the parties
And staying out late
I'll wake up before noon everyday, 
Even Saturday.
I'll find a man to call my own
And say 'good-bye'
To my nameless lovers.
Someday.

Someday I'll live out my dreams
In a large house 
In a fancy neighborhood
With gates and security patrol.
I'll have a beautiful garden in the backyard
Where I'll spend my Sundays,
With Roses and Tulips
And a small fountain
And a barbecue for entertaining on hot summer nights
While the children swim in our pool
With the neighborhood kids.
Someday.My life...Someday...

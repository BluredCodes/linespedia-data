They fall softly, graceful
From up above
With no purpose,
No direction
Eventually landing on the Earth,
The dirt,
That empty field once so full of life.
Their many colors scaatter
All over the Earth,
Creating a unique piece of art
With each one that falls from above,
Leaving our overhanging shade;
Protection on hot days,
Barren and emptry,
Like your soul 
when you've given all
You had to give.

---
title: "A Trip to the Grocery Store"
writer: "colleen"
slug: "a-trip-to-the-grocery-store"
source: "AllPoetry"

We were too embarrassed
To admit we were 
the ones with the food stamps.
We'd play near the soda and gumball machines,
The magazine racks
Reading of celebrities
Living our dream lives
While we turned our backs 
To you swallowing your pride
In front of the check-out girl
Who drove mommy and daddy's new BMW 
To work that afternoon.
And we walked giant steps ahead
Laughing about the gossip at school
Then, once we were a safe distance
From the discount grocery store,
We'd walk side by side with you,
Unaware of the tears in your eyes
As you struggled with the heavy bags
And felt the shame
Flow from your daughters' faces
Who played oblivious to their own poverty
While they danced around in uniforms
For a school you'd be paying off
Until you're well in your grave.

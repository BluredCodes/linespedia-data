STRESS

No naked toddler ‘round the house
Streaking by with chasing spouse
No pressing matter of the state
T-Ball practice, how we’re late

For that matter tennis camp
Swimming class, saving stamps
Acting, dancing and art school
Run me round like I’m a fool

Wake up, Gas, and pay the bills
Live a life without the frills
Feel the throb that’s in your feet
Traffic Cop pulls o’er for speed

T-Ball practice was called off
Eldest son develops cough
Go to doctor you don’t like
You don’t want to? Take a hike!

Remind yourself to smile at kids
They won’t think that monsters live!
Hug and laugh and wink at spouse
Say you wish to stay from house

Eat at place you can’t afford
Look at price say, “Oh Lord!”
Pay with card at least you tried
Laughing waiter, “Card denied!”

Lay in bed and cry to sleep
Listen spouse can also weep
Reach on over and give a kiss
Run through house while doing this

Pick up kids and bounce on knee
Hug a spouse and then a tree
For all that’s life is under roof
And stress relieved is all the proof.

If you have no kids and spouse
Maybe lonely is your house
Mom and Dad don't get along?
Maybe stress is all that's wrong

See the signs and know there's care
Behind the bitter that is there
Soon they'll know that you adore
Just throw a smile or do a chore

Stress is something we endure
Only treatment not a cure
Stop and breathe and then press on
And curse at cop once he's gone

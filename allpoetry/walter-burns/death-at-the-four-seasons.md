title: "Death at the Four Seasons"
writer: "Walter Burns"

I danced and I fiddled 
in the middle of spring

Disencumbered 
the number
of what summer 
can bring

Gathered 
the leaves
where thieves 
would have got them
and cherished 
the perish
of green
in the autumn

I splintered
in winter
a pile
at the bottom
and to  
all of my seasons
too soon
I forgot them

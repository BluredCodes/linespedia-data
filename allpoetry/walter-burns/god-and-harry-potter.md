---
title: "God and Harry Potter"
writer: "Walter Burns"
slug: "god-and-harry-potter"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/18784-God-and-Harry-Potter-by-Walter-Burns"
---

If God were not real-let’s only pretend
Just a fairy tale, so nicely was penned
Then comes the movie and all of the fans
Buy up the tickets with sweat-clenched hands
That hold the mint cards, all collector items
Any scripture asked, they easily can site ‘em
And all the shirts would say, “God: Soon Is Coming”
It would be the longest ever movie running
Sitcoms would prevail and cartoons would too
And people would watch them, eyes like glue
Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers to follow
All the truths were found not hard to swallow
The coming of Christmas God would be known
The sparkling toy that children must own
God would pose for pictures at every mall
With jolly old lap in which you could crawl
Up and tell him, this year, you wanted God
You won't hear him speak just watch him nod
And God would be "in" for the rest of the year
Until a a new movie was looming near
Plastic God toys break off at their arms
Kids will trade them in for ruby red charms
Nothing would happen to our sons and daughters
Here would come a slither of a Harry Potter
I heard a kid ask his dumbfounded friend
"Is there anything wrong if we just pretend?"

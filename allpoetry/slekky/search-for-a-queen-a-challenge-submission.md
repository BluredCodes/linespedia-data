She appeared before her 
Slithering in and out of her legs 
Rubbing silk against play-bruised skin 
Purring softly, as if to announce herself 

The young girl stopped playing her lonely game of scutter-ball 
A game that only boys should play 
But she never let that stop her 

Tacia picked the cat up carefully 
And cradled it gingerly in her arms 
She listened intently to its purr 
For it sounded more like a whisper 

The cat spoke, voice soft and charming 
Like the sound of a bird’s lullabye, carried away by the wind 
She told Tacia that a Queen was needed to rule the lost Kingdom 
And that Tacia was the chosen one 

The little girl listened in disbelief 
For she did not wish to be Queen 
She did not wish to wear elegant clothes and move with grace 
But most of all, she feared ruling an entire Kingdom 

Her eyes turned cold 
And her body stiffened with determination 
Her answer was no as she lowered the cat to the ground 
“Why does everyone wish to change me?” 
the question ran through her mind over and over again 

The cat followed Tacia and begged 
She pleaded as if there were no other choice 
As if Tacia was the only answer 
That could save the Kingdom from complete chaos 

“This Kingdom has been without rule for 75 years!” 
The cat’s soft voice was replaced by one more forceful, more urgent 
“We need a Queen………we need you.” 

But Tacia walked away, relishing her freedom 
The liberty to be herself, to act as she wanted 

As she skipped away, the cat looked on in despair 
But Tacia smiled from deep within 
Completely contented with her pronouncement 
Oblivious to the trouble that lie ahead 

And the search continues……….Yay!!!!!  I won!

---
title: "Insomnia"
writer: "Jewel"
slug: "insomnia"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/22309-Insomnia-by-Jewel"
---

Clearly in my Valium head I recall
the one to fall at your touch
I was she, tired from my surrender 
your knife dug deep
from friend become foe
the biggest pain to encumber a body
ending a last holy call towards hope.
Its your life in my veins
my blood boldly calling upon you,
resisting its own resistance
so I rest here, I can not listen to your words
its far too cold, in my soul I've lost you.
We saw the betrayal of a holy man
bought by the kiss of judas
the putrid bloodshed of the innocent
the empty unanswered calls
such moments remain buried in your pride 
beckoning you to your solitude
so now explain your anthology,
as we, unafraid at a mere thought of being real
doubt how love can become nothing
when it was always everything
yet refuse to ourselves to believe
and we see it not
wrenched in insomnia
too weak to make a stand
tired eyes catching the evidence
the slaughter of creation
by colour of skin, place of birth
just where was it that you went.

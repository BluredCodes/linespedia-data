(This is usually done as slam poetry, I don't know how it will translate on paper.  Usually, I preface this by saying, "My 22nd birthday was Sept. 11th, and at my birthday party on the 15th, I learned what I should celebrate.")

-I rallied the friends,
20 of them.

To a happy hour party,
To feel something,

Thats old and new again,
Good People.

We began with well drinks,
44 of them.

Piled on kids toys,
22 of them.

By drinks 5 and 6, I'm on the bar, being,
The best hip-hop dancer you've ever seen.

-I shake my J-Lo ass,
I shake it good.

I hold the netting above my head,
As I dangle, nearly float,
from the ceiling.

I contort and jiggle and tease,
Men, they'd like me to please,

-But I won't.

Cause I'm dancing and feeling.
-Alive as ever.

I'm downing shots and singing along,
With 12 other girls,
Who don't know this song.

-Holding onto the bar with one foot, one hand,
Dancing, sexy, for 2 to 20 men.

I'm looking round, 
Through palm trees, neon lights,
The companys' flaky -there's frat-boy fights-

-And its then that I feel it,
Of war, closing in,
-But I fight it back-

-At the moment, I dance again.

Over a sea of bodies bumping,
Of bright lights and dim.

Here, shadows merge into one,
Where I bounce my body, 'till I belong.
In the feeling of being more than I am.
-In a ritual, believing I'm ok within.

In the smoke, on the bar,
I shake my ass, move my arms,
'till all I feel is me.

-Alive, at this moment,
Where I shake the madness off.
-Hips swing sultry,
Lips wet with licks, and drinks, and sweat.

-Which say- 
"I don't know you honey, but I'm glad we met."

-And this feels good,
My belly's showing,
My eyes are glowing,
-My friends are screaming,
"You GO, girlfriend!"

-Tonight, a belated birthday explodes
In pent-up elation,
-That I am still here,
For this meaningless occasion.

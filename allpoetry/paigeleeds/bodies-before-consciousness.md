title: "Bodies Before Consciousness"

I wake to you mumbling my name
Through soft kisses on the nape of my neck
Its morning, a gentle ritual begins

Of sun filt’ring through window shades-
Of your careful love saying, my name.
I feel your love deeply, moments before I wake.

As my mind climbs to consciousness,
Your hands reach for my chest
-I roll over, pull you closer, but I’m not aware of this.

There’s subtle conversation
Between bodies before minds wake, singing, haleluah!
In divine duets amongst twisted sheets.

In this dreamland, where we reside,
Deep sighs, slow movements,
Legs intertwine

In a dialogue of still exploring, 
Each bodily possibility,

My arms reach around you.
Your kisses roam my head.
Fingers wander aimlessly towards
Simple joy in bed.  

You’re conscious now, I’m not fully
As you turn me over,
My mind recognizes feelings

Of your lips, suckling mine.
They’re sweet, (even this early)
Which I lick and kiss in kind.

My eyes open, staring
Into big grey eyes, -startling.
As you smile and say, “Good Morning.”

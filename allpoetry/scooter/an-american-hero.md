American Hero
In Loving Memory of Todd M. Beamer
Copyright 2002 Scott James All Rights Reserved. Used By Permission

He was headed for the airport
Had to catch his plane
But little did he know 
His world was about to change
He wouldn't walk away alive
He'd leave behind a family
One of just many
Who was killed aboard United Flight 93

It was on that tragic September day
He risked his all to try to gain control
As he stormed into the cockpit
His voice echoed out "Let's Roll"

He's an American Hero
A hero who lost his life
Whose wife gets through the day knowing
He's an angel in heaven tonight
She'll go on hoping
She will  have the strength to survive
And through her children
His memory will always be alive
He's an American Hero

So hard to believe
Six months are about to pass her by
Her children don't even understand
Why all mommy does is cry
She tells them she feels so alone
Since daddy isn't coming home
But  still they  wait at the front door
Like they always have before

It was on that tragic September day
He risked his all to try to gain control
As he stormed into the cockpit
His voice echoed out "Let's Roll"
Oh Let's Roll

He's an American Hero
A hero who lost his life
Whose wife gets through the day knowing
He's an angel in heaven tonight
She'll go on hoping
She will  have the strength to survive
And through her children
His memory will always be alive

As she closes her eyes at night
She hears him say wipe away all the tears you've cried
He tells her how much he loves her
And that with time, her tears will subside

He's an American Hero
A hero who lost his life
Whose wife gets through the day knowing
He's an angel in heaven tonight
She'll go on hoping
She will have the strength to survive
And through her children
His memory will always be alive
He's an American HERO

A hero who lost his life (who lost his life)
Whose wife gets through the day knowing
He's an angel in heaven tonight (oh he's with God Tonight)
She'll go on hoping (hoping hoping baby)
She will  have the strength to survive (will she have the strength to survive)
And through her children (through their eyes)
His memory will always be alive (forever be alive)
He's an American HERO (an American hero)
He's a hero, hero, an American Hero

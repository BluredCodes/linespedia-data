I stand on a wooded ridge,
gazing across a rolling plain,
less than a hundred years ago,
buffalo dotted the range.
Yet now I can’t see a one,
where once thousands ran,
the echo of their hoofs,
once a fixture of this land.
I can not help but wonder,
if a hundred years from now,
another species stands here,
in my footprints,
and wonders how.

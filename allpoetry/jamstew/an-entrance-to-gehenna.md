My childhood is far from safety;
It shall be crushed at the gate.
From dusk to dawn it is broken to pieces;
Unnoticed, it perishes forever.

When I think my bed will comfort me, 
And my house will ease my complaint, 
Even then I am frightened by dreams 
And terrified by visions.

Call if you will, but who will hear?
Who will answer?1997,

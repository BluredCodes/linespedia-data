---
title: "fidgeting"
writer: "donnamarie"
slug: "fidgeting"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/49899-fidgeting-by-donnamarie"
---

i place my head
upon this rock
so that i may 
sleep sound tonight.

i fixed my skirt
over my hips.
i took my feet
out of my sandals
and i stood on them.

"i love you.
i don't think 
you'll ever grasp
exactly what that means.
i don't love anyone
anymore.
what i did to you
can never be undone.
i wasn't about to 
fake romance
to save you face.
we will not strum 
on our heart-strings
with our lips.
your fingers will not
linger on my hips.
i love you 
with all my being
that is known unto me.
i don't want this 
to be like the other
times i've done this."

i blinked a tear
and choked the scream.

i fixed my skirt
over my hips.
i shoved my feet
into my sandals
and i walked away.

i place my head 
on this rock
so that i may
sleep sound tonight.

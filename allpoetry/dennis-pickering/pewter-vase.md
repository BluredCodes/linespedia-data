© 6/28/01 Dennis L. PickeringThe pewter vase has just a sceneAnother’s life so fresh and cleanTime is frozen immortallyUpon the vase so prettilyOr is it only our desire?To catch a memory afireWithin your mind oh don’t you seeYour chance to celebrate with glee.This vase contains a grace it’s ownWhich will enthrall those in your homeIt may contain a scene of RomeOr maybe picture someone’s gnomeYou have to decide on the senseThat it might bring to you your fenceWhich you have built for recompenseWithin this world which can be denseBack to the vase I think I’ll goAnd maybe get a laugh HO! HO!Or you might want a cup of JoeTo contemplate this tale of woe!Please center this in the space!

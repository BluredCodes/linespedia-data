2/25/2002 Dennis L. Pickering

Moonlit Angel it is time you acquire
Pegasus, Unicorn for your desire
Just one or the other is what you need
Satisfy those desires grown with the seed

I do not know what sound you like the best
However, that sound helps you ride the crest.
Into diff’rent worlds or planes, if you will
Helps you acquire the desire of your will.

Where you can request the ride of your life
Unicorn, Pegasus giving no strife.
Requesting’s vital from Moonlit Angel
’Cause they want to fulfill your desire, Bell.

I’m about out of this composition
It’s time to get what you want on your thumb.
Just take an apple and a sugar lump
Because if you don’t, they’ll think you’re a chump.

Lie down to relax, at night when you dare
Relax every muscle that you do share 
After you get all of yourself relaxed
Open the doorway you’ve closed, that’s the fact.

Relax, close your eyes and wander around
Within your mind no, you won’t hear a sound.
You turn around now and walk towards your face
A door you see there, light under the place. 

The door opens when you easily push.
Here are the things about which you did gush. 
Fly now, just do it, and there on the ground
See them there waiting for you to abound.

You hear them nicker joyfully to you
’Cause they are waiting to give you your due
Recall those things you have in your pocket?
It’s time to provide fuel for your rocket.

Walk up to them gently, hold out your hand.
Offer freely what you’ve brought from your land.
They do munch gently right out of your hand
As they invite you right into their land.

I know imagination is the key
Remember it provides things that aren’t wee.
After-all recall from where all things come.
Things come from someone’s mind who’s not hum-drum.

Grab hold of the mane and pull yourself up
You can quickly mount them after their sup.
Grasp hold of their mane to steady your ride
Because by love is how they do abide.

After you’ve mounted it’s easy to guide
They’re just like horses, if ever you ride.
Press on their neck with the palm of your hand
They will turn easily, “Oh!  It’s so grand!”

Easily they fly up into the sky
Take you to places you’re told are on high.
Next you will land there upon streets of gold
The mount you’ve chosen says, “You must be bold.”

It’s a soft clatter, you walking along
Sounding like music so you sing a song.
Your mount likes the sound of your voice so clear
So it joins in and sings, with lovely cheer.

All things you meet here are what you decide
And they are things by which you can abide.
There you do see at the end of the street
A crowd of people getting so replete

MoonLit desires to be obsequious
To all others there who aren’t in a fuss
And she can do it ’cause she so desires
To walk most brilliantly on the high wires.

MoonLit must look at my other poems
They give you ways you can overcome domes.
There are but two and the names are this,
“Sweet Spice” and “The Gate” are what you must kiss.

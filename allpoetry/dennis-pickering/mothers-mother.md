Mothers
© 2000 Dennis L. Pickering

Mothers are a shining star
On the bitter pill of life.
Always know just what to say
When queried by progeny.

She works and works upon lives
Attempting to make them true.
What's right to her may be though
Different than lineage wants.

Mothers change to grandmothers
When things get to be just right
For partaking descendants
To bless us with a new life.

Then she's more on which to work
As though she hadn't enough.
But delectation's her goal
For her old and new kinfolk.

Mom, I thought I would compose
For you a little poesy.
I thought it might be something
More chiefly from me to you.

Than some acquired present
God knows if you'd want or need?
Who needs a bunch of clutter
Occupying the pathways?

I am sure Dad will agree
Though Moms beg to conflict with
An ideology of
Sparseness in the domicile.


Mothers Day 2001

© 2001 Dennis L. Pickering

Happy Mothers Day Mom
I hope your day is bright
Year Two-Thousand-One is
A year of/for renewal

To rebuild all of you
Is not such a hard task
Your body does healing
Itself if you desire

I’m told all that’s needed
Is want what was before
I know not if that works
It is a good thought though

Are we to comprehend
All lifes unburdened quest
Or just seek all we can
Upon this spinning globe

That is the solution
To questions that we have
Within our spinning minds,
Which spin just like this earth

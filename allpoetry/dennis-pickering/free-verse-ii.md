© 11/22/2001 Dennis L. Pickering

Free verse composition is but for
Those poets unwilling to implore
Their language to enter in your door
And help you to rise up from the floor

Onto which you have gotten yourself
You may be on an out-of-sight shelf
That causes you to become a lamb
That hunts ideas over the dam

The dam that blocks things flowing from you
This dam is what can make you feel blue
But you only need to change your hue
To one not red, green, yellow, but Blue

Why into Blue I say you must change?
Because Blue is highest on the train
That you have chosen to occupy
And to consume like a piece of pie

Physical life is but a journey
You embark on looking for the key
That you can occupy and just be
Like when you sat on your father’s knee

Which may have been a long time ago
Probably previous to the show
To which you most happily did go
And saw the things that were done by “Moe”

To the others that were in the script
And the circumstance could be a whip
Onto which “Moe” could get a firm grip
Making him “best” of everyone’s trip

Finis

Common lessons we must learn     One do not break     The eternal cocoon that we spin      Round our filtered-colored souls     With cotton candy and fluffy pink lies     Endowed with defensive systems     Ready to leap into a shrillingly whine     Of thunderous swirl of red multitudes     Flashing like angry ants when they were attacked     Bite me not, oh wearied traveler     With synthetic smiles that flexes its muscles     I am but a fellow guerrella     Trapped in a forest of dizzy revolution     With machines guns so rubbery     I could fight anyone, man or roaches     And the mannequins that walked on the streets.

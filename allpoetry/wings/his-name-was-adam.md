In Memory of James Adam Stotler

We only knew you for a little while 
but you were
so much a part of this family. 
We were waiting to know you better
when you turned 18. 

At every family gathering
you were there in our spirits 
and in our hearts. 
We did not get to see you learn to walk 
or hear your first words. 
We did see you through pictures 
and in our minds we watched you grow. 
You had your fathers’ eyes as I remember.
The last time I saw you, 
you were being christened at the church. 

They tried to keep you here but 
teenagers are still under the control 
of their parents so the battle was lost. 
We kept the dream that when you turned 18 
you would come and find us. 
We waited and the time was almost here.
 
Had you fallen in love or had you first kiss?
Did our love for you reach across the miles 
through the years? 
Could you feel our prayers,
did you know how much we loved you?

We heard it happened on Halloween, 
but no one knew until a few days later. 
You were buried today, 16 years old, 
life was taken from you. 
Murdered for material possessions, 
Your adoptive mother and your friend 
were there with you.
Did you join hands on your journey into heaven? 

I want to believe that before your spirit 
left this earth, you came and looked 
into our hearts and saw there the silent 
love our family had for you.
 
Be at peace Adam and forever locked into the 
places of our hearts.

title: "De-Constructing a Beautiful Thing"

he comes home to find dinner waiting
a greedy plate of misery set before her
strawberries in a smile on his
she spears a heart-shaped relic
lifts it as an offering
he has no appetite for single-serving happiness
but he's always loved to watch her fingers
looks up to see a face he's never kissed
falls into the hollows of her cheeks 
cradling ice-eyed brothers holding hands
from the furrow on her forehead
plays her sister on the only swing
she leans in and asks about his thoughts 
he once thought it was interest 
but now he knows 
softly slipping breath would topple her
her stomach growing bigger every day
her fitful sleep more frantic
whispers in his ear 
staring at the ceiling through his tears
"it's empty... it's empty..."
days pass and she is staggering
her hand feeling for pills on the nightstand
coming to rest on his pity
she cannot bear his pity
she must continue
his work is getting sloppy
but there is only concern on his rival's faces
he cannot bear their pity
he must continue
they are in sticky pieces hidden
a swinging briefcase
an apron pocket
a wrinkled navy tie
the smell of burning eggs
a corporate memo 
"it's empty..." 
he comes home to find dinner waiting
she looks up and
five bloody fingers wave hello.this is ultra rough so rip it open and lead the way to a new and better me!

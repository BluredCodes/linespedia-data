I’ve built a life for myself
Out of other’s used furniture, used clothes, used cars 
And left over compassion.
("Here - take this.
I don’t want it.")

Like a bird feathering its nest 
I have scavenged for bits of string and moss 
To pad my little nest.

Oh Jesus, when you were here,
And even now,
Did you, do you get sick of getting leftovers?

Kindness without a tax write-off
is not convenient, you know.

Bless those who give 
From unfettered hearts,
Even if all they have to give
Is a bit of string.

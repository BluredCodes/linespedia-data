Short subjects II

Leaves in the sun,
Caressed by morning breezes,
A Rhododendron blooms.
**************************************

Toes writhing like spiders in the sand,
A child sits, fondled by gentle waves,
And smiles.
********************************************

Velvet smooth,
Glistening among leaves,
A snake escapes.
***************************************

Eyes bright, sunlight reflecting,
Its tongue a phantom flame tasting the air,
A lizard basks.
****************************************************

Moonbeams
Glisten through the curtained sill
And strike the heart.
*******************************************

Frogs
Chirping in the distant night,
Tidings in the wind.
***********************************************

Silently stalking, rippling muscle,
Python strikes.
Coils crushing, Jaws devouring,
and slumber, sated for a while.
************************************************These shore minimalistic haiku-like pieces were written back in 1972-73 and were basically some of my first experiments in poetry.

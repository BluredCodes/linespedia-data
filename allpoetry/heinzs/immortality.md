Immortality

What a concept!
Never to cease, 
never to die...
Time's endless eons
beckoning,
promising a perpetual future.

But what of the present?
What of the past?

Man was never intended
for a life eternal.
His spirit continues on,
of this I am convinced,
but the physical shell,
this earthly existence,
serves a greater purpose
best delivered by 
finite mortality.

This is the lesson,
the dichotomous nature
of the Universe.
Without death,
what is life?
Without sorrow and pain,
what is joy?  Pleasure?
Without the existence of evil,
how can we define good?
Without the perception of hate,
how do we appreciate love?

So in our sojourn
on this spinning marble,
let our lives reflect
all with which we are imbued.
Remember the past.
Live in the present.
Look to the future.

The lesson is never done,
even after death,
for death itself
is only a small part
of the lesson of life.

Adrift, Again

Giant kelp entwines me ~
like a floating sea otter ~
an oyster on my chest
and a rock with which to crack it open.
There is nothing but silence in the bay.
No breeze stirs the waters,
yet the surface heaves and swells
as if breathing with a life of its own.
I break the silence with the
tap-tap-tap rhythm,
rock against shell,
shell against chest.
Will it crack?
Will there be a pearl?

I reside 
within a shell of my own creation.
The rock seeks my attention
with its incessant
tap-tap-tap.
Who wields this irritating instrument?
Slowly I feel myself
pried open... exposed...
naked and unprotected.
There is no place to hide,
no shelter from scrutiny.
I cower desolately in the corner,
the light much too bright
and glaring.

Thus psychoanalysis seeks
to break through hardened defenses,
baring the tormented psyche
in a desperate attempt
to make logical contact.
I wish to retreat
to my gently rocking bay,
safely tangled and afloat.
Locked within myself,
unavailable, impervious to hurt,
the unbearable pain of waking 
cannot then touch my soul.

Am I the dreamer,
or the dreams?
Nothing at all
is as it seems.

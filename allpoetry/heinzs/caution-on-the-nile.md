Caution on the Nile

River bank, lush and green -
swirls and eddies mar the surface.
Water murky - greenish brown -
conceals another world
within its depths.
Buffalo bellows displeasure,
begrudgingly pulls the plow -
encouraged by flicks of the whip.
Time for planting -
soil newly enriched
by spring floods.
A hippo, ears waggling,
gazes lazily at the bank.
The farmer feels the heat,
pauses from plowing
and walks to the cooling water's edge.
Quick as a flash
he is gone in a splash -
never even saw the crocodile.

Truth or Dare

We aspire to achieve
the least attribute ~
to become the lowest
common denominator.
Goals and standards set low
to preclude genuine effort.
We reward sloth and perfidy.
Liars are our heroes.
We emulate thieves and usurpers,
pirates, hustlers and bullies.
From viking marauders
to Spanish conquistadors,
Marco Polo or Columbus,
we honor them all.

But then we, too, are immigrants,
living in a land where we
are not the natives.
The wilderness conquered,
the great plains crossed and settled,
and the land taken from the weaker
civilization already here.
Divide the tribes,
introduce disease,
brainwash them with our religion,
break all our promises
and relegate them to
second class dregs.
Where is honor among thieves?

We need new heroes.
Pontiac, Joseph, Kicking Bear,
true "freedom" fighters,
struggling against marauding
landgrabbers and murderers.
But, to the victor belong the spoils,
and the ability and self-given
right to reconstruct history
in his own image.
Do we dare preach the truth?
Do we dare take umbrage
at the fostered lies?
Do we dare strike out
and attempt recompense?

Too little and too late,
we're prisoners of our fate.

11-13-2001A tribute to aboriginal Americans.

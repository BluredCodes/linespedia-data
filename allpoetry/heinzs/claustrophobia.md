Claustrophobia

It was pitch dark.
I flexed my eyelids
to assure myself they were open.
There was not a sound -
total silence, total dark.
I felt that I was lying on my back,
but I couldn't be sure.

I was afraid to move -
afraid to try.
What if my hand should touch a wall -
an enclosure surrounding me?

What was that?
That rasping sound?
I held my breath
and it stopped ...
it was me!

Fear gripped my chest
in its cold steel-hard grasp.
The beating of my heart
became overpowering -
all-encompassing.
I struggled to cry out
but a cloying paralysis
pervaded my body.

"O.K., ma'am -
your 20 minutes are up."
Someone removed the black goggles
protecting my eyes from the UV
and I shakily stepped out
of the tanning booth.

Too great a price
to pay for vanity's sake...
the first and last
tanning session I would take.

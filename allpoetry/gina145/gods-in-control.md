“Why worry?” the sign said,
“God’s in control.”
I looked out of the window,
Saw food for the soul –
A garden of beauty,
A bouquet of love,
Was this man’s work
Or a gift from above?

But this was a moment
In a place not my own.
As I travelled homeward
Through a world made of stone,
I looked at the people
Begging for their daily bread,
Read posters of violence,
Saw photos of the unnaturally dead,
And then I remembered
“God’s in control,”
And that sign meant to comfort
Sent a chill through my soul.

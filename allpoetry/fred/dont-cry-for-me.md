In Loving Memory of my aunt:

Mrs. Gloria Downing Akins
"And I heard a voice from heaven say to me: Write blessed are the dead who die in the Lord from now on!  Yes says the Spiit that they may rest from their labors and their works do follow them."  Revelation 14:13


I'm sorry that I had to go away
And that your sun's lost its golen rays.
Though sorrow consumes like the deep blue sea
I've one last request, please don't cry for me.

The way I left was not very rosy
But the Lord's house is still very cozy.
Just know that I'm smiling down happily
Remember my wish that you don't cry for me.

Instead, how about a nice, happy smile
Because I've completed my last mile.
No more of earth's pain will I have to see
Once more I instruct you, don't cry for me.

Don't cry when life deals you a bad hand
Give it to God, He knows what you can stand.
He'll make it better, you just wait and see
And when all's said and done, still don't cry for me.

Don't cry, don't cry, for I do still exist
Just the Lord called me home, I couldn't resist.
You'll hear my voice when wind blows through the tree
Whispeing softly, please don't cry for me.

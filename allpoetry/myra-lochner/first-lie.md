Oh, 
Jesus!
do You remember walking with me
through the pregnant orchards
of Glenheath:
You, my Omnipresent Shadow,
shielding me against the glare
of the persistant summer sun;
keeping me from the sneaking snake?

Ah!
I remember when You called my name ...
that year when I turned four -
no more than a baby girl:
so very small
and easily forgiven
because of clear blue eyes;
a halo of golden hair;
and an angelic smile ...

I - innocent sweet child -
was telling my very first lie:
what juicy peach I picked
yelling:
"Snake!"
(unaware that the Snake
indeed  was there ...)

And there You stood:
calling my name with tenderness
and reprimanding me in Silence
You whispered without words:

"I am Truth"

The self-inflicted sorrow
shook me and  I tried 
to regain the sweetness
of that happy baby child
but then You took my hand
and showed me the Light
of pureness and forgiveness
and turned my guilt to Sight

Yes,
in the orchards of Glenheath
I saw You
in that warning from Above:
On that summer day You endowed me
with Wisdom, Truth
and Love

I want to die held gently in your arms
But not today, for now is far too soon.
When years to come present much greater charms
Than sleep beneath the waning of this moon.
For many silvered moons shall pass for me
Before the call of rest becomes too sweet
Now I shall spend my days in vanity
And dance beneath a sun of golden heat.
I challenge Death to cheat me from my aim
Of lying in your arms for my last breath
For I shall surely beat him at his game
Or bargain for the moment of my death.
If I cannot die cradled at your breast
Then I've no use for sleep nor final rest.1999,

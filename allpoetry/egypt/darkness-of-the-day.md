---
title: "Darkness of the Day"
writer: "Egypt"
slug: "darkness-of-the-day"
source: "AllPoetry"

A crash of the door, hands around my neck
My arms bound behind me as I think of the room next to mine
I close my eyes and say a brief prayer
'Keep them safe, and in your arms, I ask of Thee
They're the next generation, Your Appointed Ones.'
I smile softly, my work will continue
My girls, kept safe
Thrown over a strong shoulder, told to not scream
I feel many hands hold me in place and grope as they laugh
'Took us a while to find you, Mistress
A while indeed, your time is short.  Where is your guard?'
Laughter continues as we move down the hall
I'm thrown on a horse and we gallop deep into woods
I hear loud screams and shouts as my blood runs cold
'They've found them.' and begin to cry 'They'll watch me die.'
I kick one next to me and begin falling down 
One grabs me hard and I'm plunged into darkness
'You'll pay', I whisper harshly.  'Not this lifetime, but mark my words'
We move further in and I begin to prepare
Life will be ending soon, and with my daughters there
I gain strength from Gods and Goddesses around, I sit straight and proud
My heart quickens as we stop and I feel something enclose my throat
The hood removed, my girls in front of me
I pray to the One above me, 'Give me strength and give them love'
A brief scuffle in front of me, I have to smile
I send them both a thought, as I hear the men yell
The horse moves under me and I close my eyes
No pain is felt as I drop violently down
My last thought is of my girls and can feel the storm brew
'They've heard me, My Love.  I'm coming to you.'No comment on this one really.  I think it speaks for itself.

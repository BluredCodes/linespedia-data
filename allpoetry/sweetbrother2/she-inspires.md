title: "She Inspires"

She inspires
dreams in me
then shows me how
to make them real

Gradually,
she taught me to build
houses made of pictures
that move
and inspire

She taught me
of the beauty in pain
of how bricks fired in a kiln of misery
can make the most beautiful homes

There is a hint of steel
behind her radiant face
as if she needs protection
from those who might show her
the wrong kind of love

And yet she dives among us
for Wednesday morning
communion
she could have had 
a tranquil bourgeois life
of tea & scones
and art museums
but her luminous eyes
see beauty
in the weathered faces 
around our breakfast table

And we see beauty in her;
she inspires
an urge to brush wisps of straw-colored hair
back from her face

But no,
she makes a distance,
a steely shield
to ward off hurt
and I watch her,
as if through a window
she is like a gem,
displayed
but not to be held

Eleven months after meeting her,
I tap out words to delight you
snapping pictures through a lens she made
sharing a taste of the beauty
that she inspires....a tribute to my poetic mentor, originally written & posted January 2002...

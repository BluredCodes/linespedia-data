title: "Once Upon a Saturday Night"
writer: "Kenneth M."

The other night, I went out to get drunk and pick up women 
I found God in the process 
Standing silently in a dusty corner in my mind 
Where thoughts were racing past to touch him, but obviously not reaching 
I guess he was laughing inside 

It happened while walking alone downtown 
I met an old man at the corner of Caroline and 3rd 
He asked me why I was out? Why was I walking around? 
As if there weren't so many other things to do 
Volumes and volumes of words that could be said 

I walked into a drug store shortly before midnight 
A sign above the cigarette shelf told me to let go of what once was 
Whatever paradise or pain I once had 
Is just todays garbage, why let it hold me back? 
But that is my past I thought 
It inspires me, made me who I am and pushes me to the future 
How could you tell me to let go of that? 

And as the hours of the night slipped by 
The planets and the stars hung lightly above 
Singing their eternal song in a rythm 
difficult for our minds to follow 
Yet they washed the night of total illusion 
They flowed with the darkness 
So even a lone voyager like me, could find my way home 

Later, just before the sun rose 
I went to the city docks, and gazed at the water below 
I saw the bounty of the shore floating there 
Leaves and grass, food and wasted trash 
Caught in conflicting currents, they just sat there in the water 
But the river nevertheless flowed to the east 
As far as my eyes could see, accompanied by the first rays of summer sunlight

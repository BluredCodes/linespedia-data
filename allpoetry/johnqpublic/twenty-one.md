---
title: "Twenty-One"
writer: "johnqpublic"
slug: "twenty-one"
source: "AllPoetry"

I am too old
to kneel before
a goddess in blue jeans

She sees me
as kind & sweet & cuddly;
I see her
as the dream I missed when I was young
(& full of promise, like she is now)

I see her, in my internal eye
weeping over my poems about her
never knowing
I say only half of what I feel-

The trembling adulation
(heavily laced with sweaty lust)
she inspires in me
would disgust and frighten her, I am sure,
and I keep some thoughts about her
safely hidden
(& yet, at times, a hint sneaks out,
she responds with a nervous smile,
her dignity intact, mine forever shredded)

I marvel
at her grace & wisdom
& the way she looks in a tight black skirt
(um, yes, when I visit her campus,
I must be careful
with my hungry eyes)

Sitting with her, over coffee
I feel a comfort that only her presence gives me
but even then, my heart faces a nagging question
where was she
when I was twenty-one?

She's known him for two weeks
(or is it three?);
a quick courtship
led to promises
of eternal bliss

She's just regained custody
of her brood of six;
he's just informed his parole officer
of his new address

A lovely household they will make

His friends say marriage will be good for him;
he promises
no more brawls in the neighborhood pub
no more
chasing jailbait and cocaine;
he's looking forward to domestic life

She has stars in her eyes
at the promise of
no more lonely nights of tears.
He's strong & handsome
& devoted to her
(and her six kids he's never met)
&, he swears, his violent streak is behind him
and she doesn't believe the slanderous tales
told by his first wife

She can forgive long-ago errors
("possesion with intent..."
is such an ugly phrase)
if he can overlook her loss of youthful beauty
& the brittle edge in her voice
as she calls his name

May their life together
be blessed with joy.Yes, these people and their situation are real. Stay tuned for the sequel

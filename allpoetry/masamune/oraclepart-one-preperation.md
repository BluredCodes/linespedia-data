As the night drew closer in,
She knew the horror would soon begin.
The war would continue that she could not win:
The war of peace&love and evil&sin.

Then her candle flickered by itself
On her alter, the linen covered shelf.
Next to her beads and books of prayers,
That's where she focused her intense stares.
As she awaited the darkness in the corner to appear.

In a wisp of smoke,
And an expected choke, 
She felt the things which could not be spoke
As the darkness filled the room.

She drew a breath with hesitation
As she began her meditation
Her body shook with the sensation
That what had come had filled her expectation.

Her knuckles grew white as they griped the chair
All of this trouble was for more than just a scare.
This dark oracle was not to beware,

I remember a time, circa 1959
when our culture was infinitely better
A kid like me, sliding by with a 'C' knew
lead and deal shared the same four letters

I remember September, we were kids until
the day that school began
We had to sit and listen, taking in what we're now missing,
all taught with a simple plan

We played poker over at Pete's house, if they ever
knew we'd be grounded under curfew with no say
There were things that used to haunt me,
even worse, sounds would taunt me many times each day

Those things that once inflamed me no more do
they shame me alas! I finally get my say
Now I am older and, I must say much bolder
collecting thoughts for so many years

Each day that I am writing truly is exciting,
God has allayed all my fears

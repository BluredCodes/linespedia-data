Dear Sir

You stumbled across my path today
And piled my limp arms full
Of snapshot frames.
You said they were a memorial
To your late wife--
A gallery,
A mosaic of a life,
A mosaic of a love,
A tribute to compassion.
I’m sorry you lost her
But today you saved a
Poor
Anonymous
Life.
A poor exchange I’m sure from your perspective.
You strolled in the door
And a tiny bell hailed your arrival.
An angel may not have gotten its wings
But a crippled butterfly received a future.
“You are a wonderful, good person.”
You instilled a mantra in my mind
And because I wouldn’t sing that song myself
You reminded me
Twenty three times.
A smile and a nod threw me an
Affirming end
To this eternal 15 minutes.
They weren’t your fame
But they were your brightest moments.
You said I’d be seeing you again.
You shop here often.
Maybe when you come back
For tools to expand your shrine
You’ll remember
Poor
Anonymous
Me.
Then maybe I can give you
The angel wings you deserve.

You saved an undeserving life today.

You saved an underserving me.

title: "Blending into the World"

I’m sorry I leave bloodstains on the tile.

I’m sorry I cry wolf when I’m all alone at night.

I’m sorry I can’t even take care of a goldfish.

I’m sorry I kill red roses at the very first touch.

I’m sorry I can’t hold sand between my fingers.

I’m sorry I can’t breathe fire nearly as potent as a dragon’s.

I’m sorry I wake you in the night to turn your dreams to nightmares.

I’m sorry I kiss princes and turn them into frogs.

I’m sorry I get caught in my own webs.

I’m sorry I imagine sweeter scents into being.

I’m sorry I paint sunsets than can never appear before mortal eyes.

I’m sorry I can’t talk so well on the phone.

I’m sorry I don’t have enough poison in my veins to be more like you.

I’m sorry I can’t quite master the vice of adaptation.

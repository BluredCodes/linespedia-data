The alarm clock it is buzzing
I turn and switch it off
Its five AM, not again,
Time to get up
Stagger to the bathroom
Raise my eyes for the first time
There’s rain…there’s always rain.

Ten minutes later, dressed,
I push for the front door
Every step my sanity cries
Its cold! Its wet!
Go back to bed
I open the door and the wind whistles in
Step out, a typical Scotland morning.

My foot falls onto pavement
And I begin to run
Already, there’s an anvil on my lungs
Pushing down, I’ve gone a mile, easy
But I forgot my hat
I feel a headache growing
Great, I close my eyes and struggle on

Now here at last, the lock gates,
Three miles down
My feet, where the hell have they gone
I sure can’t feel them,
But I can feel my legs
With burning brands instead of shins
They urge me to give in

Five miles, two to go
My breathings heavy, I slip
Now my ankle grinds against my shoe
I pass houses, no lights on
I wish to god I was them
And as I pass, no streetlights now
I run in darkness, by the canal

Six miles, all down hill from here
The pain in my ankle grows 
I think, hey…I could stop
Walk the rest, nobody would know
And it’s wet after all
But I have only I mile left
Not the twenty I will have, so push it!

One hundred yards,
I feel like I am flying now
Its finished, all bar the shouting
I imagine three months time
Seven miles is torture, will I fall
But I remember the feeling of crossing my door
And the finishing line, will be sweeter, by far.in less than 3 months i am running the London Marathon. its a scary prospect. i have yet to run more than 10 miles!

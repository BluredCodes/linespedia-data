Broken Windows

By Darmok 1/22/02



(At times)



I liken myself to an obscure rock

Or piece of glass that has found

It’s way upon a beach.   

Tumbled and worn, the cutting edges 

And corners that gave it character, 

Are weathered smooth into generic conformity

By the will of overwhelming circumstance. 



Ages have long since

Plucked me from my majestic rest.

There along the cliffs that over look

The vast and endless blue.  

Through windows shattered and fallen, 

A journey of diminishing dreams 

Seems an eternity of forever.  



Chaos and fates guide me 

To what inevitably will be my rest; 

There buried, drowned, or forever aloft 

On currents that have no respect for the broken,

The forgotten, the dust of the earth.



Should I ever settle and join the 

Ilk of all that has passed 

Within the layers of sediment 

And sentiment, the record of my life 

Will be placed.   In clumps of clay

There I will lie, a journey far

From the rocks and windows I broke.A reflection of those moments when dispair felt so eternal.

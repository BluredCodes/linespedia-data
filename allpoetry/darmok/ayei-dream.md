Aye...I dream
by Darmok
12/28/01

Aye, ...I dream of home,
in every morn that I wake,
and smell the day about to play,
she sends me her song in the wind
or’e the grass of golden hay.

In the willow’s by the brooks,
she whispers from afar. 
There waits a homeland far 
from here, where a pot of stew
and a pint awaits my cheer.

Aye, ... I dream of home,
in every stream and brook
that babbles  me to sleep,
and takes me there upon
wings of night,  home
embraced, this dream I keep.

In every lilly and wild flower groves,
there be her face, and scent of cloves.
And when I pass a field o’green,
clover and emeralds,  her eyes I've seen.
Aye,... I dream of home, and long
for her, in dreams like these I cry,
a heartache longing, silent...
deepend sigh.

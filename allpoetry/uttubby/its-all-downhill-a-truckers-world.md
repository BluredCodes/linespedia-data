I left my heart on the Oregon
trail
It had begun to shudder
It began to wail.
The sign read,
"Trucks Use Lower Gear"
I sat there patiently waiting.

My feet had begun to shiver and shake,
My eyes had swollen like an ocean.
But, I stood tall in that Big Rig,
stuck in my downhill slow motion.

My truck, held its solid ground
and I never had made a spill.
I sailed on down around the bend
looking for relief from that hill.

All the weight that was on my back
had sunk right down to my feet.
But, I had shifted my gears 
like the sign read 
and the mountain I did beat.

It's all downhill and I'm safe from harm,
But Oh! How that mountain stood tall for others.

---
title: "My Eulogy"
writer: "UTTubby"
slug: "my-eulogy"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/17912-My-Eulogy-by-UTTubby"
---

Here lies...my simple print
laid to rest on a backdrop,
like newly etchings scribed on stone,
Listless until one has read.

I'm resting in peace,
Plastered beneath the purest white snow,
Like a crow's footprints on sugar sand,
Unlike the burnt grass slowly dying.

I'm sheltered from the scorching heat
As live oaks carefully watch over me,
Like a mother's eyes laid upon her nest,
Silently asleep in its crib.

I'm tucked away in black,
Laid upon my pillows of fluffy silk,
My remains rest from pain and sorrow,
Unlike a wounded deer heard in the forest.

Here I lie...wrapped in a neat bundle,
What once begun, now been put asunder,
Worn-out script till I fade to dust
Or till my beautiful snow does melt.

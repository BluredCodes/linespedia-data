I stepped out of my Big Rig... 

In the stillness of night 
I heard crickets talking 
The moonlight was bright 
Their shadows were walking. 

In the deepening of the night 
They appeared to have crept slow 
The wind was calm and light 
But, who knows where they would go 

The breeze seemed thickened to me 
Yet, still, crickets wandered around 
Humidity filled the air I breathed 
They had begun to hop to the ground. 

The moon had begun to spin 
The crickets had begun to scatter 
Dust blew in the wind 
But the crickets seemed awfully fatter 

The moon did set to an early sunrise 
The crickets have all gone home 
The sunlight blinded my weary eye 
I bet they were full to the bone... 

And, I said, Oh! Waitress, 
Please pour me a cup of coffee 
Sure looked like good weather to me.

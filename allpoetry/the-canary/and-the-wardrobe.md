title: "and the wardrobe"
writer: "The CanarY"

now waiting at the door to a ghost
lacerated by the grey speech
of terrible and great infancy
we cherish the sunset
falling down the horizon
like a curtain call
our world is indeed a stage
and meaningful lines lie unused
in strangled throats all around us
the towering truth
of days sleeping behind us
under a great big beach umbrella
could be the woman you loved
the man you left bleeding
the child you let go
but the time is still waiting
like a melted dali clock
for the ghost 
to come stepping through
on disjointed silence feet
telling you
in the whisper of your grandmother's dress
that it is now

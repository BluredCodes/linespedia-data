a cup of cofee
wake me up
take me up
a couple copies
of your picture
of your scripture
read the bible 
revival
tent revival
southern baptist
southern cross
rail crossing
don't you cross me
won't you crave me
if you leave me
if you need me
i'll be waiting
i'll be weightless
when we're flying 
dying
crying for the sky
i try & try & try
i couldn't hold her 
could've sold her 
got much colder
than your shoulder
now she's leaving
she's stopped breathing
SHE'S STOPPED BREATHING
...now she's goneI 'm never one for rhyming poetry.  I rarely even forray into the world of rhythm.  I like good old blank verse that doesn't rely on Shakespearean rhyme schemes and iambic pentameter to sound catchy.  But sometimes, I just let it go.

title: "in from the cold"
writer: "The CanarY"

she wakes up from a dream
that means nothing to her
dim fragments of a life past
she cannot ask us what it is
that keeps the world held together
and maybe she never thinks about it
quite like that 

all the answers are unfolding below us
spiralling off into a chaotic demise

between the old man in his dusty bones
and the sparrows outflying another winter
there is a space that fills nothing at all
a difference in light
and a difference in the way we fold our hands
one over the other 

have all the children 
pouring rainbows into the stars
forgotten where they belong?

she says it's daylight now
and turns to leave

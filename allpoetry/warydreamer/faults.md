Sometimes we are blind to our own faults
  and we are unable to see something
    that is so obviously apparent to everyone else.
But just as often, the reverse is true
  and our own faults loom enormously out in front of us
    because we know what they are to their fullest extent.
Other people are more tolerant of these faults
  and don't judge us as harshly
    because they see only a result of a fault
      that we ourselves view as a weakness within us.
Somehow,
  it's the WEAKNESS more so than the fault
    that we have difficulty forgiving.
                 ~~~~~~~
I don't think others are oblivious to our shortcomings.
  In fact, I think they choose to see or ignore certain ones.
The golden truth is, we don't have to live up to their expectations,
  but rather to set our own goals with standards we won't regret.

                                mNovember 1986

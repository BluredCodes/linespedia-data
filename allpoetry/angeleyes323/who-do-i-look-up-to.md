The other day while watching TV 

I saw an ad that didn't make sense to me 



They said to find someone to look up to 

To show and teach me the right things to do 



A mentor, an influence to mold me like clay 

To grow into a respectable adult one day 



I look at the world and all I see 

Isn't much that will help a little girl like me 



I grow up in a home without any love 

Praying, God take me home with you above 



Nights are filled with fear and pain 

From beatings and hunger, I'm so ashamed 



At school I'm made fun of because I dont fit in 

Wearing tattered clothes, worn shoes as if it's a sin 



My teacher is prejudice, I'm always chosen last 

I have no friends 'cause they think I'm poor white trash 



The world around me is filled with poverty and crime 

I can't look up to daddy, he's still doing time 



Mom is filled with anger and takes it out on me 

My face is pretty, but with the bruises you can't see 



Innocence was lost a long time ago 

By my uncle, even though I repeatedly screamed no 



I asked my neighbor for something to eat 

Not candy or cake or a special treat 



He yelled , spit at me and slammed the door 

Saying get away from here or I'll show you what for 



Who can I look up to, a little girl like me 

When hatred and prejudice are all I seeThis is not a story of my own childhood, but an insight to the lives of many forgotten children...some that I have known personally. I have been a mentor to many children, especially teenagers and this poem is based on a compilation of their lives.

title: "Chrysalis (Assembly of Limbs)"

When we came here
Candy canes and sugar drops
Glazed over, unmolded
Fed from puddles day to day
Some refused to lap up the fluids
Some sucked them dry
For some, they were just not enough
On some days, these puddles were sour
On other days, crusted over
 
We found that
All our orifices crystallized
And we took on a new face
As the new limbs snapped into place
Legs to walk many miles
Arms to lift many boulders
Hands to craft our own fates
 
From cradle to playground
From hallway to alleyway
From the paved lane
To the unknown expanses
To the stellar highways
 
The monsters rear their heads
From the carcass of our fears
Let it rot beside our innocence
Happiness is slowly stoked
Like a furnace, fed by hope
 
The path we tread winds like
The blood in our veins
And is shaped by our sweaty hands
Even when the path is straight
No one said you had to traverse it
Even when this path leads to a tombstone
Our lifeless grasp will hold the weight
Until the future has the power to bear it

---
title: "Lake Erie"
writer: "Riverette"
slug: "lake-erie"
source: "AllPoetry"

You're there for me when he won't be
Greater than the river yet smaller than the sea
Your cool wind whips across my face
To you I know I'm no disgrace
From the first rays of sun on the warm soft sand
To the midnight breeze, how it holds my hand
The seagulls drift and swoop so gracefully
As I hear the waves near silent plea
I lie down on your rocks as you catch my tears
My hand falls in and you calm all my fears
I look out at night and see the unending sky
Your beauty makes me pray that I never die
Watching every color break over the lake
As the sun sets, the sight makes my heart ache
From the fireworks over you on the Fourth of July
To the days in November when the geese wave good-bye
When the ice piles up and sends chills up my spine
And the April picnics, on the pier we'll dine
My whole life you've been here and forever you'll stay
Deep in my heart until I move away
But I'll always come visit weather happy or dreary
To me you'll always be my home, my Lake ErieThe only good thing about living in this shitty little town

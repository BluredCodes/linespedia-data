Mercy 

Living inside a dark empty world
I can only think about the cold
I feel deep in my soul, in my heart
My walls are wanting to fold

Clutching my hands to my chest
Looking for warmth in my eyes
Heat that blooms like a flower
Which soon after birth regrettably dies

Gazing up I see a hole in the sky
Golden light streams through and down
My savior, to the earth to relinquish
My conscience in which I always drown

Stepping into the light, where the fire lies
To save me from the cold pain
The cold grows more, no warmth for me
Sacrifice it for others, in vain

Stricken with sorrow, no point to try
Society the only thief I see
Stealing from me what's rightfully mine
There's no mercy there for me

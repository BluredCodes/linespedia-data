The trees wear a bare look
And so does my porch.
Not so long ago
Not so void, so empty was
My porch.

Somebody was there—
To play in it
To laugh in it.
Somebody. To share the joys
And bear the sorrows…
That somebody is where this year?

The ground is covered with crisp leaves. 
It brings back remembrances—
Of sweet evenings
And playful days
Of last autumn.

With that somebody.
Who left my porch…
To hold unto another.

Will that somebody ever return?
Or have I got to hold
Onto some other? 
And wait—
Just wait for
Another
 
An autumn?

A mother holds her crying child
Refusing him her breast
For I am the disease that runs through her
I am the disease that destroyed mankind
I am the disease that you created
Having God-like power
You never stopped to think
Never wondered about the chances taken
Or just what you were creating
So lost up in the marvel
In the wonder...
In the power of Gods
No one saw me coming
Like a thief in the night
Stealing your breathe
Slowly...one... at... a... time...
I would ravage your insides
Eating you away
Turning your stomach inside out
Twisting the intestines
As they ate away at themselves
Make you hunger for more
But afraid to eat
Most of you die of hunger
Instead of me
More than half the world was in pain
Before you stopped to see
It took presidents and Kings dying
Before research even began
This strange virus
Seeming unstoppable
Unknowing the cause
And the fighting began
French blamed the Italians
Italians blamed the Irish
Irish blamed the Scots
The Scots blamed the English
The English blamed the Americans
And the Americans blamed the world
Ten countries studied me
Each separate, hoping for the first to claim victory over me
Everyone vied for my attention
Trying to find my roots... looking in all the wrong places
Years flew by and millions died
No closer to the answer than a cure
And I hung out round laughing
Enjoying my intrigue
Twelve years it took to realize 
To finally see the answer was the beginning of the end
What you saw as new hope 
Was God's finally answer to population control
It start with a sheep, so fondly called "Dolly"
To the cows you would "breed" to feed your hungry
Had I not come on so strong
Not had been so fierce an enemy
You may have stopped me 
Had you not been so proud
Your research better done
I may never had grown
But the cows produced your food
And in them I grew
The cloning had a side effect
A strange manifestion
A combination of a chain of events 
Leading to my creation
I wonder sometimes if the thought
The idea that it was YOU
That destroyed YOU
That killed you that much more
I could only laugh at the irony
And your cloned food, grew your food
As I passed safely through my vessel of birth
To the ground, in your gardens
Hiding in the fruits of your labor
Laughing as you calmly feed me to your childern
And when they complained of a stomach aches
You blamed it on the chocoalate cakes
And sent them off to bed, ignoring your own churing pain
By the time you found me
I had safely destroyed hundreds of millions of you
By the time you realized the cause
I was in all of your food supply
By the time I was done
Your world was broken
Kings died and gone
Presidents were weeped and forgotten
Mothers held dying childern 
In their own dying arms
Crying from the pain
(of me or them?)
Slowly I die out
Each and every one of you 
Lost to the great void 
Until there is but two humans left
A woman and her newborn child
A single mother left alone
Holding her crying child in her arms
Refusing him her breast
Hoping she could give him some peace from the pain
Knowing that feeding him will kill him
And to not...
Still the same
And I wait as I destroy her
For my last victory seems so sweet
The innocent I will barely touch

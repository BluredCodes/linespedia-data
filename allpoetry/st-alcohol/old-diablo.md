what time for tea
saveing tea for time
oh what a time for tea
when whill whe break?
oh what tea-time see?

and as dark
oh candle-pierced
as love to a candle burns
(will burn)

as simplton
of good hell
his deamon eyes a-burning
his tail a-twitching
his twitch a-turning

and rainbowed into corners
the old men say
(a power unto themselves, perhaps, perhaps)
the old men say 
its just a day
its just a day, today

and i wonder on past their rocking-porch
a youth in my confusion
a youth in my envy
to dream of days when i'm a dreamer
a success! oh snappy buttons!
to what this honer i do recieve
tis but a dream, a dream.!.

sally forth, old ye future
bind fortune in a raper's grasp
and smile as a blind man smiles
among the sunrise warmth

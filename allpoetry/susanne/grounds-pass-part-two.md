---
title: "Grounds Pass - part two"
writer: "susanne"
slug: "grounds-pass-part-two"
source: "AllPoetry"

the cell doors open

and let me out

not quite as full

of fear and doubt


I've earned an hour

of release from the tower


Feel some better now

Strength in my steps

even though they're purposeless


What path did I walk             

that brought me here?


I look out and up to the sky


It's a beautiful day

I smell spring in the air

Lush green leaves on the tree

Liliacs in bloom


Father John scurrys

but stops for a moment

from his worries


And blesses me...


then pumps my hand to the count of three


A gentle soul is he


I think of the Wizard of Oz

As I follow the winding brick path

that circles the tower of Phelps Hall


How precious this hour of reprieve

Freedom to breathe

I find myself humming

though still I cannot sing

remembering the sweet relief

a soulful song would bring


Someday I hope to sing again


I notice more now...

Like the Gothic design of many of the buildings

Steel bars on some of the windows


I make my way

to the far side

of the grounds

through dense shrubbery near the gazebo


I hear footsteps behind me

the jingle of a heavy key ring...


the voice familiar as he calls my name

It's the male attendant from the boy's ward

the one who likes to play games.


I quicken my pace

as my eyes dart about

there's no one in sight


Gotta handle this right

"Hey Suzie-Q talk to me when I speak to you"


I have no choice but to turn around

for I've reached the limit...

the boundary of the grounds


I look straight at him and make not a sound


He offers to buy me things

if I do him a favor

and be his "special friend"


I said no before 

now I say "NO" again.



Thunder strikes

fear rolls

clock on the tower says

it's quarter past the hour


Try to run but can't

feet full of lead

eye swollen shut

blood on my brow


I'm in big trouble now

gotta make it

somehow

back 

to

the 

cell

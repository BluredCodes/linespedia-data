---
title: "Rainbows"
writer: "susanne"
slug: "rainbows"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/51806-Rainbows-by-susanne"
---

In pastel shades of royal hue

yellows, pinks, greens and blues

an arc doth stretch across the sky

interwoven with clouds nearby.

In awe and reverence they humble me

from the first I'd heard of them

around the age of three.

In bible school they taught

of a never ending love

and that a rainbow in the sky

was a covenant from God.

A promise made to man

and all creatures here below

never again to flood the world

regardless of the seeds we sow.

Then along about the age of four

playing in the sunshine in my back yard

I felt a raindrop touch my nose

a pitter patter across my toes.

A gentle rain began to fall

yet the sun was warm and shining tall.

I looked up to the sky above

to see the sign...the arc of love.

I layed down in the warm wet grass

and watched the colors come to pass.

I prayed that I would always feel

the peace I felt that day

and God saw to it that I would

in his omnipotent way.

As a crying child at the age of seven

he comforted me one day

when I gazed to the sky 

and a rainbow appeared

to wipe all my tears away.

At the age of nine

(though not remembered till the age of twenty-two)

this spectrum of love rose in the sky

drawing me into its view.

Hard hands pushed me down

to a cold wet ground

innocense lost

shame had been found.

My hands reached out and up to the sky

as the colors of the rainbow

permeated my mind.

I prayed that I would always feel

the peace I felt that day

and God saw to it that I would would

in his omnipotent way.

In the summer of my life

with heart full of joy

I saw reflections of a rainbow

as I held my new baby boy.

I whispered in his ear

of this beauty to behold

and told him 

it would always be with us

regardless of the seeds we sow.

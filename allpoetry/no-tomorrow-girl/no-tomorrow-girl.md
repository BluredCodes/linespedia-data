I am No Tomorrow Girl. 
I have no place where 
I rest my head 
That I can lay claim to. 


My pillows lie on fitted sheets 
That belong to others. 
Yes, they have caught 
My tears and sweat and blood 
But still, they are not mine. 


There must be places that I fit 
I, a silver molded key 
That seems to fit a lock 
But will never open it. 
No keyhole has yet been made 
For me to properly turn in it. 


My backpack has all I need: 
              my books 
              my journal 
              a toothbrush 
              some gum 
Yes, I can live off it, 
But never in it. 


So I make my way 
From place to place 
carrying my satchel of possessions 
knowing this is 
  Not my bed. 
  Not my room. 
  Not my house 
  Not my rules. 


I am running out of places to go 
For each time I leave unwanted, unwelcome back 
For reasons I do not comprehend. 


And like this I remain, 
Crawling desperately from shelter to shelter 
Until one day I find a way 
To be anybody but this 
No Tomorrow Girl.

All you looking down from Olympus 
Hear my pleas 
There’s been a grave mistake 
They’ve trapped me 
In the bodies of Tantalus and Sisyphus 

I am Tantalus 
Eternally thirsting for your love 
That your love 
Would be the water to the fire 
My parched soul 

Your love is the fruit that I reach out for 
Bringing nourishment to these dusty bones 
Filling up the empty space 
So sweet and mellow 
Overripe and juicy 

And yet I struggle as Sisyphus 
Pushing the rock up the hill 
Trying to gain your love 
Only to feel like the boulder 
Has flattened me out 

Now I must start all over again 
Reaching for water 
For my nourishment 
Getting knocked over 
Everything just within reach 
Never achievable 

Why do you oh cruel gods up there on Olympus 
Look down upon this soul 
Fighting to be happy and satisfied 
And simply laugh at your little parties 
At this mere mortal.

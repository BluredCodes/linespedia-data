The little boy walked to the garden
To hug his gradma goodbye
He took his time, walking the long way
So in his mind, the flowers would stay
He saw the tuplips, daisies, and roses
Felt the warm wind as the gate he slowly closes
He walked down the path
Between the lettuce and corn
He saw footprints, he followed them
Finding his grandma laying on the ground
Still, silent, without a sound.
He knelt to his knees
In the warm summer breeze
Said, “Gran-ma wake up”
But. .there was no reply
The little boy began to cry.
“Gran-ma, wake up, please”
Water-filled eyes, he could not see.
He leaned over, and whispered to his friend
“I love you gran-ma, I wish this wasn’t the end.”
He saw a butterfly land on her shoulder
This memory stuck in his mind
Although he’s gotten older
He hopes his grandma’s ok
And wishes he could have hugged her goodbye that day

---
title: "Don't They Do"
writer: "Treas"
slug: "dont-they-do"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/14972-Dont-They-Do-by-Treas"
---

I look up 
I walk around
Once I jump
I won't be found

Unless someone does know
My thought out plan
Of where I will go 
To jump and land

I just need one hand
In this life of mine
To reach out, and help me stand
Someone who would be ever so kind

But it seems that no one does want
To help me at all
All they do is tease and taunt
It's such a long fall

It may be worth it
It might not at all
I will at least admit
It's quite a hard call

If someone would say something nice
I might have learned
Life has no price
It would take just one word

But they never, ever said
Not one thing
So here is where my life was led
So now the end I will bring

I walk to the cliff 
From which I will fall
Soon my soul will lift
After I hit the horizontal wall

Right as I walk to the ledge
I heard someone say
"Please don't walk off the edge"
Something inside me told me to obey

I walked away, trembling with tears
Thought of what would be left behind
Leaving the people I'd known for years
All the thoughts ran through my mind

I am glad I made the choice 
About what I didn't do
Because again I heard that voice
"People really do love you."

---
title: "You Don't Get What You See"
writer: "Treas"

Cutting through the surface
Sanding down as much as I can--
Just to get it perfect
Not perfect–I can not stand
Washing away all the dirt
That’s formed up on the top
Making it look better
Please. .don’t make me stop
Putting on the formal mask
That is used to disquise
No one knows me once again
Because I live in all these lies
No one suspects anything
From this normal, popular girl
That’s just what you think
Because, I’m alone in this world
Everyone thinks “Oh she’s just fine”
Well you know, everything’s wrong
In that one simple line
Everything’s been wrong, forever, all along.
Just because I wear a mask
And no one else knows
Sometimes, if you look hard enough
My pain, deep down--it shows
Maybe when I find a true friend
That I knows going to be there in the end
I’ll finally remove my mask
Hopefully forever, without a question asked.
But until that dream of a day
This lonley old gray mask–it will stay
It doesn’t really bother me
I just want someone who will see
I’m just who I am, 
And that’s the way it's going to be
Just becuase I’m really not happy
Changing for you, isn’t what I’ll do
Remember this, I can see
When I meet that someone
A suprise it wont be
Just be glad of who I am, and who I will be
But heh, remember, you don’t get what you see.

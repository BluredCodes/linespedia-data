A secret kept in pale for any girl untouched at the throne
In life so it seems, to be led by an army of erections
Waiting, as cold lips gutter from the hills
And a mirror of wine and nails
Stand firm with any treasure to find
In lace and daisy grins, there is much to sleep
Where public displays of mockery and sex
Lay sweet on the tongue, calming the messiah and his men
Most remember a song, played with violins and spoons
Dancing a duet, followed by fingers down their spine
And in any exhibit, a girls heart behind glass
Beating an awful red, Sounding dying rabbits in winter
Desire despoiled the flesh once so emollient
Caught between juicy depictions
The girl lay with ash and wishes
Marching on, thrusting upon her ravaged humors
When the first cannon is fired
She can only duck and spit
And When the first sword is drawn

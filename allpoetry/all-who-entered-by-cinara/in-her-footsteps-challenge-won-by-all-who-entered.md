Challenge Description: write about someone who changed your life for the better somehow, was it a teacher? A grandparent? a stranger? a friend? How did they change your life, and are they still a part of it now? 



In Her Footsteps 

She watched from off the moving deck, a lady with a flame 
Held high above to welcome all who ventured forth and came 
With little shoe she stepped upon the Ellis Island pier 
A friendly smile could not erase a child’s falling tear  

A little girl in German braids and crystal eyes of blue 
Stepped onto Ellis Island to begin a life anew 
With hand wrapped tightly in her mother’s, shielding her from harm 
A doll of painted porcelain clutched tightly in her arm 

The story of an immigrant, a shipping list depicts 
She stepped on Ellis island at the tender age of six 
No log records the heartache of their having left behind 
A brother and a sister still so vivid in her mind 

A strange and different life before her wondering eyes unfurled 
She stepped on Ellis Island and she saw a different world 
Though pain of separation would in time leave sorrow’s scar 
Of two she loved in childhood, now memories from afar 

She grew and raised a family, but closed the painful door 
Of what had passed before she stepped on Ellis Island’s shore 
She faced adversity with strength, and hardship without fear 
From the moment that she took a step off Ellis Island’s pier 

Her courage shown throughout her life were lessons duly taught 
To a daughter so inspired by the battles she had fought 
A teenager without a home, I stand and gaze in awe 
At diplomas with her name engraved that hang upon my wall 

The untold story missing from the written history’s page 
Of a little girl who once had stood on Ellis Island’s stage 
The truth about her living pain, no history book depicts 
Of a lovely girl who came here at the tender age of six 

My Mother

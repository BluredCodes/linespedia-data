On the Shores of East Haven                                 

flesh rotten corpse and sullied super heros
sacred men super locks down-beaten bores
flesh coloured glow in the dark Christ-like Doors
Here I sit drunken on the shores of East Haven
The ocean waves, salt with fish-like smell, wash over me
My legs are cleansed
My body spent
My mind rent			
and now I glow in the dark
Blue-gold paint

On the Shores of West Haven

listening to a roar sitting drunk on the beach
vomit here a little child there
holding a magnet up to the light of the fly buzzers
hearing without knowing dying without trying
a little town dancing on the roof tops on the shores of West Haven
sister city separated by more than distance 
more than religion more than morality more than God, you or I
ever dreamed it could be
yet still alone and without a friend without a dog with only a cigarette
wondering why you are not here seeing illusions of you throughout the night
thought you left and seeing engraved forever in my imagination
never forget those moments quiet basement bliss
experienced talking in the stories of the attic
to you and you alone drink a toast of brewsky
on the shores of West Haven 

On the Shores of North Haven

Alone losing
I'm society's whore
Naked in the sand
night's descent
no alcohol tonight only misery
No mirrors to see the sea, just my refracted,
decieved, cracked,
non-linear vision treats.
Here's to all you out there who think you've found love
and down goes the sea water and out goes the vomit
on the shores of North Haven

On the Shores of South Haven

In love yet again with his reflection
the man turns from the sea
he turns from the beach and looks out to be heard
no nightingales fill the air
nor ecstacy factor considered valuable
no longer alone yet still solitaire
mention the mangler and you're only see broken teeth
to leave, to leave, all want to leave
and no one has issued, triumphantly
their departure in fantastic spectacles
save for this man
he leaves while the rest wonder

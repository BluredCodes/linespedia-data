Have you ever awakened
wondering what it's like
to be your lungs,
pushing air in and out
so faithfully in the night
while you sleep?

What if those dark
prodding caverns conspired?
One slowing its rhythm slightly,
cajoling the other, 
as if they were dancing.
A small misshuffle of the feet
and she's with him
and as they rotate slower and slower
they gravitate toward each other.

Here is where I gasp.
My pupils, large as dimes,
blow out of my hot face,
trying to suck in air
and I contort
like a wild accordion,
seen but not heard.

Meanwhile, my lungs
stop moving altogether,
look deep into each other's eyes,
and hearing an echo of thrashing,
tremble once,
then curl together like naked lovers
and kiss.

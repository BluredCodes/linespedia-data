In times of quiet, I often can hear,
That moment of silence, so crystal clear.
When solitude surrounds me, I speak a soft prayer,
And ask God to watch you . . . somewhere out there.
 
Then I ask Him to guard me with a watchful eye,
As I fight back the tears, and refuse to cry.
I know that my friends and my family so dear,
Are not far away - but in my heart - here.

So to guard you all, against any harm,
And to keep you loved, and safe, and warm.
I then close my eyes, and see all your smiles,
Then I slumber, and dream . . . and throw away the miles.Learning to deal with prison life has many unexpected surprises.  One of those for me was finding a solution for the dilemma of distance.  Distance of miles between myself and those I loved so dear.  This was "my solution".

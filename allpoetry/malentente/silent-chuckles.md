Not wishing to entertain boredom,
or appear in a book with a hat . . .
my cat . . .
Runs and Tears and Jerks . . .
 . . . until . . .
                                                     . . . he stops!
- and for no apparant reason -
He [[freezes]] time . . .
. . . then . . .
with cool, he strolls lazily away,
(laughing inside) . . .

You see . . .
cats have no lips.

S a u n t e r i n g    o n  . . .  he diligently 
works!
for that perfect place . . .
                                          . . . to dream . . .
                                                                  . . . of mice.This poem was written for, about, and in spite of my favorite cat.  He was one of my most cherished pets, and I miss him.

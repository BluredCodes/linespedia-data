title: "Werewolf"

Night draws its dark cloak over me 
As I sit by the dying cinders 
I weep for what would never be 
A vision of you still lingers. 
I sang a lullaby for you 
When you lay in your bed at night 
You laughed and said I love you too 
But I knew that it wasn't quite right. 
The backs of your hands were covered with hair 
You fixed my eyes with a terrible stare 
The moon shone brightly; you howled for fun 
And when day came you hid from the sun 
You killed my friends, your mum and dad 
You just couldn't help it 
I know you're not bad. 
And then one black night in bed 
"I fancy puppies," is what you said. 
I lay there so quiet and still 
To have puppies wasn't my will 
So during the bright sunlit day 
I left your bed and crept away.

Death comes in many sizes

One cell tall
One cell thick

One cell wide
One-cell sick

That’s all it takes
One disfigured cell 
To end your life
 
Shall it be heaven or hell?
Why play safe? 
Go have fun!

Enjoy the wonders of this world, before you pass on
Take time to treasure a moment, and hold on to it tight
To keep you happy, as you pass on through the light


You might get better
You could end up worse
Take on the side effects

Of those horrible drugs
Anything to prolong
This pre-existing love
for this thing known as life

As the treatments begin
You look to the past
For times of laughter…
To hide from
What lies in your path… 

Health through poison
Pain for pleasure
Death for life
Longevity through decay

That is how I live through mine
The enemy awaits
For the chance to multiply

I do not wish to die
Yet I already know this shall be my demise 
One cell at a time

A
Cell
At 
A 
TimeI started to think of death and how I will probably die. 

In a hospital bed...alone...getting treatment...hoping for the best

yet knowing that death is coming around the bend, and me smiling and waveing my middle finger at it

just like I greeted life

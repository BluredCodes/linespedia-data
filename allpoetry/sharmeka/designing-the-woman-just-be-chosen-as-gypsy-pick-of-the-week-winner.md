When I was with you I had to be perfect
and you almost made it possible
Becoming the magician, waving your hands
Making me into the modern day  Mona Lisa
my cheeks becoming a  blushing center stage

You nursed my lips
took away the metallic taste out of my mouth
You dropped the bridge of my nose, 
so that the world can see my eyes
my body became a rough draft 
your imagination of what a woman should look like 

You told me the dimple in my chin was interesting
My crows feet was stepping stones for turning 40
You enhanced my attributes
my restricted body
replacing my bones with plexiglas.
until nothing mean in this world could break me

When there was nothing left to paint over
you enlisted me in your cabinet of super models
I was outdated before I even got discovered 
I was old material and soon to be your manicon 
obeying because you put me on your tab and I owed you respect
I depended on you to keep my smile energized

Now with out your talented
state of art technology
and weird science
I'm normal

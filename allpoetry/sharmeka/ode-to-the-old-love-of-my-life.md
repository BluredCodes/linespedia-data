I had always loved You 
You were the love of my life 
I always stroked your brase mouth 
sent my songs through the obstacles of your mangled structure 
I would reach in with a sweaty hand to muffle your sound 
pucker my lips and press them into the small opening of your silver piece. 
supporting you, as you bare down your weight on my thigh 
I loved to dust off my reflection in your gold mirror 
strike your slick valves with the determination to make you feel what I was feeling 
untiled you howled, rang, echoed and conquered the acoustics of the room 
I miss dragging you around in the rain 
rubbing your back, polishing your face  
feeling your warmth on my skin 
the purple discolarations from your deep and almost permanent imprints 
If I had you back,I would never put you on the shelf 
I would give you life 
that way you gave it to me

I left the 
dandelines dancing in the yard out back 
the plump red rose bobbing her head 
I left the crickets 
skipping in the grass 
the rabbits fleeing down the hillside 
the sweet wine of sunday mass bitter, and aging 

I left it all because of you and the changes of time 

You left the 
cloth journals tucked under your bed 
the fierce chicago wind searching 
to scatter your hair 

You left the candy necklaces 
ribbons and bows 
your innoscent eyes locked 
in the black and white photos 

You left it all because of me and the changes of time 

We united in a normal life 
dull and complex 
surfing the Want Ads 
sitting in coffee shops 
scheduling quality time 

Now we have 
the wantings of woman and men 
attachments and expectations 
the bliss of matromony, 
the future in our grasp 

That is all we know now 
to be beautiful 
to be somewhat comfortable 

Now often we yearn 
miss and look back on the strange days, 
the freedom to love and like 
the little things 
we took to the heart of our gullible spiritswritten 6 years ago

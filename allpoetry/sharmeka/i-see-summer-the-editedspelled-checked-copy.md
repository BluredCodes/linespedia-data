I see 
the summer fun 
of sun 
rise each day 
to blind glorious sights 
of men with big crouches bulging in denim shorts 
their sticky tongues sticking out in lost breaths 
the Nubian gloss of their legs and arms 
moving systematically 
in the routine of jogging 
on the hot evening of any given day 
in summer 
where athletes 
stop only to catch the beat of their hearts 
to wipe their heat stroked foreheads 
with the back of their hands 
and then go on 
around the block 
a couple times or more 
with sun shined abdomens 
goals for desired appearances 

I see summer 
open without me 
it comes 
bronzing the collarbone of lilies 
young full figured 
single 
really single 
women 
filling in tub tops and slipping in spaghetti strap dresses 
batting lashes 
with the sunlight tip toeing on their eyelids 
They come from no where 
they just appear 
posing at cross walks 
fanning themselves with lusty stares 
of married men 
that will piss on their wedding vows
to feel free of obligations 


I see summer 
fulfillment's 
for everyone 
but me 
who is hungry 
desperate 
hot in the face 
sitting on the porch pouting 
looking out at the horizon, 
with highlights in my hair 
watching strange flesh float by 
wondering why I can't show mine 
wondering will I ever be free of guidance 
the boundaries of youth 
to never step overthis poem came in pieces.  finally written and done december 19, 2001

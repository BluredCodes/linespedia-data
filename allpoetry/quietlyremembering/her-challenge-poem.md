Her eyes are as beautiful as the ocean on a moon-lit night, 
Her hair as flawless as the sun's gentle grace and the meadow's endless sway, 
Her smile as warm and comforting as everything I used to have, 
But now her eyes are blurred with tears, 
Her hair not combed in days, 
And even thought I try to make her laugh I haven't seen her smile since I was in second grade. 

I know it's there somewhere, 
And that is the hope that drives me to stay alive. 
It helps me to live because maybe someday she will smile again, 
And I don't want to miss that day, 
Because she is too beautiful to frown.This was for webdreamer's challenge i think. It was like: Write a poem about your tool to fight depression.

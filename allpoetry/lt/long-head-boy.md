Daddy saw the ring,
On my left hand,
Fourth finger,

I always thought of it 
as an engaement ring,
Not just a piece of stupid metal,

What long head boy,
you engaged to,
None I said,

At the time I knew,
What long head boy,
I wanted as my husband,

Not caring that,
he had already broken my heart,

Long head boys,
come and go,
But Daddy's are always there to tease you about them

They're sometimes there to,
even see you walk down the isle

A diamond tiera,
Beautiful dress,
Tuxedo.
Fancy shoes,
My maid of honor, and his best man,
Bride's maid's, and groom's men.
The whole shabang

I just cant wait til that day,
Me and my long head boy turned manThis poem was written for my daddy.
It's aboput how he's always teasing me.
When we talk about boys he always call them " Long Head Boy"

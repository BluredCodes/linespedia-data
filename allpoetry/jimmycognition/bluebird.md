I spend my days chasing a bluebird
until the effort wears holes in shoes and mind alike.

She always flits just out of reach
singing her sweet song for all to hear.

"No-one can appreciate her song more than I",
my jealousy tells me. "I should be the one to hear it."

"Perhaps if I were a tad swifter," my self-loathing grumbles,
"I could catch that lovely bluebird."

"Ah! But the song is sweet and true," intones my purest sense,
"whether it is mine or not."

So I continue the chase for my bluebird,
knowing full well that I shall grow no swifter,
only to hear her magnificient song.

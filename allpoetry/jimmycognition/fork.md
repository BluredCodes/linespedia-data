A lonely fork sits at my feet
Cast aside through some careless decision of mine
Lying dormant and inactive

It reminds me of the man at McDonalds
Who gives you your change
Not the teenager, but the old fellow
With the tired eyes, clinging to his last connections
To the life he once had

Does he have a family now?
Do his friends know if he's quite alright?
Or does he slip out of the shallow existance
Brought by the silly striped uniform?

I slide the fork back to its dark, dark drawer
And close it without another thought

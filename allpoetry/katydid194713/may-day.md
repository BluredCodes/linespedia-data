I always made May baskets
for our son to take everywhere.
This was the tradition
when people used to care.

I decorated each and every cup
with crepe paper in styles.
Every one was different and
brought many happy smiles.

Our grandchildren would not
have known about this fun.
Because the mother never took time
to learn about this May One.

I would make the baskets
and show them what to do.
How to put them all together
using the crepe paper and glue.

Let them cut with scissors,
using their own imagination
of how to style the basket,
to carry on the tradition.

We would then put in popcorn
and fill them with lots of candy.
The kids thought this was the best;
they'd always "snitch" some; real handy!

They thought it was really great
to try to sneak to a house.
Push the doorbell and run
quietly as a mouse.

They wanted to take a basket
to our older people, too.
This made the people so happy
who had been so down and blue.

No more will I get to carry on,
with the kids, this tradition.
The mother has custody and
won't let us have visitation.

So May One is just
another day to me now.
It will be very sad,
but, I'll get through it, somehow!

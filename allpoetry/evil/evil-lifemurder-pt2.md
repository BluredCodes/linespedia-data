We all claim to be true to the game-

but most niggaz ain't real nowadays

I love my man for the fact that he real

And nobody in the factory squeel

Us niggaz don't like rats

We catch one we drop on 'em wit bats

heatin em up wit 45's an' gats

From prison cells to chronic

my niggaz more hi-tech than Johnny Knuemonic





All this perjury and controversy,

Ill niggaz talkin bout they new albums

but that's the reason I let my shit be

let it drop bout early two thousand and three

I know you loathein and lovin your bitch

Claimin she was sent to you from above

But still us niggaz don't give a shit about love

Especially since most of yall mother fuckers ain't real artists

That's why my shit continues to hit the hardest

Whether it be the strip or the yard

my rhymes still continue to hit real niggaz hard

right up in they soft spot, cus they mans is lame

It's easy how real niggaz lay down the game

In claim to they stated fame, and there ill fated name

I'm not sayin that your shit is wack, 

But my shit's still better wit lesser tracks



That's why niggaz actin all foolish these days

the blue flags are ablazed

To this shit I am really amazed

crip and blood niggaz screamin they names,

claaaack!- I be my b's down wit two c's acrossed my chest

Hurtin niggaz, at least tryin my best

Wearin em down w/bullet proof vests

while my niggaz provin em wrong with the strong armin niggaz tests

This is the shit that I be talkin about 

Like an Aboriginee nigga on his walkabout

It's my journey into manhood

Whether you like it or not

The crip city, was my neighboorhood.

Growin up with Mr. Rogers,

Throwin up with Sister Roberts

Hittin on hot latin bitches at church

While wavin the blue flags we drag the red rags

On top of all this my life is still hurtThis is the second part to my rap serires, so for those of you who like rap, read away, if not stay away.

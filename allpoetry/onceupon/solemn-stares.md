I saw the house,
One lonely day.
There were no lights
And gone away
Were the people who had once lived
And shown at least some interest
In its solemn life.

I asked to see
Somebody there,
They looked at me,
With solemn stares
And walked away with heads hung down
Trying to shake the sudden frown
That had come over them.

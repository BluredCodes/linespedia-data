It stands there in beauty,
Poised and magnificent,
A flawless picture,
Painted by death.
The more I stare,
In awe of its beauty,
Thoughts of a greater beauty,
Swim through my head.
A love so strong,
Healthy, and beautiful,
A flawless picture,
Ending with death.
Now I sit, 
Pondering two beauties,
On this last hour of night,
Both ending in death,
Our last hour of life.
It’s gentle silk, 
Caressed my lips,
Reminding me of love’s past,
A God-like taste.
I came upon her,
Her falsities of love,
One last kiss, 
Just to say good-bye,
One last kiss,
Before we die.
A sensational feeling,
Now together forever.
We fall to our knees,
Wilting like the flower of the fall,

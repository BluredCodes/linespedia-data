SPANISH MOSS

With supple grace
it drapes and moves 
in shadowy silhouette,
stirred by unseen breezes
lovingly nudging its nape,
forming a gray vignette;

Mystery implied
by the veiled strands
waving there,
devouring  the very breath of life
from the oaks
so aged and bare.

		Joyce GuyI love the Spanish Moss, and yet it sounds a death knell for the  hardwood trees it inhabits.

FORGETFULNESS

I've gone to wash dishes
A dozen times or more;
Overflowed the sink,
And flooded the floor.

I no sooner get 
the water turned on
Than I leave the room,
or answer the phone.

The first clue I have
Of a memory lapse
Is my sloshing in water,
As I return in my path.

The waterfall tumbling over
sink edge with foam
Makes me doubly thankful
that I'm home alone;

For how can you explain
A gal in her prime
forgetting entirely...
A lapse of the mind.
 
Joyce Guy
7/96

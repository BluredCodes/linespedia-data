This scene takes place within the labyrinth as mentioned before in the previous poems.

The night remains still in their quiet solitude,
within the maze of hedges trimmed to perfection.
Statues stand, immovable by hand,
scattered throughout this garden of perplexion.
Deep within its confines, right at the center,
sits a fountain, four tiers high,
with Cupid waiting so that he may finish his job.
Above the clouds hover, barely outlined in the moon,
and stars appear, like salt on a velvet drape.
With an ambiance rivalling that of Monet,
our two lovers walk through this garden of dreams,
oblivious to all other worldly ways.

Taking a a left and down a long channel,
the labyrinth consumes them, providing them peace.
Another left with a quick turn right,
a statue appears, duplicating Da Vinci.
They keep moving on without any sense of direction,
just walking together, cherishing the moment.
Statues appear and then lost without sight,
as they pass by benches not wanting to break.
After a period of time lost in memory,
they take one final turn, and their stands the fountain.

Him: Would you like to sit yet again,
so that we may grow more comfortable?

Her: Declining such an offer,
would surely be without wise form of discourse.

And so they sit gazing up at the sky,
as it becomes darker with clouds taking pride.
The moon now merely a silhouette fading to darkness,
disappears quietly, as the stars too oblige.
A light snow begins to fall as he makes his first move,
holding her tight, both for warmth and the thrill of her touch.
She gasps inwardly as a smile spreads across her rosy face,
for this is the moment finally realized, as foretold by fate.

They sit together hearing each other's heart beat,
passion growing within, and falling deeply in love.

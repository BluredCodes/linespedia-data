When you lose someone close
Nothing compares to the pain,
it's worse because the pain reminds you
as if you're watching that person die
You can see them getting closer
To the shadow of the valley of death
imagine their fear and wish you had more time
And wonder, do they know what's coming?

You never know what you've got till it's gone
The absent mindedness, and painful reminders
It seemed like such a chore when they remembered 
It hurts because it's like they've just been told
Because they're asking for their mother
and she died when they were oldI wrote this poem for school, and its the first real attempt i made at poetry. So it's sort of different from the others.

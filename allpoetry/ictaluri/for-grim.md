There are oceans to climb,

And mountains to swim,

There are storm clouds to mime,

And ebonies to dim.

But nothing I will ever do

Could compare

To the weirdness of you.



I think I'll go bathe

In the oak tree outside,

And use the new acorns

To rinse off my hide.

And when I'm done, I'll shed the skin,

Where ever you touched,

While I was still in.

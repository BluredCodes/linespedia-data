---
title: "The War"
writer: "Shashama"
slug: "the-war"

Never once did I cry
Until I felt his kiss
“Now don’t you worry ‘bout a thing
Please don’t cry, dear sis.”

And then he was gone…
Just like that, and now
I don’t know where he has gone
Not where, not why, not how

Oh my dear big brother
Why was I so mean?
You could get hurt, in the war
Where enemies can be seen.

The news came so much later
Big brother safe from harm
I realize now, there was no need
For all of my alarm

Rejoice! The war is over
The soldiers welcomed back
My brother hugged me tightly
And then gave me some slack

I said, “I love you brother.”
He said, “I love you sis.”
I don’t remember how long
My parents hugged and kissed.

I feel sorry for the families,
That lost their only son
And now that the war is over,
It doesn’t matter that we won.

The burden has been lifted
The black bows all around
The doves above are flying
Never landing on the ground

The sorrow, it still lingers
Never going far
The families that have lost,
Are left with painful scars.

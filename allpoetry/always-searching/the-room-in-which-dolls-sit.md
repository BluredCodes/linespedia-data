My mother had a room with dolls
some cloth, some porcelain
they sat
from wall to wall

I called this room the world, you see
for though their skin is differed
I saw their eyes, all blank,
staring back at me

Within this room, I sit, alone
my skin, not durable,
may tear and bleed
my soul is not my own

They stare at me with empty eyes
empty, cold, uncaring,
these dolls I love
for them, I wish to die

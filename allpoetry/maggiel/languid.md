Languid

The summer sky 
knows nothing
of desire
The air sits heavy
hot and silky
it drapes my body
like a cape
My sweat entices me
it reminds me
of long hours 
entwined with you
No,
the summer sky 
knows nothing
of desire 
Of damp flesh
rubbing friction
that shocks and jolts
mutually absorbed
in our selfish pleasure
No,
the summer sky
knows nothing
of desire

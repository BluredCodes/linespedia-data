---
title: "lost child"
writer: "Dilionn"
slug: "lost-child"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/39152-lost-child-by-Dilionn"
---

you were a paragon 
a perfect rebel 
causes need not apply 
beautifully careless 
unconcerned, wild and free 
you made us feel alive 
there in the old foundry 
sleeping on the quarterpipe 
up all night telling stupid jokes 
outrageous angry statements
preaching to the converted 
weeks passing in a haze 
crystal glittering in our eyes 
hot, flushed, and tired
desperately aware of life 

perhaps every clown 
conceals a broken child 
we'll never know...you didn't say goodbye 
when we found you there,
inert grey metal in your inert grey hand 
your laughing face smearing the walls 
life in a puddle, spreading like wings 
from your sloped shoulders 
every one of us died, changed forever 
damned souls, our last 
unknown vestige of innocence 
faded, gone...we feel so old
without youmiss you, Lucky.

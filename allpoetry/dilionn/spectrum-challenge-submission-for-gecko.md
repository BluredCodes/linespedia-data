my love for her is a damask rose
the sable death-time of my empty heart
illuminated by her argent soul 
lifted trembling from amaranth despair
the lupine depths long covering me fade
hyacinth memory fades to ash, 
gives way to jonquil wishes
--to gaze into her eyes of sienna
see the absinthe flashes within
secret incarnadine kisses placed gently
upon rose madder lips, to trace 
nacre-white teeth with questing tongue
catch my fingers in silken auramine
delicate as celadon faerie-wings
such longing, a garnet ache
my twin, platinum to my ebony

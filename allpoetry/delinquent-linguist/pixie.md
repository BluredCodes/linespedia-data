Invading my thin flat window 
           in a night so deep 
           the moon is buried

           the sound of a pickpocket princess arrives
           skipping over rainy cobblestones
           reminding me of a diamond shower

           cascading crystalline prisms
           and aurally 
           hallucinogenic
           sine waves

           following the homing beacon eminating 
           from her fairy-tale footwear

           be it her witchcraft
           or pagan gypsy charm

           I become engrossed
           by rainbow tracers
           watching her "Riverdance" in the 
           threads of a streetlamp

           loosening my damp cravate
           I step shuddering
           to her hollow
           luminous 
           womb
           and dance 

           when dawn raises it's bleeding eye
           I know what souless damnation 
           will come to me
           but to all there will come a great cost 
           for just a night's worth of feeling
           so freeno this poem is not about acid.

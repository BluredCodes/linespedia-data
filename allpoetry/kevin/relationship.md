she comes to me, in heat,
saying 'I've longed
to run my fingers through your hair'
sitting, we take tea

Stand up and dance for me
I say, but I join her,
and our skin glistens in the moonlight
and the music grows behind us

our music,
the nettling symphony of stars
pinpoint violins in the distance
no-one near, we're so. far. away.

and the distance creeps between us
silent cold, like ice covering and protecting
years of new ideas grow cold, settle
around us

their gray bodies litter
forming a castle, keeping in&out seperate
and it holds us down, despite
the clean breeze, and empty shellin explanation (12/1/03), this is about a past relationship of mine, that seemed to stagnate over the years.  Which is the cold blanket holding us down (until we decide to take it and the castle away...  I don't think all relationships are thus doomed, but it is certainly one of the biggest things to fear.  Dynamicism is hard to come by, and finding someone you will STAY interested in is the most important part of matchmaking for me

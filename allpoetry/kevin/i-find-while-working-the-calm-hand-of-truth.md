i find, while working, the calm hand of Truth
my niche at the corner of the world
martyrdom: serving, to the destruction of myself, O
how the winds turn cold at the passing of Summer.

some of us still wear red shoes
to the office, & beautiful mascara
but my eyes have been dead, since
since I lost humanity, and it lost me.

what's the good of service to a dead world
all I hold on to is a fragment of the past
there are no more dreams among us:
the sky is silent with sorrow, it's lost son dead.


children wander by me, and I
can't see any hope in them, either.
They're nailing shut the coffin -
the hole is dug: push it in.stanzas, images

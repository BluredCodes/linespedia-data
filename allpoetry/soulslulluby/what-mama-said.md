I am my own brand of cool

At least that’s what mama said

I thought she meant I was foolish

I never understood her

At least not until today

She never meant it to hurt

She knew that I was different

And wanted me to know that

I wasn’t like other girls

I didn’t flirt or date

I didn’t wear pretty make-up

I only dressed up for church 

She knew I’d bloom on my own

She knew I’d be a lady

She just didn’t know when or how

She wanted me to be strong

Beauty was another goal

Most of all just to be me

It didn’t matter to her

What the other mothers said

Only that I had fun and played

When the kids would laugh and tease

She’d tell me to ignore them

“You are your own brand of cool”

Now I know that she was right

People will like me for me

And if they don’t then who cares

Because my mama told me… 

I am my own brand of cool.

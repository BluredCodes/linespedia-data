Buried Alive!


 

The dry dead leaves crunched beneath his feet  

As the setting sun and the tall trees meet

Casting long dark shadows, as he went on

On his empty face no remorse was shown

 

Toting his burden, his steps were sure

Cradled like a baby, wrapped in a tarp 

He carried it with ease and never once stopped

Even with the  shovel resting on top

 

Finding a clearing ,near a oak tree

He drops his gruesome bundle, to the ground

It rolls and it moves but he does not see

He so busy digging the grave, almost with glee

 

With the grave dug, he pushes in the body at last

Quickly refilling the earth now its getting so dark

As he walks away he never hears the faint bark

Of some ones pet he hit, driving dangerously too fast!

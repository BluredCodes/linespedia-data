---
title: "Yesterday"
writer: "atia"
slug: "yesterday"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/38164-Yesterday-by-atia"
---

"Glorious, I tell you. Glorious!"
Even though I risk sounding like a preacher,
With the same tone of undying conviction,
Like I know what all the world need do
For salvation.
But you know we wouldn't need salvation
If we had no life.
So what came before?
What brought the need to be alive?

It was a beautiful day though,
And I could go on for no other purpose
Than those dark clouds
Daring to touch me,
And the rain
That danced across my face
As I breathed in
And cared that I did. 
I ran under a falling sky,
Tried to dampen all that wants me sane.
Seems like I give you naught
But the belief in lunacy.

I wonder why
I might even need infinite wisdom,
When there has never been a better day.
So I will not have seen it all
But all that was meant for me - 
What makes my life a jest
And what makes going stray
All the more worthwhile.
Who says life is to have a meaning?
I live for the feeling...I owe this one to yesterday's thunderstorm :)

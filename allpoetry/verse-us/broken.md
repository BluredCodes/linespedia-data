title: "broken"
writer: "Verse Us"
slug: "broken"
source: "AllPoetry"

you broke me into tiny peices
     crushed my naked, withered soul
     ripped the scab of heart from me
     you shattered my little world

     you scraped the blood from my hands
     and removed me from the flesh, I'm told
     my silver tongue, you tore from me
     you shattered my little world

     you wiped my tears with abundant mercy
     washed me whiter than snow in blood
     you dug the remnents of sin from me
     you shattered my little world

     you cradled me in your gentle arms
     while I shook with fear and emptiness.
     you breathed new life in deflated lungs
     you shattered my empty world

     you heard my cries of pain in me
     the angst that ate away at me
     you took away all that I believed
     you shattered my empty world.

     you dressed me in white, and righteousness
     patiently dealt with my paralysis
     rehabilitated this borrowed existence
     you shattered my empty world

     you replaced my isolation and sorrow,
     with assurance of the upcoming sun.
     I hold your hand to let you guide.
     you shattered my empty world

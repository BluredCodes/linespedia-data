A spring rising from arid lands
Puts water in the driest hands
But water is not to be held
It is to be drunk till thirst is quelled
So it is with a man's own eyes
For which seeing they always vie
But when a man must learn to live
His perfect sight has nothing to giveI copied this from a notebook.  I didn't like it, but my friends seemed to.  Any suggestions?

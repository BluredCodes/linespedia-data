Some of my best friends have scales
Forked tounges, sticky tounges, long tounges
People tend to think they are slimy, and cold
Like slugs, or snails
But they are smooth like silk
They don't talk too much, but always
Friendly, in a greeting touch

Some of my best friends, Have 180 eyes
And I know it's love, if they look at me
With both of them clearly
People always looked at me
Like I was alittle off my rocker
Put a cricket on my nose
Watch that as that long tounge goes
What a shocker!

You couldn't really tell from my outer shell
The things I have in my house, or refridgerator
Snakes, lizards, frogs, newts are household guests
There's always the odd mouse or two
In the fridge, in loo of feeding time
I've lost a few human friends along the line
Gained a few, who thought I was cool

Think twice, when you see a friend with scales
Perhaps, think of me. 
...No I do not have scales!
What do think I am? A freak?
...Ok so maybe a little
But scales don't mean Satan
Nor does it mean their owners
Are quite keen on black

So if you do not like scales,
Just go away
I'd rather have my scale-ish friends
Any day!...Odd, I know. But truth is, I am odd. I've made a ref to my reptilian friends in poems before. Read them, you might find which ones I mean. People usually get the wrong message when they see my animals. Think perhaps I am goth, or a devil worshiper. But nothing is farther from the truth. Tell me what you think. Not typical of me, but I am in a strange mood tonight!

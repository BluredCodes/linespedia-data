Everyone else remembers the last year of loving themselves
They all have their stories, their over sung self righteous glories
1989, didn't do too much to me
Honestly, I can't remember many faces or special places
MTV was at the so called height of it's infamy
There was some bad rock, some good stuff too
Parties and lovers that left me emotionally black and blue

Who cares about 1989?
I try but I forget what you look like
Were you the first face who spoke deeply
Of bad love and over rated poetry
I can't quite wrap my mind around your image
Though your voice speaks to me on the other end of the line
You ask how I'm doing, hopefully well and fine
You speak of that year, of 1989

1989, just another year to disappear inside
I think I'm the only one who doesn't care
How this rocker wore their hair
Or what drugs you and your friends were on
I can fry my brain on the plain insanity of life
You tell me stories, of your divorced wife
And two kids, the others you don't know about
Who needs to hear more? I don't care
Do I need to shout?

Who cares about 1989?
I try but I forget what you look like
Were you the man with the child eyes
Who told me lies and showed me art?
Stole my heart, just to break it
You tell me of the good old days
Of parties and the 'love me haze' in the air

1989, most of us were only half way there 
I don't remember much, though some of us tried to stay in touch
You call me on the phone, after over ten years past
You expect your memory to last inside my tipsy mind?
You were just another face in the memory flip book
An average rating of "fine"
And who really cares about 1989?

O Mother dear, listen now ---
This is your son roaring:
I am a beast back from my horror,
To his family returned,
Returned to this pale pink country
Where miscreants are spurned.

You, mother to a monster,
Kiss me now and rejoice!
I am not ashamed to call you mother
Although I have no choice:

You have no fur to stink with,
Nor claws to rip rich silk,
Nor fangs to chew up relatives
And others of that ilk;

You have no ears to hear
The wicked calls of witches,
Dancing nakedly at night;
At the sight of beasts like me
You mistakenly take fright;

You fear to hear the music
The sirens sing at sea;
I have listened, let them lead
Me to minstrelsy;

So now like them I run abroad
Armed with song and dance
To mislead young ones to my horde
And give bad manners a chance.

O Mother dear, womb of my wildness, 
Do not run away;
I am dating the sweetest witch
This side of Saturday.

That evening Mary hailed a cab
without a clearly thought-out plan
except her thirst for fresh new buds
and pale-blue dreams of human blood,
then might perhaps her foe abate:
the Time that turned the present-day
into a place beyond her reach
and her a lonely irksome leech.

How fair, what Mary felt just then!
To stop herself from being a pest
she had but drain a strong, young heart
of life and in its stead impart
the strangest gift the world contains:
eternal slumber wanting pain.

Repentance briefly crossed her mind
for deeds not yet performed when lights
across Miami Beach bedazed
her eyes already in decay
from peering at the world outside
through windows stained by passing Time,
again Its tangled web finespun
with careless mastery all outdone
until of souls the windows called
would rather shut and not appall
inquiring folks that come around.

-The nasty clubs are underground-
the driver stated with demur
afraid to learn of such impure
designs beneath the glorious gray
that should have signalled placid days,
yet Mary knew his desperate grip
upon the offered handsome tip
would see him gone without more qualms,
his conscience bought he'd sleep with calm.Its the first part of four. Mary and Eleanor.

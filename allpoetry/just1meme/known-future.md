To dream things that become real
I am not sure how I am to feel
My last dream scared me the most
I am sure it would turn anyone white as a ghost

We were invaded by an unknown foe
And with all of our power it took only one blow
The roads are gone and so is our food
And the only means of travel are the soles of our shoes

People are hungry and very scared
And the sad thing about it is that no one was prepared
Our government said everything would be fine
And that everything would fall in line

Men dressed in grey-green clothes
Shoot at us now as if we were their foes
The shrieks of terror you hear in the night
As we move on in our sad plight

My parents are gone, I know not where
I go to their house but they are not there
I cry and shed many tears
Not knowing what happened is one of my fears

We hide in their broken house
having to be quiet as a mouse
Afraid of what might be coming
Getting ready to do some running

People who would normally be our friends
Become our enemies in the end
Food and water are priceless now
One man will take it, he had vowed

One shot fired, now two
The man lay bleeding and dead at my shoes
My husband is standing there wiping his face
As we grab our kids and run out of that place

We go to the beach to move up the coast
There we meet some people who can help us, they boast
The 'resistance', the grey-green men call
The ones who are to help us all

My dream starts to get fuzzy
But Ido remember some humming
Helicopers and me flying
With bulletts shooting and my children crying

My life was not meant to be this
God get me out of this mess
I am meant to be a wife and a mother
Not someone who worries about helicoper rutters

God let this just be a dream
However real it may have seemed
Let me have peace at night
Even though I have second sight.This is from a specific recurring dream that I have had since childhood.  Through my dreams, I have seen the future before and it has been correct. I wrote this poem MANY years ago when I was 13 years old. Considering what has happened,I needed to share

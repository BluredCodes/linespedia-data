---
title: "the dance"
writer: "catseyes"
slug: "the-dance"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/29554-the-dance-by-catseyes"
---

You saw me
dancing freely around the room, 
vibrating with life.

You asked for my hand,
and we became partners.

I performed the more delicate moves
for the beauty of two forms 
joined in one song.

Within the lights and the music, 
we transformed
into a man and woman in love.

Now, 
you etch lines on the floor,
the confines of ownership.

Your boundaries
and my strong independence collide.

You maneuver to control the beat,
but I will not be told 
which tempo suits me.

My vitality cannot be persuaded 
to sit on the sidelines and die.
I am too alive.
I am too alive even without you.

I would have waltzed
the moon.
But I will not fade into you.

I will keep dancing.
I will keep dancing on.

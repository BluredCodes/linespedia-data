---
title: "Frozen Heart"
writer: "GhettoRaisedPoet"
slug: "frozen-heart"
source: "AllPoetry"

My eyes have lost their once passionate gaze
And my hands have lost the urge to hold and touch another
Blood that once flowed to my heart so pure
Froze solid the instant you decided to love another

I awake in the morning
And though the sun may shine it's glorious rays into my home
It cannot free my heart from it's frigid cage
Nor take away my condition of forever being alone

Every breath from my lungs that I exhale
Is a cold hard wind of pain
And every time I see them disappear into the air
It only reminds me of how my love was given in vein

My Saddend Heart was once the color of the reddest most beauiful rose
Until it was stricken with Rejection and Loss
For now it is frozen for eternity and slowly dying
As if it had been crucified upon a holy cross

The veins in my body are no longer active
They are only battlescars to show where I was hit with deadly force
The old saying held true here
When it said to kill something 
You must take it out at the heart which is it's origin and source

So here I shall remain shivering and shaking
Until the last chapter of my life has been written
It will say that I loved with reckless abandon
And died because my heart was lonely and to badly frostbitten

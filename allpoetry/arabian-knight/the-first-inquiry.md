title: "The First Inquiry"
writer: "Arabian Knight"

In every heart..in every mind

this common question one will find

is there beyond this world we see

a god to whom our eyes are blind?



An ancient question we all pose

and wonder what we should suppose

it is the first inquiry

the one this poem shall expose



Upon this earth we all reside

amidst a silent space so wide

its like a grain thrown in a sea

an outcome we did not decide



The fact is that we do exist

we didn't choose this nor resist

so how did this life come to pass 

a thought that surely will persist



Our hearing, sight and intellect

are tools that we should not neglect

in solving this old mystery

on everything we should reflect



The planets swim around their sun

electrons through their orbits run

they all comply so eagerly

all motions are in unison



Observing life in any form

one sees that it is uniform

this world is run so orderly

an order where all things conform



This world with all its natural laws

and beauty that is free of flaws

perfection one can not dismiss

is chance indeed its only cause



Through logic one can clearly state

that chance alone can not create

the slightest form of discipline

nor organize ..nor formulate



There had to be a conscious will

that caused this world and runs it still

intelligence so infinite..

which has a purpose to fulfill



Although He's hidden from our eyes

His signs are seen throughout the skies

and on this earth His signs are clear

and in ourselves His signs arise



We see His might in what we fear

His beauty when the stars appear

His wisdom in coherency

His mercy in a mothers' tear



The one eternal entity

the ultimate reality

the origin of origins

the refuge for humanity



He granted us the gift of thought

which other creatures all have not

that we may recognize the truth

and learn the lesson life has taught



Not only reason did He grant

not only signs did He implant

but also sent us messengers

with books of guidance to enchant



Through them He has conveyed his plan

the truth of life ...the role of man

He aided them with miracles

performing what no mortal can



I end this hoping every word

will focus visions that are blurred

and that the first inquiry

will not continue unanswered 



I wrote this poem to invite

the minds of men to see the light

and contemplate upon their lives

and choose a path they feel is right

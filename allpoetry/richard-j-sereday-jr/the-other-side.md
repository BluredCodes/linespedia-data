---
title: "The other side"
writer: "Richard J Sereday Jr"
slug: "the-other-side"
source: "AllPoetry"

Some say that 
                                the grass is greener 
                                  on the other side. 

                              Some are so preoccupied 
                                with the hypnotic hues 
                                 of that other side, 
                                willing to forsake all 
                               just for the opportunity 
                                   to cross over. 

                                 They work so hard 
                               to get to that other side 
                             only to find disappointment. 

                           Once there they turn to find that 
                              where they just came from 
                           now appears to be much greener 
                                 than they realized, 
                                  but because they 
                                  worked so hard 
                                   to cross over, 
                            they may not have the strength 
                               or the means to return. 

                               Some have seen enough 
                                    of the world 
                                 to know the truth. 

                               They are aware of this 
                       "Greatest Illusion that Life has to Offer". 

                                 A human tendency. 
                                 To want what they 
                                  do not possess, 
                                unable to appreciate 
                              what life has given them. 

                            A desire for something better. 

                                  Not knowing that 
                                 the greener grass 
                                  is just an illusion, 
                                 a trick consisting of 
                             angles, light, and perception. 

                             Those on the other side are 
                                lustfully looking at us, 
                                seeing what we have, 
                                willing to throw away 
                                  all that they have 
                                for just a piece of that 
                             greener grass that they see. 

                             How much life must we live 
                                before we realize that 
                                 the greenest grass 
                            we could ever hope to possess 
                               is beneath our own feet. 

                              The greater we care for it, 
                               the greener it becomes. 

                                Lack of proper care 
                               will surely kill the grass 
                               which lies on either side. 

                          The wise try every known technique 
                           to care for what they already have 
                              so that it might burst forth 
                                 with a color so vivid 
                           all else seems pale in comparison.

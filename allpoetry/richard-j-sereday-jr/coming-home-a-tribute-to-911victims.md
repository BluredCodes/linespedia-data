You're not coming home, are you,
for it's not within your power?

You went to work today,
never to return.

And had you known 
your end was at hand,

would you have said to me
more than you did
as you walked out our door?

Would you have held me longer,
tighter, closer to your heart,
in our last good bye?

For I wish that I had held you
in my arms so tight 
and not let you go,

but how were we to know,
you'd be taken away,
used as a pawn, 

in some kind of sick battle,
between misguided souls?

Two sides to a story,
and which one is right?

They bomb and we bomb,
as it comes to full circle,

but can the answers be found
in a cloud of smoke,
and the blood of the innocent?

The answer to that,
I believe to be no.

So, as I wait for your return,
which is never to be,

I'll hold you in my heart,
ever so tightly

as never to let go of
the memory of you,

until the day comes

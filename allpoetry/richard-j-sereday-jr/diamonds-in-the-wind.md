---
title: "Diamonds in the wind"
writer: "Richard J Sereday Jr"
slug: "diamonds-in-the-wind"
source: "AllPoetry"

---

What a lovely ring was my wedding gift from her,
                       so many beautiful diamonds, and she said.....
                                     ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
                                     "we are through,
                                   I wanted a divorce",

                               and my heart, it stood still,
                            and stopped beating then and there.

                           Fifteen years flashed before my eye,
                                  over and over for days,
                                 the good times, the bad, 
                            the accomplishments, the mistakes.


                                 And there was the sleep 
                                     that never came,
                                   hoping for the sleep,
                                  to sleep through this 
                                 heavy heavy heartache. 

                            But in time the sleep, it did come,
                          after a multitude of sleepless nights.

                                    And with the sleep 
                                the nightmares did begin.

                            And I found myself walking in a field 
                            along side a fence of barbed wire,

                         when my ring, I did catch upon that fence,
                          and it cut me deeply, and I jerked away,

                         and into the wind, the diamonds did fly,
                          a million tiny little pieces scattered,
                          and the pieces were our lives together.

                   Franticly I did chase after these tiny little pieces
                       but they were too many, and in all directions
                  they did fly never to be found, in spite of my efforts.

                     Hopeless was the task, but I tried just the same 
                  to gather up the broken pieces for repair and restore,
                       but it was not to be, so screaming I awoke, 
                     two A.M. it was, never to sleep again that night
                                and many others to follow.

                                And I never could pick up 
                                  the pieces of the ring,
                                  and there was no repair
                                        or restore,

                                     but the day came 
                                 that pick up the pieces 
                                    of my life I did. 

                                    Somehow stronger, 
                              the price we pay for strength, 
                                       "all to high".

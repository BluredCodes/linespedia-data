I surprise myself sometimes with
	the words I
	say and the things
	I
	drag out from
	underneath the old kitchen
	mat

	It’s gotten to the point where I barely recognize the eyes that meet my gaze in the
	pot belly of my soup spoon anymore.

	but still
	
	I braid my hair
	I pour the cereal
	I trace my initials
	(and maybe a little part of my voice)
	into the fog that’s settled onto the surface of the window 
	over there

	and I smirk
	
	because we’re shaking out 
	that mat again
	today.

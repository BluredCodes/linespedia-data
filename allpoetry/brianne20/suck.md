Suck it in! says the woman on t.v.
It's what your mother always told you,
and it works!

my mother told me that I am fine the way I am.
what lies these mothers propogate.
it's the fault of her genes that my skin is curdled like cottage cheese
and I will never be able to have sex again
or wear a bathing suit.

I have been cursed.
Every single thing is against me.
'It's easier to lose your gut than your butt!  So thank Nature
for giving you that extra fat for birthing babies!'
I can't have a baby if I can't have sex.  And I can't have sex
with cellulite.
'You can't get rid of fat cells.  But you can shrink them somewhat
with exercise.'
'It's all a matter of genetics - if your mother has cellulite
SO WILL YOU.'
'Women with thin skin who don't exercise have the worst cases
of stubborn cellulite.'
'You can't get rid of cellulite.  But you can smooth it
somewhat
with exercise.'
'Thin thighs - FINALLY!'
'You can't get rid of cellulite.'
'You can't
 get rid
 of 
 cellulite.'

So much for magazines.
On the television there are
fashion shows
videos
teen drama
small, cute, perky bottoms - not a trace of flab
thin, perfect thighs striking the air
BAM! BAM! (step, step)
thrusting their thighs out legs out
turning on their heel and then
the walk back
the perfect back-
side
the kind that makes men want to give it to them
from the back

The kind of girl that he compares you to when he
undresses you and finds you lacking.
'You look so much nicer in your clothes.'
Or worse, a joke.
'Check out the jiggle on this ass! (SLAP!)
Loose your gym membership, dollface?'
Fat girls try harder.
Flabby thin girls with cellulite give up.

You can look but don't touch.

I can look but won't touch.
every mirror,
every reflective surface,
how do I look now?
how fat do I look now?
how good is my disguise today?

You can look but don't touch.
In the clubs -
'That's a great outfit on you.  But it would look even better 
in a crumpled pile on my floor.'
'Your pants must be made of Windex cause I can see myself
in them.'
Licks finger.  Wipes my shirt.  'Let's get you out of those
wet clothes.'
Not even they deserve the disappointment.
and when they're hurt who knows
what they will say
what they will strike at
which part of you they will ridicule first
with their freinds
I cannot stand their rejection.
You can look but don't touch.
(you might feel my fat).

Somehow I gave birth to this cellulite
I made it with my body
by myself
to betray me

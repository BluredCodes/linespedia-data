title: "ladybird she"

(ladybird she) is bent and winking wet

before me, and

i grow closer, subtle by inches.



she lets me touch her velvet, 

rubber and slick seductions. my hands,

my breath, my taste and my eyes

are all welcome guests

and i am all i can be here.



her meat is flung around



her spiralling is sweet

i'm hung around her neck, a toy



her skin touches my face



i grant myself a slight retreat



i am whole,

i am alive,

i am vivid and her textures grace me.



(seductive she) pushes back,

moving to me and away, my doll is moving



her skins touch

her lips grace

her angles fold, contract.

complex math confines her beauty

to one small space,

then releases it to boundlessness.



she winks at me.this, like many of my "erotic" poems, is about one particular incident which struck me as powerful and intimate and inspiring.

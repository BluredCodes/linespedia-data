---
title: "cocktails of some color"
writer: "vox et praeterea nihil"
slug: "cocktails-of-some-color"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/9389-cocktails-of-some-color-by-vox-et-praeterea-nihil"
---

and nothing else involved in us nothing
else involved us in one another
and nothing

banal, i bear fruits to this, i concoct,
and the elixirs i've made, cocktails of some color,
i only drink alone

and when we're involved in this, this thing,
thin and weary, dying or dead, overfed,
i still drink alone, i drink without telling you
i've drunk.

i drink without telling you i'm drunk.

i cough, and nothing else involved
my nervousness revolves around this thing
we've got ourselves involved in,
developing a pair of wings and orbiting
around this thing, this thing
before we've caught ourselves evolving.

and nothing else resolved in us nothing
else is solved for us in one another
and nothing

simple, open-faced.  satin panties lined with lace.
and all the liquors i have made, dreams i've played 
and beds i've laid in, cocktails 
of some color, i think
i only think alone,

i think alone without telling you what i'm thinking.
i drink alone without telling you i've drunk.

and nothing else can come of this, no staring back
the eyes of sleep, no conquering the feeble weak and nothing
else can come of this, i've drunk
my cocktails

without telling you i made them,
without selling you the maiden voyage, and
i would conquer all the new worlds
you would open up between your sighs,
between your unrepenting crimes

compatible and hollowly free from any destiny,
empty, i could fill your gaping cavities, but
without selling you the maiden voyage,
without telling you the plans i've plotted,

all my moments spoiling rotten,

i drink alone without telling you my thoughts.
i seek alone without finding what i've sought.

I wish I were your coffee cup,
What you reach for first thing
In the morning.
The bearer of the stuff without
Which you would not wake up 
To have your lips
Wrap
Themselves around mine,
Your hands about
My person as you sip
Again and again and again
Until nothing remains.
Then to have you turn on
The tap
And rinse me out
Sometimes slowly
Sometimes quickly
Depending
On how much time
You can spend on me
Without it becoming
A crime.

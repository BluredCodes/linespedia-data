---
title: "Off With The Head"
writer: "Quabben"
slug: "off-with-the-head"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/49832-Off-With-The-Head-by-Quabben"
---

I have a mission, so I’ve been told
A princess was taken, I must bring her home
Her hand is the prize if I win in this quest
I need to prepare, I need to be bold
Sir Grippon la Brogue is surely the foe

My armor is on, my sword at my side
This day will bring the start of my ride
Titan my horse standing tall in the stall
Is ready for action with snort after snort
he stomps and he tromps his  eyes wide for sport

Off to Glenellen I’ll get la Brogue
His head will adorn a post on his land 
a warning to others disposed to his ways
I approach through the forest, my blood is on fire
I see Glenellen Castle, time to begin

His dragon stands guard fierce to behold
the Serpentine’s posted
between the princess and I
Titan moves fast as I swing my sword
The dragon is dead its head on the floor.

La Brogue comes out, ready to fight
No chance I give him, I cut him down
His head on a post a reward for his life
The hencemen scattered with the point of my sword
Glenellen is mine, my quest all but done

A slash with my sword, the princess is free
Her bonds lie in tatters at the soles of her feet
She raises her arm, I lift her up
Titan is ready his head held high
Gently we canter, time to go home

The princess then told me she’d promised her hand
To Wilbur of Gayland the Prince of du Ponces
In shock and distress I took off her head
My sword has been busy I thought to myself
Now back to the forest, no more of these quests

My Mother Was A Working Girl
You think you know the type?
The times were hard, so many were poor
In truth, not many had enough to eat
or proper clothes to wear
Hitler’s war had took its toll
Thousands never did come home
Nations stripped of fine young men
Families left in dire distress

My Mother Was A Working Girl
No father then had I
Working hard for bills to pay
She did the time, twelve hours a day
on a factory floor six days a week
I never will forget my past
When I grew up so young
Pride swells my heart when telling you
My Mother Was A Working GirlI wish I had written this when she was still alive

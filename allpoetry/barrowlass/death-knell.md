The first day
the guns muttered in the hills
like newborn thunder
impatient to escape.

The second day
mortar bombs, flying death,
rocked ancient squares
which wearily bore the onslaught.

The third day
death's smoky flowers
thrust their heads
above the silence.

The last day
was a void.this was written during the Balkans conflicts in the mid 1990s and seems to be as apt today as then.  It was written in a bid to show the futility of war.

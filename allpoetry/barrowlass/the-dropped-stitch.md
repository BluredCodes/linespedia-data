---
title: "The dropped stitch"
writer: "barrowlass"
slug: "the-dropped-stitch"
source: "AllPoetry"

Her slippers huddle on the hearthrug
before the embering fire.
The companion set stands in solitary state
behind the brass fire fender.

Her feet rest upon the wooden footstool
he made when their marriage was new.
Patina-ed by childrens' hands,
it stands solid, foursquare.

The clock nibbles away at time
with relentless tick-tock rhythms.
"For long service", his only token
of fifty years subjugation.

Inconsequential noise from the TV
merges with the budgie's prattle.
The bird adores his mirror image,
preening for his insubstantial mate.

She was knitting a shawl
to keep out the creeping cold.
She didn't notice that an hour ago
she dropped a stitch.

I threw flowers
in that long ago Prague Spring.

Flowers grew from tank guns,
petals replaced bullets.

We knew nothing of the Summer of Love
the preceding year.

Tanks don't make love
only war.

I threw flowers
in that long ago Prague Spring.

They wilted in the cold wind
of totalitarian night.

I don't throw flowers
but I haven't forgotten
the Prague Spring.A comment on the apparent democratisation of the then Czechoslovakia in 1968 - it's author, Alexander Dubcek was subsequently imprisoned by the USSR.  The country is now known as Czech Republic and Slovakia.

The mirror shows meunadorned.Fraying at the edges,past my prime.And still I say to everything-'I'll do it - if I have the time.'But time scatterslike summer rain, whichdries up and disappears,evaporates into formless mist,clouding bygone years.Time was when time itself stood still;each moment savoured on youthful tongues.Inhaled as a priceless essence,enwrapped in chiming song.Now time races by, each moment slipslike sand between my fingers.And only my heart and soul and mindknow how the memories linger.

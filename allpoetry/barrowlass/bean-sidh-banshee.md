title: "Bean Sidh (Banshee)"

The boy stood at the edge of the sea.
Wet sand sucked at his infant toes.
Laughing, he tried to catch sun-sprites
which danced, just out of reach, upon the wavelets.

The seaweed caressed his bare legs.
Gulls called his name.

A cloud covered the sun
and the boy's mother wiped the sand
from his infant toes,
after closing his drowned eyes.

The wailing woman waited upon a nearby rock.




NB:  in Irish folklore the Banshee (Wailing Woman) was a harbinger of death

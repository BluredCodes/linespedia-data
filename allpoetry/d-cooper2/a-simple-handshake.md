A Simple Handshake

I will know when
I have arrived
Closer to where
I want to be.

When my touch is warm,
And no cold fingers
Hang me up
In compromise.

When a simple handshake,
Willingly offered,
And accepted with dignity,
Is no surprise.

When  I no longer linger
With my expectations
Unfulfilled, long enough
To surmise

That I have been slighted.
Or let kindnesses unreciprocated
Remain with me 
As injured pride.

When a simple handshake
becomes symbolic
That my need for validation
Comes only now from me.

Iwill know when
I have arrived
Closest to where
I want to be.

When I can accept the  truth
About myself 
With enough humility
And awareness to forgive

My own spirit its frailty,
And become a soul  made free 
To release this crusty layer
Of accumulated grief

And anger.  Helplessness
And hopelessness
That took lifetimes to learn, 
Let go in one short prayer.
 
I will know when
I have arrived 
At just the place
I need to be.

When that simple handshake
Is with God
Bringing ultimate relief
Of  reunion with the Universe and eternity.

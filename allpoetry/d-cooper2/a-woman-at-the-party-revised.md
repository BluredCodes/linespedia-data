I was posturing ever so innocently
At a party given recently.
The kind that is given to impress,
An excuse to fawn and overdress.
The kind where rings and gold chains shine;
Conversations about what's yours or mine;
Where prejudice hides quite decently
Amidst hors d'oeuvres , champagne, and wine.

This party was literally wallowing
In angst so bitter that swallowing
Full lip-service given to deafened ears
Had become a game, "charade of tears."
I was about to take leave of this gloom
When a late-comer suddenly entered the room.
A woman , "persona mysterious," allowing
Some sunshine into this chamber of doom.

What well hidden secret so disarming
Made her open,  honest smile so charming?
Her lack of jewelry and simple gown
Put all of our " phony glitter" down.
In trying to cross the room to meet her,
I joined the crowd closing in to greet her.
Soon  frozen follies were thawing, then warming,
To the seeds of sincerity this 'flower' had sown.1983,

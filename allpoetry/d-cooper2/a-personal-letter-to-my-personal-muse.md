A  Personal Letter to My Personal Muse


I write and write, my dear,
Stringing words, like beads, together
Year after year after year.
Stringing words  end over end,
Not one word of which is new.
Just helping old ideas to bend
Into my personal point of view.

I write and write, my dear,
And pray my words reach out
To someone... someday.... somewhere....
Who may've begun to doubt
That so few words rhymed into verse 
Could shape a smile or fetch a tear.

I write and write, my dear,
And I have every writer's fear..
No,  not rejection or ridicule,
Or that some might call me "fool,"
Or that my meaning won't be clear.

No, my muse, my greatest fear
Is of that awfu.l, wretched day
When you depart and leave me here,
When you decide to fly away.

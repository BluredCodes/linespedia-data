title: "The Calling"

I don't choose this for popularity.
I get no personal kick
Out of power.
I am not a social stigma.
I am not a notch on a belt.
I am not "cool".
I paint, each day,
The winds,
The driving rains, the freezing snows,
The heat of the sun, the cool of the night,
Each twinkling star, each trickling stream,
Every inch of your flesh,
Every bone in your body,
Every strand of your hair:
I am the voice of your dreams
And the tool
Of Divinity.
I am the passive reflex
That forces breath.
I am not trying to be,
Nor am I forcing becoming.
It just happens, or,
More often than not,
It doesn't.
It is not concious
Nor conceived,
But steeped
And stripped and whittled
And,
In many ways,
Burned down to it's core essence.

We are not all chosen for every path.

There are a thousand paths to walk
On this earth,
And even more
With every life we live,
But we can only walk one at a time.
We cannot be the master of all trades,
Instead we must be masters of ourselves
And not tyrants for others.
We must listen to the voices
And trust their words.
We must look to our elders,
To our ancestors
And respect them for the paths they have walked,
For they were not ours
And yet they lend so much
To our experiences.
But:
Do not mock my age
Or take liberties with my youth.
I am much older now
Than you will ever be.

I must be still and silent
And listen for the voice,
Seek instrucion
In the running stream,
The flights of birds,
In smoke and steam and cloud,
I must find direction
In every small omen
And heed every sign
Along the way.
I make choices not for myself
But for the world around me.
I did not choose this path
But was chosen for it
By rote, perhaps,
Or simply by being born

Blinky was a charming lightbulb
Not very bright by which to read,
While intensity and smarts she lacked
She was a warmhearted friend indeed 

Anytime you opened up a storybook
She smiled at all the wordy pages,
Delicious recipes for a budding cook
Blinky even shined patiently on birdcages

To keep Tweety safe, 
And out of harm’s way
Sweet Blinky was naïf,
She burned out one day

Her outdated bulb turned black
The dear’s little light faded inside,
Yet she just fought right back
With anger, tears, and pride

Blinky refused to let go
She didn’t want to die,
Life had amused her so
‘Tho never knowing why

She just knew that it did
By making people so pleased,
Illuminated upon their kids
In bed sleeping, appeased

Sadly, poor Blinky is nearly dead,
Nevermore will she flicker or gleam
Soon her tiny incandescent filament head
Will be but an electrician’s bad dream

Just as her glass vessel was tossed
Into the trashcan, ready to be replaced,
A used-up thread of cotton dental floss
Appeared in front of her 20 watt face

“I know you, you're the light bulb fairy,
You were always there for my Mistress Mary
Provided lighting to floss her white teeth,
So she could see the back molars beneath”

“Blinky, you delightful bulb, I will never forget
Those wonderful desk lamps you turned on as of yet”,
At that, the charred old bulb suddenly emitted a glow
Twinkling like a star, a golden aura began to grow

Brighter grew her intense sparkle
Some folks would say it’s remarkable,
A miracle perhaps or merely chance?
Now Blinky had a new life and romance

Ross Floss, the dental strand and her became wed,
They lived out their grandest years in Club Med
Blinky won a “Best Candle of the Month” award, 
Ross was the floss of choice for poets and bards

They thrived, happily ever after
In a home filled with much laughter,
Their love survived the common law marriage
Strolling with 3 triplets in a neon baby carriage

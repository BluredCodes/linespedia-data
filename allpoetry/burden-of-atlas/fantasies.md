---
title: "Fantasies"
writer: "Burden of Atlas"
slug: "fantasies"

She turns out the light and darkness sets in
To keep at bay the torment that tears from within
She curls up in her blanket at the end of the day
And all of her problems seem to seep away

And as her dreams set in she enters her other lives
Where there is no pain and only happiness thrives
Where she's not the same girl that gets treated like a zero
But Joan of Arc, Aphrodite, or some other hero

Where she's full of confidance and any problems she meets
She finds a way to cope with or simply defeats
And her friends and her loved ones are always with her there
And her dreams are fulfilled in a world that is fair

Yet when she awakes she feels somewhat sad
As though being taken from a better life that she at one time had
But she can take comfort in the fact that when the day is done
She can return to these other lives with the setting of the sunIf our real lives were as good as our dreams, we'd need never sleep.

Sweetness I can't have
for the sugar is concealed
I fight to tear for this taste,
I struggle is loss,
Harmonized in time,
To touched the sweetness,
Oh why...
can't I
have what I need?
I need a fix,
I want a grain,
A crystal,
A white piece of powder,
Why does it rule my life so?
Lamented sugar packets,
Can you understand my strife?
Not to have a taste of nectar
within material I know I can break,
But Damn it this
plastic...
It was a mistake!
The rush that I love for,
The sweet that keeps me on,
Being stole by a shiny clear film.

June 30, 2000

By B.E. Whitehorn, Adrennia Cooper, Allan Perry & William Randol

This Can’t Be Me

Upon closer inspection 
In my mirror,
I see, in the corners of my eyes,
Or when I pull at my nostrils,
Or open my mouth to speak,
That I am a massive clump
Of writhing maggots,
A paper-thin skin-full.
But this can’t be?
This can’t be me?
I walk and talk,
I seem to play.
And yet, 
When they 
Poke holes in me
The maggots tumble out
Like from road-kill
On a hot summer’s day.Much of what I write seems to address mortality issues.  I don't know where this came from, probably from my mind's eye???

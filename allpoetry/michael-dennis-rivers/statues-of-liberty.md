(Die! I can't die! If I die,
who will pick my nose?)

We are all moving around,
Statues, floating inches
Above the ground/lawn,  (you can use ground or lawn)
Coming and going
And going then gone.Arthur said of my dwelling on death
that I should "get over it," it is inevitable
but I cannot... I think it is the single
most important thing that happens to us
after birth.  Nothing else matters.  And the
more we respect and savour life, the more
important death becomes.

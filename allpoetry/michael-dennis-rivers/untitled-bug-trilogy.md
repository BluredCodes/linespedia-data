I

Grasshopper, scared by the 
Shadow of a passing bird,
Jumped into the cold ashes
Of yesterday's campfire.
He broke through the delicate
Outer crust and sank like
A skater on thin ice
Into the gray darkness.
In a moment he
Regained his footing
And hopped out,
feeling stupid.
A passing ant laughed.

         II

The wormed fruit
Taste good
To the worm.

         III

Between the window and the screen,
Where old flies go to die,
Where spider webs don't ripple
In a breeze,
Where water doesn't freeze
'Til late November,
Where dirt accumulates and
No one cleans
Until next May.
This is where my mind is
On a late summer's day,
Vacation ending pending,
Sad to say.
We all must go away,
Shut the windows on the flies,
Entombed, about to die
And our return will
Find them in the sill
Curled legs,on their backs,
Fragile still,
Unmounted records of last summer's stay.
I suppose, no better this
Than go away.

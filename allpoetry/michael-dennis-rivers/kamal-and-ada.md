Kamal and Aïda

She loves him not
This homely man
But loves his love of her.

She’d have him not
As God would have
A woman love a man.

She’d rather not
This commoner,
Though death he would prefer.

She uses him,
This merchant’s son,
A chesspiece in her plan.

Aïda feigns
An interest in
This pawn to win her man.

She leads him on,
This lanky lad,
To fan her lover’s flame.

And in the end,
The high-born win
And Kamal goes insane.(With inspiration from Palace of Desires 
by Naguib Mahfouz)

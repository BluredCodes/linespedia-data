---
title: "Lauderdale Sunrise"
writer: "Michael Dennis Rivers"
slug: "lauderdale-sunrise"
source: "AllPoetry"

The sun comes up,
The sky erupts
In pastel shades of peach.

To richest first,
They quench their thirst
In high-rise condo niche.

That swelling light
That’s warm and bright
That further cannot reach.

The rest are blocked
By stucco stock
The condos on the beach.

“How do you dare?
Do you not care?”
The poor the rich beseech.

“Fairness first,
Sunrise we thirst,”
They say, “to each from each.”

Let sunlight through,
Give us our due
They screech and screech and screech.

“But Shadow faire,
That is your share!”
The rich a lesson teach:

“Money counts,
We’ve earned our mounts,
If you would have our reach,

You’d get good jobs,
You lazy slobs,
Not lay about the beach.”

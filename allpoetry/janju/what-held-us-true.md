What Held Us True

Not music in my head
Of songs long dead
That clutches my heart
Makes me hold my breath

Not the sound of your step
Or the way you wept
At a movie's sad ending
And the stub I kept

More than bouquets
Sent on the days
When memory failed
Or when you were away

Only time tells truth
After snatching our youth
What gave us endurance
When others lose

On days trouble entered
Threatened to splinter
We writhed in pain
But still held together

With little affection
We chose a direction
And often it brought
Bitter reactions

Still we danced the dance
And we took the chance
That in pain or joy
Strength would enhance

And now we ponder 
All the roads we wandered
While we worked and lived
And we grew fonder

Not the heady passion
Of this days' fashion
But a velvet love
Lined with compassion

A sort of glue
Now shared by few
One word--commitment
Held us true.

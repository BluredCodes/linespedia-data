OH CHOCOLATE!


Oh Chocolate! Latin lover that you are,

Seducing me from all vitality.

Captured by your smoothness, so small a bean.

Knowing you, I would be wise to run.

A single taste, a moment's warmth I've found,

A mink or sable wrapped around my tongue.

You may take another form or shape,

Disguised in grains, concealed within legumes,

Or brightly colored coats for seasons' play.

You cannot hide.  I sense your presence in the room.

Oh Chocolate!  Though I wrestle to resist,

Partake of your perversion, I insist!

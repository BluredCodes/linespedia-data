I could hear the phone calls, 
"We've just got to nip this in the bud..." 
They wanted to lock me away 
The door slammed above 
"It's for your own good, trust me," 
But they don't know what's for my good. 
Sure I'm starving for 
Their attention a bit too absurd. 
Screaming and running, I tried 
To escape, but silenced too young 
In a cage of the strange 
I was different than those among 
my withering body, they thought I was Funny 
In and out of this place 
Each holiday that passed 
My family stopped speaking grace 
Of my face, I disappeared, excepth 
In their whispers 
But they could not know 
What it felt like to be watched by video camera's 
They did not feel the isolation 
Of knowing the only fresh air would 
Be a field trip to another floor of the hospital 
To give blood or bile 
And my name forgotten, a number, that's all

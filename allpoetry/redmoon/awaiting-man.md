I am not sure if I'm ready 
for tommorow to come.
Polished and bright-eyed i wait for
the morning to sum-
mond me.
I await like a child lost,
hoping his mother is still searching; yet,
I've known of my mother's failure for years now.
Immune from excitement i remain
discontent.
Wishing the moon took over tonight, I
watched with intent.
The sun deserves to die, 'cause oh I
have aqquired my tan.
The stars deserve to suffice to point me to
my a-waiting man.
The softness of the wind apparent, creating my cringe.
If only the flowers fell and petals
made into the fringe.
This world to be less harsh than the last,
the reds taking on the 
blues.

ition, there's never
a place, where i can fill the whole
space, I'm simply drawn into your mystical world, I
could never expect this 
longing to be witheld in your
sweet embrace of my

title: "Before The Masses"

With fickle zest we raise them
Like a scythe before the wheat,
And crown with prickly diadem,
Ivy tossed before their feet.
Give urns of tart libation,
And pedestals of concrete.
No matter what their station,
Like lemmings or widow's mate,
They pine for their rotation
To be a part of history
And burn with wanton glory;
    For even those we strip of fame
    Will be remembered just the same.

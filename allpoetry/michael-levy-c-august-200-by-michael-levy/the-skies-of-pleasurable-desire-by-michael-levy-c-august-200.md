The Skies of Pleasurable Desire by Michael levy c August 2001There’s a heavy silence this breeze-less early morn,A contrast to the chaos, the day cities were born.Not a breath of air, the stillness of each leaf,The splendorous picturesque landscape, beauty beyond belief.The solitude is perforated by a happy mockingbirds song,A precocious solo to the chorus that will surely join along,And sure enough, the air  becomes magically transfuseVia the ambiance of a choir with technicolor coordinated  views.And now the scene is attuned for the Sun to take it’s cue,Conducted by a timekeeper, united to earths milieu,The horizon suddenly awakens in a blaze of sugared crimson fire,Flames of warmth radiate across the skies of pleasurable desire.M.

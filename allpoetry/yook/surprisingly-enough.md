---
title: "Surprisingly Enough"
writer: "Yook"
slug: "surprisingly-enough"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/48085-Surprisingly-Enough-by-Yook"
---

Surprisingly enough, I've forgotten
how to miss you,
which only makes me sadder
than the way I felt before.
Your voice, your scent, your siren's song
have fallen down the holes I've
burned into my memory
while
having a good time.
Maybe it's a good thing that
obsession's gone away,
but knowing that I knew you
was a comfort.
Not to say I still don't wonder,
ponder, question where you've gone,
or spend a lot of restless hours
trying to sleep though
your memory.
It's just that urgency and need have
melted into faint nostalgia
and quaint, quiet grief.
I'm hollow now, and only echoes seem
to reach me from the hole you
left inside.
Somehow, I don't think you'd care.
Which, in a way,
I'm happy for.
One of us should be normal, in the end.
I wish I could replace you, or
at least your space inside, but I
don't think I remember
what to do. So I
rummage through my baggage,
and I sit in lonesome splendor
and I fantasize of women much like
you. Looking nothing like your
faded pictures or
your quiet whispered voice, only
fitting somewhat
like you used to fit.
I was spoiled by your interest,
never knew why you showed up, so,
in my ignorance of the dance steps
I'm left sitting by the door.
I don't care, you know, about
my worries, I'm afraid I'm
used to being lonely,
scared of what I'd find, out on
the dance floor.
Did you see the silence in me?
Was that why you said goodbye?
Or was it
something else and
nothing to do with me?
I've forgotten many things
now, and I'll forget even more, 
so ignore my rambling nature,
pretend you never saw me,
and go merry on your way.This is old, from 1996. The imagery is that of a young and fevered man, and unsubtle. My apologies.

Part V: The Entrance



[The foolish oft tread where angels fear to go….alas, I fear I am one of those…]

The door is open; I fear I must go in,
For there is nothing but woe behind me.
So I step forward, look into the dim,
Hoping for a hint of my destiny.

The threshold I cross, fearing no loss,
But, still, tis so dim, no feature I see.
Two steps more, I’ll see even more,
And, WHOOM! 	The door closes behind me!

No turning back now, the door is closed,
But the closing comes with a surprise.
For, what once was dark, is now exposed,
This will not be easy, I realize.

Though now I can see what confronts me,
It is bigger than I had imagined.
It’s the inside of a mountain, you see,
Spacious, gracious, and bright light imaged.

I take in the grandeur of it all,
In the distance, I see a shelf on high.
Someone dancing, having quite a ball,
“Bzarradon!” I think, watch him spin by.

So, now I know, where it is I must go,
I must reach that shelf near the ceiling.
I look at the floor, a way to go bro,
It’s the entrance, a maze not appealing.

The walls are made of the same black stone,
“One way in, no way out”,  or so I think.
The way into the maze, is brightly shone,
Through the opening I walk, or maybe slink.

Yes, here I am, bathed in light, black walls surround,
Facing a fountain, water flows aplenty.
Drink the fountain, then go right or around,
For once, a door didn’t close behind me.

So which way to go, the right or the left,
I have no way of knowing, or care.
A golf tee I use, I found on a cleft,
Flip it in the air, it will point where.

The tee points right, so go there I might,
Into the core and the heart of the maze.
Hunting my foe, must get him in my sight,
Blow him away for the rest of my days!

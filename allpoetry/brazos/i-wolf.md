I, WOLF

AaWooawWoooooooAaaaWooooooooAooooooooooOooOooooooo…..

I, Wolf, stand alone on a knoll in a clearing by a stark, lonely tree;
I swing my head side to side, slowly, looking everywhere, anywhere.
My breath plumes from my mouth in wispy white clouds, rising, free;
My paws plunge down in three inches of snow, but the cold I can bear.
I’m hungry; the cold means nothing when you haven’t eaten for days;
Though the wind slants little flakes of ice and snow against my face;
Giving me the look of a graybeard, frozen flakes and saliva displays.
But, I must keep the vigil, my empty stomach freezes me in place.
There’s a hare there, I know, I can smell her, when the wind is right;
She’s probably hungry too, and needs come out and search for food.
I need her to take a chance, come out, for I am very hungry tonight;
For I am lean, my bones show through; a rabbit would be very good.
 
AaWooawWoooooooAaaaWooooooooAooooooooooOooOooooooo…..

But wait, what’s that, is something stirring in the edge of the wood?
I quiet my body and stand still, a wolf temporarily frozen in time;
I  hold my breath, no more white plumes; to detect me is not good.
For I see, this must be my hare, edging out of the forest sublime.
The wind is in my favor, she cannot scent me, though I can her;
She’s still too close to the woods, I must wait, I must wait longer.
Too many hiding places in the forest, and she knows just where
To go, and I can’t get in there; too big, even though I’m stronger.
So I stand my place, and wait for my chance, for I might have a shot;
A bush with berries in the middle of the clearing, go for it bunny!
O precious joy! She’s making her way out, about me knowing not;
She’s at the bush, time for action, for being hungry is just not funny!

AaWooawWoooooooAaaaWooooooooAooooooooooOooOooooooo…..

And, land squarely on my prey…..

AaWooawWoooooooAaaaWooooooooAooooooooooOooOooooooo…..

Quickly, my mouth finds her soft neck and I tighten my jaws;
Hot, syrupy blood gushes down my throat, O bliss!
She’s dead now, I start ripping away, holding her with my paws;
First her belly, with its soft insides, nothing there to miss!
And then the rest of her, muscles, tendons, even the head;
Fur and bones, crunch everything, who knows when I’ll eat again!
Now she’s gone, but the snow is red, so I lap it up instead;
It’s not meat, but I need all I can get, my strength to regain!
It’s over now, I’m finished, and for once I have a full tummy;
Morning draws near, I think I’ll pad over to that warm boulder;
And lay myself down to sleep, on the side where it will be sunny.
I am refreshed now, my life goes on, I live to be one day older!

And, I, Wolf, will be looking for you the next time I get hungry!

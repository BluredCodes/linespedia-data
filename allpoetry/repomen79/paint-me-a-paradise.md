Paint me a paradise, that beauty dwell,
Within this heart where darkness fell.
so very very long ago.
Speak poetry, so I might know,
That in the end dark won't remain.
That love and only love will reign.
you bare your loving soul in rhyme,
Taking me from space and time.
To places far that truley are,
As brilliant as a shinning star.
Oh gentle spotless dove of white,
My heart soars with your love this night.
In this glad heart such does suffice,
To be a taste of paradise.

Paint me a paradise that I might see.
The angel in the poetry.
The tender words that say so much,
Like far off hands that somehow touch,
The heart where all of hope abide,
The wanting soul I cannot hide.
No greater love I've ever known.
My love I send to you my own.
Bloom full, my gentle garden flower,
All the poets passions power,
Bends to send you happy word,
Of the love you have secured.
And he'd be glad to tell you twice.

Poetry was clear as a summer day

in November
when there had been no rain
for twenty days
& the secret thoughts the word had
ran over all things
without shame
& it was one of those days 
when it becomes easy to sail
many thousand of miles away
from the page
but toward poetry
or toward one who has a habit
of falling in love with words
& falling out fast,too,
because the word
is not made to believe the reality
of another's experiences.

Stalking the unknown self,
approaching Fishing Creek,
the word became woman,
a totality best known
by a poet
or a hero,
& all the mysterious things
a woman wants not to give to people
like being taught never to be satisfied
or to guard secret places
or to draw life in
or to pretend to let things happen
while actually making them happen
the way she wants them to--
such was the day.
The word, becoming a woman,
neither speeding ahead
nor jumping backward
& missing what's happening
right in front of her--
saw everything.

On a November day,
clear as a summer morning,
the woman saw a fog bank.
The beauty of it overcame her.
Erogenous as brain, toes, belly were 
the grey cliffs ahead not yet engulfed.
With eyes accustomed to observing,
she chose
instead,
to caress that moment
free from all watchers.

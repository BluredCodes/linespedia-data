It is a poem

Tho I write the words,
I can take no more 
credit
for a poem 
than I can make the wind
go round & round
in a storm

I can not hold beauty
in form
nor make my words
into amethyst fingerprints
that show up on a woman's body
when she stands naked
before her own reflection

I can not make a thirst
for the enchantment
that is poetry
into a young man
thirsting for her skin
beneath his fingers
never finding enough to quench passion

These words
can not reveal
a woman's secret history
nor her desires
nor place her heart
on the line
for all to examine

This is a poem 
for winter
that goes over bare mts--
they rise in majesty.
In words,
the poet walks ever the balance
of artistry.

Words to my heart

I thought
I could write
at sixteen
after reading
Whitman
& Dickinson
& some sensual
woman poet
whose name I have long
forgotten--quite.


I thought 
I could write
words to my heart as lean
as e e cummings wrote, words leading
me after his following, this man,
these women, into the inner dimension
of my soul. As is, however, usual
I was the last to know: it
was not my heart that is the song
I would come to write.

When I undressed my first word,
the moon was small
& passing the island of Poros,
getting out of the way of sunrise.

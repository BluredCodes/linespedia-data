title: "She walks in sunlight"

She walks in sunlight

toward the savage sea,
the girl,
a siren of 20 years

she waits upon the
page, to dance--to twirl
& lures poets to tears

perhaps toward the words
of her own verse
& in a voice more mist

than a wave is wet--words
more less of an ancient curse
than sea of sun--kissed...

She walks in sunlight
deeper than the sea
& calmer than all wrong

& on the beach she writes,
dragging toes thru sand, the
song, that luring siren's song.

It lingers but a moment
& then is gone
swept clean in tide's receding.

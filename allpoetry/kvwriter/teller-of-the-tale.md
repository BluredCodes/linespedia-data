Teller of the Tale 
by 
Kelly Varner Johnson 

Her creative blood did flow through the arteries of her mind; 
The hero must be a valiant sort; the villain, most unkind. 

The computer screen beckoned; smoke from a keyboard burning, 
As she pounded the story into life and left her readers yearning. 

Her plot must wield a two-edged sword, conflict slicing strong, 
Be true to life with plenty of strife where everything goes wrong. 

The sequel gives her readers rest, allows them time for slack, 
Until the scene opens to announce, "The villain's comin' back!" 

Her characters live and breathe; they laugh and cry and fight. 
Some will survive the desert sun, yet fear the dark of night. 

The book poses a mystery, where the hero's undercover. 
Or a romance kisses each page, in turn, as the hero takes a lover. 

A western gallops across the prose, a posse on the go, 
Or a knight in shining armor will face his deadliest of foe. 

A fantasy rears its head with wizards, warlocks and such. 
Yet, an inspirational should breathe forth a book with a spiritual touch. 

A troubling vein must permeate this highly robust tale, 
To not just quake, but rattle and break, reading ten on the Richter scale.

Flight

I chanced upon a pair of wings,
They came not from a bird.
T'was not a sparrow gifted me;
There was no song I heard.

A plane flew by me overhead
Into the space of air.
T'was not a plane that gifted me;
Steel wings I could not wear.

Where did these wings of Flight come from,
That bid my every deed?
I did not care or fear that day;
I did not want or need.

In my mind these wings were formed.
They carried me through the night.
My imagination gifted me
With wings that gave me Flight!

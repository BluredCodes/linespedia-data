---
title: "A Lonely Girl"

If you look into her eyes you'll see that it is there
That mysterious sadness that seems too much to bear
She covers it well, held deep down inside
But there are moments you'll see it through all her pride

If you see her you'll think its all fun and games
You'll never actually know how she's been burnt by the flames
She seems so outgoing, and so limitlessly carefree
Yet the pain is there, though vertually impossible to see

A past life of torture, she keeps locked away
All wounds tend to scar, and forever seem to stay
She lives with broken promises, lies of those who were there
And heartache and pain of those who pretended to care

She's been used, traumatically abused, but you would never tell
She carries herself forward, in grace, so very well
She craves the love she dreams of in her fantasy
I know so much about her, that lonely girl is me... 

(Bruce, this poem is for you! I will overcome what you have put me through! I know it will be hard, but I have my little angel, my baby and neither you, nor my past will ever be able to break me again)

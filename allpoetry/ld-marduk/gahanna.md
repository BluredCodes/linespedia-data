I find I am condemned,exiled unto a place,a wasteland of the heart and soul,where love hath no estate—Where passion, and desire,are feelings that are damned,cast into a darkened pit,for thou…dist not…understand—That such were held in awe of thee,seeking, merely, but to be…given freely, in return,for they did hunger and did yearn—To gather thee, as one would flowers,and to while a way the hours,for ‘twas thought…’twas destiny,but alas thou dist not understand,nor could fathom…comprehend,what I dist seek of thee—Thus I find, I am condemned,exiled…unto a place,a wasteland of the heart and soul,where there is n’er a trace,of the passion and desire,And where love…hath no estate.

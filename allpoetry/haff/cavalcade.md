---
title: "Cavalcade"
writer: "Haff"
slug: "cavalcade"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/34649-Cavalcade-by-Haff"
---

Marching on thro' deathless ages
Prophets, Teachers, Fools, and Sages
Men who lost thier lives in glory
Writing on thier death-bed gory
Page on page of heros story.
These are they who go before us.
These are they who were victorious.
What are we but those who follow?

Marching on through deathless ages
Still they come the fools and sages
Hark the sound of bugles blowing
See the tide of crimson flowing
Rank on rank an army going.
Kings upon thier tottering thrones
Starving people, threatening tones.
This the fate of those who follow?

"Come my people, I am waiting"
(In the din of wars abating
Came a voice of gentle yearning)
"Oh, my people, are ye learning
Sad the wages of sins earning?
Ye are weary, cease this hating
See my Churches, Empty, Waiting
Peace I give to all who follow."

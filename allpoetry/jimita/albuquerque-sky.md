Lightning crashes in the Albuquerque sky 

each drop a silver arrow

piercing the dust

and dissolving the hills



within seconds

the current rages

canons boom louder and nearer

and shock waves slam into the ground



with my eyes closed

I feel the lightning 

before the flash and the reverberation

echoing through space

a glimpse of divine power

illuminating the sundered sky





but the storm blows away in the wind

fading to mere flashes behind the mountain

the flood cascades down the arroyo

washing away my painAlbuquerque is a schizo kind of place. At first, it's just sand and sage, boiling hot in summer and bitter cold in the winter. After a while, though, you notice the play of light across the sky, the power of the weather. You get to understand how it became known as Tierra Encantada.



Dedicated to Rob Becher.

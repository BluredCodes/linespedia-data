Thirty-six hours ago
 I was in your arms.
 Safe, secure, warm,
 And all that nonsense. 
 It was dark outside then,
 Five am or so.
 Time passed faster
 Than men of science could understand.
 We were surrounded with plesant smells,
 Colourful images,
 Wonderous delights.
 The smell that plagues me had vanished
 Replaced by your pheromones.
 All was well.

 The sun rose.

 Thirty-three hours ago
 You were accross the room.
 Scared, angry, cold,
 And all that rationality.
 It was light outside now.
 Time began to slow.
 We were surrounded by empty beer bottles,
 Pizza boxes,
 And seemingly dead bodies.
 The stench returned.
 All was chaotic.

 The sun set.

 Twenty-one hours ago
 I was alone again.
 Depressed, afraid, nostalgic,
 And all that emotion.
 It was dark outside again
 And time was stopped forever.
 I was surrounded by four empty walls,
 A photograph,
 And thoughts of you.
 Your smell still surrounded me;
 I hope it never goes away. 
 All was gone.

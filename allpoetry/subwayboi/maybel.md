Her words are often very harsh, 
 She lacks a certain grace.
 You notice giant things on her,
 Like the mole upon her face.
 She acts all cold and laughs a lot,
 At other people's flaws.
 You wouldn't want to find yourself,
 Between her sharpened jaws.
 She dresses well and talks a lot,
 In fact she rarely stops.
 She speaks to me as though she'd like
 To whack me with a box.
 If you just met this pretty lass,
 You'd think she's awful mean.
 If you had known her longer still,
 She'd be a Drama Queen.
 But I don't hate this girl I know,
 Like doctors hate the flu.
 In fact I think she's pretty cool,
 And I know she loooooves me too. 
 So let's make peace, oh Maybel dear,
 Let's put the past aside.
 I don't just say this 'cause you know,
 About a certain guy.
 I think we'd be the best of friends,
 We'd walk along the sand.
 We'd fall in love and kiss madly,
 Then walk off hand in hand.
 Actually, forget that part,
 I don't think it would work.
 'Cause you think I'm a stupid whore,
 And I know you're a jerk.A lot of people have commented to me that my poems rhyme too often, and that the rhymes are predictable. To them I say that I make my poems rhyme when I want them to be fun, and if they have a problem with that, they can stick a wooden spoon up their anus. Enjoy!

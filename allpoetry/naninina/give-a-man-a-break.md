I am a man, breathing the same air as yours

I am delivered to the World, by a mother who's now my child
She dresses in black, thinks she's a nun
My bitter heart shrinks when she says I am her son Jesus
I take her to the doctor, I'm not sure she understands her sickness

I am a man, my heart is not a stone
I care for this woman, a best friend and a lover
Sweet and beautiful, she is the Queen of Jealousy
One forgotten phone call and she puts on a grueling drama 

I am a man, surrounded by hard bricks of walls 

I am determined to cherish two siblings who have become
my dependents 
They are caring but always running 
for my guidance and approval
One unwatched step and they fall apart

I am a man, I cry aloud yet no one could hear 

I am a man, fortunate to embrace true friends who have
grown to be obligations
One forgotten birthday and I am at fault


Call them insensitive! Cruel and unfeeling! I am not them 
I am a man with a heart as tender and as soft as a woman's 

I am a man, I am not perfectInspiration comes from "A Man" (Alanis Morissette)

---
title: "A simple Tree"
writer: "Dragonpoet"

A simple tree I may be
With limbs stretched wide and far,
But here I stay, perched upon my hill,
Watching God's children hither and dither,
Enjoying my simple gifts.

A simple tree I may be
Made of oak through and through
With leaf's of green or gold in fall,
But here I stay, perched upon my hill,
Watching the seasons come and go.

A simple tree I may be
With rings foretelling time
But here I stay, perched upon my hill,
Remembering all that has been,
And all that will be.

A simple tree I may be
With my face smooth or rough,
But here I stay, perched upon my hill,
Recording all I see and hear,
Never uttering a word.

A simple tree I may be
With no voice to whisper my tales,
But here I stay, perched upon my hill,
Watching all of God's children,
Knowing I am who I am.

Always there and strong.
We would climb into her branches.
Read, play, hang. She was a friend.
He and I would sit and mock the younger ones,
Too small to climb.
Maybe an escape. To not feel alone.
Hang from the low, and climb
Higher. 
The leaves were shelter.
I watch those walking by, I wonder.
Do they see?
A girl sitting in a tree, 
Watching you.
Watching me.
But not alone.

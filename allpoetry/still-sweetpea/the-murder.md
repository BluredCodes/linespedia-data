---
title: "The Murder"
writer: "Still Sweetpea"
slug: "the-murder"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/14293-The-Murder-by-Still-Sweetpea"
---

I've thought long and hard about this, 
deciding it has to be done 
Not only do you have to die, 
but I have to be the one 

I watch you from the window 
as you lay and watch T.V. 
I see you 
but you're oblivious to me 

Slowly I creep around, 
finding my way into the house, 
Making sure you don't hear me, 
quiet as a mouse 

I walk into the room, 
your expression freezes with the look of surprise, 
as you realize, 
you're about to meet your demise 

I stare into your cold eyes 
and from my pocket I slowly draw a gun, 
I want to ensure you know your killer 
I want you to know I'm the one 

I'm the one who is going to inflict 
suffering and pain upon you 
I'm going to make you pay, 
for the terrible things you do 

Never again will I allow you 
to make my mama cry 
Never again will you hurt her, 
not after you die 

I raise the gun up, 
point blank, at eye level, 
You deserve everything you get 
I swear you're the devil 

I wrap my finger around the trigger 
and begin to squeeze; 
I fire, you're hit between the eyes 
there, I've done the deed 

You fall back,slumped on the floor, 
blood gushes from your head; 
You suffer only a few moments 
before you are dead 

Your last words are terrible, 
as everything you've ever said before 
You tell me I was, and always will be 
nothing but a whore 

I stand there watching, 
as you bleed all over the floor 
I can finally rest now, 
you cant hurt anyone, not any more 

I have no regrets 
and actually I'm proud, 
I've taken you out of MY misery 
just as I vowed 

You'll never again hurt anyone 
especially my mother, 
I've unsured that, 
with one simple murder

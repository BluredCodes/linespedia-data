Lingerie Shopping

Creeping thru the lacy jungle,
Pungent smell of beauty creams,
Feels like eyes across the room,
Head tucked low to hide from beams.

Slits and hoops, hang real close,
Red on face, but cant reveal,
Laughter deep inside my chest,
Women call these things a steal.

Prices that boggle the minds of men,
Books to show there sex and flair, 
Sequences dot the edge of one,
Appears to be, but threads and air.

Stuff the black one in my coat,
Race to counter, cash in hand,
Place the gift inside my bag,
Lingerie shopping is not for man.

Tho adventure has its blessings,
She slips it on as nighttime falls,
My eyes pop out, and body hardens,
I think next week, I'll search the mall.

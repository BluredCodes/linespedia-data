Sailing Free Air (Poem Verse)Sails puff west,thrusting with eager force,as the eastern wind,blows-up his gray nostrils;he glides his wife across the salted plainsin search of escaping thoughts;away from confined societies,that live and work,within clean, boxed edges.Spitting splashes,wash away his past stains,frees his mind of spent thoughts.His gain; to begin fresh in soul,focusing on the, plain;feasting pure joy,without distractionsof naked itemsto dragtrivial infractions into view.Only eventual momentum,can pull his thoughts,back towardthe direction of consciousness.Forth; toward soil once more;to anchor in the harbor of experiences,where, mingling mindscreate crafts to live by;back to,the box of life's intrusions.© J Fossier 1/01

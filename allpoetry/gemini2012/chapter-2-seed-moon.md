I could hear the rain,
Hit the tin roof of my shead,
I didn't feel to sleepy,
And I couldn't go to bed,
Grab my dark black rain coat,
And put on so tight,
And then I kissed my baby brother,
A far well and good night,
I step out to the weather,
The lock out the way,
I walked unto the stones,
That I normally play,
I walked into the forest,
I walked near our tree,
And then I saw the river,
Which weas so clear to me,
To night the waters brewed,
Fasts under the wind,
And So I walked across the brigde,
It twirled and was to bind,
I walked unto the land,
On the other side of the brigde,
I crept into the fields,
To which i work each day,
I walked past the buldings,
Then what I saw amazed me,
I felt a shadow past me,
And I saw a small slow smile,
And when I saw his handsome face,
I wished to stay a while,
I couldn't even hear,
My own self think,
But soon after I hurried,
My self home,
So that I could blink

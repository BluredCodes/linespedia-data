Tonight was the Dyad Moon,
My sister said I was acting loony,
There was no man at every Moon,
That I should stay for her tune,
I crept out that night to see his face,
Hoping to be in his embrace,
I ran through the same old path,
And walked back up to his side,
This time I hope he might just talk,
Like he's never done before,
But this time his face was serious,
And very intense,
I looked into his beautiful blue eyes,
And started croning lullibies,
He looked up into my face,
Unhealing here in time and space,
I looked up to the moon,
Singing as I usely do,
He slowly smiled a how do you do,
I stoped and looked wildly at him,
What did you say,
I shouted at him,
Don't tell me if I"m wrong,
He replied with a soft sweet smile,
I couldn't be mad at him,
Not even for a while,
I just sighed at my moon man,
And said with a smile,
What is your name?
He smiled Kyle,
I smiled a wide soft smile,
And didn't care if I were a child,
I rested my head upon his shoulder,
chanting until the next moon my dear.

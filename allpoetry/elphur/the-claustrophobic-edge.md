---
title: "The Claustrophobic Edge"
writer: "elphur"
slug: "the-claustrophobic-edge"
source: "AllPoetry"

I
I see pale blue little fingers outstretched 
to wrap around 
the candleshaft of my only desire, 
with twitch in the palm 
and grip in the blood.  

II
The flame leaves the wick 
by my next breath, 
a current carrying the last of my hope 
and frosted in a wavering disposition
of what is to come.   

III
A child’s pallid face stood watching 
as my feet drowned in 
the mirage I encountered yestereve 
of an ocean slowly ascending 
to the air above.

IV
In my veins rushed 
anxiety and suspicion and the 
world sped close to the body 
within my skin, prickling with 
the fear of wide eyes and strained nerves.

V
I’ll confess the weakness 
that through midnight dreams 
fogs the glass of a force I contain, and 
hope that the tide goes down 
for now it is to my shivering knees.

VI
Salts of my tears take refuge 
in the buds of my tongue, diving 
to join the waves below 
in which I cannot stand with pride 
of mine eyes and what they have seen.

VII
A pressure on my thigh
of small hands I cannot have for mine own
brings the suffocating need 
to reach down 
and peel the flesh away.

VIII
Mine own hand trembles against 
a delayed body of simple purity  
in attempt to request an air of comfort, 
a new morning to shine,
or any forgiveness at all. 

IX
To my shoulders hath the ocean risen, 
and in my ears a song of drowned souls 
rumbles until it fills 
the nakedness I observe of myself, 
escaping through the pores. 

X
With eyes to a borderless sky, 
it’s my youth I watch 
before the water of my pain melts 
conception and blare into serenity 
for all to find afterward.

XI
Release as an error only found 
in reach of my impractical and petty salvation, 
climbing my throat like the 
sounds I can’t control, resounding 
until they reach a forever.

XII
In loyal dischord, 
the infant mouth of my reason places 
a kiss on my feverish brow, 
and with it mine eyes fall closed 
until they become the rising sea.Fall 2001,

The miracle of life
    and its peculiarities
       can wake one
         in the middle of the night.
Like a dream 
     freezing itself solid,
       its magnificence, its mass,
         crystallizing, day by day,
           year by year,
     can thaw as sudden
       as a summer storm.


Yet the majesty remains
     to kindle 
         like the mystery of flame,
       to leap gelatinous heat,
         consume the air
           we breathe,
to conceive absurdities
     of histories and Hell.

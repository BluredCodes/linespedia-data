---
title: "Ice Dreams"
writer: "butch"
slug: "ice-dreams"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/36571-Ice-Dreams-by-butch"
---

The tears are frozen to my cheek, my eyes wide open
against this anonymous tree in these last moments
posed in helpless abandonment, worn out
the cold, laughing at me in silent amusement;

Down the ridge, the light shines from your window
illuminating the rest of this frigid hell
beckoning me, mocking me in ironic twists
ice dreams of your smile, and soft lips;

This heart beats slower now, playing out
it is filled with you, wrapped around you
each beats reminds me that you are so close
each breath heavy with frost and regret;

I meet the face of Death and his icy stare
he waits for me, patience his only acknowledgement
maybe he was hiding, just waiting for me
knowing my time was up, he quietly hid;

And, now with minutes left of this frosty reality
I dream of you, and wonder what you will say
will your life be different, changed by my end
will you ever be happy again, tears stalking you;

Ice dreams are all that is left, I now must leave
I took a walk, on snowy forest trails
bringing you my love, and something to bind us
my last glimpse of this world is this ring I bear......

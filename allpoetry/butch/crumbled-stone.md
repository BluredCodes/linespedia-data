---
title: "Crumbled Stone"
writer: "butch"
slug: "crumbled-stone"
source: "AllPoetry"

I took a walk
amongst the markers
to visit my kinsman
in his resting place.
the stones neatly laid
greensward trimmed
flowers softly placed
monuments of remembrance
shining out to me
as if to say
"I was here before
do you remember?"
I looked upon them
this son, this wife
this daughter, this man
and, I knew love
for that is a legacy
of life and time.
but, as I ambled
and reminisced
I came to a section
with no care.
the cairns fallen
the grass unmown
the names rubbed
gone from view.
forgotten lives
memories left behind
dust in time
forever overlooked
adoration in the past.
I wondered who lay there
had they fortune
or, misfortune.
then I knew
that we are only
what we give.
love is our heritage
our time is precious.
so, spread your love
give it freely
and, time 
will carry your name
forever.........

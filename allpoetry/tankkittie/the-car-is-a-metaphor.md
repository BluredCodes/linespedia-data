---
title: "the car is a metaphor."
writer: "tank.kittie"
slug: "the-car-is-a-metaphor"
source: "AllPoetry"

someone once raised a hand to me
and told me that girls like big hands, 
strong and masculine
by definition
i guess he was
and had strong and masculine ways 
of proving this;

he could pick me up with ease,
drink more than the 'boys',
and yell louder when drunk
he could push me-
'fragile woman',
the polite word for kitchen appliance-
across the room effortlessly
and in the climax of the night
he could prove his manhood.

and afterwards 
he could be sensitive
because even strong and masculine
has a soft side.
he could spoon me
with a fist in my stomach
other hand around my neck,
and whisper the sweetest reminders
that he could kill me at any time
and to think
i actually thought
he never gave me anything
but he could be sensitive
for me.
his skin would crawl against me,
the weight of his body
aching through my muscles,
hands eventually fumbling for more

and i
returning to the bathroom,
to check for blood again,
avoiding my glare in the mirror
because my bruises
looked too much like war paint
and his hands left their prints on my body

he also told me
his car had been broken into once-
exterior stripped, glass shattered
door forced open,
interior torn apart-
so that someone could take a joy ride
he said
he felt violated,
like someone had stolen
more than his car
but a part of him as well.

i nodded-
fearing no response would inspire his big hands,
the things girls love-
and i told myself
he didn't know what he did to me
even though i knew he did
he bragged about it to the 'boys'

"men", he said, "always call us men."

'defiantly an eight...no no nine maybe. yeah...a nine. she is so hot.' i could hear the mummers of some guys behind me as they rated girls looks at the long, soiled lunch tables in the cafeteria. some of the girls i knew, some i didn't. they gave my friends megan, lisanne and danielle all eights. lindsay, stephanie and kelly received nines. some girls got fours and threes only beacuse they were a bit overweight or didn't appeal to their tastes. their superficial tastes that is.

these were the guys girls in grade nine admired from a far. they wished one day they would look at them in -that way- and talked to them as if they were the only ones there. of course, if your hair wasn't blond, your tits weren't as big as your head and you weren't easy, you stood no chance. naturally, none of these girls could see that, and for that, I pitied them. deeply.

i listened further to them talk about girls as lunch went on. they talked about one of my friends, megan, and how she was pretty, but wouldn't give head so she was a bitch. then they moved on to lisanne, of course they all agreed she was pretty and she -would- give head at the right time, so she was 'hot'. 

back to rating girls. 'jackie would be an eight point five', said one of them. 'yeah man, she's hot' they rest grunted. then my name came up. i tried my best to cover my face if there was the slight chance of them looking back at me. 'a six. she's not that pretty. or skinny. just a six.'

and so that was that. i got up and went in the direction of my next class. not a person, not as a girl, as a number. as a six. not that pretty, not that skinny. not anything more than a six...

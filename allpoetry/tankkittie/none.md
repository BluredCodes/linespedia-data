untitled.

she once gave him her soul, 
written down on a tattered piece of wallpaper.
he took it and left it at the bus stop one rainy day.
it stuck to the ground and was walked upon by busy feet and quick stomps.

she once gave him her heart,
cut out from shiny, red wrapping paper.
he took it and tore it as he stuffed it in his pocket.
she cried and he told her to make him a new one,
no realizing that once a boy breaks your heart,
you can never give it to him again.

she once gave him her dreams,
contained in a yellow balloon held together by blue string.
he took it and let go of the string on a windy day by the lake.
she jumped after it.
but he only held her back.

she once tried to give him her respect,
but soon realized he did not deserve it.
she tried to give her love and trust to him before,
along with her dreams, her soul, and her heart.
pieces of the respect she had for him have been left behind,
and flown to places unknown.

'will i ever see you again?' he asked.

she looked into him and knew he didn't deserve to.

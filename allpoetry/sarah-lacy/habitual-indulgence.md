Habitual Indulgence

This morning I woke to a closet
full of wrinkled attire and the
smell of black coffee
brewing from Nanny's addiction.
The absence of authority seemed
to make the sun shine
a little brighter, hiding the 
artificial light in its
glass dwelling. As I turned on 
the iron I was met by an
unfamiliar, yet almost
comforting sight. So many years 
we begged for her to stop,
but there it sat...
Nanny's pink lighter.
Now it seems to me
a symbol of liberation,
a stand against misguided
doctors and freedom from the
disheartening chains of 
adultery. As she raises 
that cancerous stick to her 
delicate lips all I can
do is smile and know that
my Nanny is truly content.

~Sarah Lacy~

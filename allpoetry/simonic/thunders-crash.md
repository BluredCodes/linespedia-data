---
title: "Thunder's Crash"
writer: "Simonic"
slug: "thunders-crash"
source: "AllPoetry"

The night is dark
The clouds are low
Yet, are high
This world is dark

To the east
The wind blows
To the north
The wind howls
To the south
The wind screams
To the west
The wind roars

All winds
Hit me
With tremendous force
Yet, I stand
Stand against it all
Oh, how longer may I stand?
Stand against the torrent of winds
Yet, to fall
It would sweep me up

Away from my point
The point where I stand
The point, I live
It all hits me
The winds never cease
What am I to do?
The clouds swirl 
In confusion
I am confused

They gleam with that eerie glow
The glow of the mad
What is mad?
Who is mad?
My shell is stripped
The shell no longer matters
Yet, I stand
Stand against the winds
Knowing there is no gain
Upon the flesh

Oh, Storm!
How much longer will you last?
I am losing my footing
Swaying in confusion 
Blinded by everything
The wind drys my eyes
I am blind
My muscles are wore
Yet, somehow I stand

Images hit my blind eyes
I see them
All of them
They are falling
Against this great wind
Am I to remain
Or fall as they?
Why have I not fallen?
I no longer feel my feet
Are they even there?
How am I to know
I can not see
Nor can I feel any longer

What is that sound?
The sound that’s heard
Over the winds?
It’s the sound of thunder
Rolling in from all sides
The sound is deafening
I think I cover my ears
Yet, I continue to hear
Hear it’s violet crashes
Terror fills my numb shell
It approaches

The Thunder
It strikes
No longer do I ache
The wind has stopped
The cries and howls
Gone
The loud noises
Gone

I can see and feel
I can rest
Now, my legs give way
And I fall upon these
Apparently golden stones
Feeling their warm and cool touch
Sending me into a rest 
A rest of bliss
I survived the storm
Now, I may rest

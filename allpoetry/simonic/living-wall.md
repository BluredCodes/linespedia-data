Behind the Wall
There lies he
He, who made it
Who is not before it

The Wall is still
Peaceful and unmoving
He, behind
Cries

The Wall cries not
For it is not correct
He, shrinks in the corner
Intimidated

The Wall fights
Defending the builder
He, screams at the pain
Pain brought upon him

The Wall holds no features
Only a smooth cold surface
He, behind burns with pain
Heart and mind shattering pain

The Wall is the one
One behind the wall
He, behind has now become
The living WallThis poem was written in the light of how we often put up a mental 'wall' for others to face. It is not our true selves, but how we think we should be.  And behind that wall, we hurt, wanting to be free, but for 'acceptance' we stay behind the wall until it consumes us and our old way of thought is gone.

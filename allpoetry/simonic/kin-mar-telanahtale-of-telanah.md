Ramblings of a Wizard 

Kin Mar Telan’ah
-Tale of Telan’ah-



Quietly I sat, amid a lushly green
Grassy hill, with a soothing creak
Flowing upon my right, gently splashing
Upon the water smoothed stones

The day’s sun was on it’s last breaths,
Slowly falling down behind sight, casting
That gorgeous ray of pinks and purples
Before meeting that darkened blue

As I sat, breathing in the crisp and sweetening
Air, I heard something walking silently behind
In my head, I had thought the worst, for I was
Far off in the great grassy lands of Sandra

So, slowly I turned expecting to see my death
By a silent dagger to my back, yet as my eyes
Met what was before, I literally gasped, for here
Now stood majestical Telan’ah, the icon of gods

Telan’ah was one of the eight unicorns, bred
By the gods for the great men during the 
War of Ictar, the immortal under torment, for
They’re beauty mesmerized even the cruelest

And during the Ictarian War, the unicorns carried
The leaders of men and imbued them with the 
Powerful might of the gods, and even granted them
Some resistance to harm, when upon their shiny backs

When the majestical godly steeds first entered the war,
Ictar in his hunger for power created something far
Horrendous to men, summoning into being the Wyvern,
A lesser dragon, yet almost as deadly, with Teslor's aid

And upon the jet black draconic steeds, rode Ictar’s
Leading generals, giving their assault with fire from above
The heads of the great men of Rinmar, causing brave
Men to scream in terror when they rose into the skies

It was now, when the great generals of men, flew in
Upon their glowing steeds, with a majestically twisted horn
Causing men from both armies to stop and gasp, seeing
The work and beauty of the gods, pass by over head

Yet, the hatred within Ictar shielded his eyes, and
Commanded his generals to bring down the great
Works of the gods with blistering flames and spells
Casting them to the bloodied ground below

Keeping watch, Bela held the day longer, as she too
Watched with keen eyesfrom above, as the sixteen beasts 
Fought in the murky air, as smoke and dust rose from below
As the groups circles around, mid air, eyeing each other

Until Jenin, Lord of Zabos, spurred his majestic steed
Into the fray of thorny black wyverns, and with his 
Glowing blade, sought the thick flesh of the foe’s beast
Catching him upon the right thigh, before a cry arose

From within his steed’s throat, as a sharp claw slashed It’s
Side, spewing out silver blood, falling to the ground below 
Slowly bringing Jenin down with it, where he quickly met his 
Death to the dark creatures below, under Ictar’s command

And so the battle in the air continued, with the great
Generals of men defeating four of the beasts created
By Ictar, with the Death’s Hand aid, only to have all
But two of the great unicorns still amid the air, gasping

It was now, when I noticed a change, in both the air
And in the men upon the ground, a presence was about
That all seemed to know and all feared, however I was
Not able to place what it was, so I spun about looking

And to the north, I saw him, draped in robes glimmering
With utter power, robes sewed by Macy, and worn by
Lylirn, shimmering with all the power of the great and
Mighty deathless gods, and in his hand he held a staff

For moments both battles in the air and earth halted
Looking to the new figure who entered the fields of 
Battle, gliding smoothly upon air, holding his silvered
Staff in his right hand, as his glowing red robes, scintillated

His eyes were black, and his hair was silver, down to his
Shoulders, he held no beared, but did not look all too
Young, as his eyes gazed up into the sky above, he saw
The gorgeous works of the gods, flying and breathing hard


And pointing a finger into the sky, at one of the great
Unicorns he called out to them, in a voice which sent
Every ear quaking at it’s great strength and authority
And he said:

“Telan’ah!  Great and majestic steed of the gods, come
Down now and aid me in the conquest of those who 
Stand up against those who bred you!”
Spoke the great wizard who floated amid the air

And from the sky, one of the gorgeous steeds flew to 
The ground and landed upon the bloodied stones there
And Marchuk, King of Rinmar rolled off the great steed
And bowed to the wizard saying:

“Great spell lord, Simonic, man from the lands men have
Not defiled, I grant you the blessing given to me by the 
Gods, so they may aid you in the battle ahead of you,”
And with head cast down, the great king backed away

And I saw, Simonic, glide himself gently upon the back
Of the steed, and as he took to the murky air, he uttered
A word, and the steed glistened with unspeakable light
And beauty, casting aside the filth and smoke of the air

As he rode higher into the sky, meeting up with the rest,
Galen upon his shiny steed let loose a foolish war cry
Presuming his assured victory and charged into the four
Flying black wyverns, only to meet his fiery death

Smoking, Galen and his squealing steed, fell to the earth
Shattering their corpses upon the bloodied rocks of the
Ground, and now the four remaining men and beasts of Ictar
Glared into the ominous eyes of Simonic, as he spurred Telan’ah

From his waist, he drew out a thin silvery blade, that shimmered
As it caught the light of the sky, and brought it across the 
Neck of a beast and continued on into the torso of it’s rider
Sending both, spewing hot blood as they fell to the earth

Fear stricken, the remaining riders of the cruel beasts spat
Crimson red flames at the spell lord, only to have them wash
Harmlessly over an unseen globe, before fate hit their eyes
And they pulled on chain reigns and attempted to flee

But, Simonic, spell lord of men began to weave a spell
That caused his words to sound as thunder to those below
Sending many to their knees, praying to their gods, to not
Bring them death from the massive storm, yet the spell

I knew was to a god, to great Rann, ruler of the winds, and
Before the jet black wyverns a huge being appeared, with
Wispy features akin to the air, and hair that swayed effortlessly
Within the lacking wind, all upon the ground gasped, even I

Rann, the Great Wind, stood now before the beasts,
Glowering at them with hate, before he took his great
Godly hand, and slammed it down over their heads, causing
Fire to flail madly as they spun about till they hit the ground

Adding to the splashing blood upon the ground, unable to be
Soaked up by the hot rocks.  And so it was, Simonic, 
The spell lord, remained upon the great and glorified unicorn 
Telan’ah, till moments before he was to confront Ictar

Sighing now, capturing back my moment within the Ancient
Halls, I remembered seeing that steed near the gentle stream, kneeling
It’s soft and majestic head and taking sips from the stream, while I
Admired the great craftsmanship of the gods, within a living creature

Telan’ah, the last surviving unicorn created by the gods, was
Given to Simonic, as a gift for saving the lands from the powerful 
Immortal wizard, Ictar.  And with it a pact made, that no other
Unicorns will ever grace the skies, for all the beauty

Left within the gods, has failed in the modern times, and
Their only true beauty lies within their cherished creation
The great and gorgeous steeds that Simonic rode in his

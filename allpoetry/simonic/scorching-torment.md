I fell before you
Dropping to my knees
Holding on to your legs
As tears streamed down

Things about me shattered
My life, fallen
I, had fallen
Now at the your feet

Begging for help
Your help
Your life
To be with you again

You spoke
But I did not understand
I did
But didn’t want to

Your hand touched my cheeks
Wiped away tears
Tilted my head to look at you
Searing pain, burns

Your eyes aflame
Your touch like fire
Burning through my skin
My life

How did this come to be
That I fell before you
Begging and crying
Only to have you burn so bad

I wanted to be with you
I loved you
Truly loved
Not falsely

Tried I did
Gave I did
Loved I did
Died I did

Only to be burned
By your scorching touch
Rising pain
Tortured upon my kneesI wrote this referring to the night I emotionally crumpled, and finally realized that I was never going to be with the girl.  A year of being together, then a year being her best friend, then half a year struggling to be back with her.  Love burns.  And if you read many of my poems, I do not use 'death' in the normal sense often.  That was a mix up in an older poem of mine.

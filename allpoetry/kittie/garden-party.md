In the midst of the forest
Beneath all trees
Lives a little leprechaun
With the name of A’aron McGee

At the bottom of the hill
Where the mist is the thinnest
You’ll find him sipping tea
With the Fey Folk in attendance

The faerie wings shimmered a
rainbow
As they danced in the moon light
On the fiddle played by one
Spriggle McDougal

Wee sprites with star burning eyes
Drops of the moonlight they
become
When the night’s nearly
done

Undines with water sparkling laughs
Entertained the gnomes (husbands)
While their nymph children played
In the stream of Split O’plash

The banshees sing
Though not the wailing kind
A song of a tale about The Lady and the Whale

As the celestial Moon Goddess turns to have her rest
The Gods of the Sun leave their mistress’

Seeing the Gods come
The undines return to their Lake
With them their children they take

The sprites vanish
Into the blades of grass they become

Spriggle McDougal takes her fiddle
Goes into her mushroom with her servants
the gnomes
They help to make the sprig feel more
‘at home’

Banshees absorb into the
trees
While they become one
Turning into leaves that gently fall onto the
stream

With the upcoming of the Sun
A’aron McGee’s hosting duties are done

Until to-morrow night
When Kit’e the Pixie (wife) returns
And wants some unbidden fun...i'm just trying something different...

I weigh each grief I meet
  With analytic eyes;
I question if it weighs as much as mine,
  Or is it less.

I ponder if they upheld it long,
  Or did it just begin?
I cannot recall the day of mine,
  The pain feels so old.

I wonder if life is pain,
  And if they have to try,
And if they ould choose between them,
  They would rather choose to live and not die.

I wonder if when the years have passed-
  Thousands of them- on the question
Of early pain, if such a pass
  Could make them stop;

Or would they let the pain continue
  Through centuries ahead,
Enlightened to a greater pain
  With the comparison of Love.

Many grieve, I am told;
  The reason is more-
Death comes only once,
  And only closes the eyes.

There's many greifs-
  A type called gloom
There's exile of familar eyes
  In familar land.

And I may not know the kind
  Correctly, to me
A stabbing comfort it emits
  In passing Calvary,

To comment the style of the cross,
  Of those whom are outcast

Christ comes in rem,
His light crowding the eyeballs
while he stands as if to heal
an old shell's cracks and bruises
to make it whole again, 
but he slowly rises from rem,
without a touch so promised,
and glides over the pillows,
through the screened window,
into the night, scattering prayers
over the old quarry up the road
where crutches are neatly piled.

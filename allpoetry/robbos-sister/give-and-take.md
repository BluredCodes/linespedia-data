And I will take these things away from you:
I will give you love
But without the golden case
The space in which love sings.
I will give you joy
But extract the sweetness in its call
And all it does possess inside.
I will give you hope, 
But not the dawn. Nor sun,
The one that rises from the dark

And I'll replace such things for you:
Instead of space I'll give you shame, 
Your name they'll whisper on the wind.
Sweetness will have deceit 
Repeat the pattern in your waking days
Renew the dawn with guilt
And tilt the light away from all your stars.

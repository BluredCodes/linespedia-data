I know you’ll all moanOnce my poem is doneListen to thisBeing thin isn’t funMy friends think I’m kiddingWhen I say I want to be biggerOh yeah sure you doThey giggle and sniggerTell you what they replyI’ve got more than I needIf you want to be biggerGo have a good feedBut I've tried eating moreAnd it don’t seem to workI hate being thin It drives me berserkMy shoulders are bonyMy ribs are on showTheres a gap between my thighsWhere the light shouldn’t showI’d like to have cleavageNot too much you knowBut enough to exciteAnd a little to showBut all of this moaning Won’t change a darn thingI’m stuck with a lifeOf being pathetically thin

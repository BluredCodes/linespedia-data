A long time ago
You left an imprint
In the beach
Where you rolled
With your love.
You pulled the blanket
Of the tide
Tightly
Over you and

Your imaginary lover.

You made it good
To have no form
That I could call my own.
The only form
Clay
Can posess is that which others give it.
I could not complain when I looked up
And saw who had made me be.

The wings you had upon your back,
Had lovingly been copied for me.

Just imagine how it felt when
I punched through to the other side.
My arm was coated in liquid silver and
My mouth filled with butterflies.
I saw
I felt
I thought
I knew
That a cry emerged from an inverse world.
But when I looked up with red web eyes
I saw a rusting man with a river
Of cotton spilling forth for words.
A silver hand was at his throat.

Where was the girl who knew she was gold?
She danced with eagles on the mountain top
As a killer licked his lips.
She cleansed herself beneath a waterfall
Of black rose petal lies
Whilst powder-glass gales
Stroked her serpentine face.
A battle-weary warrior fell face down in the river
With liquid silver wounds.
His body stayed where it lay
And she carried him downstream.

I saw
I felt
I thought
I knew
A vague visitation from a visceral world.
But when I saw what I thought I knew
I felt nothing but remorse.

The smell of pine assaults my nose.
Mirror this,
Before the cataclysm,

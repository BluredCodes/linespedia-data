I know you are just mere hallucination
That if I could summon enough strength
To look back, it would take you only seconds to reappear
As if you’ve known forever that I would give this last glance.

Just like an epiphany, you come to me,
You perplex me, you overwhelm me,
Yet I know you are not there;
That if I reached a hand to your metallic skin,
I would be caressing only the memory 
That still tingles on the tips of my fingers,
Like winter fights to cling to flesh. 

The sounds of snow echo,
And I fight to remember these sounds of you
Of your silence,
And of my shaky voice, 
Afraid to catch you off-guard.

Again as epiphany, you re-emerge,
And bring to me in a warming silence
A thought, a wonderful ghostly resonance.
If I wished for your haunting to cease
I could simply break this silence,
And walk away, with out this last glance.
But, I would rather caress the memory

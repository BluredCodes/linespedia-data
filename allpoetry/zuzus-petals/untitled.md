The sun rises 
Over me 
Like it does
Over bronze statues,
Making my cheekbones
Appear golden,
Consumed by 
A fiery blaze
Of fine silks.
But my eyes,
So hollow,
Seem non-existent
In their sockets.

I blink, 
Shade my eyes,
Then crouch over
Waiting for the rain
To come. 

Finally,
A breath of relief
Turns loose
The storm,
Like a hiccup 
Of diamond
Misted fingertips
And dew 
In the
Early hours

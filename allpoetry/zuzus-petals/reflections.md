Each time
I crane my neck
To see the small
Plastic lipstick stains
On the mirror
I remember 
The reflection
I no longer see
In the frame 
On my bathroom wall.
Each time 
I strain my ears 
I can hear the silence, 
Which used to ask, 
“Have you ever
Seen yourself 
With your eyes closed?” 
Each time I remember, 
I remember 
Only what remains 
Of the lipstick 
And the scent of 
Lotion knees 
That used to kiss 
Each star struck curl.
Each time 
My eyelids shut 
I wonder if there 
Has been a time 
Where I have seen myself 
With my eyes closed. 
I try to recall the memory 
But all I 
Can envision 
Are ruins
And shattered reflections
Of white,
Only white.Just to let anyone who cares know, this poem is about a very dear friend of mine who I can say I miss very much.

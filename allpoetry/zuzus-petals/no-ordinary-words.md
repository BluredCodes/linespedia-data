No ordinary words could make your lips
Flush white then burn so deeply.
No innocent kisses could be softly lain 
On a barren path that stretches past the horizon.
Only the roundest curves of your face
Could mimic a dissected rose,
With velvet petals caressing silk
And stems stripped of their thorns.
You reveal to me each cloaked moment;
Too tranquil to disturb with any sort of noise,
Like the tick-tock of time flying away from anxious hands.
All you allow to emanate throughout these hollow walls
Is the moaning of the flooded sea;
Iron waves, smashing the shivering shore. 
But no words, does this silence break
No thoughts, or tears does it bring or take away.
No kisses does it rest
Upon this plane that expands far beyond 
The point where sky meets earth

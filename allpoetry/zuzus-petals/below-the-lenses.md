Below the lenses
I have seen only brown.
But, as I look twice,
Or perhaps for the millionth time,
I see clouds
With milky swirls
That bring only darkness.

Time has 
Left those wells to settle
And separate;
To see,
Or to see nothing.
It takes all my strength
To divert my hands
From smoothing out
Those liquid-wrinkles.

I form reservoirs 
Of my own
And dread the day
When they are drained;
When the boundaries
Between color and white
Make my soul
Too difficult to navigate,
Even for my own
Shaky hands.

Perhaps below the lenses
I will be seen as only brown
Until my razor edge
Is faded,
And a dark and violent storm
Can’t even break its surface.I always notice that as people age sometimes their eyes begin to get cloudy...that's where this poem mainly comes from (I guess its mostly about getting closer to death)-zuzu (ps i haven't written in so long...it's good to be back hehe)

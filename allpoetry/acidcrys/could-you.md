Could you blame me
if I touched your face,
and felt so much more
than I could ever see...

The faded ribbons
falling from golden curls,
as blood from a busted knee
stains concrete rainbows.

Could you blame me
if I listened to your words,
and couldn't hear anything
but the beat of your heart...

The cries forgotten
bleeding from a child's soul,
as a sung lullaby softly
fades into an unending nightmare.

Could you blame me
if I yearned for your taste,
and drowned in the need
deeper than anything I've known...

The absent love
like thorns on a budding rose,
pricking naive little fingers
hidden behind worn leather gloves.

Could you blame me
if my mind filled with doubt,
and my heart ached with pain
as I decided to walk away...

The stitchs torn open,
a wound growing larger,
and a scar never healed.

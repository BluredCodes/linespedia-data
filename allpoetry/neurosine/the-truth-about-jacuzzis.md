Like a chocolate valium,
a vivid jacuzzi dream,
I thought about existentialism,
crushed it into sad pieces.
While it went through me.
I cloistered around pointless death.
I sensed a pointless lie.
Still crying over some imagined loss,
I pay the cost.
Happily.
For I will jacuzzi again.

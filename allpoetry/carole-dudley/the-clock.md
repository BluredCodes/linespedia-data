The clock that ticks upon the wall
     ticks on in spring and fall, and fall.

It ticked for him; it ticked for me
     as if to tell eternity.

It ticked when to his eyes I clung,
     and when the New-Year song was sung,

And when he died in summer's glow,
     the pendulum sighed of snow, of snow.

It preaches of time to a barren house,
     to vacant rooms, a feathery mouse.

For you, my love, whom I adored,
     its ticking 

Stopped.

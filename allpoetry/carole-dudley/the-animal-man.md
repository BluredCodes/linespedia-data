The animal, man, is as blind as a stone,
As deaf as a fence post,
As dense as a bone.

He sees very little of that which is
Subtle; the world of the hornet is less
In a muddle.

He tunnels through time like a mole in
Its burrow, though time is an ocean
Awash with tomorrow.

His food is too salty, or sweet as
Molasses; the air, from his workings
Grows thicker with gasses.

His senses grew puny when cortex cerebral
He grew in his calcium cranium.
From my point of view, as a sensitive 
Beast, I wish he had grew a 
Geranium.

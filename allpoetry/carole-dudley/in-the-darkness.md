---
title: "In the Darkness"
writer: "Carole Dudley"
slug: "in-the-darkness"
source: "AllPoetry"

iam
blanketed in darkness
OM.

The rain is breathing
in the darkness.

Like a waterfall,
a heavenly benediction,
a stream plunging from
that precipice
to which no mind
has climbed.
The rain is breathing.
OM.

iamgoingintotheoneness,
the hinted unity
of man and stone
dragging this frightened
flesh, this
reticent Child.
OM.

iam burnt by event
by pain,
by foreshadow of
my first breath
in eternity.
iam
inthe darkness,
breathing.
OM.
We slide along
The axis which 
holds infinity and
all its concerns.
mutable, through
shades of good and
evil,
moved by
love and hate,
sanely inflexible,
lunatic free.
Balance, balance.
OM

In the symphony 
of existence,
i pipe one note
once
softly.
OM.

The best of myself
moves
in sleep,
tapping the fevered river
of unconscious being.
i listen to its
flow, my heart
beating its delicate drum.
OM.

Pride sickens the virtuous boy,
but he dies of
humility.
Lust ignites the furnace of
life, but
life drifts to ashes
from the fires of lust.
OM.

Life is death, and death
is life.  Darkness is
the womb of life.
Darkness is OM and
perfect peace.

Here in the darkness 
under this rain,
the waters of my Self
would draw into
the crucible
of my creation.
OM.

We, who are here as in
a dream
will soon  be grievously
ill, and in our illness
cured of this life.
Atman, be with us when
we awaken.
OM.

In this darkness
integrity of atom
embraces us all,
but, constricted by form,
I see dawn creep near
from the Eastern hills,
tearing 
the flimsy bodice
of my mind.

Light blinds sight, is
ominous with lurking
cruelties.
OM.

Dawn is birth,
and the beginning.
The blade is unsheathed.
Joys.  Passion.  Sorrow.
Longing.
Strivings after wind.
OM.

Near us, a presence
throbs in 
the darkness.
Oh, rain, silken on
my soul,
rain on.

OM.

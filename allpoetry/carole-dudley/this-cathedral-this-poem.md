title: "This Cathedral, This Poem"
writer: "Carole Dudley"

Going down through consonant
     and vowel
to seagull bone of poem,
filligree of shadowed lace,
where contrasts flicker
     confusingly,

I will creep into
this arch
     of light, this poem,
a spider weaving in the lofty
     vault.

Spawn of whore angel and
     cobbler king, 
I will
gnaw hungrily into the
heart of God.

...and streak beyond God...
and beyond...
and beyond, into the
loveless and loving universe,
slick with truth.

This cathedral of poem,
writhing and shivering like
a shackled maid against
the sultan's shaft,
would be a saber
slashing
darkness from the heart.

So I will enter you, humble
     and reverent, oh,
Poem, in half light of color and
sound, my tongue's
cathedral.

Cage of glass and stone
Cage of word and bone
Cage of impotence,
Master mason of my skull,
build me a godhead nave
to enter in.  I trail my 
fingers in the marbled font
of phrase.

Pour me now into God's veins,
and fill him with my little
light again
that I may be wrapped 
in perfect and eternal 
love, and the cyclops
of longing,
keening for me,
will be full.

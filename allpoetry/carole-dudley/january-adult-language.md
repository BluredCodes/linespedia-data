This grey morning, its winter grass
hairy as an old
whore's lip,

and chilly as a dead cunt
stares at me,
and I stare back.

It sees my seed of myth
wedged in its crevasse
of sodless rock;

I see its proferred plate
of naked souls taken
(like a good host)

from the shelf of time
for my consumption,
condiments rolled

in sugar, including
Balzac,
and dark Alice.

Looming
in my path, now,
seed pods clinging to

the lifeless branch,
rumor of promise,
debunked.

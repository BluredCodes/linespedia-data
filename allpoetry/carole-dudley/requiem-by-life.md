title: "Requiem By Life"
writer: "Carole Dudley"
slug: "requiem-by-life"

At your last death by slow disintegration,
Of hope, and thought, and every freedom,
I watched a pond's eye wink at the 
Bottomless sky, goldfish flicking through
The iris like sensual thought,
And the grass came everywhere green
To my eyes,
Befuddled with sunshine,
And endings.

I saw a big dog horned with the flight
Of meadowlark and dizzy with flower,
Whose spirit had ripened cavorting in
The galaxy of Now's tumbling sensations,
Neurons firing like buckshot, leaping
Toward me to halt in my aura of
Question, to give me his eyes
Brimming with Soul.

And I saw that I, alone, of living things,
Was helpless to tell your requiem,
Though all life pulsing in this high, bright
Dream,
Could speak your smile.

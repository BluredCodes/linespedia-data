(written in memory of Deshaun Brey, R.I.P.)

He was a proud black Mauslim, Moorsh American by religion and blood he spoke~
teaching the hopeless that there still lay hope, it just lay domant 
~outside of the streets and away from all this dope
He helped crackheads into rehabilitation and into the studios~
a true twenty first century hero 
~ask with respect and he shall teach you what he knows
Yet on the wrong side of the tracks, the pushers are losing business~
(where hustlers compete) to see which one ends up the richest
And the citizens pay with their lives, kingpins snipe assasins for investments~
while the citizens savior
~is murdered for cutting into their business
(You may be gone, but the vision is never lost)

Respectfuly Dead,
~Timmy G.

title: "An Umbrella From The Rain"

Bright lights
	touched with a bit of glamor,
	But behind that facade
	comes a forgotten tale,
	Lost in the pain
	of the streets torn in blood
	with the forgotten clawing to survive,
	Not sure of where their last meal comes,
	And where is the caring?
	Cold is the eyes of the unbending stranger,
	One wonders why?
	But when you have walked
	as those with torn clothes
	and haunted eyes,
	Never judge
	but instead only hold compassion,
	Hold out an open hand,
	You never know
	maybe fate turns its tide,
	And you might be needing the same understanding
	of one who holds an umbrella
	from the rain.
	July 17, 1996

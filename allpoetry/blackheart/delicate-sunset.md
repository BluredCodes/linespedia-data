Emerging,
  From behind a starlit brawl,
  Of fingers and thumbs.
  Writhing with the moment,
  The bliss of when it comes.
  Climax met with sunburst,
  A race across my chest,
  Is the sun upon the horizon,
  As we rest...

And I emerge from behind your pillow,
  Smile in your direction,
  Gently sweep the hand upon,
  The one i call perfection.
  A graceful tone of pretty pink,
  Wrapped around my thighs,
  I place myself delicately,
  While i stare into your big brown eyes.

Snuggled against your chest,
  I realise every wonder,
  Aching round your every curve,
  Smooth and very slender.
  Blood so coarse within my veins,
  You arch with every motion,
  Back and forth i meet your eyes,
  As the sun would kiss the ocean.

I moisten my lips to tickle your skin,
  I linger on your cheek,
  I find my way onto your lips,
  As we kiss i try to speak.
  To tell you, you're amazing,
  You are brilliant beast, i cannot tame,
  As once again we do it...
  Over and over and over againThis is a take on Unclassified's poem (and uses her poem as parts of this one), "Delicate Sunrise."

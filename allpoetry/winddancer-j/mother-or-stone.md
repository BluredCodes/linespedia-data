“Mother or Stone”

You don't owe me anything, not even a son’s care.
I'm just a woman who gave you life, with pain did I bare.
Don't bother to share a moment, when you see I'm all alone.
Just think of me as a statue, something made out of stone.

I don't have feelings to hurt and I don't need your love.
Dust me off if you like or leave me on the shelf up above.
Keep reminding yourself that you don't owe me anything.
When I die, just burn me, and throw me to the four winds.

You don't owe me anything, not a moment of your busy time.
Forget that as a child I read stories that sometimes didn't rhythm.
So go on with you life, have fun and forget that I even live.
For someday you'll have children, and the same to you they will give.

Am I your mother or a piece of marble stone?
Sitting here hoping you’ll visit so I won’t be alone.
I know you’re your own man going on your own way.
But, it won’t hurt you a bit to think of me from day to day.

She went through life a mile a minute
Tasting the world like she had just been born
She ran into a heart-hardened glass wall
And came to see it as reality

She beat her fists against the glass
Till it shattered and cut into her
She lay herself bare for a moment of rest
And found herself sobbing on the floor

One minute she was a child,
Carefree... now she was a vagabond
Rough and worldly and still beautiful
Even though she didn't know it

A life, interrupted by madness
A trail of her salty tears
A truth, interrupted by beauty
A girl, interrupted by truth

I can still taste her salt on my lips
As I kiss her cold cheek good night
I have been interrupted from love
By death, that serenityto ellen

In my brain, a nursery
I shush my fears with wet kisses
A cotton candy world.
In my head, a petting zoo
A million people in and out
My soul needs a "Do Not Touch" sign

My inner child is forty five. 
A little boy, androgynous,
Reminisces, wishing he could run
Far away with me.
My inner child is a criminal lawer
Sentencing me to another day

In a cotton candy world
Nothing has to make sense
I guess that's how I get along. 
When your psyche is such 
A terrible mess, 
I am a child in a grown up's palace

In my tears, a sweetness
A candy for the inner child
Who would have it every day, 
A sweet sadness that is me
Fed to that tiny dancer
Residing in my candy house

I go into my room
To see how doth my wee bairn fair
And such a sight of magnificence I do find there
Burrowed between satiny red sheets
My little love's heart beats as he sleeps so deep
My whole being swells with pride and joy
As I gaze upon my first born child
An auburn haired, green eyed little boy
He has arched eyebrows like me
And speaks with a voice soaked with sensuality
Oh, he is so sweet
He charms everyone he meets
He smiles in a knowing way
And lours you in with his eyes
That have so much to say
Donovan is also very reserved
He is refined, debonair and very undeserved
For I was a bratty child
Who through tantrums and was wild
But my husband was very good
So I suppose our son also would be
I love him so entirely much
He is my everything and such
  I adore this mother business.

Lady Dame


One afternoon I went into my room while my son was sleeping, and I felt so 
overwhelmed with love for him that I went straight to the computer and wrote this little poem. For all those who are mothers, I am sure that you understand. My son has changed my life. I could not have asked for a better child. How did I get so lucky?!

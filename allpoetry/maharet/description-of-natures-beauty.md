In the bushes I lay, 
tangled through-out may 
Happiness in spring, 
I hide away during winter days 
Upon my bed of red
the drops of water are safely sheltered 
trays of green, 
catch the morning mist 
and fallen rain 
At night I close my gentle wings, 
I curl up and sleep 
My liquor of life is simply water 
and the sun's warmth and heat 
A gift for the ladies, 
my beauty symbolizes many things 
Although I may be pretty, 
My unseen tiny knifes 
of sharpness might harm one's fragile flesh 
Touch me carefully 
and feel my smooth lace 
I am not a thing to taste 
But smell the sweetness 
of my cherry face
All for a person 
to be more happier 
I am mother earth's most beautiful child
I am A rose.

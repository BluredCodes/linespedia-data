the difference of a kiss

(a poem of J.W.)



how different it

was I think to

kiss Adam

rather than

Eve.



she was sweet

and unfeeling to me

and left me more

empty than some

upended wine bottle.



and he… stirred me,

pushing past that

exultant edge of consciousness

like the brilliant violence

of a champagne cork,

popped.



these two, Venus

and Adonis, fought

over my love nightly and

i sat by as he

slew her with a single

glance.



no, not hands bound,

rather, i think

I offered great-silent prayers

and supplications



for her death…



and for

Beau’s fatal kiss.

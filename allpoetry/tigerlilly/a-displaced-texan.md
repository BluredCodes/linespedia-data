title: "a displaced Texan"

an August day dawned hot and bright
in a scorching Texas way
fierce sun beat down while
dry winds kept the clouds at bay

by noon the skies were darkening
streaked with black and green
thunderheads towered high
shot through with lightening keen

my place was at the old Big Spring
an ancient watering place
like iron filings to a magnet drawn
i come to see storms race

standing on the tallest stone
looking toward the sky
with arms outstretched calling
to some unknown how and why

as wind whipped long hair
lashes eyes then takes breath away
heat sears the skies
while thunder heralds my death's day

but standing clinging to this rock
this heart of Texas plains
sandstone and rattlers call to me
soaked now by summer's rains 

though since then i've journeyed
far away to the hills of Tennessee
lost spirits from the Alamo
still are saying "home" to mei do get homesick now and then...

title: "Winter Lover"

In my pool of quiet time, the hushed silence
of first snowfall magnifies the word 'alone'
as I think of you.

I'd not thought to see the racing moon revealing 
scenes of such quiet beauty without your eyes
to share them.

Surrounded by this down plucked from snow goose
clouds, pain comes to smother, isolate.

If only every heartache could be cloaked in the same
way as winter's weariness is hidden now from sight. 

Earth had been lying bare, leaf blanket long since 
swept away by gales...exposing frost heaved emotions 
gripped in tundra cold.

The Ice King sitting on North's throne must have taken
pity on Mother Earth in her brown nakedness, clothing
her as a lover would in virgin white garments.

Evergreen branches bending low with their burden, form
a canopy where I see him kneel above her, criss-crossing
her body with kisses evidenced in night creatures
paw-prints.

Voyeur, self-accused, I watched them lay together then,
hand-fasted, throughout the long night...he caressing 
her with wind sighs, she absorbing all his moisture.

There in the tenderness of Ice king and Mother Earth,
somewhere behind the bare tree pallisade of my being, 
let me harbor hope, even so little as that of a 
cardinal's wing flashing against the snow...

As she yields herself once again to his embrace, I 
will know that these throes of love in moonlight can

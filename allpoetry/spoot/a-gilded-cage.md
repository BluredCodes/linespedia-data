A sheltered girl in a sheltered home,
no freedom of speech to call her own,
locked and trapped in a gilded cage,
nothing to do but nurse her rage,
doesn't she see that she can get out?
but no, she holds to her belief without a doubt
that the only way out is death or insanity,
so now all she has to do is to find the key,
the way to unlock the door to immortality,
to find out whether it's bliss or blankness, a bitter irony,
for all of her life has been a blankness, a joke,
just her pulling at the ebony yoke
of her parent's wishes and dreams for her,
hadn't they realized that they might smother
a young girl's dreams for freedom and love,
and yet, now her only wish is for help from above,
holding up a knife, staring at this poem,

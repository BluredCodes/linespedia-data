My mother has a bingo brain
She sees numbers in her sleep
If she can't get her bingo
She'll even start to weep

She's one of the bingo junkies
You see them round about
They are usually in there sixties 
And they love to scream and shout

They get their bingo fixes
Three or four times a week
You see them packed in bingo halls
And hear there pens go squeak 

No one can ever help them
They are lost forever to the bingo balls
And if you don't want to catch it
Stay away from the bingo calls 

I did once try to rescue her
But alas it was too late
I started to mark her bingo card
It nearly sealed my fate

I shouted, "house"
And I was hooked 
The pound signs lit my eyes
But then I ran for my life
They saw through my disguise

Never will I venture 
In to them hallowed halls
People heed my warning

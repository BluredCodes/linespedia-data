Apology	
By Christopher Cooper

I didn't mean to lead you on.  
Even though 
Our soft cotton cheeks have pressed together
And the tops of our breasts caress each other's 
From the enfolding of our arms.

I never thought 
I would make your thighs moist
Or your flesh tingle with obsession
From a friendly embrace. 
Such love for me is found only in fantasy 
Not in a town of ninety-seven.

Embers now fill my children's ears 
And my husband withholds his sandpaper kisses 
After overhearing the stories 
From around unseen corners.  
Sorry, but it is my husband's lips I long for.How is my female voice

title: "Young Yeye'"

Young girl,sweet sixteen
 Struttin' down the neighborhood block
 Keys ready in one hand
 School books in the other
 Inhailing the soul food smells of 
 Chittlins, 
 Gumbo,
 Cheeba,
 N' day old trash
 While front lawns are covered with drugheads on the grass
 Peepin' the street doctors across the street
 Callin' forth the street patients to get their daily feed
 As the sun beats on her cornbread skin
 And the hairs from her long ponytail surrender to the ass end of the day
 Rushin' to get to her front door
 Tryin' to avoid the liquor infested men on her way
 Always strikin' a reason to holla' at a young tenda'
 "Hey, Due'Wa...sure do love the way you gracefully stride down
 the street like the stallion you are." 
 When you gonna' let me do your "woman-profile" so you can stop
 bein' that cymalac child?"
 Ole' girl knows the routine of her after-school life
 Lil' man, house chores, then homework
 Mamma'll be home soon to rest her tired body
 From the work place of loddy-doddies
 Girl gotta' do what a girl gotta' do
 To stay out the system
 But to keep her ass in school
 Got visions n' goals to be somethin' spectacular
 Can't live the life of tricks n' hoes
 Indulgin' in ghetto conversations of what they call...vernacular
 Sistah got plans 
 To move outta' the ghetto and to raise a strong black man
 "Dear Lord, I give thanks for another day,
 my momma', and my child. 
 Bless us with good sleep, guidence and a place in heaven
 when our lives here are done.
 Do somethin' positive with me and my son on this life-filled journey...
 cause' I know it's just begun
 for alot lies before me.
 In Jesus name
 Amen"

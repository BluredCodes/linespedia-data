---
title: "Amorous moon"
writer: "turquin"
slug: "amorous-moon"

Soft and delicate, silver-white kiss
alighting upon Mother Nature's languid cheek;
such is the tender endearment
of her nocturnal lover.
His bright and solemn face
gazing down ever so lovingly
upon her reclining form,
from beyond the cloud.
'Ah, my darling, take sweet rest,'
he whispers to her in soft, aeolian breath,
'While, to you, a lullaby shall I sing
in the voice of the crickets;
to calm the rhythm of your heart.' -
Thus says the moon;
likened to a knight, in armour of silver,
as he rides high, upon his sable steed;
having once again disarmed
old fiery-tempered Sol
of his searing sword;
banishing him to his place
beyond heaven's western extreme.

There are no beasts in the supermarket,
no wildcats in the apartment next door.
It is too easy to forget.
That is why I need her.
Not in my bed, but just to walk by.
She knows.

As I sit in the cafe sipping skinny mochacinnos
(hold the cinnamon)
I can see her.
Long strides, steady gait,
hair billowing in the breeze of her own passage.
She knows that she reminds me
what it is to be a manI am attempting something different for me with this one.  Not really sure how it came out.  It's a lot more prose-like than I usually do.

This is not based on any one person, but it is came from a feeling that grew over me one day while sitting at a sidewalk cafe people watching.

title: "For Raven and her Farm"

Grounds and pond breathe
with deep antique purity.
Deer gazelle against the rainbow
of blanched fields and sun.
Mated steeds silhouette
a moon-dyed emptiness
as if eternal -
evening settles like 
homemade wine
as city slickers tour a dream.

Kitchen busy,
Raven’s father 
stones the gathering -
then walks to the barn
and smiles -
he walks to the horizon,
caller candle bobbing
in spite of its age,
smiling in its glow.

When he smiles
the serenity spreads
and blasphemers get stoned 
on the ecstasy 
of Raven’s innocence.

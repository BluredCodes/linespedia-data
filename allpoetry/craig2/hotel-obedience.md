Elevator ride,
fingers in fist,
rehearsing touch in the room –
evening in April,
no one writing our song,
naked, all the lights on.

The demon holding me
is crafty and old,
hear it breathing 
in strobes, moving about,
plaiting the ropes
binding wrists in scorn. 

Waking in sweat 
my hands and you gone,
taken carefully home 
in red velvet bags
to spells and scrapbooks,
testaments to your porn.

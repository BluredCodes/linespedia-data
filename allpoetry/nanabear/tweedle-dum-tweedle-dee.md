Tweedle-dum and Tweedle-dee,Observers of humanity,Sit smugly as they sip their tea,Quite ignorant of how they could be.To stretch their minds 'would be a chore!'(They're apathetic at the core)Thus nothing of great Light they store,And what they have they think a bore.Together they will come and go,Great mysteries to never knowBecause their minds refuse to grow.'T is sad that this is ever so.Does your regard for Truth relateIn this respect, do you equateYour growth here, in this mortal state,With 'non-essential laws' you hate?If so, you're blind and fail to seeThe endless possibilityOf who you were, are, and could be...Like Tweedle-dum and Tweedle-dee.

---
title: "I'll Sing A Lullaby"
writer: "NanaBear"
slug: "ill-sing-a-lullaby"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/2508-Ill-Sing-A-Lullaby-by-NanaBear"

I'll sing a lullaby...
and weep with empty arms.
They could not keep your soul
from sleep when heaven called.

I'll sing a lullaby...
and weep through endless nights.
With longing, seek for God
to speak and answer, "Why?"

I'll sing a lullaby...
and weep with broken heart.
How can I greet the dawn
when sleep has never come?

I'll sing a lullaby...
but reap no harvest here.
My tender wheat was cut
to keep in higher spheres.

I'll sing a lullaby...
your cheek, a memory
that I will keep with love.
How fleet mortality!

I'll sing a lullaby...
to keep my promise, child,
that we will meet again...
and greet eternity!

May 16, 2001
~
Killing Time
~
Out from the House There Holds a Man
Like Spring in Darkness
Seasons of a Melting World
All Is in Disarray
With as Much Desire as a Man
About to Jump Twenty-five Stories
~
Beyond All this Gloom 
Trouble Certainly Does Brew
He Counts Denials 
Like Grains of Rice
Though I Know Someday He Shall Weep
Sands of Dunes Shift Daily among His Winds
~
And as Dead Hands Reach out to Hold
There Shall Be No Receiver to Grasp on
He Will Die in a Hiss of Flames
And Be Swallowed Beneath the Grave
Buried on a Shoulder of a Hill
Blackbirds and Lowly Creatures Will Become Listeners
~
The Water Will Hold the Sea Gulls for Me in Waiting
With Wind Blowing Cold 
Yet I Shall Wait out this Storm
I'll Sit by the Open Window of this World
And Wait
Observing All and Witness the Mystery of Fate
~
Time to Kill
Killing Time
Awaiting Stars Falling Cold from the Heavens
Hands Folded upon My Lap
Yes Killing Time
If I Only Knew Just How Much Time
~
The Men Will Stumble
With Spades in Hand Digging
Torn and Alone He Will Lie 
Snow Will Cover Him
Where Grasses Chose Not to Grow
The Dew Will Ring Each Day as Sun Sets upon Grave
~
No Deliverance for Those That Prey upon the Weak
He Left His Burning Bride Years Ago
As She Was Still Drenched in Lovely White
Her Voice Soft and Feathered
To Have Them Plucked Away One by One
Nothing Left but Pocked Flesh
~
The Wind May Be Strong Yes
But Sun Burns Brighter
Cold Can Bite at Flesh
But Sun Brings with it New Life
Rain Can Flood a  Grave
But Never Fill a Memory Filled So Much with Pain
~
Her Hands Were Beautiful 
Sleek Slender Fingers
Now Twisted with Age
Eyes So Blue Making Both Seas and Sky Jealous
Voice Once So Sweet
Now Bitter with Age That Cannot Go Away
~
Rest Will Come One Day
And She Shall Be Taken Sweetly Away
Life May Have Been Cruel
But Death Will Bring Everlasting Peace
You Will Be Loved Again
So Sadly it must Come with Sorrow to Those Who Love You
~
Thom

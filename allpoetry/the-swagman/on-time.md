On Time

Have you ever sat and thought of time
I have - But not as much as Einstein
He thought and thought and said it's relative
I think it's related to how long we live.

We've all as children on a journey in the car
Had reason to enquire - How long or how far?
And when told there were only ten minutes to go
Thought it an eternity and knew it would go slow.

When in our teens and going to high school
We'd think of term's end and times at the pool.
There were weeks to go - So long to wait
And so many other things still on our plate.

After we're married and the babes come along
We pray for months they'll be healthy and strong.
Now it doesn't seem very long I have to say
There's one running, one walking and one on the way.

It doesn't seem long - A decade or so
And the one that was running is about to go
Away from the nest - Away to stay
But he was only born yesterday.

Now the years go by - Hot and fast
And I often sit and think of the past.
It's not until we reach this age
That life tells us how to turn the page.

When the pages are turned and we take a look
And flip back through this lifelong book
We think of that car three quarters of a century ago
And how wrong we were thinking it would go slow.


By The Swagman

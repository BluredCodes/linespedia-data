Leaning over the marbled basin,
I feel you
taking up residence in a place 
that is not yours.
Stealing my strength,
sapping the nutrients from me.
Bitter tea, metallic taste 
of near-nausea 
clings to my palate, 
knowing it is needed.
I read the package insert 
with disdain.
EXPECT MODERATE CRAMPING
And I wait.
Uninvited you crept into me;
aging my youth, changing my life.
And I cry.
You are not welcome here.

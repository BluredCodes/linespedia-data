title: "Depth Perception"

There was a time when

You could not see me

Imprisoned as you were 

At the spectrum's lower end

Peering through eyes of standard issue

Perceiving a form

While sounding no depth

Was I a barren branch?

Or exotic plant in hostile soil?

You did not know

Nor did I

For I shared your prison

Though on balcony's perch

Hearing legends of higher wavelengths

Survival became life in an inner room

Annexed to a mansion near yet far

I departed, carrying my prison

Vision improved

After a thousand attenuated years of glacial pace

Showing a door locked from within

Emerging without smile or tear

Walking through spacious rooms

I returned

Having become what I always was

Confirmation was life in your eyes

A wordless testimony of depth perception

We were gratified

And free

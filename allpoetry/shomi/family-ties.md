Mother’s little helper spilt salt on the tableshe was thinking of tigersand her brother, the astral travellerhas connections with the third worldhe’s been riding in spacecraftsand daddy on a dead-end jobthinks every day’s an epilogueand mama’s crossed the border linetrying to keep the neighbour’s eyecinderella sits and draws veilsover maids in fashion magazinesand dolly’s tossed her bedroom keyto the stars flashed on  her colour screenthe silver-headed daggers bleedthe circus stares across the streetas mother’s little helper dreams of tigersand her brother leaves for Mars.

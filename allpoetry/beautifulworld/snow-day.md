I woke up this morning with a whine and a frown
My feet frozen to the floor the second I put them down
From the window in my room, I could see the glistening white snow
I threw on my robe and scrambled down the stairs to get a closer look
At this glory below

Stomping the snow off my feet, a shower I took
Then I stuck my clothes in the dryer, though the dryer soon shook
For I had neglected to remember one simple thing:
In order to dry something, it needs to be warm
And the dryer (made in 1980) does not work like spring

Giving up on the dryer, I turned on my radio, hoping to hear about this winter storm
Looking out the window, I could see the tiny flakes continuing to form
Timothy Christian, Benet Academy, all religious schools
Then telephone rang, an anwer to my dream
A voice recording from my beloved school

School was cancelled, so it seemed

A million thoughts raced through my head
Could this be true? Was the school day truely dead?
Yes, I was sure, today was today
And all I can say is

---
title: "Ancha munakuiki"
writer: "Giuseppe Butera"
slug: "ancha-munakuiki"
source: "AllPoetry"
url: "https://allpoetry.com/poem/48894-Ancha-munakuiki-by-Giuseppe-Butera"
---

I really needed 
A huge lucid boeing
Ejaculate myself in your viscera
Pacha mama
So a long journey of love
Can follow the anguishing waiting
Of a few nine months
Of pregnancy
(or agony)
and useless and naked
(but yet alive)
like a child
leave me just strapped
by high-ceiling wind
and music
of kena and charango
seduced
crying chants
and sweet lines
and insult
and in your language you’d learn
I love you
(ancha munakuiki)
and dancing
and cherishing you eternal skin
soon to be twenty
and drinking the istant flame
of your lips
in a scent of chewing gum?

Don’t you sink your head
Please
In a scandal quicksand
To evade uncertainty
Of not knowing the answer
To the questions you question me
Let the time drive
An infinite list
Where I’m another name
So it can solve
In silence
The agressive uselessness of so much why

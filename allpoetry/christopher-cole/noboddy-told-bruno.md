No one told Bruno

Once there was a man who wore plain tights
Who strode upon the stage of life
On grandmas set of black and white
I sat in awe to watch him fight
For truth and justice and what was right
A paragon of virtue in a world of strife
He conquered evil with all his might
A muscular man with Faith and morals
A Lone Ranger who settled the quarrels
A lonely child on the emotional border
Because of him my world had order
When in the arena I sat in the first row
I cheered for my hero and cried out …go Bruno!

Defeating monstrous foes who didn’t play fair
Good triumphed over evil year after year
A giant of a man this true American hero
He had yet to learn all this meant zero
In a world changing he soon was discarded
Like a silent film star who couldn’t transition	
In a submarine world with political derision
With multicolored tights and no black and whites
His speech faltered and discouragement set in
He got his face slapped and his hand spit in
By the new Barnum who said he couldn’t win
With an old school hero stuck in the past
In a fast paced world seeking color and sin

Broken hearted he would slowly fade away
And down in my bones to this very day
His memory keeps me alive and yet I feel
No one ever told him or I it was unreal
So into an uncertain world we all did travel
Without my hero I soon did unravel
Now as an old man I pick up his load
I lift iron weights in my basement abode
In the mirror my muscles they do explode
Often there I see his face and never debate
The battle for Truth and Love over hate
As real for him as for me if you must know
In my heart I still cheer for …Sammartino

Christopher Cole

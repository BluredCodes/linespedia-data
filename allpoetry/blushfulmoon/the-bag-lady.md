The little old lady rushes across the street,
Hoping once she is there she will find something to eat
She stoops over from the weight of her garbage bag
That's slung over her shoulder,and tied with rags
It's her worldly belongings she carries around
With trinkets or such, she's either stolen or found

She travels each day, many a mile,
No one looks at her, nor even smiles
She looks for a place to lay down and rest,
A cardboard box is what she will find at best,
She once had a good life, but before her it flashed
Now it's gone,

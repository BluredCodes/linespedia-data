Under the sun with childlike laughter
The small child grabs the blackberries through the vines of green.
Then mama's fingers with a graceful grasp
Shows eager hands how to pick vines clean,
Later on that day the harvest is done
In big brass ringed jars,
Now the pantry is overflowing
For months to come on winters end
The will have blackbery jam when outside it's snowing
Day old crust leaves a small trail,
But a smile appears on mama's face
As she recalls the silver pail
Jars of blackberries well now perserved,
Holds  blackberry jam and many a smile
Whenever her table is served

A light glows in the window,
hig above the echoing traffic
Evening breezes come through the windows,
Rushing past the yards of lace,
There she sits alone, softly smiling
While her memory fades,

A little dog nestled in her plump lap,
A twinkle from her deep blue eye's
Wrinkled fingers sweep shades of grey
Whispering words of sad sighs

Her little friend id off to sleep
And yaps goodnight along the way,
But no other soul ever passes by
Not for one minute in the day
All the while she glances past
The tree's to passerbys
As she hangs her head and cries

Maybe tomorrow someone will take notice
Look up and wave hello,
Put a smile back on her face,
And faith back in her soul

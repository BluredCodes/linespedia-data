A Kentucky Dawn

Enshrouded mist lingers across the meadows,
Ghostly shadows of trees stand in rows
A fence, that bluebirds sit upon gleams with dew droplets
The whiporwill and bobwhites song fades softly
As day breaks through;
In the far off distance, dogs bark, and a tractor sputters to life
As we too awake from dreams-laden
slumbered sleep
We can see the earth's beauty
Suspended,
Glistening in the morning light.This one has been published
